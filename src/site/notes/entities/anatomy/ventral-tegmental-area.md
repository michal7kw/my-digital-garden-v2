<h1>Ventral Tegmental Area</h1>
<h2>Description</h2>
<p>The ventral tegmental area (VTA) is a group of dopaminergic neurons located in the midbrain, near the midline on the floor of the midbrain. It is the origin of the mesolimbic dopamine pathway (VTA → nucleus accumbens) and the mesocortical pathway (VTA → prefrontal cortex). These pathways are the neural substrate of reward, motivation, and reinforcement learning. The VTA is activated by natural rewards (food, social interaction, sex) and is hijacked by drugs of abuse, making it central to addiction neuroscience.</p>
<h3>Key Pathways</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mesolimbic pathway</strong> (VTA → Nucleus Accumbens) — Reward, pleasure, reinforcement</li>
<li><strong>Mesocortical pathway</strong> (VTA → Prefrontal Cortex) — Motivation, executive control, working memory</li>
<li><strong>VTA → Hippocampus</strong> — Novelty-driven memory encoding</li>
<li><strong>VTA → Amygdala</strong> — Emotional salience tagging</li>
</ul>
