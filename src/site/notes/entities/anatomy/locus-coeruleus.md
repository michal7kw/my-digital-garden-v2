<h1>Locus Coeruleus</h1>
<h2>Description</h2>
<p>Small brainstem nucleus that is the primary source of norepinephrine in the brain. Projects to virtually all cortical and subcortical regions, regulating arousal, attention, and stress response.</p>
<h3>Key Features</h3>
<ul>
<li>Contains only ~15,000-50,000 neurons per hemisphere</li>
<li>Projects to nearly every brain region</li>
<li>Activity correlates with arousal state (tonic vs phasic firing)</li>
<li>Among the first regions affected in Alzheimer's disease</li>
</ul>
