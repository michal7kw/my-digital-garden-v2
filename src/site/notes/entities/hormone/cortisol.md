<h1>Cortisol</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Cortisol is the primary glucocorticoid hormone in humans, produced by the zona fasciculata of the adrenal cortex under regulation of the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis. It follows a strong circadian rhythm with peak levels upon waking (cortisol awakening response) and a nadir at midnight. Acute cortisol release is essential for survival — mobilizing glucose, suppressing inflammation, and enhancing vigilance. However, chronic cortisol elevation from ongoing psychological stress, sleep deprivation, or HPA axis dysregulation drives a cascade of metabolic, immune, and neurodegenerative pathology.</p>
<h2>Key Functions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Glucose mobilization via hepatic gluconeogenesis</li>
<li>Anti-inflammatory and immunosuppressive effects</li>
<li>Cardiovascular tone maintenance (permissive effect on catecholamines)</li>
<li>Memory consolidation (acute) vs. memory impairment (chronic)</li>
<li>Bone metabolism regulation (excess causes osteoporosis)</li>
<li>Fat redistribution (chronic excess → visceral adiposity)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Circadian Pattern</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cortisol awakening response (CAR)</strong> — 50-75% surge within 30 min of waking</li>
<li><strong>Morning peak</strong> — Highest levels 6-9 AM</li>
<li><strong>Gradual decline</strong> — Falls throughout the day</li>
<li><strong>Nadir</strong> — Lowest at midnight</li>
<li><strong>Disrupted in</strong>: Shift work, chronic stress, depression, Cushing's syndrome</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Relevance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cushing's syndrome</strong> — Cortisol excess (truncal obesity, moon face, diabetes, osteoporosis)</li>
<li><strong>Addison's disease</strong> — Cortisol deficiency (fatigue, hypotension, hyperpigmentation)</li>
<li><strong>Chronic stress</strong> — Allostatic overload, hippocampal atrophy, metabolic syndrome</li>
<li><strong>Depression</strong> — HPA axis hyperactivity and flattened diurnal rhythm</li>
<li><strong>Insomnia</strong> — Elevated evening cortisol disrupts sleep onset</li>
<li><strong>Accelerated aging</strong> — Chronic elevation shortens telomeres</li>
</ul>
