# GABA

## Overview

GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid) is the brain's primary inhibitory neurotransmitter, present in approximately 40% of all synapses. It provides essential "braking" action to counterbalance excitatory neurotransmission (primarily glutamate). GABA dysfunction is implicated in anxiety disorders, insomnia, epilepsy, and many neurological conditions.

## Synthesis

```
GLUTAMATE
    ↓ (Glutamic Acid Decarboxylase - GAD)
    ↓ (Requires Vitamin B6/P5P as cofactor)
GABA
```

Key enzyme: GAD (Glutamic Acid Decarboxylase)
- GAD65: Primarily in nerve terminals
- GAD67: Throughout neuron

## Receptor Types

### GABA-A Receptors
- Ionotropic (ligand-gated chloride channel)
- Fast inhibition (milliseconds)
- Target of benzodiazepines, barbiturates, alcohol
- Multiple binding sites for modulation

### GABA-B Receptors
- Metabotropic (G-protein coupled)
- Slow, prolonged inhibition
- Target of baclofen
- Affects calcium and potassium channels

## Clinical Significance

### Low GABA
- Anxiety
- Insomnia
- Restlessness
- Panic attacks
- Seizures
- Chronic stress
- Racing thoughts

### GABA-Ergic Medications
- Benzodiazepines (Valium, Xanax)
- Gabapentinoids (Gabapentin, Pregabalin)
- Barbiturates
- Z-drugs (Ambien, Lunesta)
- Alcohol (initial effect)

## Supplements That Affect GABA

### Direct GABA Supplementation
- **GABA**: 250-750 mg (controversial BBB penetration)
- **Pharma-GABA**: Fermented form, may cross BBB better
- **Picamilon**: GABA + niacin (better BBB penetration)

### GABA Precursors
- **L-Glutamine**: Converts to glutamate, then GABA
- **L-Theanine**: Increases GABA, promotes alpha waves

### GABA Modulators
- **L-Theanine**: Increases GABA, reduces anxiety (100-400 mg)
- **Magnesium**: GABA-A receptor positive modulator
- **Taurine**: GABA-A agonist, calming
- **Valerian**: GABA-A modulation, sleep support
- **Passionflower**: GABA-A modulation
- **Lemon Balm**: GABA transaminase inhibition
- **Kava**: GABA-A modulation (potent, use cautiously)

### Cofactors for GABA Synthesis
- **Vitamin B6 (P5P)**: Essential cofactor for GAD
- **Zinc**: Modulates GABA receptors
- **Magnesium**: Supports GABAergic function

## GABA vs Glutamate Balance

The brain maintains critical balance:
- **Glutamate**: Excitatory (accelerator)
- **GABA**: Inhibitory (brake)

Imbalance causes:
- Excess glutamate → Excitotoxicity, anxiety, seizures
- Excess GABA → Sedation, cognitive impairment

Supporting balance:
- Adequate B6 for GABA synthesis
- Magnesium (reduces glutamate receptors)
- Taurine (modulates both)
- Glycine (inhibitory support)

## Lifestyle Factors

### GABA-Boosting
- Yoga (increases GABA 27% in one study)
- Meditation
- Regular exercise
- Deep breathing
- Adequate sleep
- Stress reduction

### GABA-Depleting
- Chronic stress
- Caffeine excess
- Alcohol withdrawal
- Poor sleep
- Inflammatory states
