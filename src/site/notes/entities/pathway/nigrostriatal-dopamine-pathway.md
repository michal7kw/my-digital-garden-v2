<h1>Nigrostriatal Dopamine Pathway</h1>
<h2>Description</h2>
<p>Dopamine pathway from substantia nigra pars compacta to the dorsal striatum (caudate nucleus and putamen). Controls voluntary movement via the direct and indirect basal ganglia pathways. Degeneration causes Parkinson's disease.</p>
<h3>Clinical Significance</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Parkinson's disease</strong> — 80%+ dopaminergic neuron loss before motor symptoms appear</li>
<li><strong>Dystonia</strong> — Striatal dopamine imbalance</li>
<li><strong>Tardive dyskinesia</strong> — D2 receptor supersensitivity from antipsychotics</li>
</ul>
