<h1>HPA Axis</h1>
<h2>Description</h2>
<p>The hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis is the central stress response system. The hypothalamus releases CRH (corticotropin-releasing hormone), which stimulates the anterior pituitary to secrete ACTH (adrenocorticotropic hormone), which in turn triggers cortisol release from the adrenal cortex. Chronic activation leads to allostatic overload, cortisol dysregulation, and downstream metabolic and immune dysfunction.</p>
<h2>Cascade</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Hypothalamus</strong> -- detects stress, releases CRH and AVP</li>
<li><strong>Anterior Pituitary</strong> -- CRH stimulates ACTH secretion</li>
<li><strong>Adrenal Cortex</strong> -- ACTH stimulates cortisol synthesis from cholesterol</li>
<li><strong>Negative Feedback</strong> -- cortisol inhibits CRH and ACTH release (when functioning normally)</li>
</ol>
<h2>Dysregulation Patterns</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Acute stress</strong> -- elevated cortisol, appropriate response</li>
<li><strong>Chronic stress</strong> -- sustained elevation, loss of circadian rhythm, DHEA-S depletion</li>
<li><strong>Burnout/exhaustion</strong> -- blunted cortisol response, adrenal fatigue (controversial)</li>
<li><strong>Allostatic overload</strong> -- cumulative energetic cost exceeds recovery capacity (Energy Resistance Principle)</li>
</ul>
