<h1>IL-6 Signaling Pathway</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Interleukin-6 (IL-6) is a pleiotropic cytokine that signals through two distinct mechanisms: classical signaling via membrane-bound IL-6R (restricted to hepatocytes, leukocytes, and megakaryocytes) and trans-signaling via soluble IL-6R (sIL-6R) shed by ADAM17, which enables IL-6 to activate virtually any cell expressing the ubiquitous co-receptor gp130. Both pathways converge on JAK1/JAK2 activation and STAT3 phosphorylation, driving transcription of acute phase proteins (CRP, fibrinogen, hepcidin), pro-inflammatory genes, and survival factors.</p>
<p>In rheumatoid arthritis, IL-6 is the dominant driver of systemic inflammation -- responsible for elevated CRP, anemia of chronic disease (via hepcidin-mediated iron sequestration), fatigue, and B-cell hyperactivity. In the synovium, IL-6 trans-signaling perpetuates chronic inflammation by recruiting monocytes and promoting Th17 differentiation. Therapeutic targeting with tocilizumab (anti-IL-6R) and sarilumab (anti-IL-6R) produces rapid normalization of CRP and dramatic improvement in systemic symptoms. The endogenous negative regulator SOCS3 provides feedback inhibition by targeting JAK-bound gp130 for degradation. Natural modulators include curcumin and omega-3 fatty acids, which reduce IL-6 transcription via NF-kB suppression.</p>
