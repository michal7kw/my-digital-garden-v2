<h1>Glutathione Metabolism</h1>
<h2>Description</h2>
<p>The metabolic pathway governing synthesis, recycling, and utilization of glutathione (GSH), the cell's primary intracellular antioxidant. Includes de novo synthesis from cysteine, glycine, and glutamate via gamma-glutamylcysteine synthetase and glutathione synthetase, as well as the glutathione redox cycle (GSH/GSSG) mediated by glutathione peroxidase and glutathione reductase.</p>
<p>Glutathione plays critical roles in:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Antioxidant defense</strong> -- neutralizes ROS and lipid peroxides via glutathione peroxidase</li>
<li><strong>Detoxification</strong> -- conjugation of xenobiotics via glutathione S-transferases</li>
<li><strong>Immune function</strong> -- required for lymphocyte proliferation</li>
<li><strong>Mitochondrial protection</strong> -- transported into mitochondria to protect ETC complexes</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Metabolites</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>GSH</strong> (reduced glutathione) -- active antioxidant form</li>
<li><strong>GSSG</strong> (oxidized glutathione) -- recycled back to GSH by glutathione reductase + NADPH</li>
<li><strong>Cysteine</strong> -- rate-limiting amino acid for GSH synthesis</li>
<li><strong>N-Acetyl Cysteine (NAC)</strong> -- cysteine donor that bypasses rate-limiting step</li>
</ul>
