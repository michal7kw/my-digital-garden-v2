<h1>Reward Processing</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Reward processing encompasses the neural computations involved in evaluating outcomes, anticipating future rewards, and using reward feedback to guide behavior. Wolfram Schultz's seminal work on dopamine neurons revealed that they encode reward prediction errors — the difference between expected and received reward — making them the biological substrate of reinforcement learning. The mesolimbic dopamine pathway from the ventral tegmental area (VTA) to the nucleus accumbens (NAc) is the core reward circuit, but it interacts extensively with the prefrontal cortex (value-based decision making), amygdala (emotional salience), and hippocampus (contextual memory of rewards).</p>
<h2>Reward Components</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Reward anticipation (&quot;wanting&quot;)</strong> — Incentive salience driven by mesolimbic dopamine (nucleus accumbens)</li>
<li><strong>Reward consumption (&quot;liking&quot;)</strong> — Hedonic pleasure mediated by opioid and endocannabinoid hotspots (ventral pallidum)</li>
<li><strong>Reward learning</strong> — Updating value estimates through prediction errors (VTA dopamine neurons)</li>
<li><strong>Motivation</strong> — Energizing and directing behavior toward rewards (dorsal striatum, ACC)</li>
<li><strong>Incentive salience</strong> — Attribution of motivational significance to reward-associated cues (Berridge's model)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Neurotransmitter Modulation</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dopamine</strong> — Reward prediction error signal; drives &quot;wanting&quot; (incentive salience) more than &quot;liking&quot;</li>
<li><strong>Endogenous opioids</strong> — Hedonic &quot;liking&quot; responses; mu-opioid receptors in ventral pallidum and NAc shell</li>
<li><strong>GABA</strong> — Inhibitory gating in ventral pallidum; disinhibition = reward signal propagation</li>
<li><strong>Glutamate</strong> — Cortical input to NAc encodes contextual and outcome information</li>
<li><strong>Serotonin</strong> — Modulates patience, behavioral inhibition, and long-term reward valuation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Relevance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Addiction</strong> — Hijacking of reward prediction error signaling; dopaminergic sensitization to drug cues</li>
<li><strong>Anhedonia</strong> — Reduced capacity for pleasure; core feature of depression (reward circuit hypofunction)</li>
<li><strong>Depression</strong> — Blunted reward anticipation and motivation (mesolimbic hypoactivity)</li>
<li><strong>Parkinson's disease</strong> — Impaired reward learning and motivation (dopamine depletion extends beyond motor)</li>
<li><strong>Schizophrenia</strong> — Deficits in reward prediction and motivation (negative symptoms)</li>
</ul>
