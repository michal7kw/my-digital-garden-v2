<h1>HSP90AB1</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>HSP90AB1 (Heat Shock Protein 90 Alpha Family Class B Member 1) encodes HSP90beta, the constitutively expressed cytoplasmic isoform of the HSP90 molecular chaperone family. HSP90beta is one of the most abundant proteins in the cell (comprising 1-2% of total cytoplasmic protein under non-stress conditions) and is essential for the folding, maturation, stabilization, and activation of a diverse set of client proteins including kinases, transcription factors, steroid hormone receptors, and E3 ubiquitin ligases. The HSP90 chaperone cycle is ATP-dependent and requires co-chaperones (Hop/STIP1, Cdc37, p23/PTGES3, Aha1) that regulate its ATPase activity and client loading.</p>
<p>HSP90beta functions as a homodimer with each monomer containing an N-terminal ATPase domain, a middle domain that interacts with client proteins and co-chaperones, and a C-terminal dimerization domain ending in a MEEVD motif that recruits TPR-domain co-chaperones. Unlike its stress-inducible paralog HSP90alpha (HSP90AA1), HSP90beta (HSP90AB1) is expressed constitutively at high levels and maintains protein homeostasis under basal conditions. It is essential for embryonic development, as HSP90AB1-knockout mice are embryonic lethal.</p>
<h2>Function</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Protein folding and maturation</strong>: Chaperones over 400 client proteins to their active conformations, including kinases (AKT, RAF, SRC, EGFR, HER2, CDK4), transcription factors (p53, HIF-1alpha, steroid receptors), and E3 ligases</li>
<li><strong>Proteostasis maintenance</strong>: Triages misfolded clients to the ubiquitin-proteasome system via the CHIP (C-terminus of HSP70-interacting protein) E3 ligase when folding fails</li>
<li><strong>Signal transduction</strong>: Stabilizes signaling kinases (AKT, RAF-1, SRC, EGFR) in their activation-competent conformations, maintaining signaling pathway integrity</li>
<li><strong>Stress response coordination</strong>: Works with NRF2 to coordinate the cellular stress response; HSP90 stabilizes NRF2 protein and enhances antioxidant gene expression</li>
<li><strong>Steroid hormone signaling</strong>: Maintains steroid hormone receptors (glucocorticoid, estrogen, androgen receptors) in ligand-binding-competent conformations</li>
<li><strong>Immune surveillance</strong>: Chaperones antigenic peptides for MHC class I presentation; extracellular HSP90 activates innate immune cells through TLR2/TLR4</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cancer</strong>: HSP90 is a validated oncology drug target; cancer cells are &quot;addicted&quot; to HSP90 due to chaperoning of numerous oncoproteins (HER2, BRAF-V600E, ALK, mutant p53, BCR-ABL); HSP90 inhibitors (geldanamycin derivatives, ganetespib, luminespib) show activity in clinical trials</li>
<li><strong>Neurodegenerative diseases</strong>: HSP90 modulates tau phosphorylation and aggregation in Alzheimer's disease; inhibition promotes tau degradation; HSP90 also influences alpha-synuclein aggregation in Parkinson's disease</li>
<li><strong>Autoimmune disease</strong>: Extracellular HSP90 acts as a DAMP/danger signal, activating innate immunity through TLRs; anti-HSP90 antibodies are found in rheumatoid arthritis and SLE</li>
<li><strong>Viral infections</strong>: HSP90 is co-opted by numerous viruses (influenza, HBV, HCV, Ebola) for viral protein folding and replication; HSP90 inhibitors show antiviral activity</li>
<li><strong>Drug resistance</strong>: HSP90 stabilizes drug-resistant mutant kinases (T790M-EGFR, gatekeeper mutants); HSP90 inhibition overcomes resistance to targeted therapies</li>
</ul>
