<h1>CYP2D6</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Cytochrome P450 2D6 is one of the most clinically important drug-metabolizing enzymes, responsible for the metabolism of approximately 25% of commonly prescribed drugs including codeine, tramadol, tamoxifen, SSRIs (fluoxetine, paroxetine), beta-blockers (metoprolol), and antipsychotics. CYP2D6 is highly polymorphic with over 100 known allelic variants, leading to four metabolizer phenotypes: poor (PM), intermediate (IM), extensive/normal (EM), and ultra-rapid (UM). Metabolizer status significantly impacts drug efficacy and adverse effect risk.</p>
