<h1>YBX1</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>YBX1 (Y-Box Binding Protein 1) encodes a multifunctional nucleic acid-binding protein that belongs to the cold shock domain (CSD) protein superfamily. The CSD is an evolutionarily ancient nucleic acid-binding domain homologous to bacterial cold shock proteins. YBX1 binds both DNA (recognizing inverted CCAAT box/Y-box sequences, 5'-CTGATTGGC/TCAA-3') and RNA, enabling it to function as a transcription factor, translational regulator, and mRNA processing factor. It is one of the most abundant mRNA-binding proteins in the cell.</p>
<p>YBX1 shuttles between the nucleus and cytoplasm, with its localization determining its functional role. In the cytoplasm, YBX1 is a major component of messenger ribonucleoprotein (mRNP) particles and P-bodies, where it stabilizes mRNAs and regulates their translation. In the nucleus, YBX1 functions as a transcription factor for genes involved in cell proliferation, drug resistance, and stress response. Nuclear translocation of YBX1 is induced by UV irradiation, genotoxic stress, and growth factor signaling, and is mediated by phosphorylation at Serine 102 by Akt and RSK kinases.</p>
<h2>Function</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Translational regulation</strong>: A major component of cytoplasmic mRNPs; globally represses cap-dependent translation at high YBX1:mRNA ratios while selectively enhancing translation of mRNAs with structured 5' UTRs</li>
<li><strong>mRNA stabilization</strong>: Binds and stabilizes oncogenic mRNAs including MYC, EGFR, CCND1 (cyclin D1), and MDR1 (multidrug resistance), protecting them from degradation</li>
<li><strong>Transcription regulation</strong>: Activates or represses transcription of genes including MDR1, EGFR, MMP2, cyclin A, and cyclin B1 by binding Y-box elements in promoters</li>
<li><strong>DNA repair</strong>: Participates in base excision repair (BER) and mismatch repair (MMR); interacts with PCNA, MSH2, and endonuclease III</li>
<li><strong>Pre-mRNA splicing</strong>: Regulates alternative splicing of numerous transcripts; associates with the spliceosome and influences exon inclusion/skipping decisions</li>
<li><strong>Stress granule formation</strong>: A core component of stress granules that form during cellular stress; sequesters and triages mRNAs during translational arrest</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cancer</strong>: Nuclear YBX1 localization is a strong independent prognostic marker for poor survival in breast, ovarian, prostate, lung, and colorectal cancers; drives epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT), drug resistance (via MDR1 induction), and cancer stem cell properties</li>
<li><strong>Drug resistance</strong>: Transcriptionally activates the MDR1/ABCB1 gene encoding P-glycoprotein efflux pump, conferring multidrug resistance in chemotherapy; also stabilizes drug-resistance-associated mRNAs</li>
<li><strong>Inflammatory disease</strong>: YBX1 is secreted from immune cells and acts as a damage-associated molecular pattern (DAMP); extracellular YBX1 signals through TLR/NF-kB to promote inflammation in sepsis and kidney disease</li>
<li><strong>Kidney disease</strong>: Urinary YBX1 is a biomarker for lupus nephritis and diabetic nephropathy; involved in renal fibrosis through TGF-beta signaling</li>
<li><strong>Viral infections</strong>: YBX1 interacts with viral RNAs and is co-opted by HIV-1, Dengue, Zika, and SARS-CoV-2 viruses for replication</li>
</ul>
