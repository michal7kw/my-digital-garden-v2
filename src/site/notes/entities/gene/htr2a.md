<h1>HTR2A</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>HTR2A encodes the serotonin 2A (5-HT2A) receptor, a Gq-coupled GPCR that is the most abundant serotonin receptor in the cerebral cortex. The gene is located on chromosome 13q14.2 and contains 3 exons. 5-HT2A receptors are densely expressed on layer V pyramidal neurons in the prefrontal cortex, where they modulate glutamatergic excitability, perception, and higher cognitive functions.</p>
<h2>Key Polymorphisms</h2>
<h3>rs7997012 (Intronic)</h3>
<p>Identified in the STAR*D trial as significantly associated with SSRI treatment response:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>A allele carriers</strong>: Better response to citalopram treatment</li>
<li><strong>Effect size</strong>: Modest but replicated in multiple studies</li>
<li><strong>Mechanism</strong>: May affect receptor expression or splicing efficiency</li>
</ul>
<h3>T102C (rs6313)</h3>
<p>A synonymous coding variant in linkage disequilibrium with the promoter variant -1438A/G (rs6311):</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>C/C genotype</strong>: Reduced 5-HT2A receptor density (measured by PET)</li>
<li><strong>T/T genotype</strong>: Higher receptor density</li>
<li><strong>Associations</strong>: Schizophrenia risk (meta-analysis OR ~1.1), suicidal behavior, antipsychotic response</li>
<li><strong>Note</strong>: Functional effect is primarily through the linked promoter variant rs6311</li>
</ul>
<h3>-1438A/G (rs6311)</h3>
<p>Promoter variant affecting transcription:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>G/G genotype</strong>: Higher 5-HT2A expression → potentially more cortical excitability</li>
<li><strong>A/A genotype</strong>: Lower expression</li>
<li><strong>Associations</strong>: Antidepressant response, eating disorder risk, migraine susceptibility</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>SSRI response</strong>: rs7997012 predicts citalopram response — pharmacogenomic testing increasingly used</li>
<li><strong>Psychedelic response</strong>: 5-HT2A receptor density (influenced by genetics) correlates with psychedelic drug response intensity</li>
<li><strong>Antipsychotic mechanism</strong>: Atypical antipsychotics (risperidone, quetiapine) work partly via 5-HT2A antagonism</li>
<li><strong>Suicide risk</strong>: T102C T/T genotype associated with higher impulsivity and violent suicide attempts</li>
</ul>
