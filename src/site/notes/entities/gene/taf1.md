<h1>TAF1</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>TAF1 (TATA-Box Binding Protein Associated Factor 1) encodes the largest subunit (250 kDa) of the TFIID general transcription factor complex, which is essential for RNA polymerase II-mediated transcription initiation at most protein-coding gene promoters. TFIID is composed of TBP (TATA-Binding Protein) and 13-14 TBP-Associated Factors (TAFs), with TAF1 serving as the scaffold that organizes TFIID architecture. TAF1 directly interacts with TBP and positions TFIID on both TATA-containing and TATA-less promoters.</p>
<p>TAF1 possesses multiple enzymatic activities including histone acetyltransferase (HAT) activity (acetylating histones H3 and H4), kinase activity (phosphorylating RAP74/TFIIF), and ubiquitin-activating/conjugating activity. These catalytic functions enable TAF1 to modify chromatin structure at promoters, regulate transcription factor stability, and integrate epigenetic signals with transcription initiation. TAF1 also contains two tandem bromodomains that recognize acetylated histone tails, anchoring TFIID at active promoters.</p>
<h2>Function</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Transcription initiation</strong>: Serves as the central scaffold of TFIID, required for pre-initiation complex (PIC) assembly at RNA polymerase II promoters genome-wide</li>
<li><strong>Histone acetyltransferase activity</strong>: Acetylates histones H3 and H4 at promoter regions, facilitating chromatin opening for transcription</li>
<li><strong>Promoter recognition</strong>: Contains a double bromodomain that reads acetylated histone marks, and a DUF3591 domain that recognizes specific DNA sequences at TATA-less promoters</li>
<li><strong>Kinase activity</strong>: Phosphorylates TFIIF subunit RAP74, modulating transcription elongation</li>
<li><strong>Cell cycle regulation</strong>: TAF1 HAT activity acetylates p53, regulating G1/S checkpoint gene expression; TAF1 is required for cyclin D1 and cyclin A expression</li>
<li><strong>Protein ubiquitination</strong>: Possesses ubiquitin-activating/conjugating enzyme activity that monoubiquitinates histone H1 at promoters</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>X-linked dystonia-parkinsonism (XDP/DYT3)</strong>: A neurodegenerative disorder endemic to Panay Island, Philippines, caused by a retrotransposon insertion in the TAF1 gene that reduces TAF1 expression in striatal neurons, leading to progressive dystonia and parkinsonism</li>
<li><strong>Intellectual disability</strong>: De novo TAF1 mutations cause X-linked syndromic intellectual disability with global developmental delay, dysmorphic features, and variable neurological phenotypes</li>
<li><strong>Cancer</strong>: TAF1 is recurrently mutated in uterine serous carcinoma and other cancers; altered TFIID function disrupts transcription programs controlling proliferation and differentiation</li>
<li><strong>Drug target potential</strong>: TAF1 bromodomains are being explored as druggable targets for cancer therapy, with selective bromodomain inhibitors in development</li>
</ul>
