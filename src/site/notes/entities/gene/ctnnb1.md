<h1>CTNNB1</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>CTNNB1 encodes beta-catenin, a 781-amino acid dual-function protein that plays essential roles in both cell-cell adhesion and canonical Wnt signaling. At the plasma membrane, beta-catenin forms a structural component of adherens junctions by linking cadherins to the actin cytoskeleton via alpha-catenin. In the nucleus, it functions as a transcriptional coactivator by binding TCF/LEF transcription factors to drive expression of Wnt target genes.</p>
<p>The balance between these adhesion and signaling pools is tightly regulated. In the absence of Wnt ligands, cytoplasmic beta-catenin is targeted for proteasomal degradation by the destruction complex (APC, Axin, GSK-3beta, CK1alpha). Wnt ligand binding to Frizzled receptors disrupts this complex, allowing beta-catenin to accumulate and translocate to the nucleus.</p>
<h2>Function</h2>
<p>In the Wnt signaling pathway, stabilized beta-catenin enters the nucleus and displaces transcriptional repressors from TCF/LEF binding sites, activating target genes including MYC, CCND1 (cyclin D1), and AXIN2 that promote cell proliferation, stem cell maintenance, and tissue homeostasis. At adherens junctions, beta-catenin bridges the intracellular domain of E-cadherin to alpha-catenin and the actin cytoskeleton, maintaining epithelial integrity and contact inhibition. Loss of E-cadherin-mediated adhesion releases beta-catenin into the signaling pool, linking cell adhesion disruption to Wnt pathway activation -- a key event in epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition (EMT).</p>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<p>Gain-of-function mutations in CTNNB1 are among the most common oncogenic alterations in human cancer. Mutations at GSK-3beta phosphorylation sites (codons 33, 37, 41, 45) prevent beta-catenin degradation, leading to constitutive Wnt activation. These mutations are frequent in hepatocellular carcinoma, endometrial carcinoma, medulloblastoma (WNT subtype), and desmoid tumors. In colorectal cancer, APC mutations (rather than CTNNB1 mutations) are the primary mechanism of Wnt pathway activation, but both converge on beta-catenin stabilization. Beta-catenin also crosstalks with NF-kB, Notch, and Hedgehog pathways, making it a hub of oncogenic signaling. Small-molecule Wnt pathway inhibitors targeting beta-catenin-TCF interaction are in clinical development.</p>
