<h1>TPH2</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>TPH2 encodes tryptophan hydroxylase 2, the brain-specific isoform of the rate-limiting enzyme in serotonin biosynthesis. The enzyme catalyzes the conversion of L-tryptophan to 5-hydroxytryptophan (5-HTP) using tetrahydrobiopterin (BH4) as a cofactor and molecular oxygen as a co-substrate. This is the first and rate-limiting step in brain serotonin synthesis:</p>
<p><strong>L-Tryptophan → (TPH2 + BH4 + O2) → 5-HTP → (AADC + B6) → Serotonin</strong></p>
<p>TPH2 is exclusively expressed in serotonergic neurons of the raphe nuclei, while TPH1 (the peripheral isoform) handles serotonin synthesis in the gut and periphery. This tissue-specific expression means TPH2 variants selectively affect brain serotonin levels without altering peripheral serotonin.</p>
<h2>Key Polymorphisms</h2>
<h3>R441H (rs120074175)</h3>
<p>A rare loss-of-function mutation identified in a cohort of patients with unipolar major depression:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Effect</strong>: ~80% reduction in serotonin synthesis capacity</li>
<li><strong>Mechanism</strong>: Disrupts enzyme catalytic activity</li>
<li><strong>Clinical significance</strong>: First genetic evidence directly linking reduced brain serotonin synthesis to depression</li>
<li><strong>Population frequency</strong>: Rare (identified in specific families)</li>
</ul>
<h3>rs4570625 (G-703T)</h3>
<p>A common promoter variant:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>T/T genotype</strong>: Altered amygdala reactivity to emotional stimuli (fMRI studies)</li>
<li><strong>Associations</strong>: Anxiety, emotional dysregulation, stress vulnerability</li>
<li><strong>Mechanism</strong>: May affect TPH2 transcription levels in raphe neurons</li>
</ul>
<h3>rs1386494</h3>
<ul>
<li>Associated with bipolar disorder and suicidal behavior in some studies</li>
<li>Effect on enzyme expression unclear</li>
</ul>
<h2>Cofactor Requirements</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Tetrahydrobiopterin (BH4)</strong>: Essential cofactor — BH4 deficiency impairs serotonin synthesis</li>
<li><strong>Iron (Fe2+)</strong>: Required at the catalytic center</li>
<li><strong>Molecular oxygen (O2)</strong>: Co-substrate for hydroxylation reaction</li>
<li>Nutritional deficiencies in iron, folate (BH4 recycling), or vitamin B6 (downstream AADC step) can impair the pathway</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Depression</strong>: R441H mutation provides direct genetic evidence for serotonin hypothesis of depression</li>
<li><strong>SSRI response</strong>: Baseline TPH2 activity may influence the substrate pool available for SSRI-enhanced serotonergic transmission</li>
<li><strong>Stress vulnerability</strong>: Common variants modulate amygdala reactivity and emotional processing</li>
<li><strong>Suicide risk</strong>: Multiple TPH2 variants associated with suicidal behavior in meta-analyses</li>
</ul>
