<h1>GATA2</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>GATA2 (GATA Binding Protein 2) is a zinc finger transcription factor belonging to the GATA family, which recognizes the consensus DNA sequence WGATAR (where W = A/T and R = A/G) in promoters and enhancers of target genes. GATA2 contains two highly conserved zinc finger domains: the C-terminal finger mediates DNA binding while the N-terminal finger stabilizes DNA interaction and participates in protein-protein interactions with cofactors such as PU.1, FOG1, and SCL/TAL1.</p>
<p>GATA2 is a master regulator of hematopoiesis, essential for the maintenance and proliferation of hematopoietic stem and progenitor cells (HSPCs). It is expressed in early hematopoietic progenitors, mast cells, megakaryocytes, and endothelial cells. GATA2 expression is tightly regulated through a distal hematopoietic enhancer (+9.5 kb), and its dosage is critical -- both haploinsufficiency and overexpression lead to severe hematological disorders.</p>
<h2>Function</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Hematopoietic stem cell maintenance</strong>: Essential for the survival, self-renewal, and quiescence of hematopoietic stem cells; GATA2-null embryos die at E10.5 due to failure of definitive hematopoiesis</li>
<li><strong>Myeloid lineage specification</strong>: Drives differentiation of granulocyte-monocyte progenitors and mast cells; cooperates with PU.1 and C/EBPalpha in myeloid gene activation</li>
<li><strong>Megakaryopoiesis</strong>: Regulates megakaryocyte differentiation and platelet production in coordination with GATA1</li>
<li><strong>Endothelial-to-hematopoietic transition</strong>: Required for emergence of hematopoietic cells from hemogenic endothelium during embryonic development</li>
<li><strong>Lymphoid development</strong>: Contributes to early lymphoid progenitor specification and NK cell development</li>
<li><strong>Vascular integrity</strong>: Expressed in endothelial cells where it regulates genes involved in vascular development and lymphatic vessel formation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>GATA2 deficiency syndrome</strong>: Autosomal dominant disorder caused by haploinsufficiency, presenting as MonoMAC syndrome (monocytopenia and mycobacterial infection), Emberger syndrome (primary lymphedema with myelodysplasia), or familial MDS/AML</li>
<li><strong>Acute myeloid leukemia (AML)</strong>: GATA2 mutations are found in ~7% of pediatric AML; overexpression drives leukemic transformation, particularly in CML blast crisis</li>
<li><strong>Myelodysplastic syndromes (MDS)</strong>: GATA2 mutations predispose to MDS with monosomy 7 and trisomy 8; early transplant is often recommended</li>
<li><strong>Immunodeficiency</strong>: GATA2 haploinsufficiency causes severe dendritic cell, monocyte, B cell, and NK cell deficiency, leading to disseminated mycobacterial and viral infections</li>
<li><strong>Chronic myeloid leukemia</strong>: GATA2 is a downstream target of BCR-ABL and contributes to blast crisis progression</li>
</ul>
