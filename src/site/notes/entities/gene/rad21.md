<h1>RAD21</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>RAD21 (RAD21 Cohesin Complex Component) is a key structural subunit of the cohesin complex, a ring-shaped protein assembly that holds sister chromatids together from S phase until anaphase during cell division. The cohesin complex consists of four core subunits: SMC1, SMC3, RAD21 (also known as SCC1/MCD1), and either SA1 or SA2. RAD21 functions as the kleisin subunit, bridging the SMC1 and SMC3 heads to close the cohesin ring. Its cleavage by separase at the onset of anaphase triggers sister chromatid separation, making it a critical regulator of chromosome segregation fidelity.</p>
<p>Beyond its canonical role in sister chromatid cohesion, RAD21 participates in DNA damage repair through homologous recombination, gene expression regulation, and three-dimensional genome organization. Together with CTCF, cohesin mediates the formation of topologically associating domains (TADs) and chromatin loops that bring enhancers into proximity with their target promoters.</p>
<h2>Function</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Sister chromatid cohesion</strong>: Holds replicated chromosomes together from S phase through metaphase, ensuring accurate chromosome segregation during mitosis and meiosis</li>
<li><strong>DNA damage repair</strong>: Facilitates homologous recombination repair by maintaining sister chromatid proximity as a template for double-strand break repair</li>
<li><strong>Chromatin loop formation</strong>: Partners with CTCF to form chromatin loops and TAD boundaries, establishing the three-dimensional architecture of the genome</li>
<li><strong>Gene expression regulation</strong>: Cohesin-mediated loops bring distal enhancers to gene promoters, modulating transcription of developmental and cell-type-specific genes</li>
<li><strong>Replication fork stabilization</strong>: Assists in maintaining fork integrity during DNA replication stress</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cornelia de Lange Syndrome</strong>: Heterozygous mutations in RAD21 cause a mild form of this developmental disorder characterized by growth retardation, limb anomalies, and intellectual disability</li>
<li><strong>Cancer</strong>: RAD21 is recurrently mutated or amplified in acute myeloid leukemia (AML), bladder cancer, and breast cancer; aberrant cohesin function leads to chromosomal instability and aneuploidy</li>
<li><strong>Cohesinopathies</strong>: Mutations in cohesin subunits and regulators cause a spectrum of developmental disorders affecting limb, craniofacial, and neurological development</li>
<li><strong>Myelodysplastic syndromes</strong>: Cohesin mutations including RAD21 are found in ~10-15% of MDS cases, affecting hematopoietic differentiation</li>
</ul>
