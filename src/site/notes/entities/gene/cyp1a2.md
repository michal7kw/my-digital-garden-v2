<h1>CYP1A2</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>CYP1A2 accounts for ~13% of total hepatic CYP content and is the primary enzyme for caffeine metabolism. The *1F variant (rs762551 A&gt;C) determines slow vs fast caffeine metabolizer status — slow metabolizers have increased cardiovascular risk from coffee consumption. CYP1A2 also metabolizes melatonin, theophylline, and some antipsychotics. Cruciferous vegetables (sulforaphane) are potent inducers; grapefruit and oral contraceptives are inhibitors. Smoking induces CYP1A2 by 1.5-2x.</p>
