<h1>FOS</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>FOS (Fos Proto-Oncogene, AP-1 Transcription Factor Subunit) encodes the c-Fos protein, a leucine zipper transcription factor that heterodimerizes with Jun family proteins (c-Jun, JunB, JunD) to form the AP-1 (Activator Protein-1) transcription factor complex. AP-1 binds to TPA-response elements (TRE, consensus 5'-TGAG/CTCA-3') and cAMP-response elements (CRE) in the promoters of target genes. c-Fos was originally identified as the cellular homolog of the v-fos oncogene of the FBJ murine osteosarcoma virus.</p>
<p>c-Fos is a classic immediate-early gene (IEG) whose expression is rapidly and transiently induced within minutes by a wide range of stimuli including growth factors, neurotransmitters, cytokines, stress signals, and membrane depolarization. Its mRNA and protein are normally expressed at very low basal levels and have short half-lives, allowing c-Fos to act as a molecular sensor that converts transient extracellular signals into long-term changes in gene expression. In neuroscience, c-Fos immunostaining is widely used as a marker of neuronal activation.</p>
<h2>Function</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Immediate-early gene response</strong>: Rapidly induced (within 15 minutes) by growth factors, serum, cytokines, UV radiation, and neuronal activity through the serum response element (SRE) and CRE in the FOS promoter</li>
<li><strong>AP-1 transcriptional regulation</strong>: Heterodimerizes with Jun proteins to activate transcription of genes involved in cell proliferation (cyclin D1), differentiation, apoptosis, and tissue remodeling (MMPs)</li>
<li><strong>Neuronal plasticity</strong>: Induced in neurons by synaptic activity; required for long-term memory formation, synaptic plasticity, and experience-dependent circuit remodeling in the hippocampus and cortex</li>
<li><strong>Bone development</strong>: c-Fos is essential for osteoclast differentiation; it induces NFATc1, the master regulator of osteoclastogenesis</li>
<li><strong>Cell cycle progression</strong>: AP-1 activates cyclin D1 transcription, driving G1/S cell cycle transition in response to mitogenic signals</li>
<li><strong>Immune regulation</strong>: Regulates cytokine gene expression (IL-2, IL-4, IFN-gamma) in T cells and macrophage inflammatory responses</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cancer</strong>: c-Fos overexpression transforms fibroblasts and is elevated in osteosarcoma, breast cancer, endometrial carcinoma, and lung cancer; FOS gene amplification drives aggressive tumor behavior</li>
<li><strong>Bone disorders</strong>: c-Fos overexpression in transgenic mice causes osteosarcoma; c-Fos is required for RANKL-induced osteoclastogenesis and is implicated in osteoporosis pathology</li>
<li><strong>Neuropsychiatric disorders</strong>: Dysregulated c-Fos expression patterns are observed in epilepsy, addiction, chronic pain, and mood disorders; serves as a biomarker for neural circuit activation</li>
<li><strong>Inflammation</strong>: AP-1 (Fos/Jun) drives transcription of inflammatory mediators; targeted by glucocorticoid receptor-mediated transrepression, explaining anti-inflammatory effects of corticosteroids</li>
<li><strong>Therapeutic relevance</strong>: AP-1 inhibition is a strategy in cancer and inflammatory disease; natural compounds (curcumin, resveratrol) modulate AP-1 activity</li>
</ul>
