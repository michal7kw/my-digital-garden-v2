<h1>HTR1A</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>HTR1A encodes the serotonin 1A (5-HT1A) receptor, a Gi/Go-coupled GPCR that functions as both a presynaptic autoreceptor in the raphe nuclei and a postsynaptic heteroreceptor in cortical and limbic regions. The gene is intronless (single exon) and is located on chromosome 5q12.3.</p>
<h2>Key Polymorphisms</h2>
<h3>rs6295 (C-1019G)</h3>
<p>The most studied HTR1A variant. Located in the gene promoter region, it affects transcription factor binding:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>C/C genotype</strong>: Normal 5-HT1A expression. Deaf1 transcription factor represses gene in raphe (lower autoreceptor density). Associated with better SSRI response</li>
<li><strong>G/G genotype</strong>: Loss of Deaf1 repression → increased autoreceptor expression in raphe → stronger negative feedback → reduced serotonergic transmission. Associated with:
<ul>
<li>Increased depression and anxiety risk</li>
<li>Poorer SSRI response (more autoreceptors to desensitize)</li>
<li>Higher suicide attempt risk</li>
<li>Reduced hippocampal 5-HT1A binding on PET imaging</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<h3>rs1364043</h3>
<p>Associated with antidepressant response variability. Effect size modest.</p>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Pharmacogenomics</strong>: G/G carriers at rs6295 may require longer SSRI trials or augmentation strategies (e.g., pindolol to block autoreceptors)</li>
<li><strong>Depression risk</strong>: Meta-analyses confirm G allele association with MDD (OR ~1.2)</li>
<li><strong>Suicide risk</strong>: G/G genotype associated with violent suicide attempts</li>
<li><strong>Anxiety</strong>: Reduced postsynaptic 5-HT1A signaling in cortex linked to anxiety vulnerability</li>
</ul>
