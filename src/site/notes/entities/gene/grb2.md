<h1>GRB2</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>GRB2 (Growth Factor Receptor Bound Protein 2) is a small (25 kDa) adaptor protein that plays a central role in receptor tyrosine kinase (RTK) signaling by coupling activated growth factor receptors to downstream Ras-MAPK and PI3K-Akt signaling cascades. GRB2 consists of a central SH2 (Src Homology 2) domain flanked by two SH3 (Src Homology 3) domains. The SH2 domain binds phosphotyrosine residues on activated RTKs (EGFR, FGFR, insulin receptor, IGF-1R), while the SH3 domains constitutively bind proline-rich motifs on effector proteins, most notably SOS1 (Son of Sevenless), a Ras guanine nucleotide exchange factor.</p>
<p>GRB2 functions as a critical signaling hub that transmits extracellular growth factor signals from the cell membrane to intracellular signaling networks. Upon growth factor stimulation, GRB2-SOS1 complexes are recruited to the plasma membrane where SOS1 activates Ras by promoting GDP-to-GTP exchange, initiating the Ras-Raf-MEK-ERK cascade. GRB2 also interacts with GAB1/2, SHP2, and other adaptors to diversify downstream signaling through PI3K-Akt, PLC-gamma, and other pathways.</p>
<h2>Function</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Ras-MAPK activation</strong>: Recruits SOS1 to activated RTKs at the plasma membrane, triggering Ras activation and the downstream Raf-MEK-ERK signaling cascade that drives cell proliferation</li>
<li><strong>PI3K-Akt signaling</strong>: Through interaction with GAB1, GRB2 recruits PI3K to activated receptors, promoting cell survival and metabolism</li>
<li><strong>Insulin signaling</strong>: Couples insulin receptor and IRS proteins to Ras-MAPK signaling, contributing to insulin-stimulated gene expression and cell growth</li>
<li><strong>IGF-1 signaling</strong>: Mediates IGF-1 receptor signaling through SOS1 and SHC adaptors, regulating somatic growth and tissue homeostasis</li>
<li><strong>Receptor endocytosis</strong>: GRB2 recruits CBL ubiquitin ligase to activated receptors, promoting receptor ubiquitination, endocytosis, and signal termination</li>
<li><strong>Cytoskeletal regulation</strong>: Interacts with WASP and dynamin to regulate actin dynamics and membrane trafficking</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cancer</strong>: GRB2 overexpression is observed in breast, lung, and bladder cancers; amplifies oncogenic RTK signaling from mutant EGFR, HER2, and other receptors; therapeutic targeting with GRB2 antisense oligonucleotides (BP1001) is in clinical trials for AML and CML</li>
<li><strong>Diabetes and metabolic syndrome</strong>: Alterations in GRB2-mediated insulin receptor signaling contribute to insulin resistance; GRB2 modulates insulin sensitivity in skeletal muscle and adipose tissue</li>
<li><strong>Noonan syndrome</strong>: Mutations in SOS1 (a key GRB2 binding partner) and other Ras-MAPK pathway components cause this developmental RASopathy</li>
<li><strong>Drug resistance</strong>: GRB2-mediated bypass signaling contributes to resistance against targeted therapies (EGFR inhibitors, HER2 inhibitors) in cancer</li>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular disease</strong>: GRB2 mediates hypertrophic signaling in cardiomyocytes downstream of angiotensin II and endothelin-1 receptors</li>
</ul>
