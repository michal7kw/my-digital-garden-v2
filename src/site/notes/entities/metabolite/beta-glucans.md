<h1>Beta-Glucans</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Beta-glucans are naturally occurring polysaccharides composed of D-glucose monomers linked by beta-glycosidic bonds. In medicinal mushrooms like Reishi, they exist as (1,3)-(1,6)-beta-D-glucans with potent immunomodulatory activity. They are the primary water-soluble bioactive compounds in Reishi and other medicinal mushrooms.</p>
<h2>Types and Sources</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mushroom (1,3-1,6-beta-D-glucan)</strong>: Strongest immune activation; found in Reishi, Lion's Mane, Cordyceps, Shiitake, Maitake</li>
<li><strong>Yeast (1,3-1,6-beta-D-glucan)</strong>: Well-studied immune activator; from Saccharomyces cerevisiae</li>
<li><strong>Cereal (1,3-1,4-beta-D-glucan)</strong>: From oats and barley; primarily cholesterol-lowering</li>
</ul>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<ol>
<li>Beta-glucans bind to Dectin-1 receptor on macrophages and dendritic cells</li>
<li>Also bind complement receptor 3 (CR3) on NK cells and neutrophils</li>
<li>This triggers phagocytosis, cytokine release, and immune cell activation</li>
<li>Activated immune cells enhance pathogen clearance and tumor surveillance</li>
<li>Promotes Th1 immune response shift (cellular immunity)</li>
</ol>
<h2>Clinical Evidence</h2>
<ul>
<li>Meta-analysis supports immune-enhancing effects in upper respiratory tract infections (Level 1a)</li>
<li>Increases NK cell activity by 20-40% in human supplementation studies</li>
<li>Enhances antibody response to vaccination</li>
<li>Cancer adjunct studies show improved immune markers during chemotherapy</li>
</ul>
<h2>Extraction</h2>
<ul>
<li>Hot water extraction required for bioavailability</li>
<li>Dual extraction (water + alcohol) captures both beta-glucans and triterpenes</li>
<li>Standardized to &gt;10% beta-glucan content for quality Reishi supplements</li>
<li>Not degraded by stomach acid; reaches intestinal immune tissue (GALT)</li>
</ul>
