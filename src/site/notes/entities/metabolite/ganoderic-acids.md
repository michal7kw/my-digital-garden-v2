<h1>Ganoderic Acids</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Triterpenoid compounds found exclusively in Ganoderma lucidum (Reishi mushroom). Over 130 structurally distinct ganoderic acids have been identified. They are alcohol-soluble and responsible for the characteristic bitter taste of high-quality Reishi extracts.</p>
<h2>Key Variants and Activities</h2>
<h3>Ganoderic Acid A</h3>
<ul>
<li>Molecular formula: C30H44O7</li>
<li>Primary anti-inflammatory agent; suppresses NF-kB signaling</li>
<li>Inhibits histamine release from mast cells</li>
</ul>
<h3>Ganoderic Acid B</h3>
<ul>
<li>Potent ACE inhibitor contributing to blood pressure reduction</li>
<li>Anti-tumor activity via cell cycle arrest</li>
</ul>
<h3>Ganoderic Acid C</h3>
<ul>
<li>Strong hepatoprotective effects</li>
<li>Reduces ALT and AST in liver injury models</li>
</ul>
<h3>Ganoderic Acid D</h3>
<ul>
<li>Induces apoptosis in cancer cell lines</li>
<li>Anti-viral properties</li>
</ul>
<h3>Ganoderic Acid R</h3>
<ul>
<li>Most potent hepatoprotective variant</li>
<li>Protects against chemical liver injury (CCl4 models)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Pharmacological Properties</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Bioavailability</strong>: Low oral bioavailability; alcohol extraction required</li>
<li><strong>Half-life</strong>: Approximately 2-4 hours in animal models</li>
<li><strong>Distribution</strong>: Detected in liver, kidneys, and brain tissue in animal studies</li>
<li><strong>Metabolism</strong>: Hepatic metabolism via cytochrome P450 enzymes</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li>Marker compound for Reishi extract quality (higher content = more bitter = higher quality)</li>
<li>Used to standardize Reishi supplements (target: &gt;4% triterpenes)</li>
<li>Potential biomarker for Reishi bioavailability studies</li>
</ul>
