<h1>Autophagosome</h1>
<h2>Description</h2>
<p>The autophagosome is a double-membrane vesicle that forms de novo around cytoplasmic cargo destined for degradation during macroautophagy (commonly called &quot;autophagy&quot;). It is the defining structure of the autophagy pathway — the cell's primary quality control and recycling system. Autophagosomes engulf damaged mitochondria (mitophagy), protein aggregates (aggrephagy), invading bacteria (xenophagy), and excess endoplasmic reticulum (ER-phagy), then fuse with lysosomes to form autolysosomes where contents are degraded and recycled. Yoshinori Ohsumi received the 2016 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for elucidating the molecular machinery of autophagy.</p>
<h2>Formation Steps</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Initiation</strong> — ULK1 complex activation (inhibited by mTOR, activated by AMPK)</li>
<li><strong>Nucleation</strong> — Beclin-1/VPS34 complex generates PI3P on isolation membrane</li>
<li><strong>Elongation</strong> — LC3-II conjugation to membrane (LC3 lipidation)</li>
<li><strong>Cargo recognition</strong> — p62/SQSTM1 and other receptors tag ubiquitinated cargo</li>
<li><strong>Closure</strong> — ESCRT machinery seals the double membrane</li>
<li><strong>Fusion</strong> — Autophagosome fuses with lysosome → autolysosome</li>
<li><strong>Degradation</strong> — Lysosomal hydrolases break down contents; nutrients recycled</li>
</ol>
<h2>Autophagy Activators</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Fasting / caloric restriction</strong> — mTOR inhibition, AMPK activation</li>
<li><strong>Exercise</strong> — Induces autophagy in muscle, liver, brain</li>
<li><strong>Rapamycin</strong> — mTOR inhibitor (pharmacological autophagy inducer)</li>
<li><strong>Spermidine</strong> — Natural polyamine that induces autophagy</li>
<li><strong>Resveratrol</strong> — SIRT1 activation promotes autophagy</li>
</ul>
