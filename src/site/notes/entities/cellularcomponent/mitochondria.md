<h1>Mitochondria</h1>
<h2>Description</h2>
<p>Mitochondria are double-membrane-bound organelles found in most eukaryotic organisms. They generate most of the cell's supply of adenosine triphosphate (ATP), used as a source of chemical energy, through cellular respiration.</p>
<h2>Structure</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Outer membrane</strong>: Permeable to small molecules</li>
<li><strong>Intermembrane space</strong>: Contains cytochrome c</li>
<li><strong>Inner membrane</strong>: Contains electron transport chain complexes</li>
<li><strong>Matrix</strong>: Contains TCA cycle enzymes, mtDNA</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Metabolic Functions</h2>
<h3>Energy Production</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>[[Citric Acid Cycle]]</strong>: Oxidizes acetyl-CoA to CO₂, generates NADH/FADH₂</li>
<li><strong>[[Electron Transport Chain]]</strong>: Transfers electrons, pumps protons</li>
<li><strong>[[Oxidative Phosphorylation]]</strong>: ATP synthesis via [[ATP Synthase]]</li>
<li><strong>[[Beta-Oxidation]]</strong>: Fatty acid catabolism for acetyl-CoA</li>
</ul>
<h3>Key Metabolites</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>[[ATP]]</strong>: Primary energy output</li>
<li><strong>[[NADH]]</strong> / <strong>[[NAD+]]</strong>: Electron carriers</li>
<li><strong>[[FADH2]]</strong>: Electron carrier for Complex II</li>
<li><strong>[[Acetyl-CoA]]</strong>: Central metabolic hub</li>
<li><strong>[[Pyruvate]]</strong>: Glycolysis endpoint, enters as acetyl-CoA</li>
</ul>
<h3>Regulation of Biogenesis</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>[[PGC-1alpha]]</strong>: Master regulator of mitochondrial biogenesis</li>
<li><strong>[[TFAM]]</strong>: mtDNA replication and transcription</li>
<li><strong>[[NRF2]]</strong>: Antioxidant defense</li>
<li><strong>[[AMPK]]</strong>: Energy sensor, activates biogenesis</li>
<li><strong>[[SIRT1]]</strong> / <strong>[[SIRT3]]</strong>: NAD+-dependent regulation</li>
</ul>

<h2>Clinical Relevance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Aging</strong>: Mitochondrial dysfunction central to aging</li>
<li><strong>Neurodegeneration</strong>: High neuronal energy demands</li>
<li><strong>Metabolic disease</strong>: Impaired oxidative capacity</li>
<li><strong>Exercise adaptation</strong>: Biogenesis enhances capacity</li>
</ul>
<h2>Recent Research (2024-2026)</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Sleep:</strong> A 2025 study identified mitochondrial &quot;electrical stress&quot; (electron leak) as the direct driver of sleep pressure in the brain. [[Nature 2025 Sleep and Mitochondria]]</li>
<li><strong>Uncoupling:</strong> The uncoupler BAM15 was shown to reverse sarcopenic obesity and extend lifespan by mimicking exercise. [[2025 BAM15 Mitochondrial Uncoupling Review]]</li>
<li><strong>Transplantation:</strong> First human trials of &quot;Mitotherapy&quot; (mitochondrial transplantation) began in late 2025. [[2025 Mitrix Bio Mitochondrial Transplantation Trial]]</li>
<li><strong>Stress Control:</strong> miR-71 identified as a master regulator of mitochondrial stress signaling. [[Nat Comm 2026 miR-71 Mitochondrial Stress]]</li>
</ul>
