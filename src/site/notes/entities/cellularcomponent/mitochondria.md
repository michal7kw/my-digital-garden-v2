<h1>Mitochondria</h1>
<h2>Description</h2>
<p>Mitochondria are double-membrane-bound organelles found in most eukaryotic organisms. They generate most of the cell's supply of adenosine triphosphate (ATP), used as a source of chemical energy, through cellular respiration.</p>
<h2>Structure</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Outer membrane</strong>: Permeable to small molecules</li>
<li><strong>Intermembrane space</strong>: Contains cytochrome c</li>
<li><strong>Inner membrane</strong>: Contains electron transport chain complexes</li>
<li><strong>Matrix</strong>: Contains TCA cycle enzymes, mtDNA</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Metabolic Functions</h2>
<h3>Energy Production</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong><a href="/garden/dev_admin/pathway/citric-acid-cycle/" class="internal-link">Citric Acid Cycle</a></strong>: Oxidizes acetyl-CoA to CO₂, generates NADH/FADH₂</li>
<li><strong><a href="/garden/dev_admin/pathway/electron-transport-chain/" class="internal-link">Electron Transport Chain</a></strong>: Transfers electrons, pumps protons</li>
<li><strong><a href="/garden/dev_admin/pathway/oxidative-phosphorylation/" class="internal-link">Oxidative Phosphorylation</a></strong>: ATP synthesis via <a href="/garden/dev_admin/protein/atp-synthase/" class="internal-link">ATP Synthase</a></li>
<li><strong><a href="/garden/dev_admin/pathway/beta-oxidation/" class="internal-link">Beta-Oxidation</a></strong>: Fatty acid catabolism for acetyl-CoA</li>
</ul>
<h3>Key Metabolites</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">ATP</a></strong>: Primary energy output</li>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">NADH</a></strong> / <strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">NAD+</a></strong>: Electron carriers</li>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">FADH2</a></strong>: Electron carrier for Complex II</li>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Acetyl-CoA</a></strong>: Central metabolic hub</li>
<li><strong><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Pyruvate</a></strong>: Glycolysis endpoint, enters as acetyl-CoA</li>
</ul>
<h3>Regulation of Biogenesis</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong><a href="/garden/dev_admin/protein/pgc-1alpha/" class="internal-link">PGC-1alpha</a></strong>: Master regulator of mitochondrial biogenesis</li>
<li><strong><a href="/garden/dev_admin/protein/tfam/" class="internal-link">TFAM</a></strong>: mtDNA replication and transcription</li>
<li><strong><a href="/garden/dev_admin/protein/nrf2/" class="internal-link">NRF2</a></strong>: Antioxidant defense</li>
<li><strong><a href="/garden/dev_admin/protein/ampk/" class="internal-link">AMPK</a></strong>: Energy sensor, activates biogenesis</li>
<li><strong><a href="/garden/dev_admin/protein/sirt1/" class="internal-link">SIRT1</a></strong> / <strong><a href="/garden/dev_admin/protein/sirt3/" class="internal-link">SIRT3</a></strong>: NAD+-dependent regulation</li>
</ul>

<h2>Clinical Relevance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Aging</strong>: Mitochondrial dysfunction central to aging</li>
<li><strong>Neurodegeneration</strong>: High neuronal energy demands</li>
<li><strong>Metabolic disease</strong>: Impaired oxidative capacity</li>
<li><strong>Exercise adaptation</strong>: Biogenesis enhances capacity</li>
</ul>
<h2>Recent Research (2024-2026)</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Sleep:</strong> A 2025 study identified mitochondrial &quot;electrical stress&quot; (electron leak) as the direct driver of sleep pressure in the brain. <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Nature 2025 Sleep and Mitochondria</a></li>
<li><strong>Uncoupling:</strong> The uncoupler BAM15 was shown to reverse sarcopenic obesity and extend lifespan by mimicking exercise. <a href="/garden/dev_admin/research/2025-bam15-mitochondrial-uncoupling-review/" class="internal-link">2025 BAM15 Mitochondrial Uncoupling Review</a></li>
<li><strong>Transplantation:</strong> First human trials of &quot;Mitotherapy&quot; (mitochondrial transplantation) began in late 2025. <a href="/garden/dev_admin/research/2025-mitrix-bio-mitochondrial-transplantation-trial/" class="internal-link">2025 Mitrix Bio Mitochondrial Transplantation Trial</a></li>
<li><strong>Stress Control:</strong> miR-71 identified as a master regulator of mitochondrial stress signaling. <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Nat Comm 2026 miR-71 Mitochondrial Stress</a></li>
</ul>
