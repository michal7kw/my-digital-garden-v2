<h1>Mthfr A1298C</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The MTHFR A1298C variant (rs1801131) is a single nucleotide polymorphism in the methylenetetrahydrofolate reductase gene that results in a glutamate-to-alanine substitution at position 429 of the protein. Unlike the C677T variant which primarily affects folate-dependent homocysteine remethylation, the A1298C variant is located in the regulatory domain of the enzyme and primarily impacts the conversion of 5-methyltetrahydrofolate and its role in tetrahydrobiopterin (BH4) regeneration.</p>
<p>BH4 is an essential cofactor for several critical enzymes, including phenylalanine hydroxylase, tyrosine hydroxylase, and tryptophan hydroxylase -- enzymes required for the synthesis of neurotransmitters (dopamine, serotonin, norepinephrine) and nitric oxide synthase. Because of this connection to the BH4 pathway, the A1298C variant has been more closely associated with neuropsychiatric conditions (depression, anxiety, ADHD) and nitric oxide-related vascular function than with the homocysteine elevation characteristic of C677T.</p>
<p>The A1298C homozygous variant (CC genotype) causes a modest reduction in MTHFR activity (approximately 60-70% of normal), which is less severe than the C677T TT genotype. The A1298C variant alone rarely causes significant homocysteine elevation. However, compound heterozygosity (one copy of C677T + one copy of A1298C) produces a biochemical phenotype similar to C677T homozygosity, with reduced enzyme activity and potential for elevated homocysteine.</p>

<h2>Clinical Interpretation</h2>
<p><strong>CC Genotype (Homozygous Variant):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Moderately reduced MTHFR activity (~60-70%)</li>
<li>Potential impact on BH4 regeneration</li>
<li>Possible association with neuropsychiatric symptoms (depression, anxiety)</li>
<li>Generally does not cause significant homocysteine elevation alone</li>
<li>May affect neurotransmitter synthesis (dopamine, serotonin)</li>
<li>Consider monitoring neurotransmitter-related symptoms</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Compound Heterozygosity (C677T + A1298C):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Clinically significant reduction in enzyme activity (~40-50%)</li>
<li>Similar risk profile to C677T TT genotype</li>
<li>Elevated homocysteine possible, especially with low folate/B12</li>
<li>May benefit from methylfolate and B12 supplementation</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>AC Genotype (Heterozygous):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Minimal clinical significance</li>
<li>Near-normal enzyme function</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>AA Genotype (Wild Type):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Normal enzyme activity and BH4 pathway function</li>
</ul>
