<h1>Hba1C</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c, glycated hemoglobin) measures the percentage of hemoglobin with glucose attached, reflecting average blood glucose over the preceding 2-3 months (the lifespan of red blood cells). It is the gold-standard biomarker for diabetes diagnosis and monitoring. An HbA1c of 5.7-6.4% indicates prediabetes; ≥6.5% diagnoses diabetes. For longevity optimization, functional medicine targets &lt;5.2% (average glucose ~100 mg/dL). HbA1c is also an independent cardiovascular risk factor — each 1% increase above 5% is associated with significantly elevated CVD risk even in non-diabetic ranges. Glycation (the process HbA1c measures) is itself a driver of aging, cross-linking proteins and generating advanced glycation end-products (AGEs).</p>
<h2>Reference Ranges</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Normal</strong>: &lt;5.7% (average glucose &lt;117 mg/dL)</li>
<li><strong>Prediabetes</strong>: 5.7-6.4% (average glucose 117-137 mg/dL)</li>
<li><strong>Diabetes</strong>: ≥6.5% (average glucose ≥140 mg/dL)</li>
<li><strong>Optimal (longevity)</strong>: 4.5-5.2%</li>
<li><strong>Target (diabetic)</strong>: &lt;7.0% (ADA), &lt;6.5% (aggressive management)</li>
</ul>
