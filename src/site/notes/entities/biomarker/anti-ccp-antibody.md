<h1>Anti-CCP Antibody</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Anti-CCP (anti-cyclic citrullinated peptide) antibody is the most specific serological marker for rheumatoid arthritis (RA), with a specificity of 95-98%. Anti-CCP antibodies target citrullinated proteins -- proteins where arginine residues have been enzymatically converted to citrulline by peptidylarginine deiminase (PAD). Citrullination occurs in inflamed synovium, creating neo-epitopes that trigger an autoimmune response.</p>
<p>Anti-CCP positivity combined with rheumatoid factor (RF) positivity is virtually diagnostic for RA (&gt;99% positive predictive value). Importantly, anti-CCP antibodies can be present 5-10 years before symptom onset, enabling preclinical identification of at-risk individuals. Higher titers are associated with more aggressive erosive disease and worse long-term prognosis.</p>
<p>The second-generation assay (anti-CCP2) is the standard clinical test. It offers improved sensitivity over the first-generation assay while maintaining excellent specificity. Serial monitoring can be useful for tracking disease activity and treatment response.</p>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Diagnosis</strong>: Most specific serological test for RA; combined with RF, provides &gt;99% PPV</li>
<li><strong>Prognosis</strong>: Higher titers predict more erosive, aggressive disease</li>
<li><strong>Pre-clinical detection</strong>: Can appear 5-10 years before clinical symptoms</li>
<li><strong>Treatment monitoring</strong>: Titers may decrease with effective DMARD therapy</li>
<li><strong>Differential diagnosis</strong>: Helps distinguish RA from other inflammatory arthropathies</li>
</ul>
