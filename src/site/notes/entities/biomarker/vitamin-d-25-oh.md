<h1>Vitamin D (25-OH)</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>25-Hydroxyvitamin D (25-OH Vitamin D, calcidiol) is the primary circulating form of vitamin D and the standard biomarker used to assess vitamin D status. It is produced in the liver by hydroxylation of vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol, from sun exposure and animal sources) or vitamin D2 (ergocalciferol, from plant/fungal sources). With a half-life of approximately 2-3 weeks, 25-OH D provides a reliable snapshot of vitamin D stores accumulated from both dietary intake and endogenous skin synthesis over the preceding 1-2 months.</p>
<p>Vitamin D functions as a prohormone with receptors in virtually every tissue in the body. Beyond its classical role in calcium absorption and bone mineralization, vitamin D modulates immune function (both innate and adaptive immunity), regulates cell proliferation and differentiation, supports neuromuscular function, and influences gene expression of over 200 genes. Deficiency is remarkably prevalent worldwide, affecting an estimated 1 billion people globally, and is associated with increased risk of osteoporosis, fractures, autoimmune diseases, cardiovascular disease, certain cancers, depression, and all-cause mortality.</p>
<p>25-OH Vitamin D is ordered as a routine screening test in patients with risk factors for deficiency (limited sun exposure, dark skin, obesity, malabsorption, older age, chronic kidney disease) and to monitor supplementation adequacy. It is also used in the workup of unexplained hypocalcemia, elevated parathyroid hormone, bone pain, muscle weakness, and recurrent fractures.</p>

<h2>Clinical Interpretation</h2>
<p><strong>Low 25-OH Vitamin D (&lt;30 ng/mL):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Insufficient sun exposure (indoor lifestyle, high latitude, sunscreen use)</li>
<li>Dietary deficiency (low intake of fatty fish, fortified foods)</li>
<li>Obesity (vitamin D sequestered in adipose tissue)</li>
<li>Malabsorption (celiac disease, Crohn's disease, gastric bypass)</li>
<li>Chronic kidney disease (impaired 1-alpha-hydroxylation)</li>
<li>Liver disease (impaired 25-hydroxylation)</li>
<li>Medications: anticonvulsants, glucocorticoids, rifampin, cholestyramine</li>
<li>Clinical consequences: secondary hyperparathyroidism, osteomalacia/rickets, increased fracture risk, immune dysfunction, fatigue, muscle weakness, depression</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Elevated 25-OH Vitamin D (&gt;100 ng/mL):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Excessive supplementation (most common cause; toxicity typically &gt;150 ng/mL)</li>
<li>Granulomatous diseases (sarcoidosis, tuberculosis) -- extrarenal 1-alpha-hydroxylase activity</li>
<li>Some lymphomas</li>
<li>Symptoms of toxicity: hypercalcemia, nausea, vomiting, polyuria, renal stones, soft tissue calcification</li>
</ul>
