<h1>HLA-B27</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>HLA-B27 is a major histocompatibility complex (MHC) class I molecule present on the surface of nucleated cells. It is strongly associated with spondyloarthropathies, particularly ankylosing spondylitis (AS), where it is found in over 90% of patients compared to only 6-8% of the general population.</p>
<p>Several hypotheses explain the role of HLA-B27 in disease pathogenesis. The &quot;arthritogenic peptide&quot; hypothesis proposes that HLA-B27 presents specific self-peptides to CD8+ T cells, triggering autoimmunity. The &quot;misfolding&quot; hypothesis suggests that unfolded HLA-B27 heavy chains activate the unfolded protein response and stimulate IL-23 production. The &quot;homodimer&quot; hypothesis posits that free heavy chain homodimers activate NK cells and T cells via KIR3DL2 receptors.</p>
<p>HLA-B27 is not diagnostic on its own -- its positive predictive value is only approximately 20% in the general population. Clinical utility is greatest when pre-test probability is already moderate, such as in a young patient presenting with inflammatory back pain. The test is performed once, as it is a genetic marker that does not change over time.</p>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Ankylosing spondylitis</strong>: Present in &gt;90% of AS patients; supports diagnosis in appropriate clinical context</li>
<li><strong>Spondyloarthropathy spectrum</strong>: Associated with reactive arthritis, psoriatic arthritis (axial), and IBD-associated arthritis</li>
<li><strong>Anterior uveitis</strong>: Strong association with HLA-B27-positive acute anterior uveitis</li>
<li><strong>Prognostic value</strong>: HLA-B27 positivity in AS may predict earlier disease onset and more severe axial involvement</li>
<li><strong>Limitation</strong>: Low PPV in general screening; should only be ordered when clinical suspicion exists</li>
</ul>
