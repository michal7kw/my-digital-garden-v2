<h1>GABA</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Gamma-aminobutyric acid (GABA) is the primary inhibitory neurotransmitter in the central nervous system. Plasma GABA is a research biomarker — its correlation with CNS GABA levels is debated, but low plasma GABA has been associated with anxiety, insomnia, epilepsy, and mood disorders. GABA is also produced by gut bacteria, linking it to the gut-brain axis. Magnetic resonance spectroscopy (MRS) provides more accurate CNS GABA measurement.</p>
