<h1>Dopamine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Dopamine is a catecholamine neurotransmitter measurable in urine (24-hour collection) and plasma. It plays central roles in reward, motivation, motor control, and executive function. Elevated urinary dopamine suggests pheochromocytoma or neuroblastoma. Low levels are associated with Parkinson's disease, depression, ADHD, and anhedonia. Plasma dopamine is less commonly measured due to rapid degradation.</p>
