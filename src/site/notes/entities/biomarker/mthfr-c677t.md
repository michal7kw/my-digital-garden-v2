<h1>Mthfr C677T</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The MTHFR C677T variant (rs1801133) is a single nucleotide polymorphism in the methylenetetrahydrofolate reductase (MTHFR) gene located on chromosome 1p36.3. This variant results in an alanine-to-valine substitution at position 222 of the protein, producing a thermolabile enzyme with reduced activity. MTHFR catalyzes the conversion of 5,10-methylenetetrahydrofolate to 5-methyltetrahydrofolate (5-MTHF), the primary circulating form of folate and the methyl donor required for the remethylation of homocysteine to methionine.</p>
<p>The C677T variant is one of the most commonly tested genetic polymorphisms in clinical medicine. Heterozygous carriers (CT genotype, approximately 40-50% of Caucasians) retain about 65% of normal enzyme activity, while homozygous individuals (TT genotype, approximately 10-15% of Caucasians) retain only about 30% of normal activity. The TT genotype is associated with elevated homocysteine levels, particularly in the setting of low folate status, and has been variably linked to increased risk of neural tube defects, cardiovascular disease, venous thromboembolism, and certain cancers.</p>
<p>The clinical significance of this variant is modulated heavily by folate status. With adequate folate intake (through diet or supplementation, particularly with 5-MTHF or folinic acid), homozygous TT individuals can maintain normal homocysteine levels. This gene-nutrient interaction underscores the importance of interpreting MTHFR genotype results in the context of folate and homocysteine levels rather than in isolation.</p>

<h2>Clinical Interpretation</h2>
<p><strong>TT Genotype (Homozygous Variant):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Reduced MTHFR enzyme activity (~30% of normal)</li>
<li>Elevated homocysteine, especially with low folate intake</li>
<li>Increased risk of neural tube defects in offspring (maternal TT)</li>
<li>Modestly increased risk of cardiovascular disease (meta-analyses show OR ~1.15-1.20)</li>
<li>Possible association with recurrent pregnancy loss</li>
<li>Reduced conversion of folic acid to active 5-MTHF</li>
<li>May benefit from supplementation with methylfolate (5-MTHF) rather than folic acid</li>
<li>Consider monitoring homocysteine and B12 levels</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>CT Genotype (Heterozygous):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Mildly reduced enzyme activity (~65% of normal)</li>
<li>Generally clinically insignificant with adequate folate intake</li>
<li>Minimal impact on homocysteine levels in well-nourished individuals</li>
<li>No specific supplementation changes typically required</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>CC Genotype (Wild Type):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Normal enzyme activity</li>
<li>Standard folate metabolism</li>
</ul>
