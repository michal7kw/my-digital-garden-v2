<h1>Glutamate</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Glutamate is the most abundant excitatory neurotransmitter in the central nervous system. Plasma glutamate serves as a research biomarker — elevated levels may indicate blood-brain barrier disruption, excitotoxicity, or neurodegeneration. High plasma glutamate has been associated with ALS, epilepsy, migraine, and traumatic brain injury. The glutamate-GABA balance is critical for neural function; disruption underlies many neurological and psychiatric conditions.</p>
