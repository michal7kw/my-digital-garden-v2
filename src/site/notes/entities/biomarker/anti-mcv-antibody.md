<h1>Anti-MCV Antibody</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Anti-MCV targets mutated citrullinated vimentin, an intermediate filament protein that is modified post-translationally in inflamed synovium. Vimentin is an abundant protein in macrophages and fibroblasts of the synovial lining. In RA synovium, PAD enzymes citrullinate vimentin, creating neo-epitopes that trigger autoantibody production.</p>
<p>Anti-MCV may detect additional patients missed by anti-CCP testing, particularly in early RA where sensitivity matters most. Some studies suggest anti-MCV titers correlate better with disease activity than anti-CCP, making it a potentially useful marker for monitoring treatment response alongside traditional inflammatory markers.</p>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Diagnosis</strong>: Comparable or slightly higher sensitivity than anti-CCP for RA</li>
<li><strong>Early RA</strong>: May identify patients earlier than anti-CCP</li>
<li><strong>Seronegative RA</strong>: Can be positive when anti-CCP and RF are negative</li>
<li><strong>Disease activity</strong>: Titers may correlate with disease activity better than anti-CCP</li>
<li><strong>Complementary testing</strong>: Best used alongside anti-CCP and RF for comprehensive assessment</li>
</ul>
