<h1>Tibc</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Total Iron Binding Capacity (TIBC) measures the maximum amount of iron that transferrin, the primary iron transport protein in the blood, can bind. It is an indirect measure of transferrin concentration and serves as a key indicator of the body's iron transport capacity. TIBC is essential for differentiating between various causes of iron abnormalities and is commonly ordered as part of an iron panel alongside serum iron, ferritin, and transferrin saturation.</p>
<p>Clinically, TIBC is most valuable when interpreted in conjunction with serum iron and ferritin levels. It rises when the body's iron stores are depleted, as the liver produces more transferrin to maximize iron absorption. Conversely, TIBC decreases in conditions of iron overload or chronic inflammation. This inverse relationship with iron stores makes TIBC a powerful diagnostic tool for distinguishing iron deficiency anemia from anemia of chronic disease, where iron may be sequestered rather than truly depleted.</p>
<p>TIBC is routinely ordered when evaluating unexplained anemia, fatigue, or abnormal serum iron results. It is particularly useful in assessing nutritional deficiencies, chronic blood loss (e.g., gastrointestinal bleeding, heavy menstruation), pregnancy-related iron demands, and monitoring iron supplementation therapy.</p>

<h2>Clinical Interpretation</h2>
<p><strong>Elevated TIBC (&gt;370 mcg/dL):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Iron deficiency anemia (most common cause)</li>
<li>Acute or chronic blood loss</li>
<li>Pregnancy (physiological increase)</li>
<li>Oral contraceptive use</li>
<li>Late-stage liver disease (occasionally)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Low TIBC (&lt;250 mcg/dL):</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Anemia of chronic disease / chronic inflammation</li>
<li>Iron overload / hemochromatosis</li>
<li>Malnutrition or protein deficiency (reduced transferrin synthesis)</li>
<li>Nephrotic syndrome (urinary protein loss)</li>
<li>Chronic liver disease / cirrhosis</li>
<li>Malignancy</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Transferrin Saturation</strong> (calculated as Serum Iron / TIBC x 100):</p>
<ul>
<li>Normal: 20-50%</li>
<li>Iron deficiency: &lt;20%</li>
<li>Iron overload: &gt;50%</li>
</ul>
