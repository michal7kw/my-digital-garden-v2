<h1>SSRI Discontinuation Syndrome</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>SSRI Discontinuation Syndrome is a well-characterized adverse event occurring after abrupt cessation or significant dose reduction of SSRI antidepressants. It affects 20-50% of patients who stop SSRIs abruptly (depending on the specific SSRI) and typically begins within 1-7 days of the last dose. Symptoms are usually mild to moderate and resolve within 1-3 weeks, but can be severe and prolonged in some cases.</p>
<p><strong>Key distinction</strong>: This is NOT addiction or physical dependence. SSRIs do not cause euphoria, drug-seeking behavior, or tolerance to the antidepressant effect. Discontinuation syndrome reflects neuroadaptive changes (serotonin receptor downregulation) that require time to re-equilibrate after SERT blockade is removed.</p>

<h2>Brain Zaps</h2>
<p>The most distinctive and pathognomonic symptom — brief (millisecond) electric shock-like sensations in the brain, often triggered by eye movement. Thought to result from abrupt loss of serotonergic modulation of lateral vestibular nucleus neurons. Unique to serotonergic discontinuation; not seen with other drug classes.</p>

<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<ol>
<li>Chronic SSRI use → sustained SERT blockade → brain adapts: downregulates/desensitizes postsynaptic serotonin receptors</li>
<li>Abrupt SSRI cessation → SERT blockade removed → serotonin rapidly cleared from synapses</li>
<li>Downregulated receptors + reduced synaptic serotonin = transient serotonergic deficit</li>
<li>Re-equilibration takes 1-3 weeks as receptors re-sensitize</li>
</ol>
<h2>Prevention and Management</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Gradual taper</strong>: Reduce dose by 25% every 1-2 weeks (minimum 4-week taper)</li>
<li><strong>Slower taper for high-risk</strong>: Paroxetine/fluvoxamine may need 10% reductions with longer intervals</li>
<li><strong>Fluoxetine bridge</strong>: Switch to fluoxetine (long half-life) before discontinuation — its 4-16 day metabolite provides gradual self-taper</li>
<li><strong>Reassurance</strong>: Symptoms are self-limiting, not indicative of relapse or addiction</li>
<li><strong>Distinguish from relapse</strong>: Discontinuation = rapid onset (days), novel physical symptoms (brain zaps); Relapse = gradual onset (weeks), original depression/anxiety symptoms return</li>
</ul>
