<h1>Piracetam</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The original nootropic drug, a cyclic derivative of GABA. Enhances membrane fluidity and modulates neurotransmission. Used in Europe for cognitive disorders; not approved in US.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Piracetam acts as a positive allosteric modulator of AMPA-type glutamate receptors, enhancing excitatory neurotransmission and long-term potentiation without directly activating the receptors. It also increases membrane fluidity in neurons and erythrocytes by intercalating into phospholipid headgroups, which improves membrane-bound enzyme function and cellular signaling. Piracetam enhances mitochondrial electron transport chain efficiency, particularly at complexes I through IV, improving ATP production under conditions of metabolic stress. Despite its structural resemblance to GABA, it does not bind to GABA-A or GABA-B receptors and has no GABAergic activity.</p>
