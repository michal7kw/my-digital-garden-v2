<h1>Acetaminophen</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Acetaminophen (Tylenol/Paracetamol) is the most widely used analgesic and antipyretic worldwide, available over-the-counter and as a component of hundreds of combination products. It provides effective relief for mild to moderate pain and fever without the gastrointestinal or antiplatelet effects of NSAIDs, making it first-line for patients with peptic ulcer disease, renal insufficiency, or bleeding risk. However, acetaminophen is the leading cause of acute liver failure in the developed world, with hepatotoxicity occurring at doses above 4g/day (lower threshold with chronic alcohol use or malnutrition). The antidote for overdose is N-acetylcysteine (NAC), which replenishes hepatic glutathione stores.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Acetaminophen's precise mechanism remains incompletely understood, but it primarily acts through central inhibition of cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, particularly a COX-2 variant in the brain and spinal cord, reducing central prostaglandin synthesis and lowering the hypothalamic set point for fever. It may also activate descending serotonergic pain-inhibitory pathways and modulate the endocannabinoid system through its metabolite AM404. Unlike NSAIDs, acetaminophen lacks significant peripheral anti-inflammatory activity because it is inactivated by peroxides at inflamed tissue sites. In overdose, hepatic glutathione stores are depleted and the reactive metabolite NAPQI (N-acetyl-p-benzoquinone imine), normally detoxified by conjugation, accumulates and causes hepatocellular necrosis.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mild to moderate pain</strong>: 325-1000 mg every 4-6 hours; max 4g/day (2g/day with liver disease)</li>
<li><strong>Fever reduction</strong>: First-line antipyretic for all age groups including pediatrics</li>
<li><strong>Osteoarthritis</strong>: First-line per ACR/EULAR guidelines; step 1 before NSAIDs</li>
<li><strong>Combination analgesia</strong>: Combined with opioids (Percocet, Vicodin) for moderate-severe pain</li>
<li><strong>Post-surgical pain</strong>: IV formulation (Ofirmev) for perioperative pain management</li>
<li><strong>Pregnancy</strong>: Considered the safest analgesic during pregnancy (Category B)</li>
</ul>
