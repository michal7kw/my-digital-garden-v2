<h1>Quinidine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Quinidine is the optical isomer of Quinine (from Cinchona bark). It is a <strong>Class IA antiarrhythmic</strong> that stabilizes cardiac membranes. While historically important for maintaining sinus rhythm in atrial fibrillation, its use has declined due to pro-arrhythmic side effects (Torsades de Pointes) and the availability of safer alternatives.</p>
<p>It is also a potent inhibitor of <strong>CYP2D6</strong> and P-glycoprotein, making it a source of significant drug interactions. It is notably used in the Nuedexta combination (Dextromethorphan/Quinidine) solely to block the metabolism of Dextromethorphan.</p>
<h2>Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Atrial Fibrillation/Flutter</strong>: Cardioversion maintenance (historical).</li>
<li><strong>Ventricular Arrhythmias</strong>: Treatment.</li>
<li><strong>Malaria</strong>: Severe P. falciparum infection (IV).</li>
<li><strong>Pseudobulbar Affect</strong>: As a booster for Dextromethorphan.</li>
<li><strong>Brugada Syndrome</strong>: Prevention of V-fib storms.</li>
</ul>
