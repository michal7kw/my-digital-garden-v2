<h1>Quetiapine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Quetiapine (Seroquel) is an atypical antipsychotic with one of the broadest approved indication sets: schizophrenia, bipolar mania, bipolar depression, and adjunctive treatment of major depressive disorder. Its sedative properties (via H1 and alpha-1 antagonism) make it one of the most commonly prescribed medications for off-label insomnia, though this practice is controversial due to metabolic risks. The extended-release formulation (Seroquel XR) allows once-daily dosing. Quetiapine's unique metabolite norquetiapine has potent norepinephrine reuptake inhibition, which contributes to its antidepressant efficacy.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Quetiapine has a complex receptor binding profile that varies significantly by dose. At low doses (25-100 mg), H1 histamine and alpha-1 adrenergic antagonism predominate, producing sedation. At moderate doses (150-300 mg), 5-HT2A serotonin receptor antagonism and the norepinephrine reuptake inhibition from norquetiapine contribute to antidepressant effects. At higher doses (400-800 mg), dopamine D2 receptor blockade becomes sufficient for antipsychotic efficacy. This dose-dependent pharmacology explains its use across different indications. The rapid dissociation from D2 receptors (similar to clozapine) accounts for the low rate of extrapyramidal symptoms. Metabolic side effects (weight gain, dyslipidemia, hyperglycemia) remain significant concerns.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Schizophrenia</strong>: 300-800 mg/day; effective for positive and negative symptoms</li>
<li><strong>Bipolar mania</strong>: 400-800 mg/day; as monotherapy or adjunct to lithium/valproate</li>
<li><strong>Bipolar depression</strong>: 300 mg/day; one of few drugs FDA-approved for this indication</li>
<li><strong>Major depression (adjunct)</strong>: 150-300 mg/day XR; added to antidepressant for treatment-resistant cases</li>
<li><strong>Insomnia (off-label)</strong>: 25-100 mg; widely prescribed but controversial due to metabolic risks</li>
</ul>
