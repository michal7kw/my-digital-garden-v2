<h1>Atorvastatin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Atorvastatin (Lipitor) is a high-intensity HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor (statin) and the most prescribed cholesterol-lowering medication in history, with over 29 million US prescriptions annually. At maximum dose (80 mg), it can reduce LDL cholesterol by 50-60%. It is used for primary and secondary prevention of cardiovascular events, hyperlipidemia, and familial hypercholesterolemia. Unlike some statins, atorvastatin has active metabolites (ortho- and para-hydroxy derivatives), extending its effective duration of action. It is metabolized by CYP3A4, creating clinically significant interactions with CYP3A4 inhibitors. Statin therapy is associated with a modest increase in new-onset diabetes risk, which is generally outweighed by cardiovascular benefit in high-risk patients.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Atorvastatin competitively inhibits HMG-CoA reductase, the rate-limiting enzyme in the mevalonate pathway responsible for hepatic cholesterol biosynthesis. By reducing intracellular cholesterol, hepatocytes upregulate LDL receptor expression on the cell surface, increasing clearance of LDL-cholesterol from the bloodstream. Beyond lipid lowering, atorvastatin exerts pleiotropic effects including improved endothelial function, reduced vascular inflammation, decreased oxidative stress, and stabilization of atherosclerotic plaques. These anti-inflammatory properties are partly mediated through inhibition of isoprenoid intermediates that are required for post-translational modification of small GTPases like Rho and Ras.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Hyperlipidemia</strong>: 10-80 mg once daily; dose-dependent LDL reduction of 39-60%</li>
<li><strong>Primary cardiovascular prevention</strong>: Recommended for patients with 10-year ASCVD risk &gt;=7.5%</li>
<li><strong>Secondary prevention post-MI/stroke</strong>: High-intensity (40-80 mg) is standard of care</li>
<li><strong>Familial hypercholesterolemia</strong>: Often combined with ezetimibe or PCSK9 inhibitors</li>
<li><strong>Chronic kidney disease</strong>: Cardiovascular risk reduction in CKD stages 1-4</li>
</ul>
