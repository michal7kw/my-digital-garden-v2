<h1>Penicillin V</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Penicillin V (phenoxymethylpenicillin) is an oral natural penicillin antibiotic that differs from penicillin G by the addition of a phenoxyacetyl side chain, which confers acid stability and allows reliable gastrointestinal absorption. Developed in the 1950s as an oral alternative to penicillin G, it is widely used for the treatment of mild to moderate infections caused by susceptible gram-positive organisms, particularly group A streptococcal pharyngitis, and for prophylaxis of rheumatic fever recurrence.</p>
<p>Penicillin V achieves lower peak serum concentrations than parenteral penicillin G but provides adequate tissue levels for less severe infections. It is available as penicillin V potassium tablets and oral suspension.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Like all beta-lactam antibiotics, penicillin V inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis by binding to penicillin-binding proteins (PBPs). It blocks the transpeptidation reaction that cross-links peptidoglycan chains, weakening the cell wall and resulting in osmotic lysis of dividing bacteria. The mechanism is identical to penicillin G: the beta-lactam ring acylates the active-site serine of PBPs, forming an irreversible covalent bond. Penicillin V has a similar antibacterial spectrum to penicillin G but is somewhat less potent against gram-negative organisms such as Neisseria. It is susceptible to the same resistance mechanisms: beta-lactamase hydrolysis, PBP modification, and efflux pumps.</p>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<p>Penicillin V is the standard oral treatment for group A streptococcal (GAS) pharyngitis (strep throat), where it is preferred over broader-spectrum antibiotics to minimize resistance selection. A 10-day course achieves greater than 90% bacteriological eradication and effectively prevents acute rheumatic fever. It is also used for dental infections, mild skin and soft tissue infections caused by streptococci, and prophylaxis in patients with prior rheumatic fever or anatomic/functional asplenia. Penicillin V is generally well tolerated; the most common adverse effects are gastrointestinal (nausea, diarrhea) and hypersensitivity reactions. As with all penicillins, serious allergic reactions including anaphylaxis can occur, and cross-reactivity with other beta-lactams should be assessed before prescribing.</p>
