<h1>Upadacitinib</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Upadacitinib (Rinvoq) is a selective JAK1 inhibitor developed by AbbVie, engineered through structure-based drug design to achieve greater than 50-fold selectivity for JAK1 over JAK2 and greater than 100-fold over JAK3. This improved selectivity profile represents a second-generation approach to JAK inhibition, aiming to maximize efficacy through targeted JAK1 blockade while minimizing off-target effects associated with JAK2 inhibition (anemia, thrombocytopenia) and JAK3 inhibition (lymphopenia).</p>
<p>Upadacitinib holds one of the broadest indication profiles among JAK inhibitors, with approvals for rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, atopic dermatitis, ulcerative colitis, and Crohn's disease. It is administered as a once-daily extended-release tablet (15 mg or 30 mg depending on indication). In head-to-head trials against adalimumab in RA (SELECT-COMPARE), upadacitinib demonstrated superior ACR50 response rates and clinical remission, establishing its position as a highly effective option in the RA treatment algorithm.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Upadacitinib was specifically engineered for JAK1 selectivity using crystal structure analysis of the JAK family ATP-binding sites. By preferentially occupying the JAK1 catalytic domain, it blocks signaling from IL-6 (via gp130/JAK1), type I and type II interferons (IFN-alpha/beta via JAK1/TYK2, IFN-gamma via JAK1/JAK2), and gamma-chain cytokines (IL-2, IL-4, IL-7, IL-9, IL-15, IL-21 via JAK1/JAK3). The net effect is broad suppression of inflammatory and adaptive immune signaling through a single molecular target.</p>
<p>The key pharmacological advantage of JAK1 selectivity is preservation of JAK2-dependent pathways, particularly erythropoietin receptor signaling (critical for red blood cell production) and thrombopoietin receptor signaling (platelet production). This theoretically reduces the hematologic toxicity seen with less selective JAK inhibitors. Clinical data supports this, with lower rates of significant anemia compared to baricitinib, though the class-wide venous thromboembolism risk remains a concern reflected in the FDA boxed warning.</p>
