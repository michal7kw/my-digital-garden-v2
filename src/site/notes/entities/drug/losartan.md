<h1>Losartan</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Losartan (Cozaar) was the first angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB) developed and remains widely prescribed for hypertension, diabetic nephropathy, and stroke prevention in patients with left ventricular hypertrophy. It provides similar cardiovascular and renal benefits to ACE inhibitors but with significantly lower rates of cough and angioedema, making it a preferred alternative for ACE-inhibitor-intolerant patients. Uniquely among ARBs, losartan has a uricosuric effect (lowers uric acid), which may be beneficial in patients with concurrent gout or hyperuricemia. The active metabolite EXP-3174 is 10-40 times more potent than the parent compound.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Losartan selectively antagonizes the angiotensin II type 1 (AT1) receptor, blocking the effects of angiotensin II on vasoconstriction, aldosterone release, and sympathetic activation. Unlike ACE inhibitors, losartan does not inhibit bradykinin degradation, which explains the lower incidence of cough. It is metabolized by CYP2C9 and CYP3A4 to an active carboxylic acid metabolite (EXP-3174) that is 10-40 times more potent than the parent compound and accounts for most of the pharmacologic effect. Losartan also has a unique uricosuric property among ARBs, reducing serum uric acid levels by inhibiting urate reabsorption in the proximal tubule.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Hypertension</strong>: 25-100 mg once daily; effective as monotherapy or combination</li>
<li><strong>Diabetic nephropathy</strong>: Slows progression in type 2 diabetes with proteinuria (RENAAL trial)</li>
<li><strong>Stroke prevention</strong>: In hypertensive patients with LVH (LIFE trial); superior to atenolol</li>
<li><strong>Heart failure</strong>: Alternative to ACE inhibitors in intolerant patients</li>
<li><strong>Combination therapy</strong>: Available as Hyzaar (with hydrochlorothiazide)</li>
<li><strong>Hyperuricemia/gout</strong>: Unique uricosuric effect provides added benefit</li>
</ul>
