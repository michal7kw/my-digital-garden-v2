<h1>Allopurinol</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Allopurinol is the first-line urate-lowering therapy for gout. As a structural analogue of hypoxanthine, it inhibits xanthine oxidase, blocking the conversion of hypoxanthine to xanthine and xanthine to uric acid. Its active metabolite oxypurinol has a much longer half-life (18-30 hours), providing sustained urate lowering throughout the dosing interval.</p>
<p>Treatment should be initiated at a low dose (100mg daily, or 50mg in renal impairment) and titrated every 2-4 weeks to achieve a target serum urate below 6 mg/dL. HLA-B*5801 screening is strongly recommended before initiation, particularly in patients of Southeast Asian or African American descent, due to the risk of severe hypersensitivity reactions (DRESS syndrome, Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis). Colchicine or NSAID prophylaxis should be co-prescribed during the first 3-6 months to prevent initiation flares.</p>
