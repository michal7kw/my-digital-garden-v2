<h1>Febuxostat</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Febuxostat (Uloric) is a non-purine selective xanthine oxidase inhibitor used as urate-lowering therapy for chronic gout. Unlike allopurinol, which is a purine analogue, febuxostat inhibits both the oxidized and reduced forms of xanthine oxidase without requiring metabolic activation and without affecting other enzymes in purine or pyrimidine metabolism.</p>
<p>Febuxostat is more potent than allopurinol 300mg at lowering serum urate and does not require dose adjustment for mild-to-moderate renal impairment, making it an important alternative for patients who cannot tolerate or have contraindications to allopurinol. However, the CARES trial demonstrated a higher rate of cardiovascular mortality compared to allopurinol, leading to an FDA boxed warning. Febuxostat is now generally reserved for patients who have failed or are intolerant to allopurinol. Concurrent use with azathioprine or mercaptopurine is absolutely contraindicated due to risk of fatal myelosuppression.</p>
