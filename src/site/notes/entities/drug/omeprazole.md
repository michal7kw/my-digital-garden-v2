<h1>Omeprazole</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Omeprazole (Prilosec) was the first proton pump inhibitor (PPI) and remains one of the most prescribed medications globally, available both by prescription and over-the-counter. It irreversibly inhibits the gastric H+/K+ ATPase proton pump, providing potent and sustained acid suppression for GERD, peptic ulcer disease, H. pylori eradication (as part of triple/quadruple therapy), and Zollinger-Ellison syndrome. Long-term PPI use (&gt;1 year) is associated with risks including vitamin B12 and magnesium deficiency, increased C. difficile infection, possible bone fracture risk, and impaired absorption of calcium, iron, and certain medications. Omeprazole inhibits CYP2C19, which creates a clinically significant interaction with clopidogrel (reduced antiplatelet effect).</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Omeprazole is a prodrug that accumulates in the acidic environment of gastric parietal cell canaliculi, where it is protonated and converted to its active sulfenamide form. This active metabolite irreversibly binds to cysteine residues on the hydrogen-potassium ATPase (proton pump), the final common pathway for gastric acid secretion. Because the inhibition is covalent and irreversible, acid secretion resumes only after new proton pump molecules are synthesized, resulting in prolonged acid suppression (24-72 hours) despite the drug's short plasma half-life. At standard doses, omeprazole reduces basal and stimulated gastric acid output by approximately 80-95%.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>GERD</strong>: 20-40 mg once daily for 4-8 weeks; maintenance 20 mg/day for chronic GERD</li>
<li><strong>Peptic ulcer disease</strong>: 20-40 mg daily for 4-8 weeks for healing</li>
<li><strong>H. pylori eradication</strong>: 20 mg BID as part of triple therapy (with clarithromycin + amoxicillin)</li>
<li><strong>Zollinger-Ellison syndrome</strong>: 60-120 mg/day; higher doses required for gastrinoma</li>
<li><strong>NSAID gastroprotection</strong>: 20 mg/day for patients on chronic NSAIDs at GI risk</li>
<li><strong>OTC use</strong>: 20 mg/day for 14 days for frequent heartburn (Prilosec OTC)</li>
<li><strong>Stress ulcer prophylaxis</strong>: IV omeprazole in ICU patients</li>
</ul>
