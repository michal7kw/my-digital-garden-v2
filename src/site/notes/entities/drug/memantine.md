<h1>Memantine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Memantine (Namenda) is an uncompetitive NMDA receptor antagonist FDA-approved for moderate to severe Alzheimer's disease. It is the only approved drug for Alzheimer's that works outside the cholinergic system, making it mechanistically complementary to cholinesterase inhibitors like <a href="/entities/drug/donepezil/" class="internal-link">Donepezil</a>. The fixed-dose combination of memantine XR and donepezil (Namzaric) is available for patients already stabilized on both drugs. Memantine's voltage-dependent, low-affinity NMDA blockade selectively attenuates pathological excitotoxic signaling while preserving normal synaptic transmission required for learning and memory. It is generally well-tolerated with fewer GI side effects than cholinesterase inhibitors.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Memantine is an uncompetitive (open-channel) NMDA receptor antagonist with moderate affinity and rapid blocking/unblocking kinetics. Under normal physiological conditions, the NMDA receptor channel is blocked by magnesium ions, and brief glutamate signals displace this block to allow calcium influx for synaptic plasticity. In Alzheimer's disease, chronic low-level glutamate elevation causes tonic NMDA receptor activation, sustained calcium influx, and excitotoxic neuronal damage. Memantine replaces the lost magnesium block under these pathological conditions, reducing tonic calcium influx (neuroprotection) while its rapid off-rate allows normal phasic signaling to pass through (preserving learning and memory). This mechanism is fundamentally different from high-affinity NMDA antagonists like ketamine, which block both pathological and physiological signaling.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Moderate to severe Alzheimer's disease</strong>: 5 mg titrated to 10 mg BID (IR) or 7 mg to 28 mg daily (XR)</li>
<li><strong>Combination therapy</strong>: Often combined with <a href="/entities/drug/donepezil/" class="internal-link">Donepezil</a> for additive benefit in moderate-severe AD</li>
<li><strong>Namzaric</strong>: Fixed-dose combination of memantine XR 28mg + donepezil 10mg</li>
<li><strong>Vascular dementia</strong>: Off-label use with some supporting evidence</li>
<li><strong>Mild Alzheimer's disease</strong>: Not recommended; no demonstrated benefit in mild stages</li>
<li><strong>Other dementias</strong>: Investigated for frontotemporal dementia, Lewy body dementia</li>
</ul>
