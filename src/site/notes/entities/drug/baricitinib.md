<h1>Baricitinib</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Baricitinib (Olumiant) is an oral Janus kinase inhibitor that selectively targets JAK1 and JAK2, distinguishing it from tofacitinib's JAK1/JAK3 selectivity profile. This JAK1/2 selectivity makes baricitinib particularly effective at blocking IL-6 and interferon signaling pathways, which are central to the pathogenesis of rheumatoid arthritis. It was developed by Eli Lilly and Incyte Corporation and is approved for moderate-to-severe RA in patients who have had an inadequate response to one or more DMARDs.</p>
<p>Beyond rheumatoid arthritis, baricitinib has gained approvals for alopecia areata (where it was the first systemic therapy approved) and atopic dermatitis. Its 12-hour half-life supports once-daily dosing, improving patient convenience and adherence compared to twice-daily regimens. During the COVID-19 pandemic, baricitinib received emergency use authorization for treatment of hospitalized patients, based on its ability to inhibit both inflammatory cytokine signaling and viral endocytosis via AAK1/GAK kinase inhibition.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Baricitinib binds to the ATP-binding site of JAK1 and JAK2, preventing their phosphorylation and subsequent activation of STAT transcription factors. JAK2 inhibition is particularly relevant for blocking IL-6 trans-signaling (via the gp130/JAK2/STAT3 axis) and interferon-gamma signaling, both of which drive synovial inflammation and joint destruction in RA. JAK1 co-inhibition provides additional blockade of IL-6 classic signaling and type I interferon pathways.</p>
<p>The downstream effect is reduced phosphorylation of STAT1 and STAT3, leading to decreased transcription of pro-inflammatory genes, reduced acute phase reactant synthesis (CRP, ESR), and suppressed Th1/Th17 differentiation. Unlike JAK3-selective inhibitors, baricitinib's JAK2 activity can modestly affect erythropoietin and thrombopoietin signaling, which accounts for the occasional hematologic effects observed in clinical trials.</p>
