<h1>Vilazodone</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Vilazodone (Viibryd) is a novel antidepressant approved by the FDA in 2011 for major depressive disorder. It is classified as a SPARI (Serotonin Partial Agonist-Reuptake Inhibitor), combining two mechanisms:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>SERT inhibition</strong> (Ki 0.1 nM): Among the most potent SERT inhibitors, comparable to paroxetine</li>
<li><strong>5-HT1A partial agonism</strong> (Ki 0.2 nM): Acts at the same receptor as buspirone, potentially enhancing serotonergic transmission while reducing autoreceptor-mediated negative feedback</li>
</ol>
<p>This dual mechanism is designed to address two limitations of pure SSRIs: the 2-4 week delay in therapeutic onset (by accelerating 5-HT1A autoreceptor desensitization) and the high rate of sexual dysfunction (by modulating 5-HT1A-mediated downstream signaling).</p>
<h2>Key Clinical Considerations</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Must take with food</strong>: Bioavailability approximately doubles with food (from ~33% fasting to ~72% with food). Empty stomach significantly reduces drug exposure</li>
<li><strong>Titration required</strong>: Start 10mg → 20mg after 7 days → 40mg after 7 more days</li>
<li><strong>Diarrhea</strong>: Most distinguishing side effect (up to 28% vs ~10% for other SSRIs), usually transient in first 1-2 weeks</li>
<li><strong>Sexual dysfunction</strong>: Clinical trials suggest lower rates than paroxetine/sertraline, but head-to-head data is limited and some meta-analyses show no significant difference</li>
</ul>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>SERT Inhibition</h3>
<p>Vilazodone binds SERT with very high affinity (Ki 0.1 nM), blocking serotonin reuptake similarly to other SSRIs. At steady state, ~50% SERT occupancy is achieved (less than the &gt;80% seen with most SSRIs), likely because the 5-HT1A agonism contributes to therapeutic effect.</p>
<h3>5-HT1A Partial Agonism</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Presynaptic (raphe)</strong>: Partial agonism at autoreceptors may facilitate faster desensitization compared to the indirect activation by SSRIs alone → potentially faster onset</li>
<li><strong>Postsynaptic (cortex/hippocampus)</strong>: Partial agonism enhances downstream serotonergic signaling in cortical and limbic regions → anxiolytic and procognitive effects</li>
<li><strong>Net effect</strong>: Similar to combining an SSRI with buspirone, but in a single molecule</li>
</ul>
<h2>Pharmacogenomics</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>CYP3A4</strong>: Primary metabolic pathway — CYP3A4 inhibitors (ketoconazole, clarithromycin) increase levels significantly; reduce dose to 20mg</li>
<li><strong>CYP2C19</strong>: Minor pathway</li>
<li><strong>CYP2D6</strong>: Minor pathway — less affected by CYP2D6 polymorphisms than paroxetine/fluoxetine</li>
</ul>
