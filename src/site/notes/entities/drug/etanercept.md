<h1>Etanercept</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Etanercept (Enbrel) is a dimeric fusion protein composed of the extracellular ligand-binding domain of the human p75 TNF receptor linked to the Fc portion of human IgG1. Unlike monoclonal antibody-based TNF inhibitors, etanercept functions as a soluble decoy receptor, sequestering circulating TNF-alpha and TNF-beta (lymphotoxin-alpha) before they can engage cell-surface TNF receptors. This distinct mechanism accounts for several clinically relevant differences from anti-TNF antibodies.</p>
<p>Because etanercept does not bind transmembrane TNF with sufficient avidity to induce complement-dependent cytotoxicity (CDC) or antibody-dependent cell-mediated cytotoxicity (ADCC), it does not lyse TNF-expressing cells such as activated macrophages. This explains its reduced efficacy in granulomatous diseases like Crohn's disease and sarcoidosis, where transmembrane TNF on macrophages plays a central pathogenic role. However, etanercept is highly effective in conditions driven primarily by soluble TNF, including rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, and plaque psoriasis.</p>
<p>Etanercept is administered subcutaneously, typically 50 mg once weekly or 25 mg twice weekly. Its shorter half-life compared to monoclonal antibodies means faster drug clearance if adverse events occur. It has a relatively low immunogenicity, and anti-drug antibodies are generally non-neutralizing.</p>
