<h1>Certolizumab Pegol</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Certolizumab pegol (Cimzia) is a unique TNF inhibitor consisting of a PEGylated Fab' fragment of a humanized anti-TNF-alpha antibody. Unlike all other TNF inhibitors (adalimumab, etanercept, infliximab, golimumab), certolizumab lacks an Fc region entirely. This structural distinction has three clinically important consequences: (1) it does not cross the placenta via FcRn-mediated transport, making it the preferred TNF inhibitor during pregnancy and breastfeeding; (2) it cannot activate complement-dependent cytotoxicity (CDC); and (3) it does not mediate antibody-dependent cellular cytotoxicity (ADCC).</p>
<p>The PEGylation of the Fab' fragment extends its circulating half-life to approximately 14 days, enabling dosing every two to four weeks. Certolizumab binds both soluble and membrane-bound TNF-alpha with high affinity, neutralizing its pro-inflammatory signaling. It is approved for rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, Crohn's disease, and non-radiographic axial spondyloarthritis. The CRIB and CRADLE studies demonstrated minimal placental transfer and negligible breast milk concentrations, providing the strongest safety data for anti-TNF use in pregnancy among all agents in this class.</p>
