<h1>Tirzepatide</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>First-in-class dual GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) and GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) receptor agonist, nicknamed &quot;twincretin&quot; for its unique dual-incretin mechanism. Tirzepatide is a 39-amino acid linear peptide based on the native GIP sequence, engineered with GLP-1 receptor agonist activity through strategic amino acid substitutions. A C20 fatty diacid moiety enables albumin binding and extends the half-life to approximately 5 days, permitting once-weekly subcutaneous dosing. The SURPASS program demonstrated superiority over all comparators in type 2 diabetes, including semaglutide 1mg (SURPASS-2), with HbA1c reductions up to 2.4% — the highest in the GLP-1 receptor agonist class. The SURMOUNT program showed unprecedented weight loss of up to 22.5% at 72 weeks (SURMOUNT-1) and 26% over 88 weeks (SURMOUNT-4), establishing tirzepatide as the most effective approved pharmacotherapy for obesity. Available as Mounjaro (FDA approved December 2022 for T2DM) and Zepbound (FDA approved November 2023 for obesity).</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Tirzepatide is the first approved dual GIP and GLP-1 receptor agonist. It is a 39-amino acid linear peptide based on the native human GIP (1-39) sequence, with key amino acid substitutions that confer GLP-1 receptor agonist activity while retaining full GIP receptor agonism. The molecule incorporates a C20 fatty diacid (icosanoic acid) moiety linked via a spacer to a lysine residue at position 26, which promotes non-covalent binding to serum albumin. This albumin binding dramatically reduces renal clearance and extends the plasma half-life to approximately 5 days.</p>
<p>At the GIP receptor, tirzepatide activates Gs-coupled signaling on pancreatic beta cells, enhancing glucose-dependent insulin secretion with greater potency than native GIP. At the GLP-1 receptor, it provides full agonist activity comparable to native GLP-1, enhancing insulin secretion, suppressing glucagon during hyperglycemia, and delaying gastric emptying. The GIP component uniquely stimulates glucagon secretion during hypoglycemia, providing a protective counter-regulatory mechanism not seen with pure GLP-1 agonists.</p>
<p>The dual receptor activation produces synergistic metabolic effects: (1) enhanced glucose-dependent insulin secretion superior to either incretin alone, (2) appetite suppression via GLP-1 receptor activation in the hypothalamus and brainstem (nucleus tractus solitarius and area postrema), (3) improved adipose tissue lipid buffering and energy expenditure via GIP receptor-mediated effects on adipocytes, (4) delayed gastric emptying contributing to postprandial glucose control and satiety, and (5) anti-inflammatory effects demonstrated by reductions in CRP and pro-inflammatory cytokines.</p>
<h2>Key Clinical Trials</h2>
<h3>SURPASS Program (T2DM)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>SURPASS-1</strong> (Lancet 2022; PMID: 35319304): Monotherapy in T2DM, HbA1c reduction up to 2.0% vs 0.4% placebo, weight loss up to 9.5 kg - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>SURPASS-2</strong> (N Engl J Med 2022; PMID: 35319302): Head-to-head vs semaglutide 1mg. Tirzepatide 15mg achieved HbA1c reduction of 2.4% vs 2.0% for semaglutide. Weight loss 12.4 kg vs 10.2 kg - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>SURPASS-3</strong> (Lancet 2022; PMID: 35319303): Superior to insulin degludec in HbA1c reduction and weight outcomes - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>SURPASS-4</strong> (Lancet 2022; PMID: 35319305): Superior to insulin glargine in patients with high cardiovascular risk - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>SURPASS-5</strong> (Lancet 2022; PMID: 35319306): Superior to placebo as add-on to insulin glargine - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>SURPASS-CVOT</strong> (ongoing): Cardiovascular outcomes trial assessing MACE reduction; results expected 2025 - Evidence Level pending</li>
</ul>
<h3>SURMOUNT Program (Obesity)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>SURMOUNT-1</strong> (N Engl J Med 2023; PMID: 37253064): 22.5% weight loss at 72 weeks (15mg) vs 2.4% placebo in non-diabetic obese/overweight adults. 36% of participants achieved ≥25% weight loss - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>SURMOUNT-2</strong> (Lancet 2024; PMID: 38114378): 15.7% weight loss at 72 weeks in T2DM patients with obesity (15mg) - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>SURMOUNT-3</strong> (Lancet 2024; PMID: 38340537): 26.6% total weight loss including 12-week lead-in period, intensive lifestyle intervention - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>SURMOUNT-4</strong> (JAMA 2024; PMID: 38011981): 26% weight loss over 88 weeks, continued treatment sustained loss vs regain with withdrawal - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>SURMOUNT-OSA</strong> (ongoing): Obstructive sleep apnea improvement with tirzepatide - Evidence Level pending</li>
</ul>
<h3>Emerging Indications</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>NAFLD/NASH</strong>: Significant reductions in hepatic fat content (Phase 2 trials) - Evidence Level 2b</li>
<li><strong>Heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (HFpEF)</strong>: Ongoing clinical trials - Evidence Level 3</li>
<li><strong>Sleep apnea</strong>: SURMOUNT-OSA interim data show significant AHI reduction - Evidence Level 2b</li>
<li><strong>PCOS</strong>: Improved metabolic parameters and insulin resistance - Evidence Level 2b</li>
</ul>
<h2>Pharmacokinetics</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Absorption</strong>: Subcutaneous bioavailability approximately 80%. Peak plasma concentration reached 8-72 hours post-injection</li>
<li><strong>Distribution</strong>: &gt;99% protein binding (albumin) via C20 fatty diacid moiety. Extensive tissue distribution</li>
<li><strong>Metabolism</strong>: Proteolytic cleavage by endogenous peptidases; not metabolized by CYP450 enzymes. No significant drug-drug interactions via CYP pathways</li>
<li><strong>Elimination</strong>: Metabolites excreted primarily in urine and feces. Minimal renal clearance of intact drug due to albumin binding</li>
<li><strong>Half-life</strong>: ~5 days, enabling once-weekly subcutaneous dosing. Steady-state achieved after approximately 4 weeks of weekly administration</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosing and Administration</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mounjaro (T2DM)</strong>: 2.5mg subcutaneously once weekly for 4 weeks (initial dose), then titrate by 2.5mg every 4 weeks as tolerated to maintenance dose of 5mg, 10mg, or 15mg once weekly</li>
<li><strong>Zepbound (obesity)</strong>: Same titration schedule — 2.5mg weekly for 4 weeks, then increase by 2.5mg every 4 weeks to target maintenance dose of 5mg, 10mg, or 15mg once weekly</li>
<li><strong>Administration</strong>: Subcutaneous injection in abdomen, thigh, or upper arm. Rotate injection sites. Can be administered at any time of day, with or without meals</li>
<li><strong>Dose escalation</strong>: Slow titration minimizes gastrointestinal side effects. If a dose is missed, administer within 4 days; otherwise skip and resume next scheduled dose</li>
</ul>
<h2>Adverse Effects Profile</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Common (≥10%)</strong>: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, abdominal pain, dyspepsia, injection site reactions</li>
<li><strong>Less Common (1-10%)</strong>: Hypoglycemia (with concomitant insulin or sulfonylureas), headache, fatigue, dizziness, abdominal distension, eructation</li>
<li><strong>Rare (&lt;1%)</strong>: Acute pancreatitis, cholelithiasis, diabetic retinopathy complications, acute kidney injury (secondary to dehydration from GI effects), hypersensitivity reactions</li>
<li><strong>Serious Warnings</strong>: Thyroid C-cell tumor risk (observed in rodent studies with GLP-1 agonists; clinical significance unknown), contraindicated in MEN2 and personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma. GI adverse effects are generally transient and dose-dependent, most common during dose escalation</li>
</ul>
