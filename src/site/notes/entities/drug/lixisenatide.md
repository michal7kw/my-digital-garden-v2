<h1>Lixisenatide</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Short-acting GLP-1 receptor agonist administered once daily by subcutaneous injection. Lixisenatide is a modified exendin-4 analog that provides predominantly postprandial glucose control through pronounced delay of gastric emptying. Available as Adlyxin in the US and Lyxumia in the EU, it occupies a distinct niche among GLP-1 agonists due to its strong effect on post-meal glucose excursions rather than fasting glucose. Its short half-life (~3 hours) differentiates it from longer-acting agents like liraglutide and semaglutide.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Lixisenatide is a 44-amino acid peptide based on the exendin-4 scaffold, modified with the addition of 6 C-terminal lysine residues. It shares ~53% sequence homology with human GLP-1 and is resistant to DPP-4 degradation. Upon binding to GLP-1 receptors on pancreatic beta cells, it enhances glucose-dependent insulin secretion with a low risk of hypoglycemia. Its most distinguishing pharmacological feature is a pronounced delay of gastric emptying, which makes it particularly effective at controlling postprandial glucose spikes. It also suppresses postprandial glucagon secretion from alpha cells and promotes satiety through central GLP-1 receptor activation in the hypothalamus and brainstem.</p>
<h2>Key Clinical Trials</h2>
<h3>GetGoal Program</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>GetGoal-Mono</strong> (Diabetes Care 2013; PMID: 23378621): Monotherapy efficacy - significant HbA1c reduction vs placebo - Evidence Level 1b</li>
<li><strong>GetGoal-Duo 1</strong> (Diabetes Obes Metab 2014): Combination with basal insulin - additive postprandial glucose control - Evidence Level 1b</li>
<li><strong>GetGoal-Duo 2</strong> (Diabetes Obes Metab 2013): Combination with basal insulin after titration - Evidence Level 1b</li>
<li><strong>GetGoal-L</strong> (Diabetes Obes Metab 2013): Efficacy in patients with low baseline insulin secretion - Evidence Level 1b</li>
<li><strong>GetGoal-S</strong> (Diabetes Obes Metab 2013): Add-on to sulfonylureas - Evidence Level 1b</li>
<li><strong>GetGoal-F</strong> (Int J Clin Pract 2013): Flexible dosing in real-world setting - Evidence Level 2b</li>
</ul>
<h3>ELIXA Trial</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>ELIXA</strong> (N Engl J Med 2015; PMID: 26348504): Cardiovascular safety post-acute coronary syndrome - neutral outcomes, no increased CV risk - Evidence Level 1a</li>
</ul>
<h2>Pharmacokinetics</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Absorption</strong>: Moderate subcutaneous bioavailability, peak concentration at ~1-3.5 hours post-injection</li>
<li><strong>Distribution</strong>: Minimal protein binding; limited tissue distribution</li>
<li><strong>Metabolism</strong>: Proteolytic degradation and glomerular filtration; not CYP-mediated</li>
<li><strong>Elimination</strong>: Renal excretion via glomerular filtration and proteolytic degradation</li>
<li><strong>Half-life</strong>: ~3 hours, requiring once-daily dosing (shorter than liraglutide but longer than exenatide BID)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosing and Administration</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Starting dose</strong>: 10mcg subcutaneous injection once daily for 14 days</li>
<li><strong>Maintenance dose</strong>: 20mcg subcutaneous injection once daily</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Inject within 1 hour before the first meal of the day</li>
<li><strong>Administration sites</strong>: Abdomen, thigh, or upper arm (rotate injection sites)</li>
<li><strong>No dose adjustment</strong> needed for mild-to-moderate renal impairment; use caution in severe renal impairment</li>
</ul>
<h2>Adverse Effects Profile</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Common (≥10%)</strong>: Nausea (25-30%), vomiting (~10%), diarrhea, headache</li>
<li><strong>Less Common (1-10%)</strong>: Hypoglycemia (particularly with concomitant sulfonylureas), injection site reactions, dyspepsia</li>
<li><strong>Rare (&lt;1%)</strong>: Acute pancreatitis, allergic reactions including anaphylaxis</li>
<li><strong>Serious Warnings</strong>: Thyroid C-cell tumor risk (rodent studies), contraindicated in MEN2 and personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma</li>
</ul>
