<h1>Golimumab</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Golimumab (Simponi, Simponi Aria) is a fully human IgG1 monoclonal antibody that targets tumor necrosis factor alpha (TNF-alpha). It is distinguished from other TNF inhibitors primarily by its convenient once-monthly subcutaneous dosing schedule, the longest dosing interval among subcutaneous anti-TNF agents. An intravenous formulation (Simponi Aria) is also available for rheumatoid arthritis, administered every eight weeks after loading doses.</p>
<p>As a fully human antibody generated using transgenic mice, golimumab binds with high affinity to both soluble and transmembrane forms of TNF-alpha, neutralizing TNF-mediated pro-inflammatory signaling including NF-kB activation. Unlike certolizumab pegol, golimumab retains its Fc region and can therefore mediate antibody-dependent cellular cytotoxicity (ADCC) and complement-dependent cytotoxicity (CDC) against cells expressing membrane-bound TNF. It is approved for rheumatoid arthritis (in combination with methotrexate), psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, and ulcerative colitis. Clinical trials (GO-FORWARD, GO-AFTER, GO-BEFORE) demonstrated sustained efficacy in RA patients including those with prior anti-TNF exposure.</p>
