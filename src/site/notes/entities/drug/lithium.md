<h1>Lithium</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Lithium (Lithobid/Eskalith) is the gold standard mood stabilizer for bipolar disorder and the only mood stabilizer with robust evidence for reducing suicide risk. It has been in psychiatric use since the 1950s and remains the first-line treatment for bipolar mania and maintenance. Lithium has an extremely narrow therapeutic index (0.6-1.2 mEq/L for acute mania, 0.6-0.8 mEq/L for maintenance), requiring regular blood level monitoring. Long-term use carries risks of thyroid dysfunction (hypothyroidism in 20-30%), nephrogenic diabetes insipidus, and chronic kidney disease. Beyond psychiatry, lithium is studied at micro-doses (e.g., 300 mcg lithium orotate) for neuroprotection, with epidemiologic data suggesting lower dementia rates in populations with higher lithium in drinking water.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Lithium exerts its mood-stabilizing effects through multiple intracellular signaling pathways. It directly inhibits glycogen synthase kinase-3 beta (GSK-3B), a kinase involved in neuronal apoptosis, circadian rhythm regulation, and glycogen metabolism, leading to neuroprotective effects. Lithium also depletes intracellular inositol by inhibiting inositol monophosphatase, attenuating overactive phosphatidylinositol signaling cascades implicated in mania. Additionally, it upregulates brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), promotes autophagy, and modulates glutamatergic and dopaminergic neurotransmission. The neuroprotective effects observed at therapeutic and even micro-doses include reduced tau phosphorylation and increased gray matter volume.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Bipolar mania (acute)</strong>: Target level 0.8-1.2 mEq/L; effective within 1-2 weeks</li>
<li><strong>Bipolar maintenance</strong>: Target level 0.6-0.8 mEq/L; reduces relapse of both mania and depression</li>
<li><strong>Suicide prevention</strong>: Only psychiatric medication with strong evidence for anti-suicidal effect</li>
<li><strong>Augmentation in depression</strong>: Added to antidepressant in treatment-resistant unipolar depression</li>
<li><strong>Schizoaffective disorder</strong>: Adjunct to antipsychotics for mood symptom management</li>
<li><strong>Micro-dose neuroprotection</strong>: Investigational use at sub-therapeutic doses for cognitive preservation</li>
<li><strong>Cluster headache prophylaxis</strong>: Off-label use for chronic cluster headache</li>
</ul>
