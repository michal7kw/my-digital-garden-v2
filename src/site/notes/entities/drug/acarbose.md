<h1>Acarbose</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Acarbose (Precose/Glucobay) is an alpha-glucosidase inhibitor that slows the digestion and absorption of carbohydrates, specifically targeting postprandial glucose spikes. It is FDA-approved for type 2 diabetes and has shown benefit in prediabetes prevention (STOP-NIDDM trial reduced diabetes progression by 25%). Acarbose has gained interest in the longevity community as a caloric restriction mimetic after the NIA Interventions Testing Program demonstrated significant lifespan extension in male mice. Unlike other antidiabetics, acarbose acts locally in the gut with minimal systemic absorption, resulting in a favorable safety profile but common GI side effects (flatulence, bloating).</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Acarbose competitively inhibits alpha-glucosidase enzymes (maltase, isomaltase, sucrase, glucoamylase) located on the brush border of the small intestinal epithelium, delaying the hydrolysis of complex carbohydrates and disaccharides into absorbable monosaccharides. This shifts carbohydrate absorption from the proximal to distal small intestine, flattening the postprandial glucose curve and reducing insulin demand. Undigested carbohydrates reaching the colon are fermented by gut bacteria, producing short-chain fatty acids and gas (explaining the common GI side effects). The caloric restriction mimetic properties observed in longevity research may relate to altered gut hormone signaling and microbiome changes.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Type 2 diabetes</strong>: 25-100 mg TID with first bite of each meal; reduces HbA1c by 0.5-0.8%</li>
<li><strong>Prediabetes prevention</strong>: STOP-NIDDM trial demonstrated 25% reduction in progression to diabetes</li>
<li><strong>Postprandial hyperglycemia</strong>: Particularly useful when fasting glucose is controlled but post-meal spikes persist</li>
<li><strong>Longevity/anti-aging</strong>: Being studied as a caloric restriction mimetic based on NIA ITP results in mice</li>
<li><strong>Dumping syndrome</strong>: Off-label for post-surgical rapid gastric emptying</li>
</ul>
