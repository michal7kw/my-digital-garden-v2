<h1>Lisinopril</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Lisinopril (Zestril/Prinivil) is an ACE inhibitor and one of the most commonly prescribed antihypertensives, with FDA approval for hypertension, heart failure, and improving survival after acute myocardial infarction. Unlike most ACE inhibitors, lisinopril is not a prodrug and is the only ACE inhibitor that is completely water-soluble, requiring no hepatic activation. It has demonstrated renoprotective effects in diabetic nephropathy by reducing intraglomerular pressure and proteinuria. The most common side effect is a dry cough (10-15%), caused by bradykinin accumulation; angioedema is rare but potentially life-threatening.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Lisinopril competitively inhibits angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE), blocking the conversion of angiotensin I to the potent vasoconstrictor angiotensin II. This reduces aldosterone secretion, decreases sodium and water retention, and lowers peripheral vascular resistance. Additionally, ACE inhibition prevents the degradation of bradykinin, a vasodilatory peptide, which contributes to the blood pressure-lowering effect but also accounts for the characteristic dry cough side effect. The reduction in angiotensin II signaling provides cardioprotective and renoprotective effects by decreasing cardiac remodeling and reducing intraglomerular pressure.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Hypertension</strong>: 5-40 mg once daily; effective as monotherapy or combination</li>
<li><strong>Heart failure</strong>: 2.5-40 mg/day; reduces mortality and hospitalization (ATLAS trial)</li>
<li><strong>Post-myocardial infarction</strong>: Started within 24 hours; improves survival (GISSI-3 trial)</li>
<li><strong>Diabetic nephropathy</strong>: Reduces proteinuria and slows progression of CKD</li>
<li><strong>Combination therapy</strong>: Available as Zestoretic (with hydrochlorothiazide)</li>
</ul>
