<h1>Paroxetine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Paroxetine (Paxil/Seroxat) is an SSRI with the broadest FDA-approved indication set among antidepressants: depression, generalized anxiety disorder, obsessive-compulsive disorder, panic disorder, social anxiety disorder, post-traumatic stress disorder, and premenstrual dysphoric disorder. It has the most potent SERT binding (Ki 0.13 nM) of any SSRI.</p>
<p>However, paroxetine also has the most problematic side effect profile among SSRIs:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Highest sexual dysfunction rate</strong> (65-70%) due to potent serotonergic activity</li>
<li><strong>Most weight gain</strong> among SSRIs, partly from anticholinergic (muscarinic) receptor binding</li>
<li><strong>Worst discontinuation syndrome</strong> — short half-life (21h) with no active metabolites means rapid serotonin drop upon missing doses</li>
<li><strong>Pregnancy Category D</strong> — increased risk of congenital cardiac defects (especially VSD) and persistent pulmonary hypertension of the newborn</li>
<li><strong>Potent CYP2D6 inhibition</strong> — phenocopies CYP2D6 poor metabolizer status, creating dangerous interactions with codeine, tamoxifen, metoprolol</li>
</ul>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Paroxetine selectively inhibits SERT with the highest potency among SSRIs. Unlike other SSRIs, it also has clinically significant:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Muscarinic receptor antagonism</strong>: Causes dry mouth, constipation, sedation, urinary retention (unique among SSRIs)</li>
<li><strong>Weak NET inhibition</strong>: Minor noradrenergic reuptake blockade at higher doses</li>
<li><strong>Potent CYP2D6 inhibition</strong>: Phenocopies poor metabolizer status, blocking metabolism of co-administered CYP2D6 substrates</li>
<li><strong>No active metabolites</strong>: Unlike fluoxetine (norfluoxetine), paroxetine's metabolites are inactive — rapid drug level decline upon discontinuation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Pharmacogenomics</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>CYP2D6 substrate</strong>: Primary metabolic pathway. Poor metabolizers have 3-4x higher plasma levels</li>
<li><strong>CYP2D6 inhibitor</strong>: Paroxetine is itself a potent CYP2D6 inhibitor — auto-inhibits its own metabolism (nonlinear kinetics)</li>
<li><strong>Clinical impact of CYP2D6 inhibition</strong>: Co-administration with tamoxifen (prodrug) blocks conversion to active endoxifen, potentially reducing breast cancer efficacy. The FDA specifically warns against this combination</li>
</ul>
