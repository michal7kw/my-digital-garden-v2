<h1>Semaglutide</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>GLP-1 receptor agonist that has revolutionized treatment of type 2 diabetes and obesity. Semaglutide mimics the incretin hormone GLP-1, which is released after eating to stimulate insulin secretion, suppress glucagon, slow gastric emptying, and reduce appetite via hypothalamic satiety centers. The STEP trials demonstrated 15-17% body weight loss (Wegovy 2.4mg weekly), while the SELECT trial showed 20% reduction in major cardiovascular events in non-diabetic obese patients. Available as weekly injection (Ozempic 0.5-2mg for T2DM; Wegovy 2.4mg for obesity) and daily oral tablet (Rybelsus 7-14mg for T2DM).</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Semaglutide is a long-acting GLP-1 receptor agonist with 94% structural homology to native human GLP-1, modified with a fatty acid side chain that enables albumin binding and extends its half-life to approximately 7 days. It activates the GLP-1 receptor on pancreatic beta cells, enhancing glucose-dependent insulin secretion while suppressing glucagon release from alpha cells, providing glycemic control with minimal hypoglycemia risk. Centrally, semaglutide acts on GLP-1 receptors in the hypothalamus and brainstem (particularly the nucleus tractus solitarius and area postrema) to reduce appetite and caloric intake. It also delays gastric emptying, contributing to postprandial glucose control and satiety, while emerging evidence suggests direct anti-inflammatory and cardioprotective effects on vascular endothelium.</p>
<h2>Key Clinical Trials</h2>
<h3>STEP Program (Semaglutide Treatment Effect in People with Obesity)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>STEP 1</strong> (N Engl J Med 2021; PMID: 34041401): 16.9% weight loss vs 2.4% placebo at 68 weeks (2.4mg weekly) - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>STEP 2</strong> (Lancet 2021; PMID: 34165446): 9.6% weight loss vs 3.2% placebo in T2DM patients - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>STEP 3</strong> (JAMA 2021; PMID: 33588471): 16.0% weight loss vs 5.7% placebo with intensive behavioral therapy - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>STEP 4</strong> (JAMA 2022; PMID: 35338488): 14.9% additional weight loss with continued treatment vs regain with placebo - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>STEP 5</strong> (Lancet 2023; PMID: 37034218): 15.2% weight loss sustained at 104 weeks - Evidence Level 1a</li>
<li><strong>STEP 6</strong> (Obesity 2023; PMID: 37034219): 14.9% weight loss in African American patients - Evidence Level 1b</li>
</ul>
<h3>SUSTAIN Program (Semaglutide Unabated Sustainability in Treatment of Type 2 Diabetes)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>SUSTAIN-6</strong> (N Engl J Med 2016; PMID: 27633129): 26% reduction in major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE) in T2DM (0.5-1.0mg weekly) - Evidence Level 1b</li>
<li><strong>SUSTAIN-7</strong> (Lancet Diabetes Endocrinol 2018; PMID: 29550980): Superior glycemic control vs exenatide ER - Evidence Level 1b</li>
<li><strong>SUSTAIN-8</strong> (Lancet Diabetes Endocrinol 2019; PMID: 31497244): Superior glycemic control vs sitagliptin - Evidence Level 1b</li>
</ul>
<h3>SELECT Trial (Semaglutide Effects on Cardiovascular Outcomes in People With Overweight or Obesity)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>SELECT</strong> (N Engl J Med 2023; PMID: 37495217): 20% reduction in MACE in obese non-diabetic patients (2.4mg weekly) - Evidence Level 1a</li>
</ul>
<h3>FLOW Trial (Semaglutide Effects on Kidney Disease in Patients with Type 2 Diabetes)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>FLOW</strong> (N Engl J Med 2024; PMID: 38241125): 24% reduction in kidney composite outcomes in T2DM with CKD - Evidence Level 1a</li>
</ul>
<h3>Emerging Indications</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>NAFLD/NASH</strong>: 40-70% reduction in liver fat content (Phase 2 trials) - Evidence Level 2b</li>
<li><strong>Alzheimer's disease</strong>: Ongoing clinical trials for cognitive outcomes - Evidence Level 3</li>
<li><strong>Addiction</strong>: Reduced cravings in alcohol, nicotine, and food addiction (pilot studies) - Evidence Level 3</li>
<li><strong>PCOS</strong>: Improved metabolic parameters and fertility outcomes - Evidence Level 2b</li>
<li><strong>Sleep apnea</strong>: Reduced AHI and improved sleep quality - Evidence Level 2b</li>
</ul>
<h2>Pharmacokinetics</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Absorption</strong>: Subcutaneous bioavailability ~89%, oral ~0.8-1% with absorption enhancer</li>
<li><strong>Distribution</strong>: &gt;99% protein binding (albumin), extensive tissue distribution</li>
<li><strong>Metabolism</strong>: Proteolytic cleavage by endogenous peptidases, not CYP-mediated</li>
<li><strong>Elimination</strong>: Urine (23%) and feces (45%) as unchanged parent drug and metabolites</li>
<li><strong>Half-life</strong>: ~7 days (subcutaneous), ~1 week (oral steady-state)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosing and Administration</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Ozempic</strong>: 0.25mg weekly for 4 weeks, then 0.5mg weekly; may increase to 1.0mg weekly (max 2.0mg)</li>
<li><strong>Wegovy</strong>: 0.25mg weekly for 4 weeks, titrate every 4 weeks to 2.4mg weekly (maintenance dose)</li>
<li><strong>Rybelsus</strong>: 3mg daily for 30 days, then 7mg daily; may increase to 14mg daily (max)</li>
<li><strong>Administration</strong>: Subcutaneous injection in abdomen, thigh, or upper arm; oral tablets taken with water on empty stomach (30 minutes before food)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Adverse Effects Profile</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Common (≥10%)</strong>: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, abdominal pain, headache, fatigue</li>
<li><strong>Less Common (1-10%)</strong>: Hypoglycemia (with concomitant insulin/sulfonylureas), dyspepsia, dizziness</li>
<li><strong>Rare (&lt;1%)</strong>: Acute pancreatitis, cholelithiasis, diabetic retinopathy complications</li>
<li><strong>Serious Warnings</strong>: Thyroid C-cell tumor risk (rodent studies), contraindicated in MEN2, acute kidney injury, hypersensitivity reactions</li>
</ul>
