<h1>Vortioxetine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Vortioxetine (Trintellix, formerly Brintellix) is a multimodal antidepressant approved by the FDA in 2013 for major depressive disorder. Unlike traditional SSRIs that act solely by blocking SERT, vortioxetine combines SERT inhibition with direct modulation of five serotonin receptor subtypes. This multimodal mechanism produces distinct downstream effects on glutamate, acetylcholine, dopamine, norepinephrine, and histamine neurotransmission — particularly enhancing glutamate and acetylcholine in the prefrontal cortex.</p>
<p>The most distinctive clinical feature is demonstrated <strong>pro-cognitive benefit</strong> beyond mood improvement. Multiple RCTs (CONNECT, FOCUS studies) show improvement in processing speed, executive function, and attention that is partially independent of the antidepressant effect. This makes vortioxetine particularly suited for depressed patients with prominent cognitive symptoms (&quot;brain fog&quot;).</p>

<h2>Pro-Cognitive Mechanism</h2>
<p>The cognitive benefits of vortioxetine appear to derive from its 5-HT3 and 5-HT7 antagonism:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>5-HT3 antagonism</strong>: Disinhibits pyramidal neurons in PFC → increases glutamate and acetylcholine release → enhances LTP and synaptic plasticity</li>
<li><strong>5-HT7 antagonism</strong>: Modulates glutamatergic transmission and circadian rhythm regulation (sleep architecture improvement)</li>
<li><strong>Net effect</strong>: Enhanced cortical excitability and neurotransmitter diversity in PFC → improved processing speed, executive function, and verbal learning</li>
</ul>
<h2>Pharmacogenomics</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>CYP2D6</strong>: Primary metabolic pathway. Poor metabolizers have ~2x exposure → maximum recommended dose 10mg (vs 20mg for extensive metabolizers)</li>
<li><strong>CYP3A4, CYP2C19, CYP2C9, CYP2A6</strong>: Minor pathways</li>
<li><strong>CYP2D6 inhibitors</strong>: Fluoxetine, paroxetine, bupropion co-administration requires 50% vortioxetine dose reduction</li>
</ul>
