<h1>Doxepin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Doxepin is a tricyclic antidepressant with the most potent antihistaminic activity of any TCA (approximately 800 times more potent than diphenhydramine at H1 receptors). At higher doses (75-150 mg/day as Sinequan/Adapin), it treats depression and anxiety. At ultra-low doses (3-6 mg as Silenor), it is FDA-approved for insomnia characterized by difficulty with sleep maintenance, exploiting its H1 antagonism without significant antidepressant or anticholinergic effects. Topical doxepin (Zonalon) is used for pruritus in eczema and lichen simplex. This versatility across dose ranges makes doxepin unique among TCAs.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Doxepin inhibits the reuptake of serotonin (SERT) and norepinephrine (NET) at presynaptic terminals, with relatively balanced dual inhibition. Its defining pharmacologic feature is extraordinarily potent H1 histamine receptor antagonism, making it the most potent antihistamine among all antidepressants. At low doses (3-6 mg), the H1 antagonism predominates, producing selective sleep maintenance improvement without next-day sedation. At higher antidepressant doses, the full spectrum of TCA activity emerges, including muscarinic receptor antagonism (dry mouth, constipation) and alpha-1 adrenergic blockade (orthostatic hypotension). The antipruritic effect of topical doxepin is mediated by local H1 and H2 receptor blockade.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Depression/Anxiety</strong>: 75-150 mg/day (Sinequan); comparable efficacy to other TCAs</li>
<li><strong>Insomnia (sleep maintenance)</strong>: 3-6 mg at bedtime (Silenor); FDA-approved, non-habit-forming</li>
<li><strong>Pruritus</strong>: Topical 5% cream (Zonalon) for eczema, lichen simplex, contact dermatitis</li>
<li><strong>Chronic urticaria</strong>: Off-label use exploiting H1/H2 dual antagonism</li>
</ul>
