<h1>Doxepin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Doxepin is a tricyclic antidepressant (TCA) that is widely used today for <strong>insomnia</strong> (at low doses) and <strong>pruritus</strong> (itching, often topical) due to its extremely potent <strong>antihistamine</strong> effects. While effective for depression and anxiety, its side effect profile (sedation, weight gain) often relegates it to second-line status for those conditions.</p>
<p>At very low doses (3-6 mg), it acts almost exclusively as a selective histamine H1 antagonist, promoting sleep maintenance without significant anticholinergic effects.</p>
<h2>Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Insomnia</strong>: Sleep maintenance (difficulty staying asleep).</li>
<li><strong>Depression</strong>: Major Depressive Disorder.</li>
<li><strong>Anxiety Disorders</strong>: Generalized anxiety.</li>
<li><strong>Pruritus</strong>: Itching (topical cream, e.g., for eczema).</li>
</ul>
