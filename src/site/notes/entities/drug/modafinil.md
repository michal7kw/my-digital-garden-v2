<h1>Modafinil</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Modafinil (Provigil) is a wakefulness-promoting agent (eugeroic) FDA-approved for narcolepsy, obstructive sleep apnea (as adjunct to CPAP), and shift work disorder. It is widely used off-label as a cognitive enhancer by healthy individuals and for fatigue in conditions such as multiple sclerosis, depression, and ADHD. Its mechanism is distinct from traditional stimulants (amphetamines, methylphenidate), with lower abuse potential (Schedule IV vs Schedule II) and a different side effect profile lacking significant cardiovascular stimulation, appetite suppression, or rebound hypersomnolence. Armodafinil (Nuvigil) is the R-enantiomer with a longer duration of action. Modafinil induces CYP3A4, reducing the efficacy of hormonal contraceptives.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Modafinil's mechanism is not fully elucidated but involves weak inhibition of the dopamine transporter (DAT), increasing extracellular dopamine levels in the nucleus accumbens and prefrontal cortex without the pronounced reinforcing effects of traditional psychostimulants. It also activates orexin/hypocretin neurons in the lateral hypothalamus, which are critical for maintaining wakefulness, and increases histamine release from the tuberomammillary nucleus. Additional effects include elevated norepinephrine in the ventrolateral preoptic area (suppressing sleep-promoting neurons) and increased glutamate with decreased GABA in several brain regions. Unlike amphetamines, modafinil does not cause significant peripheral sympathetic activation or rebound hypersomnolence.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Narcolepsy</strong>: 200-400 mg once daily in the morning; first-line treatment</li>
<li><strong>Obstructive sleep apnea</strong>: 200 mg/day as adjunct to CPAP for residual excessive sleepiness</li>
<li><strong>Shift work disorder</strong>: 200 mg taken 1 hour before shift</li>
<li><strong>Cognitive enhancement (off-label)</strong>: Widely used by healthy individuals for focus and productivity</li>
<li><strong>MS-related fatigue</strong>: 200-400 mg/day; off-label but commonly prescribed</li>
<li><strong>ADHD (off-label)</strong>: Alternative for patients who cannot tolerate stimulants</li>
<li><strong>Depression-associated fatigue</strong>: Adjunct to antidepressants for residual fatigue/cognitive slowing</li>
</ul>
