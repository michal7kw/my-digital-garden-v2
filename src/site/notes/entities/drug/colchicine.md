<h1>Colchicine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Colchicine is an ancient plant alkaloid derived from Colchicum autumnale (autumn crocus). It inhibits microtubule polymerization by binding tubulin dimers, which disrupts neutrophil chemotaxis, adhesion, and superoxide production. Critically, it also inhibits NLRP3 inflammasome assembly and activation, blocking IL-1beta processing and release -- a key driver of gouty inflammation.</p>
<p>Colchicine is first-line therapy for acute gout flares and for prophylaxis during initiation of urate-lowering therapy. The modern low-dose regimen (1.2mg followed by 0.6mg one hour later) is as effective as high-dose colchicine but with significantly fewer gastrointestinal side effects. Beyond gout, colchicine has established roles in familial Mediterranean fever and pericarditis, and emerging evidence supports cardiovascular risk reduction through its anti-inflammatory properties.</p>
