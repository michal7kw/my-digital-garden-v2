<h1>Amitriptyline</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Amitriptyline (Elavil) is a tricyclic antidepressant (TCA) and one of the oldest antidepressants still in clinical use. It inhibits the reuptake of both serotonin and norepinephrine, with additional potent antihistaminic, anticholinergic, and alpha-adrenergic blocking properties. While largely replaced by SSRIs for depression due to its side effect profile, amitriptyline remains a first-line agent for neuropathic pain conditions, migraine prophylaxis, and fibromyalgia. Its strong sedative effect (via H1 antagonism) makes it useful for patients with comorbid insomnia. The narrow therapeutic index requires monitoring for cardiac toxicity, particularly QT prolongation.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Amitriptyline blocks the reuptake of serotonin (via SERT) and norepinephrine (via NET) at presynaptic nerve terminals, increasing their synaptic concentration. It is a relatively balanced dual reuptake inhibitor among TCAs. Additionally, amitriptyline is a potent antagonist at histamine H1 receptors (accounting for sedation and weight gain), muscarinic acetylcholine receptors (causing dry mouth, constipation, urinary retention, blurred vision), and alpha-1 adrenergic receptors (causing orthostatic hypotension). It also blocks voltage-gated sodium channels, which contributes to both its efficacy in neuropathic pain and its cardiotoxicity in overdose. The analgesic effect in neuropathic pain is thought to involve descending inhibitory pain pathway modulation independent of its antidepressant action.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Depression</strong>: 25-150 mg/day, though largely supplanted by SSRIs due to side effects and overdose risk</li>
<li><strong>Neuropathic pain</strong>: 10-75 mg/day at bedtime; effective for diabetic neuropathy, postherpetic neuralgia</li>
<li><strong>Migraine prophylaxis</strong>: 10-75 mg/day; reduces migraine frequency by 50% or more in responders</li>
<li><strong>Fibromyalgia</strong>: Low doses (10-50 mg) improve pain, sleep, and fatigue</li>
<li><strong>Insomnia</strong>: Low doses (10-25 mg) for its sedative H1 antagonism</li>
<li><strong>Functional GI disorders</strong>: Irritable bowel syndrome at low doses (10-25 mg)</li>
</ul>
