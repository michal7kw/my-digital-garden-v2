<h1>Ibuprofen</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) for pain, fever, and inflammation. Reversibly inhibits COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes. One of the most widely used OTC pain relievers.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Ibuprofen non-selectively and reversibly inhibits both cyclooxygenase-1 (COX-1) and cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) enzymes, blocking the conversion of arachidonic acid to prostaglandins and thromboxanes. Inhibition of COX-2-derived prostaglandins (particularly PGE2) at sites of inflammation and in the central nervous system mediates its anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and antipyretic effects. COX-1 inhibition reduces protective prostaglandins in the gastric mucosa and impairs platelet thromboxane A2 production, accounting for GI and bleeding risks. Unlike aspirin, ibuprofen's COX inhibition is reversible, so platelet function recovers as the drug is cleared.</p>
