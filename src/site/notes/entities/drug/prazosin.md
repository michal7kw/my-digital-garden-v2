<h1>PRAZOSIN</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Selective alpha-1 adrenergic receptor antagonist used for hypertension and PTSD-related nightmares. Blocks peripheral alpha-1 receptors to reduce blood pressure and crosses the blood-brain barrier to modulate central noradrenergic signaling.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Prazosin selectively blocks postsynaptic alpha-1 adrenergic receptors in vascular smooth muscle, preventing norepinephrine-mediated vasoconstriction and reducing peripheral vascular resistance and blood pressure. Unlike non-selective alpha blockers, prazosin does not block presynaptic alpha-2 receptors, so it avoids reflex tachycardia from unopposed norepinephrine release. In PTSD, prazosin crosses the blood-brain barrier and antagonizes alpha-1 receptors in the prefrontal cortex and amygdala, reducing the hyperadrenergic state responsible for trauma-related nightmares and sleep disruption. It also relaxes smooth muscle in the prostate and bladder neck, which accounts for its use in benign prostatic hyperplasia.</p>
