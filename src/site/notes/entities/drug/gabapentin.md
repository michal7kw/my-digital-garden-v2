<h1>Gabapentin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Gabapentin (Neurontin) is an anticonvulsant and analgesic that, despite its name suggesting GABA activity, acts primarily by binding to alpha-2-delta calcium channel subunits. It is FDA-approved for postherpetic neuralgia and as adjunctive therapy for partial seizures, with extensive off-label use for neuropathic pain, restless leg syndrome, anxiety, and alcohol withdrawal. Gabapentin has non-linear (saturable) absorption that decreases with increasing dose, limiting its efficacy at high doses. Extended-release formulations (Gralise, Horizant) were developed to address this pharmacokinetic limitation. Gabapentin is not hepatically metabolized and is excreted unchanged by the kidneys, making drug interactions rare but requiring dose adjustment in renal impairment.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Gabapentin binds with high affinity to the alpha-2-delta-1 subunit of voltage-gated calcium channels, which are upregulated in dorsal root ganglia neurons following nerve injury. This binding reduces calcium influx at presynaptic nerve terminals, decreasing the release of excitatory neurotransmitters including glutamate, norepinephrine, and substance P. Despite its structural similarity to GABA, gabapentin does not bind to GABA receptors, inhibit GABA uptake, or affect GABA metabolism. Its anticonvulsant activity likely derives from the same calcium channel mechanism, reducing hyperexcitability in neuronal circuits.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Postherpetic neuralgia</strong>: 300-3600 mg/day in divided doses; FDA-approved</li>
<li><strong>Epilepsy (adjunctive)</strong>: 900-3600 mg/day for partial seizures; FDA-approved</li>
<li><strong>Neuropathic pain</strong>: Widely used off-label for diabetic neuropathy, fibromyalgia, radiculopathy</li>
<li><strong>Restless leg syndrome</strong>: Horizant (gabapentin enacarbil) 600 mg daily; FDA-approved</li>
<li><strong>Anxiety disorders</strong>: Off-label; particularly social anxiety and generalized anxiety</li>
<li><strong>Alcohol withdrawal</strong>: Off-label adjunct for symptom management</li>
<li><strong>Perioperative pain</strong>: Preoperative gabapentin reduces opioid requirements</li>
</ul>
