<h1>Adalimumab</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Adalimumab (Humira) is a fully human IgG1 monoclonal antibody that targets tumor necrosis factor alpha (TNF-alpha), a key pro-inflammatory cytokine. It was the first fully human anti-TNF biologic approved for clinical use, distinguishing it from earlier chimeric (infliximab) and fusion protein (etanercept) approaches. By binding both soluble and transmembrane forms of TNF-alpha with high affinity, adalimumab prevents receptor activation and downstream NF-kB signaling, thereby reducing the inflammatory cascade that drives autoimmune tissue damage.</p>
<p>Adalimumab has one of the broadest indication profiles among biologics, approved for rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, plaque psoriasis, Crohn's disease, ulcerative colitis, juvenile idiopathic arthritis, and non-infectious uveitis. It is administered subcutaneously, typically every two weeks. As a fully human antibody, it has a lower immunogenicity profile than chimeric antibodies, though anti-drug antibodies can still develop, particularly in patients not receiving concomitant methotrexate.</p>
