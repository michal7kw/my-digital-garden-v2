<h1>Haloperidol</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Haloperidol (Haldol) is a high-potency first-generation (typical) antipsychotic of the butyrophenone class. It remains one of the most widely used antipsychotics globally, particularly for acute psychosis, severe agitation, and delirium in emergency and ICU settings. It is FDA-approved for schizophrenia, Tourette syndrome, and severe behavioral problems in children. Available in oral, IM, and long-acting decanoate formulations. While highly effective for positive symptoms of psychosis, haloperidol carries a significant risk of extrapyramidal symptoms (EPS), tardive dyskinesia, and QT prolongation.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Haloperidol is a potent and relatively selective dopamine D2 receptor antagonist in the mesolimbic pathway, which accounts for its antipsychotic efficacy against positive symptoms (hallucinations, delusions). However, D2 blockade in the nigrostriatal pathway causes extrapyramidal symptoms (dystonia, akathisia, parkinsonism), and blockade in the tuberoinfundibular pathway causes hyperprolactinemia. Haloperidol also has moderate affinity for sigma-1 receptors and alpha-1 adrenergic receptors, but minimal anticholinergic or antihistaminic activity compared to low-potency typical antipsychotics like chlorpromazine. The risk of tardive dyskinesia increases with cumulative exposure and is thought to involve D2 receptor upregulation and hypersensitivity.</p>
<h2>Clinical Uses</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Acute psychosis/agitation</strong>: 2-10 mg IM or oral; mainstay of emergency psychiatry</li>
<li><strong>Schizophrenia</strong>: 0.5-20 mg/day oral; effective for positive symptoms</li>
<li><strong>Tourette syndrome</strong>: 0.5-5 mg/day; reduces tic severity</li>
<li><strong>Delirium</strong>: Low-dose (0.5-2 mg) IV/IM; widely used in ICU settings</li>
<li><strong>Nausea/vomiting</strong>: Low doses for refractory chemotherapy-induced nausea (off-label)</li>
<li><strong>Maintenance</strong>: Haldol Decanoate 50-300 mg IM monthly for treatment adherence</li>
</ul>
