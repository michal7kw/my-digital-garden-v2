<h1>CYP3A4</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Cytochrome P450 3A4 (CYP3A4) is the most abundant and clinically significant cytochrome P450 isoform. It is involved in the oxidation of the largest range of substrates of all CYPs.</p>
<p>Because it metabolizes so many drugs, <strong>inhibition</strong> of CYP3A4 (e.g., by Grapefruit juice, Ketoconazole) can lead to toxic drug levels, while <strong>induction</strong> (e.g., by St. John's Wort, Carbamazepine) can lead to therapeutic failure.</p>
<h2>Function</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Drug Metabolism</strong>: Oxidizes acetaminophen, codeine, cyclosporin A, diazepam, and erythromycin.</li>
<li><strong>Steroid Metabolism</strong>: Hydroxylates testosterone, progesterone, and cortisol.</li>
<li><strong>Bioactivation</strong>: Can activate pro-carcinogens (e.g., Aflatoxin B1).</li>
</ul>
