<h1>RANKL</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>RANKL (receptor activator of NF-kB ligand) is a TNF superfamily member that binds its receptor RANK on osteoclast precursors, driving their differentiation into mature multinucleated osteoclasts. The RANKL/RANK/OPG triad regulates bone remodeling -- osteoprotegerin (OPG) acts as a decoy receptor, competing with RANK for RANKL binding. In inflammatory arthritis, TNF-alpha, IL-1, and IL-6 upregulate RANKL expression in synovial fibroblasts and T cells, tipping the balance toward bone resorption. Therapeutic targeting: denosumab (anti-RANKL antibody) prevents bone loss in osteoporosis and may protect against periarticular erosions in RA.</p>
