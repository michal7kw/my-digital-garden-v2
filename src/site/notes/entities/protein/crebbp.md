<h1>CREBBP</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p><strong>CREB-binding protein (CREBBP/CBP)</strong> is a ubiquitous and essential transcriptional coactivator. It works closely with its paralog <strong>p300</strong> (EP300).</p>
<p>Its primary function is <strong>Histone Acetylation (HAT activity)</strong>: adding acetyl groups to lysine residues on histone tails. This neutralizes the positive charge of histones, loosening their grip on DNA and allowing transcription factors to bind.</p>
<p>It interacts with hundreds of proteins, serving as a central node in transcriptional regulation networks.</p>
