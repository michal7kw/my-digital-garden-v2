<h1>JAK1</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Janus kinase 1 (JAK1) is a non-receptor tyrosine kinase belonging to the JAK family (JAK1, JAK2, JAK3, TYK2). JAK1 occupies a central position in cytokine signaling because it partners with each of the other three JAK family members across different receptor complexes. It mediates signaling from type I and type II cytokine receptors, including the IL-6 receptor (via JAK1/JAK2/TYK2), interferon receptors (via JAK1/TYK2 for type I IFN, JAK1/JAK2 for IFN-gamma), and the common gamma-chain (gamma-c) receptor family (via JAK1/JAK3 for IL-2, IL-4, IL-7, IL-15, IL-21).</p>
<p>Upon cytokine binding, JAK1 is activated through transphosphorylation and subsequently phosphorylates STAT proteins (primarily STAT1, STAT3, STAT5), which dimerize and translocate to the nucleus to drive transcription of inflammatory, proliferative, and immune regulatory genes. The breadth of JAK1-dependent signaling makes it a powerful therapeutic target: upadacitinib (Rinvoq) is a JAK1-selective inhibitor approved for RA, psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, atopic dermatitis, and ulcerative colitis. Tofacitinib (JAK1/3) and baricitinib (JAK1/2) also inhibit JAK1 as part of their broader kinase profiles. JAK1 selectivity aims to provide efficacy while minimizing effects on JAK2-dependent erythropoietin and thrombopoietin signaling.</p>
