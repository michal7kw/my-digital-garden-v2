<h1>TYK2</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Tyrosine kinase 2 (TYK2) is a member of the Janus kinase family that mediates signaling from a distinct set of cytokine receptors compared to other JAK family members. TYK2 partners with JAK2 to transduce IL-12 and IL-23 signals (activating STAT4 and STAT3, respectively), and with JAK1 for type I interferon (IFN-alpha/beta) signaling via STAT1/STAT2. It also contributes to IL-10 family cytokine signaling.</p>
<p>The IL-12/IL-23 axis mediated by TYK2 is critical in psoriasis pathogenesis: IL-23 drives Th17 cell survival and IL-17 production, while IL-12 promotes Th1 differentiation and IFN-gamma secretion. Genetic studies identified TYK2 loss-of-function variants that protect against autoimmune diseases (psoriasis, rheumatoid arthritis, inflammatory bowel disease, type 1 diabetes) while only modestly increasing susceptibility to mycobacterial infection, suggesting a favorable therapeutic window for TYK2 inhibition.</p>
<p>Deucravacitinib (Sotyktu) is a first-in-class selective TYK2 inhibitor that binds the TYK2 pseudokinase (JH2) domain rather than the catalytic (JH1) domain, achieving high selectivity over JAK1, JAK2, and JAK3. It is approved for moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis and is under investigation for psoriatic arthritis, lupus, and inflammatory bowel disease. The selective TYK2 mechanism avoids the hematologic and cardiovascular signals seen with broader JAK inhibitors.</p>
