<h1>5-HT2A Receptor</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The 5-HT2A receptor (UniProt: P28223) is a Gq-coupled G-protein coupled receptor encoded by the HTR2A gene on chromosome 13q14.2. It is the most abundant serotonin receptor subtype in the cerebral cortex, particularly in layer V pyramidal neurons of the prefrontal cortex. Upon activation, it stimulates phospholipase C (PLC) → IP3/DAG → intracellular calcium release, leading to neuronal excitation.</p>
<p>5-HT2A receptors mediate diverse physiological effects:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>CNS</strong>: Modulation of perception, cognition, mood, and cortical excitability. Primary mediator of psychedelic drug effects (psilocybin, LSD, DMT bind as agonists)</li>
<li><strong>Vascular</strong>: Smooth muscle contraction and vasoconstriction</li>
<li><strong>Platelets</strong>: Amplification of platelet aggregation (serotonin from dense granules activates 5-HT2A)</li>
<li><strong>Spinal cord</strong>: Mediates the neuromuscular manifestations of serotonin syndrome (clonus, hyperreflexia, rigidity)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Role in SSRI Pharmacology</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Acute</strong>: SSRIs increase synaptic serotonin, which stimulates both 5-HT1A and 5-HT2A receptors</li>
<li><strong>Chronic (2-4 weeks)</strong>: Sustained serotonin elevation causes 5-HT2A receptor downregulation and desensitization</li>
<li><strong>Side effects</strong>: Initial 5-HT2A activation contributes to SSRI side effects (anxiety, insomnia, sexual dysfunction)</li>
<li><strong>Serotonin syndrome</strong>: Excessive 5-HT2A activation in brainstem/spinal cord drives neuromuscular toxicity (clonus, hyperthermia)</li>
<li><strong>Sexual dysfunction</strong>: 5-HT2A/2C activation in mesolimbic pathway suppresses dopamine and norepinephrine release, contributing to decreased libido and anorgasmia</li>
</ul>
<h2>Serotonin Syndrome Mechanism</h2>
<p>The 5-HT2A receptor is the primary mediator of the neuromuscular features of serotonin syndrome:</p>
<ul>
<li>Clonus (rhythmic involuntary muscle contractions)</li>
<li>Hyperreflexia</li>
<li>Muscle rigidity</li>
<li>Hyperthermia (via uncoupled muscle contraction and hypothalamic dysregulation)</li>
</ul>
<p>Treatment: Cyproheptadine (5-HT2A antagonist) is the specific antidote.</p>
