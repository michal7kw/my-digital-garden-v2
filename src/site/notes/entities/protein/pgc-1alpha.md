<h1>PGC-1alpha</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>PGC-1α (Peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor gamma coactivator 1-alpha) is the master regulator of mitochondrial biogenesis and oxidative metabolism. Discovered as a coactivator of PPARγ in brown adipose tissue, it has since been recognized as a central coordinator of cellular energy metabolism.</p>
<p>PGC-1α integrates environmental signals (cold, exercise, fasting, oxidative stress) with gene expression programs that adapt cells to increased energy demands. It is particularly important in:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Muscle</strong>: Exercise adaptation, fiber type switching</li>
<li><strong>Brown adipose</strong>: Cold-induced thermogenesis</li>
<li><strong>Brain</strong>: Neuronal metabolism, synaptic function</li>
<li><strong>Heart</strong>: Cardiac contractile function</li>
<li><strong>Liver</strong>: Gluconeogenesis, fatty acid oxidation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Activation Mechanisms</h2>
<p>PGC-1α activity is regulated by multiple post-translational modifications:</p>
<h3>Deacetylation by SIRT1</h3>
<ul>
<li>NAD+-dependent deacetylation activates PGC-1α</li>
<li>Links cellular energy status (NAD+/NADH) to mitochondrial biogenesis</li>
<li>Major mechanism of caloric restriction benefits</li>
<li>Target of NMN/NR supplementation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Phosphorylation by AMPK</h3>
<ul>
<li>AMPK phosphorylates PGC-1α at multiple sites</li>
<li>Increases transcriptional activity</li>
<li>Activated during energy stress (high AMP/ATP)</li>
<li>Target of exercise and metformin/berberine</li>
</ul>
<h3>Phosphorylation by p38 MAPK</h3>
<ul>
<li>Activated by exercise, cold, cytokines</li>
<li>Stabilizes PGC-1α protein</li>
<li>Reduces inhibitory phosphorylation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Arginine Methylation by PRMT1</h3>
<ul>
<li>Enhances coactivator function</li>
<li>Required for full activity</li>
</ul>

<h2>Biological Functions</h2>
<h3>Mitochondrial Biogenesis</h3>
<ul>
<li>Increases mtDNA copy number</li>
<li>Induces TFAM (mitochondrial transcription factor A)</li>
<li>Upregulates all OXPHOS complexes</li>
<li>Promotes mitochondrial fusion</li>
</ul>
<h3>Oxidative Metabolism</h3>
<ul>
<li>Switches fuel from glucose to fatty acids</li>
<li>Increases fatty acid oxidation capacity</li>
<li>Enhances tricarboxylic acid cycle enzymes</li>
</ul>
<h3>Muscle Adaptation</h3>
<ul>
<li>Mediates endurance exercise benefits</li>
<li>Promotes slow-twitch fiber characteristics</li>
<li>Increases capillary density</li>
<li>Essential for exercise-induced mitochondrial biogenesis</li>
</ul>
<h3>Thermogenesis</h3>
<ul>
<li>Induces UCP1 in brown adipose</li>
<li>Drives adaptive thermogenesis</li>
<li>Browning of white adipose tissue</li>
</ul>
<h3>Gluconeogenesis</h3>
<ul>
<li>Activates hepatic glucose production during fasting</li>
<li>Works with FOXO1 to induce PEPCK, G6Pase</li>
<li>Balanced by insulin suppression</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<h3>Aging</h3>
<ul>
<li>PGC-1α expression declines with age</li>
<li>Contributes to mitochondrial dysfunction</li>
<li>Target of longevity interventions</li>
</ul>
<h3>Metabolic Disease</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reduced in type 2 diabetes skeletal muscle</li>
<li>Impaired in metabolic syndrome</li>
<li>Restoration improves metabolic parameters</li>
</ul>
<h3>Neurodegeneration</h3>
<ul>
<li>Protects against Huntington's, Parkinson's, Alzheimer's</li>
<li>Maintains neuronal mitochondrial function</li>
<li>Potential therapeutic target</li>
</ul>
<h3>Heart Failure</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reduced cardiac PGC-1α in heart failure</li>
<li>Critical for cardiac contractile function</li>
<li>Target for cardiac protection</li>
</ul>
