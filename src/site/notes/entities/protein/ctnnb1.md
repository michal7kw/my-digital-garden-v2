<h1>CTNNB1</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p><strong>Beta-Catenin (CTNNB1)</strong> is the central node of the <strong>Wnt signaling pathway</strong>.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Absence of Wnt</strong>: Beta-catenin is phosphorylated by the destruction complex (APC, Axin, GSK3-beta), ubiquitinated, and degraded.</li>
<li><strong>Presence of Wnt</strong>: The destruction complex is inhibited. Beta-catenin accumulates, enters the nucleus, and activates genes promoting proliferation (e.g., Cyclin D1, c-Myc).</li>
</ul>
<p>It is also an essential structural component of <strong>Adherens Junctions</strong>, linking E-cadherin to alpha-catenin and the actin cytoskeleton.</p>
