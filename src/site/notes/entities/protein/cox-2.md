<h1>COX-2</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2, encoded by the PTGS2 gene) is the inducible isoform of prostaglandin-endoperoxide synthase, a bifunctional enzyme with both cyclooxygenase and peroxidase activities. While its constitutive counterpart COX-1 (PTGS1) maintains homeostatic prostaglandin production for gastric protection, platelet function, and renal blood flow, COX-2 is rapidly upregulated at sites of inflammation, injury, and neoplasia.</p>
<p>Pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-1beta), bacterial lipopolysaccharide (LPS), growth factors, and mechanical stress induce COX-2 expression primarily through NF-kB and MAPK/AP-1 signaling pathways. Once expressed, COX-2 converts arachidonic acid (released from membrane phospholipids by phospholipase A2) to prostaglandin H2 (PGH2), which is then converted by tissue-specific synthases to PGE2 (the principal inflammatory prostanoid), PGI2, TXA2, and other prostanoids. COX-2-derived PGE2 mediates pain sensitization via EP receptors on nociceptors, fever via the hypothalamic thermoregulatory center, and inflammatory vasodilation and edema.</p>
<p>COX-2 is the primary pharmacological target of non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs. Non-selective NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) inhibit both COX-1 and COX-2, while selective COX-2 inhibitors (celecoxib, etoricoxib) were developed to reduce GI toxicity from COX-1 inhibition. The cardiovascular risk associated with coxibs (rofecoxib withdrawal) revealed the importance of COX-2-derived PGI2 in vascular protection, highlighting the complex physiological roles of this enzyme beyond inflammation.</p>
