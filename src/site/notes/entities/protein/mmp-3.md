<h1>MMP-3</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Matrix metalloproteinase-3 (MMP-3, stromelysin-1) is a zinc-dependent endopeptidase with broad substrate specificity, capable of degrading cartilage proteoglycans, fibronectin, laminin, and type III, IV, V, and IX collagens. Beyond direct matrix degradation, MMP-3 plays a critical amplifying role in tissue destruction by activating other pro-MMPs including MMP-1 (collagenase-1), MMP-7, MMP-8, and MMP-9, creating a proteolytic cascade that accelerates joint damage.</p>
<p>In rheumatoid arthritis, MMP-3 is produced by synovial fibroblasts and chondrocytes in response to pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-1beta, IL-6). Serum MMP-3 levels have been validated as a biomarker for RA disease activity that correlates with synovial inflammation and, importantly, predicts radiographic joint progression independently of CRP and ESR. Elevated MMP-3 levels at diagnosis identify patients at higher risk of erosive disease, making it useful for early treatment stratification. MMP-3 is also elevated in osteoarthritis, where it contributes to cartilage degradation, although its biomarker utility in OA is less well established than in RA.</p>
