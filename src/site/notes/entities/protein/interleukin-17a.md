<h1>Interleukin-17A</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>IL-17A is the founding member of the IL-17 cytokine family, primarily produced by Th17 cells but also by gamma-delta T cells, innate lymphoid cells (ILC3), and neutrophils. Signals through IL-17RA/IL-17RC heterodimer, activating NF-kB and C/EBP transcription factors. Drives recruitment and activation of neutrophils via CXCL1/CXCL8 induction. In psoriasis, IL-17A drives keratinocyte proliferation and neutrophilic microabscess formation. In spondyloarthropathies, IL-17A promotes entheseal inflammation and new bone formation. Therapeutic targeting: secukinumab and ixekizumab (anti-IL-17A), bimekizumab (anti-IL-17A/F).</p>
