<h1>Interleukin-1 Beta</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>IL-1beta is the prototypical pro-inflammatory cytokine of the innate immune system. Produced as an inactive 31kDa pro-form (pro-IL-1beta) by monocytes and macrophages in response to pathogen-associated molecular patterns (PAMPs) and damage-associated molecular patterns (DAMPs). Processing requires the NLRP3 inflammasome complex, which activates caspase-1 to cleave pro-IL-1beta into the mature 17kDa active form. In gout, monosodium urate (MSU) crystals are potent NLRP3 activators, explaining why colchicine (NLRP3 inhibitor) is effective. In RA, IL-1beta drives synovial fibroblast proliferation, MMP production, and osteoclast-mediated bone erosion. Therapeutic targeting: anakinra (IL-1Ra), canakinumab (anti-IL-1beta antibody), rilonacept (IL-1 trap).</p>
