<h1>NRF2</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>NRF2 (Nuclear factor erythroid 2-related factor 2) is the master transcriptional regulator of the cellular antioxidant response. It controls the expression of over 250 genes involved in antioxidant defense, detoxification, proteostasis, and metabolism.</p>
<p>The NRF2-Keap1 system represents an elegant redox sensing mechanism:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Normal conditions</strong>: NRF2 is constantly degraded (half-life ~20 min)</li>
<li><strong>Oxidative stress</strong>: Keap1 is modified, NRF2 accumulates and activates genes</li>
<li><strong>Recovery</strong>: System resets to baseline surveillance</li>
</ul>
<p>This system allows cells to rapidly respond to oxidative challenges while maintaining low baseline activity to preserve normal cellular signaling.</p>
<h2>Keap1-NRF2 Pathway</h2>
<h3>Basal State (Low Stress)</h3>
<ol>
<li>Keap1 binds NRF2 in cytoplasm</li>
<li>Keap1 recruits Cullin3-Rbx1 E3 ligase</li>
<li>NRF2 is ubiquitinated</li>
<li>Proteasomal degradation (~20 min half-life)</li>
<li>Minimal NRF2 in nucleus</li>
</ol>
<h3>Activated State (Oxidative Stress)</h3>
<ol>
<li>ROS/electrophiles modify Keap1 cysteines (C151, C273, C288)</li>
<li>Conformational change disrupts Keap1-NRF2 interaction</li>
<li>Newly synthesized NRF2 escapes degradation</li>
<li>NRF2 accumulates and enters nucleus</li>
<li>Binds ARE sequences with small Maf proteins</li>
<li>Activates cytoprotective gene transcription</li>
</ol>
<h3>Recovery</h3>
<ol>
<li>Stress resolved</li>
<li>Keap1 regains ability to bind NRF2</li>
<li>Nuclear NRF2 exported and degraded</li>
<li>Baseline state restored</li>
</ol>

<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<h3>Aging</h3>
<ul>
<li>NRF2 activity declines with age</li>
<li>Contributes to oxidative damage accumulation</li>
<li>NRF2 activators extend lifespan in models</li>
</ul>
<h3>Neurodegenerative Diseases</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reduced NRF2 in Parkinson's, Alzheimer's</li>
<li>Astrocyte NRF2 protects neurons</li>
<li>Therapeutic target for neuroprotection</li>
</ul>
<h3>Cancer (Complex Role)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Prevention</strong>: NRF2 activation protects against carcinogenesis</li>
<li><strong>Promotion</strong>: Constitutive NRF2 helps cancer cells survive stress</li>
<li>Context-dependent therapeutic approach</li>
</ul>
<h3>Diabetes &amp; Metabolic Disease</h3>
<ul>
<li>NRF2 activation improves insulin sensitivity</li>
<li>Reduces oxidative stress in diabetic complications</li>
<li>Protects beta cells</li>
</ul>
<h3>Multiple Sclerosis</h3>
<ul>
<li>Dimethyl fumarate approved for MS</li>
<li>NRF2 activation provides neuroprotection</li>
<li>Anti-inflammatory effects</li>
</ul>
