<h1>BACE1</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Beta-secretase 1 (BACE1), also known as beta-site amyloid precursor protein cleaving enzyme 1, is a type I transmembrane aspartyl protease predominantly expressed in neurons. It is the rate-limiting enzyme in the production of Amyloid Beta (Aβ) peptides, making it one of the most intensively studied therapeutic targets in Alzheimer's disease research.</p>
<p>BACE1 is a 501-amino acid protein (55.8 kDa) localized to endosomes, the Golgi apparatus, and the cell membrane. Its expression is highest in the brain, particularly in hippocampal and cortical neurons, though it is also found at lower levels in peripheral tissues.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>BACE1 initiates the amyloidogenic processing of Amyloid Precursor Protein (APP) by cleaving APP at the beta-site within the extracellular domain. This cleavage produces a soluble N-terminal fragment (sAPPbeta) and a membrane-bound C-terminal fragment (C99). C99 is subsequently cleaved by the gamma-secretase complex (containing <a href="/entities/protein/presenilin-1/" class="internal-link">Presenilin-1</a>) to release Aβ peptides of varying lengths, including the pathogenic Aβ42 species. BACE1 also has physiological substrates beyond APP, including <a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">Neuregulin-1</a> (essential for myelination), voltage-gated sodium channel subunits, and other type I membrane proteins.</p>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<p>BACE1 is a central drug target for Alzheimer's disease. Elevated BACE1 activity has been observed in sporadic Alzheimer's patients and correlates with increased Aβ plaque burden. Multiple BACE1 inhibitors (verubecestat, atabecestat, lanabecestat) reached Phase III clinical trials but were discontinued due to cognitive worsening and synaptic toxicity, likely reflecting the enzyme's essential roles in neuregulin signaling and synaptic plasticity. BACE1 knockout mice exhibit hypomyelination, seizures, and behavioral deficits, underscoring the challenge of therapeutic inhibition. Current research focuses on partial BACE1 inhibition strategies and combination approaches to reduce Aβ without compromising physiological function.</p>
