<h1>Tyrosine Hydroxylase</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Tyrosine hydroxylase (TH) is the rate-limiting enzyme in the biosynthesis of all catecholamine neurotransmitters — dopamine, norepinephrine, and epinephrine. It catalyzes the first and slowest step: the conversion of L-Tyrosine to L-DOPA. Because this is the committed step in the pathway, TH activity determines the overall rate of catecholamine production throughout the brain and peripheral nervous system.</p>
<p>TH is expressed in specific neuronal populations: dopaminergic neurons of the substantia nigra (nigrostriatal pathway) and ventral tegmental area (mesolimbic/mesocortical pathways), noradrenergic neurons of the locus coeruleus, and chromaffin cells of the adrenal medulla. The progressive loss of TH-positive neurons in the substantia nigra pars compacta is the defining neuropathological feature of Parkinson's disease, and L-DOPA therapy works by bypassing this enzymatic deficit.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 5/5</p>
<h2>Key Functions</h2>
<h3>Catalytic Mechanism</h3>
<ul>
<li>Hydroxylates L-Tyrosine at the 3-position to produce L-DOPA</li>
<li>Uses molecular oxygen (O2) as the oxygen source</li>
<li>Requires non-heme iron (Fe2+) at the active site for catalysis</li>
<li>Tetrahydrobiopterin (BH4) serves as the essential electron-donating cofactor, being oxidized to BH2 in each catalytic cycle</li>
<li>BH2 must be recycled back to BH4 by dihydropteridine reductase (DHPR) using NADH — BH4 recycling is essential for sustained catecholamine synthesis</li>
</ul>
<h3>Regulation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>End-product inhibition</strong>: Dopamine, norepinephrine, and epinephrine bind to the iron active site, competing with BH4 and inhibiting activity. This provides negative feedback control.</li>
<li><strong>Phosphorylation activation</strong>: Ser40 phosphorylation by PKA or CaMKII reduces catecholamine binding affinity 300-fold, effectively relieving feedback inhibition. Ser31 (ERK/MAPK) and Ser19 (CaMKII) also modulate activity.</li>
<li><strong>Transcriptional regulation</strong>: TH gene expression is induced by CREB, AP-1, and Nurr1 transcription factors in response to neuronal activity and stress.</li>
<li><strong>Protein stability</strong>: 14-3-3 proteins bind phosphorylated TH, stabilizing the active form and preventing dephosphorylation.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Tissue Distribution</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Substantia nigra pars compacta</strong>: Dopaminergic neurons projecting to striatum (motor control)</li>
<li><strong>Ventral tegmental area</strong>: Dopaminergic neurons projecting to nucleus accumbens (reward) and prefrontal cortex (cognition)</li>
<li><strong>Locus coeruleus</strong>: Noradrenergic neurons projecting broadly to cortex, hippocampus, cerebellum</li>
<li><strong>Adrenal medulla</strong>: Chromaffin cells producing epinephrine and norepinephrine</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Relevance</h2>
<h3>Parkinson's Disease</h3>
<p>Loss of &gt;60% of TH-positive neurons in substantia nigra precedes motor symptom onset. L-DOPA therapy bypasses TH by providing the product directly. TH immunostaining is the standard marker for dopaminergic neuron survival in PD research.</p>
<h3>TH Deficiency</h3>
<p>Rare autosomal recessive mutations in the TH gene cause Segawa syndrome (dopa-responsive dystonia) or severe infantile parkinsonism, depending on residual enzyme activity. Both respond to low-dose L-DOPA.</p>
<h3>Gene Therapy</h3>
<p>TH gene delivery to the striatum is being investigated as a potential Parkinson's treatment, aiming to restore local dopamine synthesis capacity.</p>
<h2>Key Research</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Structure and regulation</strong>: Comprehensive review of TH crystal structure, catalytic mechanism, and phosphorylation-dependent regulation (PMID:21473867)</li>
<li><strong>Phosphorylation regulation</strong>: Detailed analysis of how Ser19, Ser31, and Ser40 phosphorylation sites differentially regulate TH activity and stability (PMID:21635908)</li>
<li><strong>TH in Parkinson's disease</strong>: Loss of TH-immunoreactive neurons in substantia nigra correlates with motor symptom severity; implications for neuroprotective strategies (PMID:25462890)</li>
<li><strong>BH4 cofactor</strong>: Role of tetrahydrobiopterin in aromatic amino acid hydroxylase catalytic mechanism and recycling (PMID:17884111)</li>
</ul>
