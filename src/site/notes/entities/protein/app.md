<h1>APP</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p><strong>Amyloid-beta Precursor Protein (APP)</strong> is an integral membrane protein expressed in many tissues and concentrated in the synapses of neurons. Its primary function is not fully understood, but it has been implicated as a regulator of synapse formation, neural plasticity, and iron export.</p>
<p>APP is best known as the precursor molecule whose proteolysis generates <strong>Amyloid Beta (Aβ)</strong>, a polypeptide containing 37 to 49 amino acid residues, whose amyloid fibrillar form is the primary component of <strong>amyloid plaques</strong> found in the brains of Alzheimer's disease patients.</p>
<h2>Processing Pathways</h2>
<p>APP can be processed by two pathways:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Non-amyloidogenic pathway</strong>: Cleavage by α-secretase (prevents Aβ formation).</li>
<li><strong>Amyloidogenic pathway</strong>: Cleavage by β-secretase (BACE1) followed by γ-secretase, releasing Aβ peptides (Aβ40, Aβ42).</li>
</ol>
