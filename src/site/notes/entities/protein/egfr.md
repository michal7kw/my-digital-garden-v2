<h1>EGFR</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p><strong>Epidermal Growth Factor Receptor (EGFR)</strong>, also known as ErbB1 or HER1, is a transmembrane receptor tyrosine kinase. Upon binding by ligands such as EGF or TGF-alpha, it dimerizes and autophosphorylates, triggering downstream signaling pathways that promote cell growth and division.</p>
<p>Overexpression or mutational activation of EGFR drives many cancers, making it a critical therapeutic target. Inhibitors include small molecule <strong>Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitors (TKIs)</strong> like Gefitinib and Erlotinib, and <strong>Monoclonal Antibodies</strong> like Cetuximab.</p>
<h2>Signaling Pathways</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>RAS-RAF-MEK-ERK</strong>: Cell proliferation.</li>
<li><strong>PI3K-AKT-mTOR</strong>: Cell survival and anti-apoptosis.</li>
<li><strong>JAK-STAT</strong>: Cell division and immune regulation.</li>
</ol>
