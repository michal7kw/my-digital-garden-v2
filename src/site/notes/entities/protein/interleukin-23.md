<h1>Interleukin-23</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>IL-23 is a heterodimer of IL-23p19 (unique) and IL-12p40 (shared with IL-12). Produced by dendritic cells and macrophages in response to microbial stimuli. IL-23 does not initiate Th17 differentiation (that requires IL-6 + TGF-beta) but is essential for Th17 cell survival, expansion, and pathogenic function. The IL-23/IL-17 axis is the dominant pathway in psoriasis, psoriatic arthritis, and spondyloarthropathies. Signals through JAK2/TYK2 and STAT3/STAT4. Therapeutic targeting: guselkumab and risankizumab (anti-IL-23p19), ustekinumab (anti-IL-12/23 p40).</p>
