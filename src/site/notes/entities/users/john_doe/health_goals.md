# Health Goals: John Doe

## Active Goals

### Goal 1: Reduce LDL Cholesterol
- **Target**: Reduce LDL cholesterol to under 100 mg/dL
- **Current Value**: 142 mg/dL (as of May 1, 2025)
- **Timeline**: 6 months (by November 2025)
- **Priority**: High

#### Action Plan
1. Increase soluble fiber intake to 10g+ daily
2. Add 30 minutes of cardio exercise 3x weekly
3. Incorporate plant sterols/stanols into diet
4. Reduce saturated fat consumption to <7% of daily calories
5. Take omega-3 supplement (2-3g daily)

#### Measurements & Tracking
- Full lipid panel test quarterly
- Weekly food journal focusing on fiber and fat intake
- Monthly check-in with health coach

#### Scientific Rationale
Research indicates that increasing soluble fiber can reduce LDL cholesterol by 5-10%. Combined with regular cardiovascular exercise and omega-3 supplementation, this plan addresses both dietary and lifestyle factors contributing to elevated cholesterol.

### Goal 2: Improve Sleep Quality
- **Target**: Increase sleep quality score to 85+/100
- **Current Value**: 65/100
- **Timeline**: 3 months (by August 2025)
- **Priority**: Medium

#### Action Plan
1. Establish consistent sleep-wake schedule (10:30 PM - 6:30 AM)
2. Create technology-free wind-down routine (30 min before bed)
3. Optimize bedroom environment (temperature, light, noise)
4. Morning sunlight exposure within 30 minutes of waking
5. Limit caffeine after 12 PM

#### Measurements & Tracking
- Daily sleep tracking via Oura ring
- Weekly assessment of sleep quality metrics
- Sleep journal noting pre-sleep activities and quality

#### Scientific Rationale
Circadian rhythm optimization through consistent sleep-wake cycles and morning light exposure has been shown to improve sleep quality by 20-30%. Limiting evening blue light exposure helps with melatonin production and sleep onset.

### Goal 3: Optimize Vitamin D Levels
- **Target**: Achieve vitamin D level of 50-60 ng/mL
- **Current Value**: 28 ng/mL (insufficient)
- **Timeline**: 3 months (by August 2025)
- **Priority**: Medium

#### Action Plan
1. Supplementation with 4000 IU vitamin D3 daily
2. 15-20 minutes of midday sun exposure (arms and legs) 3x weekly when possible
3. Increase vitamin D-rich foods (fatty fish, egg yolks, fortified foods)
4. Take supplement with fat-containing meal to improve absorption

#### Measurements & Tracking
- Retest vitamin D levels after 3 months
- Track supplement adherence daily
- Log sun exposure in weekly health journal

#### Scientific Rationale
Research shows that each 1000 IU of vitamin D supplementation raises blood levels by approximately 5-10 ng/mL. Combined with moderate sun exposure and dietary sources, the target level should be achievable within the timeline.
