<h1>Autoimmune Support</h1>
<p>Immune modulation and anti-inflammatory support for autoimmune conditions.</p>
<h2>Evidence Summary</h2>
<p>Vitamin D and omega-3 have the strongest evidence (Level B) for immune modulation in autoimmune conditions. NAC, probiotics, selenium, and curcumin have Level C evidence from smaller trials and mechanistic studies.</p>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Broad-spectrum supplement protocol for autoimmune conditions including SLE, Sjogren's syndrome, scleroderma, and mixed connective tissue disease (MCTD). This stack emphasizes immune modulation rather than suppression, gut barrier integrity, antioxidant defense, and anti-inflammatory support. All supplements should be discussed with the treating rheumatologist or immunologist.</p>

<h2>Important Notes</h2>
<ul>
<li>This stack is adjunctive to immunosuppressive therapy, NOT a replacement</li>
<li>Discuss all supplements with treating rheumatologist or immunologist</li>
<li>Selenium should not exceed 200mcg/day (toxicity risk)</li>
<li>NAC may interact with nitroglycerin and some chemotherapy agents</li>
<li>Monitor vitamin D levels to avoid toxicity (&gt;100 ng/mL)</li>
</ul>
