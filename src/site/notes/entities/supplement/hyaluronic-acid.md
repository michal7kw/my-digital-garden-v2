<h1>Hyaluronic Acid</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Hyaluronic acid (HA), also known as hyaluronan, is a high-molecular-weight non-sulfated glycosaminoglycan composed of repeating disaccharide units of N-acetylglucosamine and glucuronic acid. It is the principal component of synovial fluid, responsible for its viscoelastic properties that provide joint lubrication during slow movements (viscosity) and shock absorption during rapid movements (elasticity). Native HA in synovial fluid has a molecular weight of 6-7 million Daltons.</p>
<p>In osteoarthritic joints, synovial fluid HA concentration and molecular weight are significantly reduced, leading to diminished lubrication and shock absorption capacity. This degradation is driven by both enzymatic breakdown (hyaluronidase) and free radical depolymerization from chronic inflammation. HA also signals through the CD44 receptor on synoviocytes and chondrocytes, modulating inflammatory cytokine production and stimulating endogenous HA synthesis, creating a positive feedback loop when adequate HA levels are maintained.</p>
<p>Oral HA supplementation (80-200mg/day) has shown modest but consistent benefit in meta-analyses of knee osteoarthritis trials, with high-molecular-weight preparations appearing to have superior efficacy. The mechanism of oral HA action likely involves absorption of degradation fragments that signal through CD44 receptors in the gut and systemically. Intra-articular viscosupplementation, while a medical procedure rather than supplementation, provides more direct benefit with 6-12 months of pain relief per treatment series, and is recommended by several international guidelines for knee OA.</p>
<h2>Dosage Guidelines</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Oral supplementation</strong>: 80-200mg/day of high-molecular-weight hyaluronic acid</li>
<li><strong>Preferred form</strong>: High molecular weight (&gt;1 million Daltons) for oral use</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Take with food; duration of trials typically 8-16 weeks</li>
<li><strong>Intra-articular</strong> (medical procedure): Viscosupplementation series of 3-5 weekly injections or single cross-linked injection</li>
<li><strong>Onset</strong>: Oral — gradual improvement over 4-8 weeks; intra-articular — within 1-4 weeks post-injection series</li>
</ul>
