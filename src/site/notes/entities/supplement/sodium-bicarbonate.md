<h1>Sodium Bicarbonate</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Sodium bicarbonate (baking soda) is a well-established ergogenic aid that works by creating a state of metabolic alkalosis, enhancing the body's extracellular buffering capacity. While beta-alanine buffers H+ inside the muscle (intracellular), sodium bicarbonate works outside the muscle (extracellular), making them complementary strategies for managing exercise-induced acidosis.</p>
<p>Sodium bicarbonate is most effective for high-intensity efforts lasting 1-7 minutes and for repeated sprint protocols. The main limitation is GI distress at effective doses, though serial loading and enteric-coated capsules have improved tolerability.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 4/5</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Extracellular Buffering</h3>
<ul>
<li>Increases blood [HCO3-] by 5-6 mmol/L (from ~24 to ~29-30 mmol/L)</li>
<li>Raises blood pH from ~7.4 to ~7.45 (induced alkalosis)</li>
<li>Enhances H+ gradient: muscle [H+] &gt; blood [H+]</li>
<li>Accelerates H+ efflux via MCT1/MCT4 monocarboxylate transporters</li>
<li>Maintains intramuscular pH for longer during high-intensity work</li>
</ul>
<h3>Complementary to Carnosine</h3>
<ul>
<li>Beta-alanine: intracellular buffer (inside muscle)</li>
<li>Sodium bicarbonate: extracellular buffer (blood)</li>
<li>Combined use shows additive benefits in some studies</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<h3>Acute Loading</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dose</strong>: 0.2-0.3 g/kg body weight (~15-21 g for 70 kg person)</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: 60-90 minutes before exercise</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Capsules (enteric-coated preferred) to reduce GI issues</li>
</ul>
<h3>Serial Loading</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dose</strong>: 0.5 g/kg/day split into 3-4 doses with meals</li>
<li><strong>Duration</strong>: 3-5 days before competition</li>
<li><strong>Advantage</strong>: Better tolerated, sustained buffering capacity</li>
</ul>
