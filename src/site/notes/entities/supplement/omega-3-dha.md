<h1>Omega-3 DHA</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Docosahexaenoic acid, a long-chain omega-3 fatty acid that is the primary structural component of brain cell membranes. Essential for brain development, cognitive function, and neuroprotection throughout life.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5)</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>DHA (22:6 n-3) constitutes approximately 40% of polyunsaturated fatty acids in brain cell membranes and 60% in the retina. It maintains membrane fluidity, which is critical for receptor function, ion channel activity, and neurotransmitter release (dopamine, serotonin). DHA is enzymatically converted to specialized pro-resolving mediators (SPMs) including resolvins (RvD1, RvD2), protectins (NPD1/neuroprotectin D1), and maresins, which actively resolve neuroinflammation. Neuroprotectin D1 is particularly important for neuronal survival signaling, inhibiting apoptosis via Bcl-2 upregulation. DHA enhances BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor) expression, supporting synaptic plasticity, long-term potentiation, and neurogenesis in the hippocampus. It also modulates gene expression through PPAR-gamma and RXR nuclear receptors, influencing lipid metabolism and anti-inflammatory pathways.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General brain health</strong>: 250-500 mg DHA daily</li>
<li><strong>Cognitive support / aging</strong>: 500-1000 mg DHA daily</li>
<li><strong>Depression (adjunct)</strong>: 1000-2000 mg DHA daily (often combined with EPA)</li>
<li><strong>Pregnancy / fetal development</strong>: 200-300 mg DHA daily minimum</li>
<li><strong>Source</strong>: Fish oil, algae oil (vegan), or fatty fish (2-3 servings/week)</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Triglyceride form has superior absorption over ethyl ester</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Take with a fat-containing meal for optimal absorption</li>
</ul>
