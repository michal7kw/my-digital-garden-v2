<h1>Ginkgo Biloba</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Ginkgo biloba is a &quot;living fossil&quot; — the sole surviving species of the Ginkgoales order, with a fossil record spanning approximately 270 million years. It is one of the most extensively studied herbal medicines, with standardized extracts (EGb 761 being the most researched) containing two principal bioactive classes: ginkgolides (terpenoid lactones, unique to this species) and flavonoid glycosides.</p>
<p>The primary pharmacological action is improvement of cerebral blood flow and microcirculation through a combination of PAF antagonism (ginkgolides), antioxidant protection (flavonoids), and nitric oxide-mediated vasodilation. Clinical evidence supports modest but significant cognitive benefits in age-related cognitive decline and mild-to-moderate dementia, with several large RCTs (GuidAge, GINGKO) showing mixed results for Alzheimer's prevention but consistent short-term cognitive improvement. Ginkgo synergizes well with mitochondrial-targeted compounds like PQQ and Methylene Blue — Ginkgo improves oxygen and nutrient delivery to the brain while these compounds enhance the mitochondria's ability to use that oxygen for ATP production.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 3/5</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>PAF Antagonism (Ginkgolides)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Ginkgolide B is the most potent PAF (platelet-activating factor) antagonist found in nature</li>
<li>PAF antagonism reduces platelet aggregation, improving blood fluidity and microcirculation</li>
<li>Also reduces PAF-mediated inflammatory cascades (PAF is a potent proinflammatory phospholipid)</li>
<li>Unique mechanism among herbal supplements — no other plant produces specific PAF antagonists</li>
</ul>
<h3>Antioxidant Activity (Flavonoids)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Flavonoid glycosides (quercetin, kaempferol, isorhamnetin derivatives) scavenge superoxide, hydroxyl radicals, and peroxyl radicals</li>
<li>Chelate pro-oxidant metal ions (Fe2+, Cu2+), preventing Fenton chemistry</li>
<li>Upregulate endogenous antioxidant defenses via Nrf2 pathway activation</li>
<li>Protect neuronal membranes from lipid peroxidation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Cerebral Blood Flow Enhancement</h3>
<ul>
<li>Increases nitric oxide production in cerebral vasculature → vasodilation</li>
<li>Reduces blood viscosity through PAF antagonism and erythrocyte flexibility improvement</li>
<li>Enhances neurovascular coupling — matching local blood flow to neuronal metabolic demand</li>
<li>Particularly effective in small-vessel microcirculation (capillary level)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Neuroprotection</h3>
<ul>
<li>Stabilizes mitochondrial membrane potential during oxidative stress</li>
<li>Upregulates Bcl-2 (anti-apoptotic) and downregulates Bax (pro-apoptotic) expression</li>
<li>Reduces amyloid-beta aggregation and toxicity in neuronal cultures</li>
<li>Inhibits BACE-1 (beta-secretase), potentially reducing amyloid-beta production</li>
<li>Modulates NF-kB inflammatory signaling</li>
</ul>
<h3>Neurotransmitter Modulation</h3>
<ul>
<li>Enhances acetylcholine release from cholinergic terminals (relevant to memory)</li>
<li>Weak monoamine reuptake inhibition (serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine)</li>
<li>Modulates GABA-A receptor function</li>
<li>These effects are modest compared to pharmaceutical agents but contribute to cognitive profile</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<h3>Standard Protocol</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>EGb 761</strong>: 120-240 mg/day, divided into 2-3 doses</li>
<li><strong>Standardization</strong>: 24% flavone glycosides + 6% terpene lactones (industry standard)</li>
<li><strong>Onset</strong>: Effects on cerebral blood flow begin within hours; cognitive benefits typically require 4-6 weeks of consistent use</li>
</ul>
<h3>Important Notes</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Bleeding risk</strong>: PAF antagonism and mild anticoagulant effects; discontinue 2 weeks before surgery</li>
<li><strong>Drug interactions</strong>: May potentiate anticoagulants (warfarin), antiplatelet agents (aspirin, clopidogrel)</li>
<li><strong>Quality concerns</strong>: Unstandardized preparations may contain ginkgotoxin (4-O-methylpyridoxine), which can cause seizures; always use standardized extracts</li>
<li><strong>Contraindications</strong>: Active bleeding, planned surgery, concurrent anticoagulant therapy</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Research</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cochrane review</strong>: Systematic review of Ginkgo biloba for cognitive impairment and dementia — evidence of modest but significant improvement in cognition and activities of daily living at 120-240 mg/day for 12+ weeks (PMID:20236541)</li>
<li><strong>Neuroprotection mechanisms</strong>: EGb 761 protects neurons through mitochondrial stabilization, anti-apoptotic signaling, antioxidant activity, and anti-inflammatory effects (PMID:25114079)</li>
<li><strong>Cerebral blood flow</strong>: Ginkgo biloba extract improves cerebral blood flow velocity and microcirculation in healthy elderly subjects, measured by transcranial Doppler (PMID:21802920)</li>
<li><strong>Pharmacology review</strong>: Comprehensive review of Ginkgo biloba's dual mechanism (PAF antagonism + flavonoid antioxidant) and clinical evidence across cognitive, vascular, and psychiatric applications (PMID:24548139)</li>
</ul>
