<h1>Boron</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Boron is an essential trace mineral that plays a surprisingly broad role in human physiology despite being needed in only milligram quantities. It is critical for calcium, magnesium, and vitamin D metabolism, making it a foundational nutrient for bone health. Boron also modulates sex hormone levels by reducing SHBG and supporting steroid hydroxylation, which may increase free testosterone and estradiol -- effects most pronounced in individuals with low baseline boron status.</p>
<p>Beyond bone and hormones, boron supports cognitive function (EEG studies show improved attention and motor coordination with adequate intake), reduces inflammatory markers (hs-CRP, TNF-alpha), and may protect against kidney stone formation and certain cancers (prostate). Dietary intake in many Western diets averages only 1-2 mg/day, potentially suboptimal compared to the 3-6 mg/day associated with health benefits. Rich food sources include avocados, nuts, raisins, and prunes.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Boron influences calcium, magnesium, and phosphorus metabolism by modulating the activity of key regulatory enzymes and hormones. It inhibits 24-hydroxylase, the enzyme that inactivates vitamin D (calcitriol), thereby extending vitamin D's biological half-life. Boron reduces urinary excretion of calcium and magnesium, improving mineral retention for bone health. It modulates sex hormone metabolism by inhibiting SHBG binding and supporting the hydroxylation of steroid hormones, which may increase free testosterone and estradiol. Boron also reduces inflammatory markers (hs-CRP, TNF-alpha) through inhibition of NF-kB and serine protease activity. In the brain, boron appears to support electrical activity and cognitive function, with deficiency associated with decreased attention and short-term memory.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General health</strong>: 3-6 mg daily</li>
<li><strong>Bone support</strong>: 3-6 mg daily with calcium, magnesium, and vitamin D</li>
<li><strong>Hormone optimization</strong>: 6-10 mg daily (research doses)</li>
<li><strong>Upper limit</strong>: No established UL, but doses above 20 mg/day not recommended</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Calcium fructoborate (most studied), boron glycinate, or boron citrate</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: With meals for best tolerance</li>
</ul>
