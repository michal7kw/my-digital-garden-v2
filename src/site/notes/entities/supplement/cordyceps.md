<h1>Cordyceps</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Medicinal mushroom traditionally used for energy and endurance. Supports ATP production, oxygen utilization, and exercise performance. Contains cordycepin with anti-inflammatory and adaptogenic properties.</p>
<p><strong>Research Summary:</strong> Traditional use for fatigue and respiratory issues. Modern research shows benefits for exercise performance, especially in trained individuals. Cordyceps militaris is more sustainable and may be more potent than wild C. sinensis.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Cordyceps contains cordycepin (3'-deoxyadenosine), an adenosine analog that modulates multiple cellular pathways. It enhances ATP production by upregulating mitochondrial biogenesis and improving oxidative phosphorylation efficiency. Cordycepin activates AMPK, promoting cellular energy homeostasis. The mushroom increases oxygen utilization capacity (VO2 max) through enhanced erythropoietin production and improved hemoglobin oxygen affinity. Beta-glucans and other polysaccharides activate innate immune cells (macrophages, NK cells) through Dectin-1 and TLR-2 receptors. Cordyceps also modulates the HPA axis, providing adaptogenic stress resilience, and inhibits NF-kB to reduce inflammatory signaling. Adenosine content may improve blood flow through vasodilation via A2 adenosine receptors.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General health</strong>: 1-3 g extract daily</li>
<li><strong>Exercise performance</strong>: 1-3 g taken 30-60 minutes pre-workout</li>
<li><strong>Immune support</strong>: 2-3 g daily</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Cordyceps militaris extract (CS-4); standardized for cordycepin and adenosine content</li>
<li><strong>Extraction</strong>: Dual extraction (hot water + alcohol) captures both polysaccharides and cordycepin</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Morning or pre-workout for energy; consistent daily use for immune benefits</li>
</ul>
