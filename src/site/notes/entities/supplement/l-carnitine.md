<h1>L-Carnitine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>L-Carnitine is a naturally occurring amino acid derivative synthesized from lysine and methionine in the liver and kidneys. Its primary function is transporting long-chain fatty acids across the inner mitochondrial membrane via the carnitine shuttle (CPT-I/CPT-II system) for beta-oxidation -- without carnitine, cells cannot efficiently burn fat for energy. The body stores 95% of its carnitine in skeletal and cardiac muscle, reflecting its importance for tissues with high energy demands.</p>
<p>Multiple forms of L-Carnitine serve distinct clinical purposes: Acetyl-L-Carnitine (ALCAR) crosses the blood-brain barrier and supports cognitive function, nerve regeneration, and acetylcholine synthesis; L-Carnitine Tartrate has superior absorption and is preferred for exercise performance; Propionyl-L-Carnitine specifically improves endothelial function and is studied for cardiovascular and peripheral vascular disease. Strong evidence supports L-Carnitine supplementation for angina, heart failure recovery (meta-analyses show reduced mortality post-MI), peripheral neuropathy (especially diabetic), and age-related cognitive decline. Vegetarians and the elderly tend to have lower carnitine levels and may benefit most from supplementation.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>L-Carnitine's primary function is shuttling long-chain fatty acids across the inner mitochondrial membrane for beta-oxidation. The carnitine palmitoyltransferase (CPT) system works as follows: CPT-I on the outer membrane converts acyl-CoA to acylcarnitine, carnitine-acylcarnitine translocase moves acylcarnitine across the inner membrane, and CPT-II reconverts it to acyl-CoA for beta-oxidation. This is the rate-limiting step for fatty acid energy production. Acetyl-L-Carnitine (ALCAR), the acetylated form, crosses the blood-brain barrier and donates acetyl groups for acetylcholine synthesis and energy production in neurons. ALCAR also enhances nerve growth factor (NGF) receptor sensitivity and supports mitochondrial membrane integrity in aging neurons. Propionyl-L-Carnitine improves endothelial function through nitric oxide production and is specific for cardiovascular and peripheral vascular support.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General energy / fat metabolism</strong>: 500-2000 mg L-Carnitine daily</li>
<li><strong>Cognitive function</strong>: 500-1500 mg ALCAR (Acetyl-L-Carnitine) daily</li>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular support</strong>: 1000-3000 mg Propionyl-L-Carnitine daily</li>
<li><strong>Exercise performance</strong>: 1000-2000 mg L-Carnitine Tartrate, 60-90 min before exercise</li>
<li><strong>Form selection</strong>: ALCAR for brain/nerve; Tartrate for exercise/absorption; Propionyl for cardiovascular</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Morning for ALCAR (may be stimulating); before exercise for Tartrate</li>
<li><strong>Note</strong>: Oral bioavailability is 15-25%; gut bacteria convert excess to TMAO</li>
</ul>
