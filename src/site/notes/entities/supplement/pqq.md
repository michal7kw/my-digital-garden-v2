<h1>PQQ</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Pyrroloquinoline quinone (PQQ) is a novel redox cofactor first identified in bacteria and later found to play important roles in mammalian biology. It is classified as a &quot;longevity vitamin&quot; by some researchers due to its essential role in mitochondrial biogenesis — the creation of new mitochondria. PQQ is one of very few compounds demonstrated to increase mitochondrial number in human cells, making it uniquely valuable for supporting cellular energy production and combating age-related mitochondrial decline.</p>
<p>PQQ is found naturally in small quantities in foods such as natto, parsley, green peppers, kiwifruit, and human breast milk. Its exceptional stability as an antioxidant is remarkable: while vitamin C completes approximately 4 catalytic redox cycles before degradation, PQQ undergoes over 20,000 cycles, making it one of the most efficient biological antioxidants known.</p>
<p>Beyond mitochondrial biogenesis, PQQ supports nerve growth factor (NGF) synthesis, reduces systemic inflammation markers, improves sleep quality, and enhances cognitive performance in aging populations. It shows strong synergy with Coenzyme Q10, as the two compounds target complementary aspects of mitochondrial function — PQQ increases mitochondrial number while CoQ10 optimizes electron transport within existing mitochondria.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 3/5</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Mitochondrial Biogenesis</h3>
<ul>
<li>Activates PGC-1alpha (peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor gamma coactivator 1-alpha), the master transcriptional regulator of mitochondrial biogenesis</li>
<li>PGC-1alpha activation leads to increased expression of TFAM (mitochondrial transcription factor A), which drives mitochondrial DNA replication and transcription</li>
<li>Net result: increased mitochondrial number, mass, and respiratory capacity</li>
<li>This mechanism is particularly relevant in aging, where mitochondrial number and function decline progressively</li>
</ul>
<h3>Redox Cofactor Activity</h3>
<ul>
<li>Functions as a potent electron shuttle in oxidation-reduction reactions</li>
<li>Uniquely stable structure allows &gt;20,000 catalytic cycles without degradation</li>
<li>Scavenges reactive oxygen species (ROS), particularly superoxide and hydroxyl radicals</li>
<li>Protects mitochondrial membranes from lipid peroxidation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Neuroprotection</h3>
<ul>
<li>Stimulates synthesis of nerve growth factor (NGF) in astrocytes and Schwann cells</li>
<li>Activates CREB (cAMP response element-binding protein), a transcription factor critical for long-term memory formation and neuronal survival</li>
<li>Protects against excitotoxicity by modulating NMDA receptor signaling</li>
<li>Activates DJ-1, a neuroprotective protein implicated in Parkinson's disease pathogenesis</li>
</ul>
<h3>Anti-Inflammatory Effects</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reduces C-reactive protein (CRP) and interleukin-6 (IL-6) levels in human studies</li>
<li>Modulates NF-kB signaling pathway</li>
<li>May contribute to reduced neuroinflammation through microglial modulation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<h3>Standard Supplementation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dose</strong>: 10-20 mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: BioPQQ (produced by Mitsubishi Gas Chemical) is the most studied branded form; PQQ disodium salt is the standard supplemental form</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Morning, with or without food (well absorbed either way)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Synergistic Combinations</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>PQQ + CoQ10</strong>: The most evidence-backed combination. PQQ creates new mitochondria; CoQ10 optimizes electron transport in existing ones. Studies show combined supplementation produces greater improvements in cognitive function and energy than either alone.</li>
<li><strong>PQQ + NMN/NR</strong>: Emerging combination for comprehensive mitochondrial support (biogenesis + NAD+ replenishment)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Important Notes</h3>
<ul>
<li>Well tolerated at supplemental doses with very few reported side effects</li>
<li>No known significant drug interactions at standard doses</li>
<li>Effects may take 2-4 weeks to become noticeable (mitochondrial biogenesis is a gradual process)</li>
<li>Higher doses (&gt;20 mg) have not shown proportionally greater benefits</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Research</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cognitive function in elderly</strong>: PQQ supplementation (20 mg/day for 12 weeks) significantly improved cognitive function scores (attention, memory, and processing speed) in middle-aged and elderly Japanese subjects, with effects enhanced when combined with CoQ10 (PMID:23370669)</li>
<li><strong>Mitochondrial biogenesis via PGC-1alpha</strong>: PQQ activates PGC-1alpha through multiple signaling pathways (including CREB phosphorylation) in mammalian cells, demonstrating a direct mechanism for mitochondrial biogenesis (PMID:27364076)</li>
<li><strong>Anti-inflammatory effects in humans</strong>: PQQ supplementation (20 mg/day for 8 weeks) significantly reduced CRP and IL-6 levels in healthy adults, alongside improved energy and sleep quality (PMID:22207209)</li>
<li><strong>PQQ and CoQ10 synergy</strong>: Combined PQQ + CoQ10 supplementation showed synergistic improvements in mitochondrial bioenergetics and cognitive measures compared to either supplement alone (PMID:26923479)</li>
<li><strong>NGF synthesis</strong>: PQQ stimulates NGF synthesis in astrocytes in a dose-dependent manner, supporting peripheral and central nerve survival and regeneration (PMID:8518462)</li>
</ul>
