<h1>Sulforaphane</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Potent Nrf2 activator derived from cruciferous vegetables, especially broccoli sprouts. Powerful inducer of phase II detoxification enzymes and antioxidant defenses.</p>
<p><strong>Research Summary:</strong> Extensive research on Nrf2 activation, detoxification enhancement, and cancer chemoprevention. Clinical trials show benefits for autism, diabetes, and environmental toxin clearance. Broccoli sprouts contain 10-100x more glucoraphanin than mature broccoli.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Sulforaphane is an isothiocyanate that activates the Nrf2 (nuclear factor erythroid 2-related factor 2) transcription pathway by modifying Keap1, the cytoplasmic repressor that normally targets Nrf2 for proteasomal degradation. When sulforaphane modifies Keap1's reactive cysteine residues, Nrf2 translocates to the nucleus and binds ARE (antioxidant response elements), inducing over 200 cytoprotective genes including: phase II detoxification enzymes (glutathione S-transferases, NAD(P)H:quinone oxidoreductase, UDP-glucuronosyltransferases), glutathione synthesis enzymes (gamma-glutamylcysteine ligase), and antioxidant proteins (heme oxygenase-1, thioredoxin). Sulforaphane also inhibits NF-kB-mediated inflammation, induces cell cycle arrest in cancer cells via HDAC (histone deacetylase) inhibition, and enhances autophagy. The compound requires myrosinase enzyme to convert from its precursor glucoraphanin — cooking destroys myrosinase, but gut bacteria and exogenous myrosinase (from mustard seed) can perform the conversion.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General health / detoxification</strong>: 10-30 mg sulforaphane daily</li>
<li><strong>Cancer chemoprevention</strong>: 30-60 mg sulforaphane daily (clinical study doses)</li>
<li><strong>Autism studies</strong>: ~50 mg sulforaphane daily</li>
<li><strong>Source</strong>: Fresh broccoli sprouts (highest potency, 10-100x mature broccoli); stabilized sulforaphane supplements (Prostaphane); glucoraphanin + myrosinase supplements</li>
<li><strong>Enhancement</strong>: If using glucoraphanin supplements, add mustard seed powder as myrosinase source</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: With meals for better tolerance</li>
</ul>
