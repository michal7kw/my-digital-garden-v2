<h1>Alpha Lipoic Acid</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Powerful antioxidant that works in both water and fat environments. Regenerates other antioxidants, supports blood sugar metabolism, and protects against neuropathy.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5)</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Alpha lipoic acid serves as a critical cofactor for mitochondrial enzyme complexes, specifically pyruvate dehydrogenase (converting pyruvate to acetyl-CoA) and alpha-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase (in the TCA cycle). This directly supports cellular energy production. It is both water and fat-soluble, allowing it to function as an antioxidant in all cellular compartments. ALA regenerates other antioxidants (vitamins C, E, CoQ10, glutathione) and may activate AMPK to enhance insulin sensitivity and glucose uptake.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<p>300-600 mg daily; R-ALA at half dose</p>
