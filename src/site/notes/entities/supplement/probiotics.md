<h1>Probiotics</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Probiotics are live microorganisms that confer health benefits when administered in adequate amounts (WHO/FAO definition). Benefits are highly strain-specific -- not all probiotics are interchangeable, and efficacy for one condition does not guarantee efficacy for another. The human gut microbiome harbors roughly 100 trillion microorganisms comprising over 1,000 species, collectively encoding 150 times more genes than the human genome. Dysbiosis (microbial imbalance) is implicated in IBS, IBD, obesity, type 2 diabetes, depression, autoimmune disease, allergies, and even neurodegenerative conditions.</p>
<p>The gut-brain axis -- a bidirectional communication network involving the vagus nerve, microbial metabolites (short-chain fatty acids, tryptophan derivatives), immune signaling, and endocrine pathways -- means that gut health directly impacts mental health and cognitive function. &quot;Psychobiotics&quot; (strains such as <em>Lactobacillus helveticus</em> R0052 and <em>Bifidobacterium longum</em> R0175) have demonstrated anxiolytic and antidepressant effects in clinical trials. Key probiotic genera include Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium, and Saccharomyces, each with well-characterized strain-specific benefits.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Probiotics work through multiple mechanisms: (1) competitive exclusion — occupy adhesion sites preventing pathogen colonization, (2) antimicrobial substance production — bacteriocins, organic acids, hydrogen peroxide, (3) immune modulation — stimulate IgA production, regulate Th1/Th2 balance, modulate dendritic cells, (4) barrier enhancement — upregulate tight junction proteins (occludin, ZO-1), (5) short-chain fatty acid (SCFA) production — butyrate (colonic fuel, anti-inflammatory), propionate, acetate, (6) neurotransmitter production — certain strains produce GABA, serotonin precursors.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General health</strong>: 10-30 billion CFU/day multi-strain</li>
<li><strong>IBS</strong>: Bifidobacterium infantis 35624 (1 billion CFU), VSL#3 (450 billion CFU — strongest evidence)</li>
<li><strong>Antibiotic-associated diarrhea</strong>: Saccharomyces boulardii (250-500mg 2x/day — take DURING and 1 week after antibiotics)</li>
<li><strong>Depression/anxiety (psychobiotics)</strong>: Lactobacillus helveticus R0052 + Bifidobacterium longum R0175</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Take with or just before meals (stomach acid is buffered by food)</li>
<li><strong>Storage</strong>: Refrigerate unless label specifies shelf-stable</li>
<li><strong>Note</strong>: CFU count matters less than strain specificity and clinical evidence for your condition</li>
</ul>
