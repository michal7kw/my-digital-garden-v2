<h1>Beta-Alanine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Beta-alanine is one of the most extensively studied ergogenic supplements, with strong evidence for improving performance in activities lasting 1-4 minutes (the glycolytic energy system window). It is the rate-limiting precursor for carnosine synthesis — supplementation increases intramuscular carnosine by 40-80% over 4-12 weeks, significantly enhancing the muscle's ability to buffer the hydrogen ions produced during high-intensity anaerobic glycolysis.</p>
<p>The ISSN (International Society of Sports Nutrition) has classified beta-alanine as having &quot;strong evidence to support efficacy and apparently safe&quot; for improving exercise capacity and performance in high-intensity exercise.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 5/5</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Carnosine Buffering System</h3>
<ul>
<li>Beta-alanine + histidine → carnosine (via carnosine synthase in muscle)</li>
<li>Beta-alanine is the rate-limiting substrate (histidine is abundant)</li>
<li>Carnosine buffers H+ ions: H+ + carnosine → protonated carnosine</li>
<li>This delays the pH drop that inhibits phosphofructokinase (PFK) and other glycolytic enzymes</li>
<li>Net effect: extends time to fatigue at high intensity by 2-3%</li>
</ul>
<h3>Performance Window</h3>
<ul>
<li>Most effective for efforts lasting 60 seconds to 4 minutes</li>
<li>Less benefit for very short sprints (&lt;30s, phosphagen-dominant) or long endurance (&gt;10 min, oxidative)</li>
<li>Ideal for: repeated sprints, HIIT, rowing, swimming (200-400m), cycling (4km pursuit)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<h3>Standard Supplementation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dose</strong>: 3.2-6.4 g/day (most studied: 6.4 g/day)</li>
<li><strong>Loading</strong>: 4-12 weeks to saturate muscle carnosine stores</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Sustained-release (SR) reduces paresthesia; split into 2-4 daily doses</li>
<li><strong>Maintenance</strong>: 1.2-3.2 g/day after initial loading</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Consistent daily intake; not timing-dependent relative to exercise</li>
</ul>
