<h1>Spirulina</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Spirulina (<em>Arthrospira platensis</em>) is a nutrient-dense cyanobacterium (blue-green alga) that contains 60-70% protein by weight, making it one of the most protein-rich whole foods on Earth. It provides a comprehensive nutritional profile including B vitamins (especially B12 analogue), iron, manganese, gamma-linolenic acid (GLA), and beta-carotene. Its signature bioactive compound, C-phycocyanin (the blue pigment), is a potent antioxidant and anti-inflammatory agent that inhibits NADPH oxidase and COX-2.</p>
<p>Clinical evidence supports spirulina for improving lipid profiles (reducing LDL and triglycerides by 10-15%), lowering blood pressure, enhancing antioxidant status, modulating immune function (activating NK cells), and reducing allergic rhinitis symptoms. Meta-analyses confirm dose-dependent lipid-lowering effects at 2-8 g/day. NASA and the European Space Agency have studied spirulina as a complete food source for long-duration space missions. Source quality is critical, as spirulina grown in contaminated water can accumulate heavy metals (lead, mercury, arsenic) and microcystins -- third-party-tested organic sources are strongly recommended.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Spirulina's primary bioactive is phycocyanin (C-phycocyanin), a blue pigment protein that inhibits NADPH oxidase, the major source of superoxide radicals in inflammatory cells. Phycocyanin also inhibits COX-2 and scavenges peroxynitrite, hydroxyl, and peroxyl radicals. The lipid-lowering effects involve inhibition of intestinal cholesterol absorption and upregulation of hepatic LDL receptor expression, lowering circulating LDL. Spirulina's high gamma-linolenic acid (GLA) content modulates prostaglandin balance toward anti-inflammatory PGE1. The blood pressure reduction is mediated through nitric oxide (NO) production enhancement and ACE inhibition by bioactive peptides released during digestion. Immune modulation occurs through activation of NK cells and enhancement of interferon-gamma production. Spirulina also chelates heavy metals through its sulfated polysaccharides (calcium spirulan), supporting detoxification.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General health</strong>: 1-3 g daily</li>
<li><strong>Lipid management</strong>: 3-5 g daily</li>
<li><strong>Antioxidant / anti-inflammatory</strong>: 3-8 g daily</li>
<li><strong>Immune support</strong>: 2-5 g daily</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Tablets (convenient) or powder (versatile, can add to smoothies)</li>
<li><strong>Quality</strong>: Choose organic sources with third-party heavy metal testing</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: With or without food; start at 1 g/day and increase gradually to assess tolerance</li>
</ul>
