<h1>Spermidine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Natural polyamine that induces autophagy via inhibition of acetyltransferase EP300 (p300). Found in wheat germ, soybeans, aged cheese, natto, and mushrooms. Associated with increased lifespan in yeast, flies, worms, and mice. Human epidemiological data links high dietary spermidine intake to reduced cardiovascular mortality and cognitive decline.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5)</p>
<p><strong>Research Summary:</strong> Extended lifespan in yeast, flies, worms, and mice. Bruneck Study (N=829, 20-year follow-up) showed 5.7 years additional life expectancy in highest vs lowest tertile of dietary spermidine intake. SmartAge trial showed cognitive improvement in older adults at risk for dementia. Cardioprotective in multiple models.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Inhibits acetyltransferase EP300 (p300) → hypoacetylation of cytoplasmic proteins → autophagy induction via TFEB nuclear translocation and AMPK activation. Also induces mitophagy (PINK1/Parkin pathway), promotes mitochondrial function, and has anti-inflammatory effects via NF-κB suppression.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<p>1-6 mg/day from supplements; wheat germ extract is richest food source (~24 mg/100g)</p>
