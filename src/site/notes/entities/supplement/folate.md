<h1>Folate</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Folate (vitamin B9) is an essential water-soluble vitamin that serves as the backbone of one-carbon metabolism, a network of biochemical reactions critical for DNA synthesis, DNA methylation, amino acid metabolism, and neurotransmitter production. The biologically active form, 5-methyltetrahydrofolate (5-MTHF or methylfolate), donates methyl groups to convert homocysteine to methionine, which is then activated to SAMe -- the universal methyl donor for over 100 methylation reactions.</p>
<p>MTHFR gene polymorphisms (C677T affecting ~10-15% of the population, A1298C) impair the conversion of synthetic folic acid to its active methylfolate form, making direct methylfolate supplementation essential for affected individuals. Folate deficiency causes megaloblastic anemia, elevated homocysteine (cardiovascular risk factor), neural tube defects in pregnancy, and impaired neurotransmitter synthesis contributing to depression. Prescription-strength methylfolate (Deplin, 7.5-15 mg) is FDA-approved as adjunctive therapy for major depressive disorder.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Folate functions as a one-carbon carrier in the folate cycle, essential for DNA synthesis (thymidylate and purine production), DNA methylation, and amino acid metabolism. The active form, 5-MTHF (methylfolate), donates a methyl group to homocysteine via methionine synthase (requiring B12 as cofactor), converting it to methionine. Methionine is then converted to SAMe (S-adenosylmethionine), the universal methyl donor for neurotransmitter synthesis, DNA methylation, and phospholipid production. Folate is therefore essential for serotonin, dopamine, and norepinephrine synthesis through its role in SAMe production and tetrahydrobiopterin (BH4) recycling. MTHFR gene variants (C677T, A1298C) impair folate metabolism, making methylfolate supplementation necessary to bypass the enzyme deficiency.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General health</strong>: 400-800 mcg daily (as methylfolate / 5-MTHF)</li>
<li><strong>Pregnancy</strong>: 800-1000 mcg daily (begin before conception)</li>
<li><strong>MTHFR variants</strong>: 800-1500 mcg methylfolate daily</li>
<li><strong>Depression (adjunct)</strong>: 7.5-15 mg methylfolate daily (prescription-level Deplin)</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: L-methylfolate (5-MTHF) preferred; avoid folic acid in those with MTHFR variants</li>
<li><strong>Cofactors</strong>: Ensure adequate B12 and B6 for complete methylation cycle function</li>
</ul>
