<h1>NAC</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>N-Acetyl Cysteine (NAC) is the acetylated form of the amino acid L-cysteine, serving as the rate-limiting precursor for glutathione — the body's master antioxidant. Originally used as a mucolytic (breaks down mucus) and the standard antidote for acetaminophen overdose, NAC has emerged as a versatile therapeutic tool with evidence for psychiatric conditions (OCD, addiction, depression), liver protection, respiratory health, PCOS (improves ovulation), and as an adjunct to many chronic conditions involving oxidative stress.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>NAC provides cysteine for glutathione synthesis (GSH), restoring the body's primary antioxidant defense. It modulates glutamate via the cystine-glutamate antiporter (xCT) — normalizing glutamate levels in the nucleus accumbens (relevant to addiction and OCD). Direct mucolytic action breaks disulfide bonds in mucus glycoproteins. Chelates heavy metals. Inhibits NF-kB (anti-inflammatory). In acetaminophen overdose, it replenishes hepatic glutathione stores to detoxify the toxic metabolite NAPQI.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General antioxidant</strong>: 600-1200 mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Psychiatric (OCD, addiction)</strong>: 1200-2400 mg/day (based on RCTs)</li>
<li><strong>Liver support</strong>: 600-1200 mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Respiratory</strong>: 600 mg 2x/day (reduces COPD exacerbations)</li>
<li><strong>PCOS</strong>: 1200-1800 mg/day (improves ovulation when added to clomiphene)</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Take on empty stomach for best absorption; split doses if &gt;1200 mg</li>
<li><strong>Cofactors</strong>: Vitamin C enhances NAC's antioxidant effect; molybdenum supports sulfur metabolism</li>
</ul>
