<h1>Reishi</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The 'mushroom of immortality' in traditional Chinese medicine. Powerful immune modulator, adaptogen, and sleep supporter. Contains beta-glucans and triterpenes with calming and anti-cancer properties.</p>
<p><strong>Research Summary:</strong> Long history of use for longevity and immune support. Modern research confirms immune modulation, anti-inflammatory effects, and sleep quality improvement. Bitter taste from triterpenes indicates quality.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Reishi (Ganoderma lucidum) contains two primary bioactive classes: beta-glucans (polysaccharides) and triterpenes (ganoderic acids). Beta-glucans activate innate immune cells by binding Dectin-1 and complement receptor 3 (CR3) on macrophages, dendritic cells, and NK cells, upregulating cytokine production (IL-2, IL-10, TNF-alpha) and enhancing phagocytic activity. Triterpenes (ganoderic acids A, B, C, D) inhibit histamine release from mast cells, suppress NF-kB signaling, and directly inhibit 5-alpha reductase and angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE). The calming and sleep-promoting effects may involve GABAergic modulation — triterpenes have been shown to potentiate GABA-A receptor activity and reduce neuronal excitability. Reishi also modulates the HPA axis, reducing cortisol under chronic stress conditions. Anti-tumor properties involve immune surveillance enhancement and direct cytotoxic effects of triterpenes on cancer cell lines.</p>
<h2>Key Bioactive Compounds</h2>
<h3>Beta-glucans (Polysaccharides)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Water-soluble polysaccharides (ganoderans) with immunomodulatory activity</li>
<li>Activate Dectin-1 receptor on immune cells, triggering phagocytosis and cytokine release</li>
<li>Increase NK cell cytotoxicity and macrophage activity</li>
<li>Enhance adaptive immunity by promoting T-cell and B-cell proliferation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Ganoderic Acids (Triterpenes)</h3>
<ul>
<li>Over 130 triterpenes identified in Ganoderma lucidum</li>
<li>Ganoderic acids A, B, C, D are the most studied</li>
<li>Alcohol-soluble; responsible for the bitter taste of quality extracts</li>
<li>Anti-inflammatory via NF-kB suppression and COX-2 inhibition</li>
<li>Hepatoprotective — ganoderic acid R protects against chemical liver injury</li>
<li>Anti-histamine activity — inhibit mast cell degranulation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Other Compounds</h3>
<ul>
<li>Ganodermadiol — antiviral and antibacterial properties</li>
<li>Ganoderiol F — anti-HIV activity in vitro</li>
<li>Sterols (ergosterol, ganodosterone) — anti-inflammatory</li>
<li>Peptidoglycans (e.g., PS-G) — potent immune activation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Research Evidence</h2>
<h3>Immune Modulation</h3>
<ul>
<li>Meta-analysis of 12 RCTs shows significant increase in NK cell activity with Reishi supplementation (Level 1a)</li>
<li>Polysaccharide fraction increases IL-2, IL-6, and TNF-alpha production in vitro and in animal models</li>
<li>Enhances dendritic cell maturation and antigen presentation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Cancer Adjunct</h3>
<ul>
<li>Systematic review of 5 RCTs found Reishi as adjunct to chemotherapy/radiotherapy improved immune function markers and quality of life (Level 2a)</li>
<li>Ganoderic acids induce apoptosis and cell cycle arrest in multiple cancer cell lines (breast, prostate, colon)</li>
<li>Polysaccharides enhance immune surveillance — increased NK and cytotoxic T-cell activity</li>
</ul>
<h3>Sleep and Calming</h3>
<ul>
<li>RCT (n=48) showed 3g Reishi daily for 4 weeks improved subjective sleep quality and reduced sleep latency (Level 2b)</li>
<li>Triterpenes potentiate GABA-A receptor currents by 20-40% in electrophysiology studies</li>
<li>Reduces cortisol levels via HPA axis modulation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Liver Protection</h3>
<ul>
<li>Animal studies demonstrate hepatoprotection against CCl4-induced liver injury via ganoderic acids</li>
<li>Reduces ALT and AST elevation in models of chemical liver damage</li>
<li>Human case series suggest benefit in chronic hepatitis B (Level 4)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Cardiovascular</h3>
<ul>
<li>Meta-analysis of 5 RCTs shows mild blood pressure reduction (systolic -3.5 mmHg) (Level 2a)</li>
<li>ACE inhibition by triterpenes contributes to antihypertensive effect</li>
<li>Reduces LDL oxidation and improves lipid profile in animal models</li>
</ul>
<h3>Anti-inflammatory</h3>
<ul>
<li>Suppresses NF-kB nuclear translocation and downstream cytokine production (IL-6, TNF-alpha)</li>
<li>Inhibits COX-2 and iNOS expression</li>
<li>Reduces CRP levels in human studies</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General immune support</strong>: 1-2 g extract daily</li>
<li><strong>Sleep and stress</strong>: 1-3 g extract, taken in the evening</li>
<li><strong>Immune modulation / cancer adjunct</strong>: 3-5 g extract daily (clinical study doses)</li>
<li><strong>Liver support</strong>: 1.5-3g dual extract daily</li>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular</strong>: 1.5-3g extract daily</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Dual extract (hot water + alcohol) to capture both beta-glucans and triterpenes</li>
<li><strong>Quality indicator</strong>: Bitter taste indicates high triterpene content; look for &gt;10% polysaccharides and &gt;4% triterpenes</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Evening for sleep support; consistent daily use for immune benefits</li>
<li><strong>Note</strong>: Fruiting body extract preferred; spore oil is highest in triterpenes</li>
<li><strong>Onset</strong>: Immune effects may take 4-12 weeks of consistent use; sleep effects may be noticed within 1-2 weeks</li>
</ul>
