<h1>Cistanche</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Parasitic desert plant (Cistanche tubulosa/deserticola) used in Traditional Chinese Medicine for over 1,800 years as a kidney and yang tonic. Contains phenylethanoid glycosides (echinacoside, acteoside) with demonstrated neuroprotective, anti-inflammatory, and testosterone-supporting properties. Known as &quot;Ginseng of the Desert.&quot; Evidence is primarily preclinical with limited human trials.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐ (2/5)</p>
<p><strong>Research Summary:</strong> Primarily preclinical evidence. Animal studies show improved memory, increased testosterone, reduced fatigue, and neuroprotection. Limited human RCTs — one showed improved bowel function in elderly, another showed cognitive benefits. Traditional use data extensive but formal clinical evidence is early-stage.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Echinacoside and acteoside provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects. Increases nerve growth factor (NGF) expression supporting neurogenesis. Modulates testosterone via hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis stimulation. Inhibits acetylcholinesterase enhancing cholinergic signaling. Activates Nrf2 antioxidant pathway.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<p>100-300mg standardized extract (containing 15-30% echinacoside) daily</p>
