<h1>Apigenin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A natural flavonoid found in chamomile, parsley, and celery with antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, neuroprotective, and sedative properties. It is known to inhibit CD38 (boosting NAD+ levels) and modulate GABA receptors.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5)</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Inhibits CD38 (an NADase), acts as an antioxidant, binds to benzodiazepine site on GABAA receptors, and inhibits COX-2 and NF-κB pathways.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<p>Common supplemental dosage is 50-400 mg daily. Dietary intake is typically &lt;5 mg/day.</p>
