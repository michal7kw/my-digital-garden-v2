<h1>Apigenin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Apigenin is a widely distributed flavonoid found in plants like chamomile, parsley, and celery. It has garnered significant attention for its potential to promote longevity, improve sleep, and reduce anxiety.</p>
<p>Its primary mechanisms include:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>CD38 Inhibition</strong>: Apigenin is a potent inhibitor of CD38, an enzyme that degrades NAD+. By inhibiting CD38, it helps restore intracellular NAD+ levels, which are critical for mitochondrial function and cellular repair.</li>
<li><strong>GABA Modulation</strong>: It exerts sedative and anxiolytic effects by modulating GABA(A) receptors, acting similarly to a mild benzodiazepine but without the same risk of dependency.</li>
<li><strong>Anti-inflammatory</strong>: It reduces oxidative stress and inflammation by inhibiting key pathways like COX-2 and NF-κB.</li>
</ol>
