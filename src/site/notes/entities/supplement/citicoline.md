<h1>Citicoline</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Citicoline (CDP-choline) is a naturally occurring intermediate in the synthesis of phosphatidylcholine, the most abundant phospholipid in neuronal cell membranes. When taken orally, citicoline is hydrolyzed into choline and cytidine, both of which readily cross the blood-brain barrier. It is considered one of the most effective nootropic supplements due to its dual action: supplying choline for acetylcholine synthesis and providing cytidine (converted to uridine) for synaptic membrane repair and dopamine receptor upregulation.</p>
<p>Clinical evidence is extensive, spanning stroke recovery (ICTUS trial), age-related cognitive decline, ADHD, and traumatic brain injury. Citicoline improves attention, processing speed, and working memory in healthy adults, with the branded form Cognizin being the most studied. Its excellent safety profile (over 90% oral bioavailability, minimal side effects) makes it one of the best-tolerated cognitive supplements available. In Europe and Japan, citicoline is approved as a prescription medication for neurological conditions.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Citicoline (CDP-choline) is hydrolyzed in the gut into choline and cytidine, both of which cross the blood-brain barrier. Choline serves as a substrate for acetylcholine synthesis via choline acetyltransferase (ChAT) and for phosphatidylcholine synthesis, which is critical for neuronal membrane integrity and repair. Cytidine is converted to uridine, which enhances synaptic membrane synthesis and increases dopamine release in the frontal cortex by upregulating dopamine receptor density (D2/D3). Citicoline also inhibits phospholipase A2, reducing membrane phospholipid breakdown during ischemia. This dual mechanism of membrane support and neurotransmitter modulation underlies its benefits for both cognitive enhancement and neuroprotection after stroke or brain injury.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cognitive enhancement</strong>: 250-500 mg daily</li>
<li><strong>Neuroprotection / stroke recovery</strong>: 500-2000 mg daily (clinical trials)</li>
<li><strong>ADHD / attention</strong>: 250-500 mg daily</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Cognizin is the most studied branded form</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Morning for cognitive benefits; can be taken with or without food</li>
<li><strong>Note</strong>: May cause insomnia if taken late in the day; divide higher doses</li>
</ul>
