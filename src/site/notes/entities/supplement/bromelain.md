<h1>Bromelain</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Bromelain is a complex mixture of proteolytic enzymes (primarily cysteine proteases) extracted from the stem and fruit of the pineapple plant (Ananas comosus). The stem-derived extract is preferred therapeutically due to higher enzyme concentration. Bromelain has been used medicinally since the 1950s in Europe, where it holds drug approval in Germany for post-surgical and post-traumatic swelling.</p>
<p>The anti-inflammatory mechanism of bromelain is multifaceted and distinct from NSAIDs. It modulates arachidonic acid metabolism to reduce prostaglandin E2 and thromboxane A2 production, while preserving prostacyclin synthesis (unlike COX inhibitors). Its fibrinolytic activity directly reduces edema by degrading fibrin deposits in inflamed tissue. Bromelain also modulates immune cell surface receptors (CD44, CD4) and cytokine profiles, shifting the balance from pro-inflammatory to anti-inflammatory mediators.</p>
<p>Clinical trials have demonstrated efficacy for osteoarthritis pain management, with some head-to-head studies showing comparable analgesic effect to diclofenac. It is particularly effective for acute soft tissue injuries and post-surgical inflammation. A critical practical consideration is timing: bromelain must be taken on an empty stomach (30 minutes before or 2 hours after meals) for systemic anti-inflammatory activity. When taken with food, its proteolytic activity is directed toward protein digestion in the gut rather than systemic absorption.</p>
<h2>Dosage Guidelines</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Anti-inflammatory dose</strong>: 500-2000 GDU/day in 2-3 divided doses</li>
<li><strong>Osteoarthritis</strong>: 500-1000 GDU/day</li>
<li><strong>Post-surgical/acute inflammation</strong>: Up to 2000 GDU/day for 7-10 days</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Must be taken on empty stomach (30 min before or 2 hours after meals) for systemic effect</li>
<li><strong>Activity units</strong>: Look for GDU (gelatin dissolving units) or MCU (milk clotting units); 1 GDU = 1.5 MCU</li>
<li><strong>Caution</strong>: May increase bleeding risk; discontinue 2 weeks before surgery; avoid with anticoagulants</li>
</ul>
