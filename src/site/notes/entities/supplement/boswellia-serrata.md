<h1>Boswellia Serrata</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Boswellia serrata is a branching tree native to India and the Arabian Peninsula, prized for its oleo-gum resin (frankincense). The resin contains a family of pentacyclic triterpene acids known as boswellic acids, of which AKBA (3-O-acetyl-11-keto-beta-boswellic acid) is the most pharmacologically potent. Boswellia has been used for centuries in Ayurvedic medicine for inflammatory conditions.</p>
<p>The primary mechanism is direct inhibition of 5-lipoxygenase (5-LOX), the enzyme responsible for leukotriene synthesis. Unlike NSAIDs that target the COX pathway, boswellia addresses a complementary inflammatory cascade. This dual anti-inflammatory profile — reducing both leukotrienes and prostaglandins — makes it particularly relevant for conditions with strong leukotriene involvement such as asthma and inflammatory arthritis.</p>
<p>Clinical trials in osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis consistently demonstrate meaningful pain reduction, improved joint function, and reduced inflammatory markers. Notably, boswellia shows a favorable safety profile with fewer gastrointestinal side effects compared to conventional NSAIDs, making it suitable for long-term use. Proprietary extracts such as Aflapin and 5-Loxin have concentrated AKBA content for enhanced efficacy.</p>
<h2>Dosage Guidelines</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Standard extract (30-40% AKBA)</strong>: 300-500mg, 2-3 times daily with meals</li>
<li><strong>Aflapin (enriched extract)</strong>: 100mg/day</li>
<li><strong>5-Loxin (30% AKBA)</strong>: 100-250mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Take with fatty meals to enhance absorption of lipophilic boswellic acids</li>
<li><strong>Onset</strong>: Anti-inflammatory effects typically noticeable within 1-2 weeks, with full benefit at 4-8 weeks</li>
</ul>
