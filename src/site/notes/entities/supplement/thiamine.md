<h1>Thiamine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Thiamine (Vitamin B1) is an essential water-soluble vitamin that plays a pivotal role in cellular energy metabolism. Its active form, thiamine pyrophosphate (TPP), is an obligate cofactor for pyruvate dehydrogenase (PDH), the enzyme complex that converts pyruvate to acetyl-CoA — the critical gateway between glycolysis and the TCA cycle. Without adequate thiamine, this gateway is effectively blocked, leading to pyruvate and lactate accumulation (lactic acidosis) and severely impaired ATP production.</p>
<p>TPP is also required by alpha-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase within the TCA cycle itself, making thiamine doubly essential for mitochondrial energy metabolism. Thiamine deficiency is implicated in several clinical conditions including Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome, beriberi, and PDH deficiency-related metabolic disorders.</p>
<p>Benfotiamine, a fat-soluble thiamine derivative, offers enhanced bioavailability and tissue penetration compared to water-soluble thiamine, making it particularly useful for conditions requiring higher intracellular thiamine concentrations.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 4/5</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Pyruvate Dehydrogenase Complex</h3>
<ul>
<li>TPP is the essential cofactor for the E1 subunit of PDH</li>
<li>PDH converts pyruvate to acetyl-CoA + CO2 + NADH</li>
<li>This is the critical gateway from glycolysis to the TCA cycle</li>
<li>Thiamine deficiency causes pyruvate accumulation, increased lactate, and energy failure</li>
</ul>
<h3>TCA Cycle</h3>
<ul>
<li>TPP is required by alpha-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase (converts alpha-KG to succinyl-CoA)</li>
<li>This reaction produces NADH and maintains TCA cycle flux</li>
<li>Dual requirement (PDH + alpha-KG DH) makes thiamine exceptionally critical for energy production</li>
</ul>
<h3>Pentose Phosphate Pathway</h3>
<ul>
<li>TPP is the cofactor for transketolase</li>
<li>Supports NADPH production (antioxidant defense) and ribose synthesis (nucleotides)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<h3>Standard Supplementation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>RDA</strong>: 1.1-1.2 mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Metabolic support</strong>: 100-300 mg/day thiamine HCl</li>
<li><strong>Enhanced form</strong>: Benfotiamine 300-600 mg/day (fat-soluble, 5x better bioavailability)</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: With food; B-vitamins best taken in the morning</li>
</ul>
