<h1>Glutathione</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Glutathione (GSH) is a tripeptide (gamma-glutamyl-cysteinyl-glycine) that serves as the body's most abundant and important intracellular antioxidant. Present at millimolar concentrations in virtually all cells, it plays an essential role in protecting mitochondria from the oxidative stress inherent to aerobic energy production. The electron transport chain inevitably generates reactive oxygen species (ROS) as a byproduct of electron transfer, and glutathione is the primary defense against this mitochondrial oxidative damage.</p>
<p>As a supplement, glutathione has historically had poor oral bioavailability due to degradation by intestinal and hepatic gamma-glutamyl transpeptidase. Liposomal encapsulation technology has significantly improved oral bioavailability, making direct glutathione supplementation viable. Alternatively, N-Acetyl Cysteine (NAC) provides the rate-limiting cysteine precursor for endogenous glutathione synthesis.</p>
<p>Note: This is the supplement entity. The biomarker entity (measuring blood glutathione levels) exists separately.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 3/5</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Direct Antioxidant Activity</h3>
<ul>
<li>Directly neutralizes superoxide radicals, hydroxyl radicals, and hydrogen peroxide</li>
<li>Reduces lipid peroxides via glutathione peroxidase (GPx)</li>
<li>Protects mitochondrial membranes from lipid peroxidation</li>
<li>Protects mitochondrial DNA from oxidative damage</li>
</ul>
<h3>Antioxidant Recycling</h3>
<ul>
<li>Regenerates oxidized Vitamin C (dehydroascorbate → ascorbate)</li>
<li>Regenerates oxidized Vitamin E (tocopheroxyl radical → tocopherol)</li>
<li>Creates an interconnected antioxidant network</li>
</ul>
<h3>Detoxification</h3>
<ul>
<li>Conjugates with xenobiotics, drugs, and toxins via glutathione S-transferases (GSTs)</li>
<li>Phase II detoxification in the liver</li>
<li>Protects against heavy metal toxicity</li>
</ul>
<h3>Redox Signaling</h3>
<ul>
<li>GSH/GSSG ratio is a key indicator of cellular redox state</li>
<li>Influences NF-kB, Nrf2, and other redox-sensitive transcription factors</li>
<li>Modulates immune cell function and inflammatory responses</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<h3>Standard Supplementation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Liposomal glutathione</strong>: 500-1000 mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Reduced glutathione (non-liposomal)</strong>: 500-1000 mg/day (lower bioavailability)</li>
<li><strong>Alternative</strong>: NAC 600-1800 mg/day as glutathione precursor</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: On empty stomach or between meals for better absorption</li>
</ul>
