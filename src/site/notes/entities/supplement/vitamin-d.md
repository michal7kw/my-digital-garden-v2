<h1>Vitamin D</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Vitamin D is unique among vitamins — it functions as a prohormone rather than a traditional vitamin. The body synthesizes it in skin upon UVB exposure, and its active form (calcitriol) acts as a steroid hormone, binding nuclear vitamin D receptors (VDR) expressed in virtually every human tissue. This explains its remarkably broad influence: beyond its classical role in calcium-phosphorus homeostasis and bone health, vitamin D modulates over 200 genes involved in immune function, cell cycle regulation, neuromuscular function, and inflammation. The VITAL trial (n=25,871) and other large RCTs have clarified that while vitamin D supplementation reduces cancer mortality and may reduce autoimmune disease incidence, it does not prevent cardiovascular events in vitamin D-replete individuals.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Skin synthesis</strong>: UVB radiation converts 7-dehydrocholesterol → pre-vitamin D3 → vitamin D3</li>
<li><strong>Liver hydroxylation</strong>: Vitamin D3 → 25(OH)D (calcidiol) by CYP2R1 — this is the form measured in blood tests</li>
<li><strong>Kidney activation</strong>: 25(OH)D → 1,25(OH)2D (calcitriol) by CYP27B1 — the active hormone</li>
<li><strong>VDR binding</strong>: Calcitriol binds VDR, heterodimerizes with RXR, regulates gene transcription</li>
</ol>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Maintenance</strong>: 1,000-2,000 IU/day (sufficient for most adults)</li>
<li><strong>Optimization</strong>: 4,000-5,000 IU/day (to reach 50-80 ng/mL target)</li>
<li><strong>Repletion</strong>: 5,000-10,000 IU/day for 8-12 weeks if deficient (&lt;20 ng/mL)</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: D3 (cholecalciferol) preferred — 87% more potent than D2 at raising 25(OH)D</li>
<li><strong>Administration</strong>: Take with largest fat-containing meal for 50% better absorption</li>
<li><strong>Cofactors</strong>: Vitamin K2 (prevents calcium misplacement), Magnesium (required for vitamin D metabolism)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Evidence</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>VITAL Trial</strong> (2019, n=25,871): No cardiovascular benefit, but 25% reduction in cancer mortality with long-term use</li>
<li><strong>VITAL Autoimmune</strong> (2022): 22% reduction in new autoimmune disease incidence</li>
<li><strong>Depression meta-analysis</strong>: Moderate benefit in deficient individuals (effect size 0.4)</li>
<li><strong>Fall prevention</strong>: 800-1000 IU/day reduces falls in elderly by 20% (Cochrane)</li>
<li><strong>COVID-19</strong>: Deficiency associated with worse outcomes; supplementation evidence mixed</li>
</ul>
