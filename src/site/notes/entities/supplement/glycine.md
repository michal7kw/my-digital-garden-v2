<h1>Glycine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Glycine is the simplest and smallest amino acid, yet one of the most versatile: it is a structural component of collagen (every third residue), a precursor to glutathione (the body's master antioxidant), an inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brainstem and spinal cord, a co-agonist at NMDA receptors, and a key substrate for creatine and heme synthesis. Recent research highlights glycine's role in longevity — it extends lifespan in model organisms and reverses epigenetic age in human cell studies. Its sleep-promoting effects (3g before bed) occur via body temperature reduction through peripheral vasodilation.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Glycine acts through multiple pathways: (1) collagen synthesis — required for structural integrity of skin, joints, bones, blood vessels (every third amino acid in collagen is glycine), (2) glutathione precursor — combines with cysteine and glutamate to form GSH; glycine is often the rate-limiting substrate in elderly, (3) inhibitory neurotransmission — activates glycine receptors in brainstem/spinal cord (calming, pain modulation), (4) NMDA co-agonism — required for NMDA receptor activation at the glycine binding site (memory, learning), (5) thermoregulation — 3g oral glycine reduces core body temperature 0.1-0.3°C via NMDA receptors in the suprachiasmatic nucleus, promoting sleep onset, (6) anti-inflammatory — reduces TNF-alpha secretion from macrophages via glycine-gated chloride channels, (7) methyl group metabolism — glycine cleavage system is the major source of one-carbon units.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Sleep</strong>: 3g 30-60 minutes before bed (well-established in RCTs)</li>
<li><strong>Collagen/joint support</strong>: 5-15g/day (often as collagen peptides)</li>
<li><strong>Glutathione support (with NAC)</strong>: 3-5g/day glycine + 600-1200mg NAC (GlyNAC protocol for aging — Baylor study)</li>
<li><strong>General health</strong>: 3-5g/day</li>
<li><strong>Safety</strong>: Excellent — generally recognized as safe (GRAS). Sweet taste makes powder easy to take</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Before bed for sleep; any time for other benefits</li>
<li><strong>Sources</strong>: Bone broth, collagen, gelatin, meat, fish; can be supplemented as pure powder</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Research Summary:</strong> Well-established for sleep quality improvement through thermoregulation. Emerging research on metabolic health, methylation, and longevity. Remarkably safe at high doses with sweet taste.</p>
