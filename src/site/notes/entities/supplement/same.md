<h1>SAMe</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>SAMe (S-Adenosyl-L-methionine) is the body's principal methyl donor, participating in over 100 methyltransferase reactions that are critical for neurotransmitter synthesis, DNA/histone methylation, phospholipid production, and cartilage proteoglycan formation. Synthesized from methionine and ATP, SAMe sits at the intersection of three major metabolic pathways: transmethylation (neurotransmitters, epigenetics), transsulfuration (glutathione production), and aminopropylation (polyamine synthesis for cell growth).</p>
<p>SAMe has strong clinical evidence for three distinct applications: depression (multiple RCTs show efficacy comparable to tricyclic antidepressants with faster onset of 1-2 weeks), osteoarthritis (comparable pain relief to NSAIDs like celecoxib without GI toxicity), and liver disease (supports hepatic glutathione levels and bile flow in NAFLD, alcoholic liver disease, and cholestasis). In Europe, SAMe is a registered pharmaceutical drug. As a supplement, it is inherently unstable and requires enteric-coated, individually blister-packed tablets stored away from heat and moisture. Adequate B12 and folate are essential cofactors for recycling SAMe through the methionine cycle.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>SAMe is synthesized from methionine and ATP by methionine adenosyltransferase. As the principal methyl donor in over 100 methyltransferase reactions, SAMe is critical for three major pathways: (1) <strong>Neurotransmitter synthesis</strong> — SAMe donates methyl groups for the conversion of norepinephrine to epinephrine (PNMT), and supports serotonin and dopamine metabolism through methylation of catechol-O-methyltransferase (COMT) substrates. It also maintains tetrahydrobiopterin (BH4) levels, a cofactor for both tyrosine hydroxylase and tryptophan hydroxylase. (2) <strong>Transsulfuration</strong> — after donating its methyl group, SAMe becomes S-adenosylhomocysteine, then homocysteine, which is converted to cysteine and ultimately glutathione, the master intracellular antioxidant. (3) <strong>Polyamine synthesis</strong> — SAMe is decarboxylated to provide aminopropyl groups for spermidine and spermine synthesis, essential for cell growth, DNA stabilization, and cartilage proteoglycan production. The antidepressant effect involves all three pathways: improved neurotransmitter methylation, enhanced glutathione-mediated neuroprotection, and neuronal membrane fluidity via phospholipid methylation.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Depression</strong>: 400-800 mg daily (start at 200 mg, titrate up)</li>
<li><strong>Osteoarthritis</strong>: 600-1200 mg daily</li>
<li><strong>Liver support / NAFLD</strong>: 800-1200 mg daily</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: SAMe tosylate disulfate (most stable); enteric-coated tablets essential</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: On empty stomach, 30-60 minutes before meals; morning and early afternoon only</li>
<li><strong>Cofactors</strong>: Requires B12 and folate for recycling via the methionine cycle</li>
<li><strong>Caution</strong>: Avoid in bipolar disorder (may trigger mania); do not combine with MAOIs or SSRIs without supervision</li>
</ul>
