<h1>Manganese</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Manganese is an essential trace mineral with a critical role in mitochondrial antioxidant defense. Its most important function is as the catalytic cofactor for manganese superoxide dismutase (MnSOD/SOD2), the primary enzyme responsible for neutralizing superoxide radicals within the mitochondrial matrix. Since the electron transport chain generates superoxide as an unavoidable byproduct of oxidative phosphorylation, MnSOD is essential for protecting mitochondrial components from oxidative damage.</p>
<p>Manganese is required in only trace amounts (2-5 mg/day), and deficiency is rare in the general population. However, suboptimal manganese status can impair MnSOD activity, potentially increasing mitochondrial oxidative stress. Conversely, excess manganese is neurotoxic (manganism), so supplementation should be conservative.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 3/5</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Mitochondrial Antioxidant Defense</h3>
<ul>
<li>MnSOD (SOD2) catalyzes: O2•- + O2•- + 2H+ → H2O2 + O2</li>
<li>This is the first line of defense against mitochondrial superoxide</li>
<li>H2O2 is then further detoxified by glutathione peroxidase and catalase</li>
<li>MnSOD is exclusively localized to the mitochondrial matrix</li>
</ul>
<h3>Metabolic Enzymes</h3>
<ul>
<li>Pyruvate carboxylase (gluconeogenesis — converts pyruvate to oxaloacetate)</li>
<li>Arginase (urea cycle — final step converting arginine to urea + ornithine)</li>
<li>Glutamine synthetase (ammonia detoxification in the brain)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Bone and Connective Tissue</h3>
<ul>
<li>Required by glycosyltransferases for proteoglycan synthesis</li>
<li>Supports bone mineralization and cartilage formation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<h3>Standard Supplementation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Adequate Intake (AI)</strong>: 1.8-2.3 mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Upper Limit (UL)</strong>: 11 mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Supplement dose</strong>: 2-5 mg/day (often included in multiminerals)</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Manganese bisglycinate, manganese gluconate</li>
<li><strong>Caution</strong>: Do NOT mega-dose — neurotoxic at high chronic exposure</li>
</ul>
