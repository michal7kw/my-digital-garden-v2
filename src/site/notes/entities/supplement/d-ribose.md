<h1>D-Ribose</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>D-Ribose is a naturally occurring five-carbon sugar (pentose) that forms the structural backbone of adenine nucleotides including ATP, ADP, and AMP. While the body can synthesize ribose endogenously via the pentose phosphate pathway, this process is slow and rate-limited by glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase. Supplemental D-Ribose bypasses this bottleneck, directly feeding into the purine salvage pathway to accelerate ATP regeneration.</p>
<p>D-Ribose supplementation is particularly relevant in conditions of cellular energy depletion, including ischemia-reperfusion injury (heart surgery, heart failure), chronic fatigue syndrome, fibromyalgia, and intense exercise recovery. Studies in cardiac patients show that D-Ribose can restore myocardial ATP levels to normal within 12-72 hours, compared to days or weeks without supplementation.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 3/5</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>ATP Regeneration</h3>
<ul>
<li>Provides ribose-5-phosphate directly to the purine salvage pathway</li>
<li>Bypasses the slow, rate-limiting oxidative phase of the pentose phosphate pathway</li>
<li>Accelerates de novo synthesis of adenine nucleotides (AMP → ADP → ATP)</li>
<li>Particularly effective when the total adenine nucleotide pool is depleted (not just ATP/ADP ratio)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Cardiac Energy Support</h3>
<ul>
<li>Ischemic myocardium loses adenine nucleotides through degradation to inosine and hypoxanthine</li>
<li>These are washed out of the cell, causing a net loss of the adenine nucleotide pool</li>
<li>D-Ribose provides the rate-limiting substrate for rebuilding this pool</li>
<li>Clinical studies show improved diastolic function and exercise tolerance</li>
</ul>
<h3>Exercise Recovery</h3>
<ul>
<li>High-intensity exercise can deplete muscle ATP by 20-30%</li>
<li>Full ATP recovery without supplementation takes 24-72 hours</li>
<li>D-Ribose supplementation can accelerate recovery of ATP pools</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<h3>Standard Supplementation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>General support</strong>: 5 g/day</li>
<li><strong>Energy depletion / chronic fatigue</strong>: 10-15 g/day in divided doses</li>
<li><strong>Cardiac patients</strong>: 10-15 g/day (under medical supervision)</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Powder dissolved in water or juice (not heat-stable)</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Divide into 2-3 doses; take with meals to reduce hypoglycemia risk</li>
</ul>
<h3>Important Notes</h3>
<ul>
<li>May lower blood glucose — diabetics should monitor carefully</li>
<li>Effects are dose-dependent; higher doses needed for severe depletion</li>
<li>Best absorbed on an empty stomach but take with food if hypoglycemia occurs</li>
</ul>
