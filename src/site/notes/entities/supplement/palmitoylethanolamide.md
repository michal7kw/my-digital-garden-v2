<h1>Palmitoylethanolamide</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Endogenous fatty acid amide produced naturally in the body as part of the endocannabinoid system. Acts primarily through PPAR-alpha activation and indirect cannabinoid receptor modulation. Clinically studied for chronic pain, neuroinflammation, and neuroprotection. Available as a supplement in micronized (m-PEA) and ultra-micronized (um-PEA) forms for enhanced bioavailability.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5)</p>
<p><strong>Research Summary:</strong> Over 600 publications. Multiple RCTs support efficacy in chronic pain (sciatic, pelvic, neuropathic), with effect sizes comparable to NSAIDs. Evidence for neuroprotection in animal models of Alzheimer's and Parkinson's. Anti-inflammatory effects well-documented through PPAR-alpha pathway. Generally regarded as safe with no serious adverse events in clinical trials up to 1200mg/day for 60 days.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Activates PPAR-alpha nuclear receptor, reducing NF-kB-mediated inflammatory gene expression. Indirectly enhances endocannabinoid signaling via the &quot;entourage effect&quot; — inhibits FAAH enzyme, increasing anandamide levels. Downregulates mast cell activation and microglial reactivity. Modulates TLR4 signaling.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<p>300-1200mg daily, typically 600mg twice daily. Ultra-micronized form preferred for bioavailability.</p>
