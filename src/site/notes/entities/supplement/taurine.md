<h1>Taurine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Taurine is a conditionally essential sulfur-containing amino acid and the most abundant free amino acid in the heart, retina, brain, and white blood cells. A landmark 2023 Science paper demonstrated that taurine deficiency drives aging in mice, and supplementation extended lifespan by 10-12% while improving healthspan across every organ system. Taurine levels decline 80% with age in humans. It is not incorporated into proteins but acts as a free intracellular osmolyte, membrane stabilizer, and signaling molecule.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Taurine functions through multiple pathways: (1) bile acid conjugation — forms taurocholate for fat digestion and cholesterol elimination, (2) osmoregulation — protects cells from swelling/shrinking (critical in brain and heart), (3) calcium signaling modulation — stabilizes cardiomyocyte calcium handling (antiarrhythmic), (4) mitochondrial function — required for mitochondrial tRNA modification (taurine modification of mt-tRNA), (5) antioxidant — scavenges hypochlorous acid (HOCl) producing taurine chloramine (anti-inflammatory), (6) GABAergic activity — weak GABA-A receptor agonist (anxiolytic/calming), (7) anti-aging — reverses DNA hypermethylation patterns associated with aging (2023 Science paper).</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General health</strong>: 500-2000 mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular</strong>: 1000-3000 mg/day (heart failure studies)</li>
<li><strong>Exercise performance</strong>: 1000-3000 mg 1 hour before exercise</li>
<li><strong>Longevity (based on mouse study extrapolation)</strong>: 1000-3000 mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Safety</strong>: Extremely safe — no reported adverse effects up to 6g/day in clinical trials</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Can be taken any time; often combined with magnesium for calming effect</li>
<li><strong>Sources</strong>: Abundant in meat, fish, shellfish; absent from plant foods (vegans are typically deficient)</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Research Summary:</strong> 2023 Science paper showed taurine deficiency as driver of aging; supplementation extended lifespan in mice. Strong cardiovascular benefits including blood pressure reduction. Neuroprotective effects well-documented.</p>
