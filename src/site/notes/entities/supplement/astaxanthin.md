<h1>Astaxanthin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Powerful carotenoid antioxidant derived from microalgae (Haematococcus pluvialis). One of nature's strongest antioxidants - 6000x more potent than vitamin C. Protects skin, eyes, and cardiovascular system.</p>
<p><strong>Research Summary:</strong> Strong evidence for skin protection (UV damage, elasticity, hydration), eye health (eye strain, macular protection), and cardiovascular benefits (lipid oxidation). Also shows promise for exercise recovery and cognitive function.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Astaxanthin is a xanthophyll carotenoid with unique membrane-spanning antioxidant activity. Unlike most antioxidants that work in either the lipid or aqueous phase, astaxanthin spans the entire cell membrane bilayer, quenching reactive oxygen species (ROS) on both surfaces simultaneously. It neutralizes singlet oxygen with 6000x the potency of vitamin C and 550x that of vitamin E. Astaxanthin inhibits NF-kB signaling and COX-2 expression, reducing inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-1beta, IL-6). It protects mitochondrial membranes from oxidative damage, preserving electron transport chain function. In the eye, it accumulates in the retina and ciliary body, reducing oxidative stress from UV and blue light exposure.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General antioxidant</strong>: 4-8 mg daily</li>
<li><strong>Skin protection</strong>: 4-12 mg daily (clinical studies typically use 4-6 mg)</li>
<li><strong>Eye health</strong>: 6-12 mg daily</li>
<li><strong>Exercise recovery</strong>: 12 mg daily</li>
<li><strong>Administration</strong>: Take with a fat-containing meal for optimal absorption</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Natural astaxanthin from Haematococcus pluvialis preferred over synthetic</li>
</ul>
