<h1>Quercetin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Quercetin is the most abundant dietary flavonoid (found in onions, apples, berries, capers, green tea) with potent anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antihistamine, and senolytic properties. It inhibits mast cell degranulation (natural antihistamine — relevant for MCAS and allergies), activates Nrf2 (antioxidant defense), and when combined with dasatinib, acts as a senolytic agent (selectively kills senescent cells — one of the most promising anti-aging interventions). Native quercetin has poor bioavailability (~2%); phytosome forms dramatically improve absorption.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Quercetin acts on multiple targets: (1) inhibits NF-kB and COX-2 (anti-inflammatory), (2) stabilizes mast cells — inhibits histamine, prostaglandin D2, and leukotriene release (natural antihistamine), (3) activates Nrf2/ARE pathway (upregulates glutathione, SOD), (4) inhibits xanthine oxidase (reduces uric acid — relevant to gout), (5) senolytic activity — induces apoptosis in senescent cells by inhibiting pro-survival BCL-2 family proteins, (6) antiviral — inhibits viral polymerases and entry (zinc ionophore properties).</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>General antioxidant</strong>: 500 mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Mast cell stabilization (MCAS/allergies)</strong>: 500 mg 2-3x/day</li>
<li><strong>Senolytic (intermittent)</strong>: Quercetin 1000 mg + Dasatinib 100 mg — 2 days on, then off for weeks (Mayo Clinic protocol)</li>
<li><strong>Quercetin phytosome</strong>: 250-500 mg/day (20x better absorption than standard)</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Take with fat-containing meal; vitamin C enhances absorption</li>
<li><strong>Caution</strong>: May interact with some antibiotics (fluoroquinolones) and cyclosporine</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Research Summary:</strong> Extensive research supports anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and anti-allergic effects. Emerging evidence for senolytic (killing senescent cells) and antiviral properties. Poor bioavailability improved by phytosome formulations.</p>
