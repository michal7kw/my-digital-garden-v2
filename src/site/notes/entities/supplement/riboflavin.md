<h1>Riboflavin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Riboflavin (Vitamin B2) is an essential water-soluble vitamin that serves as the precursor to two critical coenzymes: flavin mononucleotide (FMN) and flavin adenine dinucleotide (FAD). These flavin coenzymes participate in over 80 enzymatic reactions in human metabolism, with their most critical roles in the mitochondrial electron transport chain.</p>
<p>FAD is the prosthetic group of Complex II (succinate dehydrogenase), directly linking the TCA cycle to the ETC. FMN is essential for Complex I (NADH:ubiquinone oxidoreductase), the largest complex of the respiratory chain. Without adequate riboflavin, both complexes function suboptimally, directly impairing ATP production.</p>
<p>Beyond energy metabolism, riboflavin is essential for glutathione reductase (which regenerates reduced glutathione from oxidized glutathione), making it critical for antioxidant defense. High-dose riboflavin (400 mg/day) has demonstrated efficacy in migraine prevention, likely through improved mitochondrial energy metabolism in neurons.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 4/5</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Electron Transport Chain</h3>
<ul>
<li>FMN is the prosthetic group of Complex I (NADH dehydrogenase), accepting electrons from NADH</li>
<li>FAD is the prosthetic group of Complex II (succinate dehydrogenase), accepting electrons from succinate</li>
<li>Both transfer electrons to ubiquinone (CoQ10), maintaining electron flow through the ETC</li>
<li>Riboflavin deficiency directly impairs oxidative phosphorylation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Antioxidant Support</h3>
<ul>
<li>FAD is required by glutathione reductase to regenerate reduced glutathione (GSH) from oxidized glutathione (GSSG)</li>
<li>Maintains the glutathione antioxidant defense system</li>
<li>Indirectly protects mitochondrial membranes from oxidative damage</li>
</ul>
<h3>Additional Enzymatic Roles</h3>
<ul>
<li>FAD/FMN required for fatty acid beta-oxidation (acyl-CoA dehydrogenases)</li>
<li>Required for amino acid metabolism (D-amino acid oxidase)</li>
<li>Required for folate metabolism (MTHFR uses FAD as cofactor)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<h3>Standard Supplementation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>RDA</strong>: 1.1-1.3 mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Mitochondrial support</strong>: 100-400 mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Migraine prevention</strong>: 400 mg/day (most studied dose)</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Riboflavin or riboflavin-5'-phosphate (active form, better absorbed)</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: With food for optimal absorption</li>
</ul>
