<h1>5-HTP</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>5-HTP (5-hydroxytryptophan) is the immediate biosynthetic precursor to serotonin, the neurotransmitter central to mood regulation, sleep-wake cycles, and appetite control. Derived commercially from the seeds of the African plant <em>Griffonia simplicifolia</em>, 5-HTP crosses the blood-brain barrier freely without competition from other amino acids, making it more efficient at raising brain serotonin levels than its upstream precursor L-tryptophan.</p>
<p>Clinical trials have demonstrated benefits for mild-to-moderate depression, insomnia, fibromyalgia-related pain, and appetite suppression. However, 5-HTP must be used with caution: co-administration with serotonergic medications (SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs, triptans) poses a risk of serotonin syndrome. Long-term use without adequate dopamine precursor support may deplete catecholamines, as 5-HTP monopolizes the shared aromatic L-amino acid decarboxylase (AADC) enzyme. Vitamin B6 is an essential cofactor for serotonin conversion.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>5-HTP (5-hydroxytryptophan) is the immediate biosynthetic precursor to serotonin (5-HT). It is produced naturally from L-tryptophan by tryptophan hydroxylase and then converted to serotonin by aromatic L-amino acid decarboxylase (AADC), requiring vitamin B6 (pyridoxal phosphate) as a cofactor. Unlike tryptophan, 5-HTP readily crosses the blood-brain barrier without competition from other large neutral amino acids. Supplemental 5-HTP bypasses the rate-limiting tryptophan hydroxylase step, directly increasing serotonin synthesis in both the brain (mood, sleep) and the gut (motility, satiety). Serotonin is further converted to melatonin in the pineal gland, supporting sleep-wake regulation.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mood support</strong>: 50-100 mg, 2-3 times daily (150-300 mg total)</li>
<li><strong>Sleep</strong>: 100-300 mg, 30-45 minutes before bed</li>
<li><strong>Appetite regulation</strong>: 250-300 mg before meals</li>
<li><strong>Start low</strong>: Begin at 50 mg/day and titrate up to assess tolerance</li>
<li><strong>Cofactor</strong>: Ensure adequate vitamin B6 for conversion to serotonin</li>
<li><strong>Caution</strong>: Do not combine with SSRIs, SNRIs, or MAOIs due to serotonin syndrome risk</li>
</ul>
