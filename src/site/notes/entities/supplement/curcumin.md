<h1>Curcumin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Curcumin is the principal curcuminoid from turmeric (Curcuma longa), one of the most studied natural anti-inflammatory compounds with 15,000+ published papers. It inhibits NF-kB (the master inflammatory transcription factor), COX-2, LOX, and multiple inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-1, IL-6). Native curcumin has extremely poor bioavailability (&lt;1% absorption) — enhanced formulations are essential. Head-to-head trials show comparable efficacy to ibuprofen for knee osteoarthritis pain with better GI safety.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Curcumin is a pleiotropic molecule acting on 60+ molecular targets: (1) inhibits NF-kB translocation to nucleus (anti-inflammatory master switch), (2) inhibits COX-2 and 5-LOX (prostaglandin/leukotriene reduction), (3) activates Nrf2 (antioxidant response element — upregulates glutathione, SOD, catalase), (4) inhibits mTOR (potential anti-cancer, pro-autophagy), (5) modulates gut microbiome composition, (6) chelates metal ions, (7) crosses BBB in enhanced forms (neuroprotective).</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Standard curcumin</strong>: 500-2000 mg/day (poor absorption — must use enhanced form)</li>
<li><strong>Meriva (phytosome)</strong>: 500-1000 mg/day (29x better absorption)</li>
<li><strong>Longvida</strong>: 400 mg/day (65x bioavailability, specifically targets brain)</li>
<li><strong>C3 Complex + BioPerine</strong>: 1000-1500 mg + 5-10 mg piperine (20x absorption boost)</li>
<li><strong>Theracurmin</strong>: 90-180 mg/day (nanoparticle — 27x absorption)</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Take with fat-containing meal for all forms</li>
<li><strong>Caution</strong>: May interact with anticoagulants (antiplatelet effect); avoid 2 weeks before surgery</li>
</ul>
