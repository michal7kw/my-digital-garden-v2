<h1>Vitamin E</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Fat-soluble antioxidant family consisting of tocopherols and tocotrienols. Protects cell membranes from oxidative damage. Full-spectrum forms preferred over synthetic alpha-tocopherol.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5)</p>
<p><strong>Research Summary:</strong> Essential antioxidant for membrane protection. High-dose synthetic alpha-tocopherol may have negative effects; natural mixed tocopherols/tocotrienols preferred. Important synergy with vitamin C which regenerates oxidized vitamin E.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Lipid-soluble chain-breaking antioxidant protecting polyunsaturated fatty acids in cell membranes. Terminates lipid peroxidation chain reactions. Tocotrienols may inhibit HMG-CoA reductase. Modulates gene expression and enzyme activity.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<p>100-400 IU daily of mixed tocopherols</p>
