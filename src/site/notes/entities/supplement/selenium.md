<h1>Selenium</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Selenium is an essential trace mineral incorporated into 25 selenoproteins as the amino acid selenocysteine (the &quot;21st amino acid&quot;). The most critical selenoprotein families are glutathione peroxidases (GPx1-4, antioxidant defense against hydrogen peroxide and lipid hydroperoxides), thioredoxin reductases (redox regulation, DNA synthesis), and iodothyronine deiodinases (DIO1-3, thyroid hormone activation converting T4 to active T3). The thyroid gland contains more selenium per gram than any other tissue, underscoring selenium's importance for thyroid function.</p>
<p>Selenium deficiency impairs all three selenoprotein systems simultaneously, leading to increased oxidative stress, impaired immune function, and hypothyroid symptoms. In Hashimoto's thyroiditis, multiple RCTs demonstrate that 200 mcg/day selenium supplementation reduces thyroid peroxidase (TPO) antibodies by 20-40% and improves thyroid ultrasound appearance. Selenium also plays a role in cancer prevention (NPC trial showed reduced prostate, lung, and colorectal cancer risk), immune defense (enhances NK cell activity and T-cell proliferation), and male fertility (selenoprotein P is critical for testicular selenium delivery). Brazil nuts are the richest food source, with 1-2 nuts providing approximately 100-200 mcg, though content is highly variable depending on soil selenium levels.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Selenium is incorporated as selenocysteine (the &quot;21st amino acid&quot;) at the active sites of selenoproteins: (1) Glutathione peroxidases (GPx1-4) — reduce hydrogen peroxide and lipid hydroperoxides, protecting cells from oxidative damage, (2) Thioredoxin reductases — regenerate reduced thioredoxin for DNA synthesis and redox signaling, (3) Deiodinases (DIO1-3) — convert T4 to active T3 (DIO1/2) or inactivate T3 to reverse T3 (DIO3), (4) Selenoprotein P — selenium transport and brain/testis selenium delivery.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>RDA</strong>: 55 mcg/day</li>
<li><strong>Thyroid support (Hashimoto's)</strong>: 200 mcg/day (multiple RCTs — reduces TPO antibodies 20-40%)</li>
<li><strong>Cancer prevention</strong>: 200 mcg/day (NPC trial — reduced prostate, lung, colon cancer; SELECT trial with selenomethionine was negative)</li>
<li><strong>Upper limit</strong>: 400 mcg/day (toxicity: selenosis — garlic breath, hair/nail loss, GI disturbance)</li>
<li><strong>Forms</strong>: Selenomethionine (organic, food-form), sodium selenite (inorganic, used in some studies), selenium yeast (mixed organic forms)</li>
<li><strong>Brazil nuts</strong>: 1-2 per day provides ~100-200 mcg (highly variable)</li>
</ul>
