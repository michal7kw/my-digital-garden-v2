<h1>Creatine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Creatine is the most extensively researched sports supplement in history, with over 500 peer-reviewed studies establishing its safety and efficacy. It is a naturally occurring compound synthesized from arginine, glycine, and methionine, and stored primarily in skeletal muscle (~95%) with the remaining ~5% in the brain, testes, and kidneys.</p>
<p>Beyond its well-established role in athletic performance (strength, power, sprint capacity), creatine is increasingly recognized for its cognitive benefits. The brain accounts for ~20% of the body's energy expenditure despite being only ~2% of body mass, making it highly dependent on the phosphocreatine energy buffer system. Supplementation increases brain creatine levels by 5-10%, supporting cognitive performance particularly under conditions of metabolic stress — sleep deprivation, mental fatigue, hypoxia, and aging.</p>
<p>Creatine monohydrate is the gold standard form, with the most evidence and the lowest cost. Despite decades of claims about newer forms (HCL, ethyl ester, buffered), none have demonstrated superiority to monohydrate in controlled studies. Creatine has one of the strongest safety profiles of any supplement, with studies up to 5 years showing no adverse effects on kidney, liver, or cardiovascular function in healthy individuals.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 5/5</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Phosphocreatine Energy System</h3>
<ul>
<li>Creatine kinase (CK) catalyzes the reversible transfer of a phosphate group between creatine and ATP</li>
<li><strong>ATP → ADP + Pi</strong> (energy use) is immediately followed by <strong>PCr + ADP → ATP + Cr</strong> (ATP regeneration)</li>
<li>This system provides ATP within 2-7 seconds, far faster than glycolysis or oxidative phosphorylation</li>
<li>Particularly critical during high-intensity, short-duration activities (sprinting, lifting, rapid cognitive processing)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Brain Energy Buffer</h3>
<ul>
<li>The brain's creatine-phosphocreatine system acts as a <strong>temporal buffer</strong>: bridging the gap between sudden ATP demand spikes and slower mitochondrial ATP production</li>
<li>Also acts as a <strong>spatial shuttle</strong>: PCr is synthesized at mitochondria and diffuses to sites of ATP consumption (synapses, ion pumps), effectively transporting energy across the cell</li>
<li>Under metabolic stress (sleep deprivation, hypoxia, aging), the PCr pool depletes faster — supplementation maintains this reserve</li>
</ul>
<h3>Neuroprotective Effects</h3>
<ul>
<li>Maintains cellular energy during ischemic events and traumatic brain injury (TBI)</li>
<li>Reduces excitotoxicity by supporting ATP-dependent ion pumps that maintain membrane potential</li>
<li>May reduce oxidative stress through direct antioxidant effects on peroxynitrite and superoxide</li>
<li>Supports mitochondrial membrane potential integrity</li>
</ul>
<h3>Additional Mechanisms</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Osmolyte</strong>: Draws water into cells, supporting cellular hydration and volume regulation</li>
<li><strong>Methylation</strong>: Endogenous creatine synthesis consumes ~40% of SAMe-derived methyl groups; supplementation spares methylation capacity, potentially reducing homocysteine</li>
<li><strong>Anti-inflammatory</strong>: Emerging evidence suggests creatine may reduce markers of inflammation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Cognitive Benefits</h2>
<h3>Sleep Deprivation and Mental Fatigue</h3>
<ul>
<li>Creatine supplementation (20 g/day for 7 days) significantly improved performance on complex cognitive tasks during 24 hours of sleep deprivation — mood, balance, and executive function were maintained vs. placebo decline</li>
<li>The effect is attributed to maintaining brain PCr levels that normally deplete during sustained wakefulness</li>
</ul>
<h3>Stress and Hypoxia</h3>
<ul>
<li>Improved cognitive performance during simulated altitude stress and oxygen deprivation</li>
<li>Brain PCr acts as a metabolic reserve during conditions that impair oxidative phosphorylation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Vegetarian / Low-Creatine Populations</h3>
<ul>
<li>Vegetarians and vegans have lower baseline brain creatine levels (no dietary creatine from meat)</li>
<li>Show greater cognitive improvement from supplementation than omnivores — up to 50% improvement on memory tests</li>
<li>Suggests that baseline creatine status is a moderating factor for cognitive benefit</li>
</ul>
<h3>Depression</h3>
<ul>
<li>Emerging evidence as an adjunctive treatment for major depressive disorder</li>
<li>May work through restored brain bioenergetics and enhanced phospholipid metabolism in prefrontal cortex</li>
<li>Two RCTs showed significant improvement when creatine was added to SSRI treatment in women with treatment-resistant depression</li>
</ul>
<h3>Aging and Neurodegeneration</h3>
<ul>
<li>May slow age-related cognitive decline by maintaining brain energy metabolism</li>
<li>Studied in Huntington's disease, Parkinson's disease, and ALS models with positive preclinical results</li>
<li>Neuroprotective in TBI animal models — reduces cortical damage when administered pre- or post-injury</li>
</ul>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<h3>Maintenance (Recommended)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Standard</strong>: 3-5 g/day creatine monohydrate</li>
<li><strong>Cognitive-specific</strong>: 5 g/day (some studies use higher doses short-term)</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Post-workout (slight preference) or any consistent time; with food or shake</li>
</ul>
<h3>Loading Phase (Optional)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Protocol</strong>: 20 g/day (4 x 5 g) for 5-7 days, then 3-5 g/day maintenance</li>
<li><strong>Benefit</strong>: Saturates muscle and brain stores in 1 week vs. 3-4 weeks at maintenance dose</li>
<li><strong>Drawback</strong>: May cause GI discomfort and water retention; not necessary for long-term users</li>
</ul>
<h3>Form and Quality</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Creatine Monohydrate</strong>: Gold standard — most studied, cheapest, proven effective</li>
<li><strong>Micronized Monohydrate</strong>: Same compound, smaller particles for better mixing</li>
<li><strong>Creapure</strong>: German-manufactured brand with high purity standards</li>
<li><strong>Avoid</strong>: Creatine ethyl ester (converts to creatinine); liquid creatine (degrades rapidly)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Research</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Brain function comprehensive review</strong>: Systematic review of creatine's effects on brain bioenergetics, cognitive function, and neuroprotection — brain PCr increases 5-10% with supplementation (PMID:29704637)</li>
<li><strong>Cognitive function meta-analysis</strong>: Meta-analysis of 6 RCTs found creatine supplementation improved short-term memory and reasoning, with larger effects in stressed and elderly populations (PMID:30058670)</li>
<li><strong>Depression adjunct therapy</strong>: Creatine (5 g/day) augmented SSRI treatment in women with treatment-resistant major depression, with significant improvement in HAM-D scores within 2 weeks (PMID:31370849)</li>
<li><strong>Vegetarian cognition</strong>: Creatine supplementation (5 g/day for 6 weeks) improved memory and intelligence test scores in vegetarians, with effects larger than typically seen in omnivores (PMID:12945830)</li>
<li><strong>Long-term safety</strong>: 5-year prospective study found no adverse effects of creatine supplementation (up to 10 g/day) on kidney function, liver enzymes, or lipid profiles in healthy individuals (PMID:29533715)</li>
</ul>
