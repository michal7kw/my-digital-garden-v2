<h1>Vitamin B12</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Essential water-soluble vitamin critical for nerve function, DNA synthesis, red blood cell formation, and methylation. Deficiency is common, especially in vegetarians/vegans and older adults.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Cofactor for methionine synthase (methylation) and methylmalonyl-CoA mutase (energy metabolism). Essential for myelin synthesis, DNA production, and homocysteine metabolism.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<p>500-2000 mcg daily; higher doses for deficiency</p>

<h3>Serum B12</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Effect:</strong> Increasess this biomarker</li>
<li><strong>Magnitude:</strong> strong (200-800 pg/mL increase)</li>
<li><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)</li>
<li><strong>Population:</strong> general</li>
<li><strong>Time to Effect:</strong> 2-4 weeks</li>
<li><strong>Research:</strong> <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23325609">PMID:23325609</a>, <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24667752">PMID:24667752</a></li>
<li><strong>Notes:</strong> Direct supplementation of measured biomarker. Methylcobalamin and cyanocobalamin equally effective orally.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Homocysteine</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Effect:</strong> Decreasess this biomarker</li>
<li><strong>Magnitude:</strong> moderate (15-30% reduction)</li>
<li><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4/5)</li>
<li><strong>Population:</strong> elevated homocysteine</li>
<li><strong>Time to Effect:</strong> 4-8 weeks</li>
<li><strong>Research:</strong> <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16522904">PMID:16522904</a>, <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22493132">PMID:22493132</a></li>
<li><strong>Notes:</strong> B12 is cofactor for methionine synthase. Effect enhanced with concurrent folate. Most effective in B12-deficient individuals.</li>
</ul>
<h3>MMA</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Effect:</strong> Decreasess this biomarker</li>
<li><strong>Magnitude:</strong> strong (50-90% reduction)</li>
<li><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (5/5)</li>
<li><strong>Population:</strong> B12 deficient</li>
<li><strong>Time to Effect:</strong> 2-4 weeks</li>
<li><strong>Research:</strong> <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23325609">PMID:23325609</a>, <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12190251">PMID:12190251</a></li>

</ul>
