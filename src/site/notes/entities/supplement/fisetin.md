<h1>Fisetin</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A plant flavonol with potent senolytic activity — selectively kills senescent cells by inhibiting pro-survival PI3K/AKT/mTOR and BCL-2/BCL-xL pathways. Found in strawberries, apples, persimmons. Also neuroprotective (BDNF induction, anti-inflammatory) and anti-cancer. Most potent natural senolytic identified in screening of 10 flavonoids (Yousefzadeh 2018).</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5)</p>
<p><strong>Research Summary:</strong> Most potent natural senolytic in Mayo Clinic screening (Yousefzadeh 2018). Extended median and maximum lifespan in aged mice. Reduced senescence markers, SASP, and inflammation. Multiple ongoing human clinical trials including AFFIRM (NCT03675724) for frailty. Neuroprotective in multiple AD/PD models.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Multi-target senolytic — inhibits PI3K/AKT, mTOR, and BCL-2/BCL-xL pro-survival pathways in senescent cells. Also activates Nrf2 antioxidant defense, inhibits NF-kB, induces BDNF, and modulates MAPK/ERK signaling. Blocks SASP production.</p>
<h2>Dosage</h2>
<p>Senolytic protocol 20mg/kg for 2 days per month; daily neuroprotective 100-500mg</p>
