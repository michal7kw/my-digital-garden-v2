<h1>Chondroitin Sulfate</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Chondroitin sulfate is a sulfated glycosaminoglycan (GAG) composed of alternating chains of N-acetylgalactosamine and glucuronic acid. It is a major structural component of the extracellular matrix of articular cartilage, where it provides compressive resistance through its capacity to retain water. Endogenous chondroitin sulfate production declines with age, contributing to cartilage degradation in osteoarthritis.</p>
<p>Beyond its structural role, chondroitin sulfate exerts meaningful anti-inflammatory and chondroprotective effects. It inhibits NF-kB and AP-1 transcription factor activation, suppresses IL-1beta-induced catabolic pathways in chondrocytes, and reduces the activity of matrix metalloproteinases (MMP-3, MMP-9, MMP-13) responsible for cartilage breakdown. It also promotes anabolic activity by stimulating synthesis of aggrecan and type II collagen, the primary structural components of hyaline cartilage.</p>
<p>The quality of chondroitin sulfate products varies enormously. Pharmaceutical-grade preparations such as Condrosulf (IBSA) and Bioiberica CS have been used in the large clinical trials demonstrating efficacy. Over-the-counter supplements frequently contain lower purity or different molecular weight distributions, which may explain the inconsistent results across trials. The ESCEO (European Society for Clinical and Economic Aspects of Osteoporosis, Osteoarthritis and Musculoskeletal Diseases) recommends pharmaceutical-grade chondroitin sulfate as a first-line treatment for knee osteoarthritis.</p>
<h2>Dosage Guidelines</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Standard dose</strong>: 800-1200mg/day (single dose or divided into 2-3 doses)</li>
<li><strong>Pharmaceutical-grade</strong>: Condrosulf 800mg/day or Bioiberica CS 1200mg/day preferred</li>
<li><strong>Combination</strong>: Frequently combined with glucosamine sulfate 1500mg/day</li>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Can be taken with or without food</li>
<li><strong>Onset</strong>: Slow-acting; clinical benefit typically observed at 4-8 weeks, with progressive improvement over 3-6 months</li>
</ul>
