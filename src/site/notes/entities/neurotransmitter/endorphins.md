<h1>Endorphins</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Family of endogenous opioid neuropeptides (beta-endorphin, enkephalins, dynorphins) produced primarily in the hypothalamus and pituitary gland. Bind to mu, delta, and kappa opioid receptors to modulate pain perception, reward, stress response, and immune function. Beta-endorphin is the most potent, with analgesic potency ~18-33x morphine.</p>
<h2>Synthesis Pathway</h2>
<p>POMC gene → pro-opiomelanocortin → beta-endorphin + ACTH (co-released from anterior pituitary)</p>
<h2>Degradation Pathway</h2>
<p>Enkephalinase and aminopeptidase cleavage</p>
