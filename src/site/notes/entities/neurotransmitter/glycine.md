<h1>Glycine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Simplest amino acid that serves as a major inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brainstem and spinal cord. Also acts as a co-agonist at NMDA glutamate receptors (excitatory role). Dual inhibitory/excitatory function depending on receptor type. Important for collagen synthesis, glutathione production, and sleep quality.</p>
<p><strong>Chemical Formula:</strong> C2H5NO2</p>
<p><strong>Molecular Weight:</strong> 75.03</p>
<h2>Synthesis Pathway</h2>
<p>Serine → Glycine (via SHMT, requires folate); also threonine degradation</p>
<h2>Degradation Pathway</h2>
<p>Glycine cleavage system (GCS) in mitochondria; also converted to serine by serine hydroxymethyltransferase</p>
<h2>Metabolites</h2>
<ul>
<li>Serine</li>
<li>Glyoxylate</li>
</ul>
