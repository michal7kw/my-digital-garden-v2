<h1>Norepinephrine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Catecholamine neurotransmitter and hormone involved in arousal, attention, stress response, and mood. Primarily produced in the locus coeruleus with widespread cortical projections.</p>
<p><strong>Chemical Formula:</strong> C8H11NO3<br />
<strong>Molecular Weight:</strong> 169.18</p>
<h2>Primary Functions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Arousal and alertness</li>
<li>Attention and focus</li>
<li>Fight-or-flight response</li>
<li>Mood regulation</li>
<li>Memory consolidation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Receptor Types</h2>
<ul>
<li>Alpha-1 (excitatory, vasoconstriction)</li>
<li>Alpha-2 (inhibitory, autoreceptor, presynaptic)</li>
<li>Beta-1 (cardiac stimulation)</li>
<li>Beta-2 (bronchodilation, smooth muscle)</li>
<li>Beta-3 (lipolysis, thermogenesis)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Synthesis Pathway</h2>
<p>Dopamine → Norepinephrine (dopamine beta-hydroxylase / DBH)</p>
<h2>Associated Conditions</h2>
<ul>
<li>ADHD (noradrenergic prefrontal deficit)</li>
<li>Depression (monoamine hypothesis)</li>
<li>PTSD (noradrenergic hyperactivation)</li>
<li>Anxiety disorders (locus coeruleus overactivity)</li>
<li>Orthostatic hypotension (NE deficiency)</li>
</ul>
