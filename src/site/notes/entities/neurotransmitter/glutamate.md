<h1>Glutamate</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Primary excitatory neurotransmitter in the central nervous system. Essential for synaptic plasticity, learning, and memory via NMDA and AMPA receptor activation.</p>
<p><strong>Chemical Formula:</strong> C5H9NO4<br />
<strong>Molecular Weight:</strong> 147.13</p>
<h2>Primary Functions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Excitatory neurotransmission</li>
<li>Synaptic plasticity (LTP/LTD)</li>
<li>Learning and memory</li>
<li>Neural development</li>
<li>Sensory processing</li>
</ul>
<h2>Receptor Types</h2>
<ul>
<li>NMDA (voltage-dependent, Ca²⁺ permeable, learning)</li>
<li>AMPA (fast excitatory, Na⁺/K⁺)</li>
<li>Kainate (modulatory)</li>
<li>mGluR1-8 (metabotropic, neuromodulation)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Synthesis Pathway</h2>
<p>Glutamine → Glutamate (glutaminase)</p>
<h2>Associated Conditions</h2>
<ul>
<li>Alzheimer's disease (NMDA receptor dysfunction)</li>
<li>Epilepsy (glutamate excess)</li>
<li>Stroke/excitotoxicity (calcium overload)</li>
<li>ALS (motor neuron excitotoxicity)</li>
<li>Schizophrenia (NMDA hypofunction hypothesis)</li>
</ul>
