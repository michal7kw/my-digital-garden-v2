<h1>Rheumatoid Arthritis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Rheumatoid arthritis (RA) is a chronic systemic autoimmune disease primarily attacking the synovial lining of joints, causing progressive cartilage and bone destruction. It is a symmetric polyarthritis — typically affecting small joints of hands and feet first (MCP, PIP, wrist, MTP joints). Unlike osteoarthritis, RA involves systemic inflammation with extra-articular manifestations (cardiovascular disease, interstitial lung disease, vasculitis). The pathogenesis involves autoreactive T cells, autoantibody production (rheumatoid factor, anti-CCP), and a cytokine storm dominated by TNF-alpha, IL-6, and IL-1. Early diagnosis and &quot;treat-to-target&quot; approaches have dramatically improved outcomes.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Symmetric joint pain, swelling, and stiffness (especially morning stiffness &gt;1 hour)</li>
<li>Small joint predilection (hands, wrists, feet)</li>
<li>Fatigue, malaise, low-grade fever</li>
<li>Joint deformities (swan neck, boutonniere, ulnar deviation — if untreated)</li>
<li>Rheumatoid nodules (subcutaneous)</li>
<li>Extra-articular: dry eyes/mouth (Sjogren's overlap), interstitial lung disease, pericarditis</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Female sex (3:1 ratio)</li>
<li>Family history (HLA-DR4 and HLA-DR1 associated)</li>
<li>Smoking (strongest modifiable risk — 2x risk, worse outcomes)</li>
<li>Periodontitis (Porphyromonas gingivalis citrullinates proteins)</li>
<li>Obesity</li>
<li>Silica/textile dust exposure</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>DMARDs (start early, within 3 months)</strong>: Methotrexate (anchor DMARD, first-line), hydroxychloroquine, sulfasalazine, leflunomide</li>
<li><strong>Biologics (if DMARD failure)</strong>: TNF inhibitors (adalimumab, etanercept, infliximab), IL-6 inhibitors (tocilizumab), T-cell costimulation blocker (abatacept), anti-CD20 (rituximab), JAK inhibitors (tofacitinib, baricitinib)</li>
<li><strong>Treat-to-target</strong>: Aim for remission or low disease activity within 6 months; escalate therapy if target not met</li>
<li><strong>Bridge therapy</strong>: Short-course glucocorticoids while waiting for DMARD onset</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Anti-inflammatory diet (Mediterranean), regular exercise (preserves joint function), smoking cessation</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Omega-3 fish oil (2-3g/day EPA+DHA — reduces NSAID use, Level A evidence), curcumin (anti-TNF), vitamin D (immune modulation)</li>
</ul>
