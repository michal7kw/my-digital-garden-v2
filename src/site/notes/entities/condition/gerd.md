<h1>GERD</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) is a chronic condition caused by frequent retrograde flow of gastric contents into the esophagus due to lower esophageal sphincter (LES) dysfunction. Affects 20% of Western populations. Chronic untreated GERD can lead to erosive esophagitis, Barrett's esophagus (intestinal metaplasia -- precancerous), and esophageal adenocarcinoma. Diagnosis is clinical for typical symptoms; 24-hour pH monitoring is the gold standard for refractory cases.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Heartburn (pyrosis) -- burning retrosternal discomfort, worse after meals and when lying down</li>
<li>Acid regurgitation and sour taste in the mouth</li>
<li>Dysphagia (suggests stricture or motility disorder)</li>
<li>Chronic cough, hoarseness, laryngitis (extraesophageal/atypical GERD)</li>
<li>Non-cardiac chest pain</li>
<li>Dental enamel erosion, halitosis</li>
<li>Water brash (sudden excessive salivation)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>PPIs (first-line for moderate-severe)</strong>: Omeprazole, esomeprazole, pantoprazole -- 8-week course; step down to lowest effective dose; most effective taken 30 min before meals</li>
<li><strong>H2 receptor antagonists</strong>: Famotidine -- for mild/intermittent symptoms or PPI step-down; less effective than PPIs</li>
<li><strong>Antacids/alginates</strong>: Calcium carbonate, sodium alginate (Gaviscon) -- for on-demand symptom relief</li>
<li><strong>Prokinetics</strong>: Metoclopramide for gastroparesis-associated GERD (limited by side effects)</li>
<li><strong>Surgery</strong>: Nissen fundoplication or magnetic sphincter augmentation (LINX) for PPI-refractory GERD with confirmed acid exposure</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle (essential)</strong>: Weight loss (strongest lifestyle intervention), elevate head of bed 6-8 inches, avoid eating 3 hours before bedtime, avoid trigger foods (fatty/spicy foods, chocolate, caffeine, alcohol, citrus), smaller frequent meals</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Melatonin (improves LES tone in studies), DGL licorice (mucosal protection), zinc carnosine (gastric mucosal repair), probiotics, slippery elm<br />
