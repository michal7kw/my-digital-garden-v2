<h1>Alzheimer's Disease</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Alzheimer's disease (AD) is the most common cause of dementia, accounting for 60-80% of cases and affecting an estimated 55 million people worldwide. It is characterized by progressive accumulation of amyloid-beta plaques and tau neurofibrillary tangles, beginning decades before symptoms appear. The earliest pathology occurs in the entorhinal cortex and hippocampus (explaining why spatial disorientation and episodic memory loss are the first symptoms). Modern understanding emphasizes multiple drivers: neuroinflammation, insulin resistance (&quot;type 3 diabetes&quot;), mitochondrial dysfunction, cholinergic neuron loss, and impaired glymphatic clearance during sleep.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Early stage</strong>: Episodic memory loss (forgetting recent conversations), word-finding difficulty, spatial disorientation, misplacing objects</li>
<li><strong>Middle stage</strong>: Confusion about time/place, personality changes, agitation, wandering, difficulty with daily activities, impaired judgment</li>
<li><strong>Late stage</strong>: Loss of ability to communicate, complete dependence, difficulty swallowing</li>
<li><strong>Behavioral</strong>: Apathy, depression, anxiety, sleep disturbance, sundowning</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Age (doubles every 5 years after 65)</li>
<li>APOE4 genotype (3-12x increased risk)</li>
<li>Family history</li>
<li>Cardiovascular risk factors (hypertension, diabetes, obesity)</li>
<li>Traumatic brain injury</li>
<li>Low cognitive reserve</li>
<li>Social isolation and hearing loss</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cholinesterase inhibitors</strong>: Donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine — modest symptomatic benefit</li>
<li><strong>Anti-amyloid antibodies</strong>: Lecanemab (Leqembi) — first disease-modifying therapy, 27% slowing of decline</li>
<li><strong>NMDA antagonist</strong>: Memantine for moderate-to-severe AD</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle prevention</strong>: Exercise (150 min/week, 30-40% risk reduction), Mediterranean/MIND diet, cognitive stimulation, social engagement, sleep optimization</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Omega-3 DHA, vitamin D, lion's mane (NGF stimulation), curcumin</li>
</ul>
