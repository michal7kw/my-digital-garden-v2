<h1>OCD</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) is a chronic neuropsychiatric condition affecting 2-3% of the population worldwide. It is characterized by two core features:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Obsessions</strong>: Recurrent, intrusive, unwanted thoughts, images, or urges that cause marked anxiety (e.g., contamination fears, harm obsessions, symmetry/ordering needs, forbidden thoughts)</li>
<li><strong>Compulsions</strong>: Repetitive behaviors or mental acts performed to reduce obsession-driven anxiety (e.g., washing, checking, counting, mental rituals)</li>
</ul>
<p>OCD is neurobiologically distinct from generalized anxiety — it involves dysfunction in the cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical (CSTC) circuit, with hyperactivity in the orbitofrontal cortex (OFC), anterior cingulate cortex (ACC), and caudate nucleus. Serotonergic dysfunction is central: only serotonergic antidepressants (SSRIs and clomipramine) are effective, while noradrenergic drugs (desipramine) are not.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Contamination obsessions</strong>: Fear of germs, bodily fluids, environmental contaminants → washing/cleaning compulsions</li>
<li><strong>Harm obsessions</strong>: Intrusive images of harming self or others → checking compulsions (locks, stove, etc.)</li>
<li><strong>Symmetry/ordering</strong>: Need for things to be &quot;just right&quot; → arranging, counting, repeating</li>
<li><strong>Forbidden thoughts</strong>: Unwanted sexual, religious, or aggressive thoughts → mental rituals, avoidance</li>
<li><strong>Hoarding</strong>: Difficulty discarding items (now classified separately in DSM-5 but often co-occurs)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Pathophysiology</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>CSTC circuit dysfunction</strong>: Hyperactive loop between OFC → caudate → thalamus → cortex</li>
<li><strong>Serotonin hypothesis</strong>: Reduced serotonergic modulation of CSTC circuit; SSRIs normalize circuit activity</li>
<li><strong>Glutamate involvement</strong>: Emerging evidence for glutamatergic hyperactivity in striatum (memantine, riluzole trials)</li>
<li><strong>Neuroinflammation</strong>: Microglial activation in CSTC regions (PET imaging studies)</li>
<li><strong>Genetics</strong>: ~50% heritability; SLC6A4, HTR2A, DLGAP1, SAPAP3 implicated</li>
</ul>

<h3>Second-Line</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Clomipramine</strong>: Gold standard efficacy (TCA with potent SERT inhibition), but more side effects</li>
<li><strong>SSRI + low-dose antipsychotic</strong>: Aripiprazole or risperidone augmentation for partial responders</li>
</ul>
<h3>Psychotherapy</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>CBT with ERP</strong> (Exposure and Response Prevention): First-line, comparable efficacy to SSRIs</li>
<li><strong>Combination</strong>: SSRI + ERP superior to either alone</li>
</ul>
<h3>Treatment-Resistant</h3>
<ul>
<li>Deep brain stimulation (DBS) of ventral capsule/ventral striatum</li>
<li>Transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS) — FDA-approved for OCD</li>
<li>Ketamine (emerging evidence)</li>
</ul>
