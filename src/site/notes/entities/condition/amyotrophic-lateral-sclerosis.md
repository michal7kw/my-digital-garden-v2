<h1>Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A fatal neurodegenerative disease characterized by progressive loss of upper and lower motor neurons, leading to muscle weakness, paralysis, and death typically within 2-5 years of symptom onset.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Progressive muscle weakness (typically starts asymmetrically in one limb)</li>
<li>Fasciculations, muscle atrophy, spasticity</li>
<li>Dysarthria, dysphagia, respiratory failure (median survival 3-5 years)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Disease-modifying</strong>: Riluzole (extends survival ~3 months), edaravone</li>
<li><strong>Tofersen</strong>: Antisense oligonucleotide for SOD1-ALS</li>
<li><strong>Symptomatic</strong>: Non-invasive ventilation (BiPAP), PEG feeding tube</li>
<li><strong>Multidisciplinary care</strong>: Neurology, PT/OT, speech, respiratory, palliative</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: CoQ10, creatine, NAC, omega-3</li>
</ul>
