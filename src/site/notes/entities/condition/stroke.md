<h1>Stroke</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Acute cerebrovascular event caused by interrupted blood supply (ischemic, 87%) or bleeding (hemorrhagic, 13%) in the brain. Second leading cause of death worldwide and a major cause of long-term disability. The FAST mnemonic (Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call emergency) guides rapid recognition. Time-critical treatment: &quot;time is brain&quot; -- 1.9 million neurons lost per minute during ischemic stroke.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Sudden unilateral facial drooping, arm/leg weakness or numbness (contralateral to lesion)</li>
<li>Speech difficulties: expressive aphasia (Broca's), receptive aphasia (Wernicke's), dysarthria</li>
<li>Sudden severe headache with no known cause (hemorrhagic stroke)</li>
<li>Vision changes: hemianopia, diplopia, amaurosis fugax</li>
<li>Loss of balance, vertigo, ataxia (posterior circulation stroke)</li>
<li>Altered consciousness in large or brainstem strokes</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Acute ischemic stroke</strong>: IV alteplase (tPA) within 4.5 hours of onset; mechanical thrombectomy for large vessel occlusion up to 24 hours (based on imaging selection -- DAWN/DEFUSE 3 trials)</li>
<li><strong>Hemorrhagic stroke</strong>: Blood pressure control (IV labetalol, nicardipine, target SBP &lt; 140), reversal of anticoagulation, surgical evacuation for large hematomas</li>
<li><strong>Secondary prevention</strong>: Antiplatelet therapy (aspirin + clopidogrel for 21 days post-minor stroke/TIA, then single agent), statins (high-intensity), antihypertensives, anticoagulation for atrial fibrillation (apixaban, rivaroxaban)</li>
<li><strong>Rehabilitation</strong>: Physical therapy (motor recovery, gait training), occupational therapy (ADL retraining), speech-language pathology (aphasia, dysphagia), constraint-induced movement therapy</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Blood pressure control (target &lt; 130/80), regular aerobic exercise, Mediterranean diet, smoking cessation, moderate alcohol or abstinence, weight management</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Omega-3 fatty acids (anti-inflammatory, vascular health), B-complex vitamins (homocysteine reduction), CoQ10, vitamin D, curcumin<br />
