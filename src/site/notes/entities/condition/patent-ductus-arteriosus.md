<h1>Patent Ductus Arteriosus</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Patent Ductus Arteriosus (PDA) is a persistent opening between the two major blood vessels leading from the heart. The <strong>ductus arteriosus</strong> is a normal part of a baby's circulatory system before birth that usually closes shortly after birth. If it remains open, it allows oxygen-rich blood from the aorta to mix with oxygen-poor blood in the pulmonary artery, straining the heart and increasing blood pressure in the lung arteries.</p>
<h2>Complications</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Heart Failure:</strong> Due to volume overload.</li>
<li><strong>Pulmonary Hypertension:</strong> High blood pressure in the lungs.</li>
<li><strong>Endocarditis:</strong> Infection of the heart lining.</li>
<li><strong>Eisenmenger Syndrome:</strong> Irreversible lung damage leading to cyanosis.</li>
</ul>
