<h1>Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>ADHD is a neurodevelopmental disorder affecting 5-7% of children and 2.5-4% of adults worldwide. It involves dysregulation of prefrontal cortex dopamine and norepinephrine circuits, leading to deficits in executive function, sustained attention, and impulse control. Three presentations: predominantly inattentive, predominantly hyperactive-impulsive, and combined. Increasingly recognized as a lifelong condition — adult ADHD is underdiagnosed and associated with higher rates of substance abuse, relationship difficulties, employment problems, and comorbid anxiety/depression. Neuroimaging shows reduced prefrontal cortex volume and delayed cortical maturation.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Inattention</strong>: Difficulty sustaining focus, easily distracted, forgetful, loses things, avoids tasks requiring sustained mental effort</li>
<li><strong>Hyperactivity</strong>: Fidgeting, restlessness, inability to sit still, excessive talking, &quot;driven by a motor&quot;</li>
<li><strong>Impulsivity</strong>: Interrupting, difficulty waiting turn, risk-taking without considering consequences</li>
<li><strong>Executive dysfunction</strong>: Disorganization, poor time management, difficulty prioritizing, procrastination</li>
<li><strong>Emotional dysregulation</strong>: Frustration intolerance, mood lability, rejection sensitivity (RSD)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Stimulant medications (first-line)</strong>: Methylphenidate (Ritalin, Concerta), amphetamine salts (Adderall, Vyvanse) — increase prefrontal dopamine/NE. 70-80% response rate</li>
<li><strong>Non-stimulant medications</strong>: Atomoxetine (NRI), guanfacine (alpha-2A agonist), viloxazine</li>
<li><strong>Behavioral therapy</strong>: CBT adapted for ADHD (organization skills, time management, cognitive restructuring)</li>
<li><strong>Exercise</strong>: High-intensity exercise acutely improves attention; regular exercise has moderate effect sizes comparable to some medications</li>
<li><strong>Environmental modifications</strong>: External structure, timers, body doubling, breaking tasks into steps</li>
<li><strong>Nutritional support</strong>: Omega-3 (EPA-dominant, 1-2g/day — small but consistent effect), zinc, magnesium, iron (if deficient), L-theanine, elimination diets (some evidence for artificial additives)</li>
<li><strong>Sleep</strong>: ADHD strongly disrupts sleep; sleep optimization improves daytime function</li>
</ul>
