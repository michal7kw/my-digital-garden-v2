<h1>Polymyositis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Polymyositis is an idiopathic inflammatory myopathy characterized by symmetric proximal muscle weakness without the characteristic skin findings seen in dermatomyositis. The pathology involves CD8+ T-cell mediated attack on muscle fibers, with endomysial inflammation and invasion of non-necrotic muscle fibers -- distinguishing it from the perivascular and perifascicular inflammation seen in dermatomyositis.</p>
<p>Patients typically present with progressive difficulty climbing stairs, rising from a chair, or lifting arms above the head. Dysphagia occurs in approximately 30% of cases. Polymyositis is increasingly recognized as overlapping with antisynthetase syndrome (characterized by anti-Jo-1 and other aminoacyl-tRNA synthetase antibodies), which carries a significant risk of interstitial lung disease. Treatment follows a stepwise approach starting with high-dose corticosteroids, with methotrexate or azathioprine as steroid-sparing agents. CK levels serve as a useful biomarker for monitoring treatment response.</p>
