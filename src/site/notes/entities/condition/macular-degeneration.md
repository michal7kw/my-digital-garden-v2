<h1>Macular Degeneration</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Age-related macular degeneration (AMD) is a progressive disease affecting the macula, the central part of the retina responsible for sharp, detailed vision. Leading cause of irreversible vision loss in people over 50 in developed countries. Two forms: dry (atrophic, 85-90%) involving drusen and geographic atrophy, and wet (neovascular, 10-15%) involving choroidal neovascularization that can cause rapid severe vision loss. Complement pathway dysregulation and oxidative stress are central to pathogenesis.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Gradual central vision loss (difficulty reading, driving, recognizing faces)</li>
<li>Metamorphopsia (distorted vision -- straight lines appear wavy, detected by Amsler grid)</li>
<li>Scotoma (dark or blank spot in central visual field)</li>
<li>Decreased contrast sensitivity and color perception</li>
<li>Difficulty adapting to low light levels</li>
<li>Wet AMD: sudden onset of visual distortion or central scotoma -- urgent referral needed</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Anti-VEGF injections (wet AMD, first-line)</strong>: Ranibizumab, aflibercept, brolucizumab, faricimab -- intravitreal injections every 4-16 weeks; stabilizes or improves vision in 90%+ of patients</li>
<li><strong>Complement inhibitors (dry AMD)</strong>: Pegcetacoplan (Syfovre) and avacincaptad pegol (Izervay) -- first FDA-approved treatments for geographic atrophy; slow progression by 20-35%</li>
<li><strong>AREDS2 supplementation (evidence-based)</strong>: Vitamin C 500mg, vitamin E 400 IU, lutein 10mg, zeaxanthin 2mg, zinc 80mg, copper 2mg -- reduces progression from intermediate to advanced AMD by 25%</li>
<li><strong>Photodynamic therapy</strong>: Verteporfin for subfoveal choroidal neovascularization (largely replaced by anti-VEGF)</li>
<li><strong>Low vision rehabilitation</strong>: Magnifying devices, large-print materials, eccentric viewing training, adaptive technology</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Smoking cessation (most modifiable risk factor), regular exercise, Mediterranean diet rich in leafy greens and fish, UV-protective sunglasses, blood pressure control</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: AREDS2 formula (above), lutein and zeaxanthin (macular pigment restoration), omega-3 fatty acids (DHA -- retinal structural component), astaxanthin, bilberry extract<br />
