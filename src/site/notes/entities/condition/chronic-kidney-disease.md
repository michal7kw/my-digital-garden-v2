<h1>Chronic Kidney Disease</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is progressive loss of kidney function over months to years, staged by GFR: Stage 1 (GFR ≥90, kidney damage present), Stage 2 (60-89), Stage 3a/3b (30-59), Stage 4 (15-29), Stage 5/ESRD (&lt;15, dialysis needed). Affects 15% of US adults (~37 million). Leading causes: diabetes (40%), hypertension (28%). CKD is a cardiovascular risk amplifier — patients are more likely to die from CVD than progress to dialysis.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Often asymptomatic until advanced stages (Stage 4-5)</li>
<li>Fatigue, weakness, difficulty concentrating</li>
<li>Decreased urine output, foamy urine (proteinuria)</li>
<li>Peripheral edema, hypertension</li>
<li>Nausea, metallic taste, anorexia (uremia)</li>
<li>Bone pain, muscle cramps (mineral metabolism disruption)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Blood pressure control</strong>: ACE inhibitor or ARB (renoprotective — reduce proteinuria). Target &lt;130/80</li>
<li><strong>Glycemic control</strong>: SGLT2 inhibitors (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin — reduce CKD progression 30-40% regardless of diabetes status — DAPA-CKD, EMPA-KIDNEY trials)</li>
<li><strong>GLP-1 agonists</strong>: Semaglutide (FLOW trial — kidney benefit)</li>
<li><strong>Finerenone</strong>: Non-steroidal MRA with kidney and cardiovascular benefit in diabetic CKD</li>
<li><strong>Dietary</strong>: Protein restriction (0.6-0.8 g/kg in advanced CKD), sodium restriction, potassium management</li>
<li><strong>Monitoring</strong>: eGFR, urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR), electrolytes, phosphorus, PTH</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Omega-3, sodium bicarbonate (metabolic acidosis), vitamin D (often deficient), iron (anemia)</li>
</ul>
