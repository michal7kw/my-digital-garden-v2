<h1>Menopause</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A natural biological process marking the end of a woman's reproductive years, defined as 12 consecutive months without a menstrual period.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Hot flashes and night sweats (vasomotor symptoms)</li>
<li>Irregular periods (perimenopause), then cessation</li>
<li>Vaginal dryness and dyspareunia</li>
<li>Sleep disturbances</li>
<li>Mood changes (irritability, anxiety, depression)</li>
<li>Brain fog and memory difficulties</li>
<li>Joint pain and stiffness</li>
<li>Weight gain (especially central adiposity)</li>
<li>Decreased libido</li>
<li>Skin dryness and thinning</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Hormone replacement therapy (HRT)</strong>: Estrogen (with progesterone if uterus intact) — most effective treatment for vasomotor symptoms. Systemic (oral, transdermal patch, gel) or local (vaginal estrogen for genitourinary symptoms). Initiate within 10 years of menopause onset for favorable risk-benefit profile</li>
<li><strong>Non-hormonal medications</strong>: SSRIs/SNRIs (paroxetine — FDA-approved for hot flashes), gabapentin, clonidine, fezolinetant (NK3 receptor antagonist — newer option)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Black cohosh (some evidence for hot flash reduction), vitamin D + calcium (bone protection — critical period for osteoporosis development), omega-3 fatty acids, magnesium (sleep, mood, bone), vitamin K2 (directs calcium to bone), phytoestrogens (soy isoflavones — modest benefit)</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Regular weight-bearing exercise (protects bone density and cardiovascular health), Mediterranean diet, stress reduction, cognitive behavioral therapy (effective for mood and sleep symptoms)</li>
<li><strong>Vaginal health</strong>: Vaginal estrogen cream/ring (safe even when systemic HRT not indicated), vaginal moisturizers and lubricants, ospemifene (oral SERM for dyspareunia)</li>
<li><strong>Bone protection</strong>: DEXA screening, ensure adequate calcium (1200 mg/day) and vitamin D (target &gt;40 ng/mL), weight-bearing exercise, consider bisphosphonates if osteoporotic</li>
</ul>
