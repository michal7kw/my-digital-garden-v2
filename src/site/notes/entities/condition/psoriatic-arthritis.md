<h1>Psoriatic Arthritis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Psoriatic arthritis (PsA) is a chronic inflammatory arthropathy that develops in approximately 30% of individuals with psoriasis. It belongs to the spondyloarthropathy family of diseases and is characterized by remarkable clinical heterogeneity, encompassing five distinct patterns: symmetric polyarthritis, asymmetric oligoarthritis, distal interphalangeal joint predominant disease, spondylitis, and arthritis mutilans. The disease can affect peripheral joints, the axial skeleton, entheses, and digits, often in combination.</p>
<p>The pathogenesis of PsA centers on the IL-23/IL-17 immune axis, with dysregulated innate and adaptive immune responses driving inflammation at both skin and joint sites. Genetic susceptibility is conferred by HLA alleles (particularly HLA-B27 for axial disease and HLA-C*06:02 for psoriasis), with additional contributions from non-HLA loci involved in IL-23 signaling. Environmental factors including trauma (the deep Koebner phenomenon), infection, and obesity can trigger disease onset in genetically predisposed individuals.</p>
<p>Early recognition and treatment of PsA is critical, as joint damage can occur within the first two years of disease onset and progresses in over 50% of patients. The CASPAR (Classification Criteria for Psoriatic Arthritis) criteria are used for classification, requiring established inflammatory articular disease plus at least 3 points from features including psoriasis, nail dystrophy, dactylitis, negative rheumatoid factor, and juxta-articular new bone formation on radiography. Modern biologic therapies targeting TNF, IL-17, IL-23, and JAK pathways have transformed outcomes, enabling treat-to-target strategies aimed at minimal disease activity.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Asymmetric joint swelling and pain</li>
<li>Dactylitis (sausage digits)</li>
<li>Enthesitis (Achilles and plantar fascia)</li>
<li>Nail pitting and onycholysis</li>
<li>Morning stiffness</li>
<li>Psoriatic skin plaques</li>
<li>Low back pain (axial involvement)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Psoriasis (30% develop PsA)</li>
<li>Family history</li>
<li>Nail psoriasis (strongest predictor)</li>
<li>HLA-B27 (for axial disease)</li>
<li>Obesity</li>
<li>Trauma to joint (Koebner phenomenon)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li>NSAIDs for mild disease</li>
<li>Conventional DMARDs (methotrexate first-line for peripheral)</li>
<li>TNF inhibitors (adalimumab, etanercept, infliximab)</li>
<li>IL-17 inhibitors (secukinumab, ixekizumab)</li>
<li>IL-23 inhibitors (guselkumab, risankizumab)</li>
<li>JAK inhibitors (tofacitinib, upadacitinib)</li>
<li>PDE4 inhibitor (apremilast for mild-moderate)</li>
<li>Treat skin and joints concurrently</li>
</ul>
