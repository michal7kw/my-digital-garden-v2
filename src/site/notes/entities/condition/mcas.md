<h1>MCAS</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Mast Cell Activation Syndrome (MCAS) is a chronic condition of inappropriate, excessive mast cell activation and mediator release affecting multiple organ systems. Distinct from mastocytosis (clonal proliferation). Mast cells degranulate releasing histamine, prostaglandins, leukotrienes, and cytokines. Episodic multisystem symptoms triggered by stress, heat, foods, chemicals, exercise, or medications. Diagnosis requires Vienna consensus criteria: symptoms, evidence of mediator elevation (tryptase, histamine metabolites), and response to mast cell-targeted therapy.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Skin</strong>: Flushing, hives (urticaria), angioedema, pruritus, dermatographism</li>
<li><strong>GI</strong>: Abdominal pain, diarrhea, nausea, bloating, food intolerances</li>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular</strong>: Tachycardia, hypotension, presyncope, anaphylaxis</li>
<li><strong>Neurological</strong>: Brain fog, headaches, anxiety, dizziness</li>
<li><strong>Respiratory</strong>: Nasal congestion, wheezing, throat tightness</li>
<li><strong>Constitutional</strong>: Fatigue, temperature dysregulation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>H1 antihistamines</strong>: Cetirizine, loratadine (daily, often high-dose)</li>
<li><strong>H2 antihistamines</strong>: Famotidine (GI symptoms, reduces gastric acid)</li>
<li><strong>Mast cell stabilizers</strong>: Cromolyn sodium (oral for GI, nasal/ophthalmic), ketotifen</li>
<li><strong>Leukotriene inhibitors</strong>: Montelukast, zileuton</li>
<li><strong>Low-histamine diet</strong>: Avoid aged/fermented foods, alcohol, leftover meats</li>
<li><strong>Trigger avoidance</strong>: Identify and avoid personal triggers (heat, stress, NSAIDs, certain foods)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Quercetin (natural mast cell stabilizer — 500mg 2-3x/day), vitamin C (reduces histamine), DAO enzyme (histamine degradation), NAC, omega-3</li>
<li><strong>Emergency</strong>: Epinephrine auto-injector (for anaphylaxis risk)</li>
</ul>
