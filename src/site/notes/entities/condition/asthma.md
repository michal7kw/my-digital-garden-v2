<h1>Asthma</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Asthma is a heterogeneous chronic inflammatory disease of the airways affecting approximately 300 million people globally. It involves variable airflow obstruction (bronchospasm), airway hyperresponsiveness to triggers, mucus hypersecretion, and progressive airway remodeling. The most common endotype is Th2-high/eosinophilic asthma (allergic), driven by IL-4, IL-5, and IL-13, but non-Th2/neutrophilic asthma (often severe, steroid-resistant) is increasingly recognized. Biologics targeting specific inflammatory mediators (anti-IgE, anti-IL-5, anti-IL-4/13) have transformed management of severe asthma.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Recurrent episodes of wheezing, breathlessness, chest tightness, and cough</li>
<li>Symptoms worse at night and early morning</li>
<li>Triggered by allergens, exercise, cold air, respiratory infections, stress</li>
<li>Variable airflow limitation (reversible with bronchodilators)</li>
<li>Exercise-induced bronchoconstriction</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Family history/atopy (strongest risk factor)</li>
<li>Allergic rhinitis, eczema (atopic triad)</li>
<li>Childhood respiratory infections (RSV)</li>
<li>Air pollution, tobacco smoke exposure</li>
<li>Obesity (increases severity and steroid resistance)</li>
<li>Occupational exposures (isocyanates, flour, animal dander)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Step therapy (GINA guidelines)</strong>: Inhaled corticosteroids (ICS) are cornerstone at all severity levels</li>
<li><strong>Step 1-2</strong>: Low-dose ICS (budesonide/formoterol PRN)</li>
<li><strong>Step 3-4</strong>: Medium-dose ICS + LABA (salmeterol, formoterol), or add LTRA (montelukast)</li>
<li><strong>Step 5 (severe)</strong>: High-dose ICS + LABA + biologics (omalizumab anti-IgE, mepolizumab anti-IL-5, dupilumab anti-IL-4/13)</li>
<li><strong>Acute exacerbation</strong>: SABA (albuterol), systemic corticosteroids, oxygen</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Allergen avoidance, smoking cessation, weight loss (if obese), regular exercise (improves asthma control despite exercise-induced symptoms)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Vitamin D (reduces exacerbation frequency in deficient patients), omega-3 (anti-inflammatory), magnesium (bronchodilator), NAC (mucolytic)</li>
</ul>
