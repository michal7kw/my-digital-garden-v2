<h1>Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A chronic inflammatory lung disease that causes obstructed airflow from the lungs, including emphysema and chronic bronchitis.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Shortness of breath, especially during physical activity</li>
<li>Chronic cough with sputum production (smoker's cough)</li>
<li>Wheezing and chest tightness</li>
<li>Frequent respiratory infections and exacerbations</li>
<li>Cyanosis (blue lips or fingernails) in advanced disease</li>
<li>Barrel chest, weight loss, and muscle wasting in severe stages</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Bronchodilators</strong>: Short-acting (albuterol, ipratropium) for rescue; long-acting (tiotropium, salmeterol) for maintenance</li>
<li><strong>Inhaled corticosteroids</strong>: Fluticasone, budesonide -- combined with LABA for moderate-severe disease (reduces exacerbations 25-30%)</li>
<li><strong>Phosphodiesterase-4 inhibitors</strong>: Roflumilast for severe COPD with frequent exacerbations</li>
<li><strong>Oxygen therapy</strong>: Long-term supplemental O2 for chronic hypoxemia (PaO2 &lt; 55 mmHg) -- improves survival</li>
<li><strong>Pulmonary rehabilitation</strong>: Exercise training + education -- strongest evidence for improving dyspnea and quality of life</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Smoking cessation (single most effective intervention), annual influenza/pneumococcal vaccination, pulmonary hygiene</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: NAC (600-1200mg -- mucolytic, reduces exacerbations), vitamin D (if deficient), CoQ10, omega-3 fatty acids</li>
<li><strong>Surgical</strong>: Lung volume reduction surgery, bullectomy, or lung transplant for end-stage disease<br />
