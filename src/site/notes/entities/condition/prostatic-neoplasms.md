<h1>Prostatic Neoplasms</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Cancer of the prostate gland, a small walnut-shaped gland in males that produces seminal fluid. It is one of the most common types of cancer in men.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Frequent urination, especially at night (nocturia)</li>
<li>Difficulty starting or maintaining urination, weak stream</li>
<li>Blood in urine (hematuria) or semen</li>
<li>Erectile dysfunction</li>
<li>Pain or discomfort in the pelvis, hips, or lower back (advanced disease)</li>
<li>Many early-stage cases are asymptomatic, detected by PSA screening</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Active surveillance</strong>: For low-risk (Gleason 6, PSA &lt; 10) -- serial PSA, MRI, and biopsies; avoids overtreatment</li>
<li><strong>Surgery</strong>: Radical prostatectomy (robotic-assisted preferred) -- curative for localized disease</li>
<li><strong>Radiation</strong>: External beam RT or brachytherapy -- equivalent survival to surgery for localized disease</li>
<li><strong>Androgen deprivation therapy (ADT)</strong>: GnRH agonists (leuprolide) or antagonists (degarelix) for advanced/metastatic disease</li>
<li><strong>Novel agents</strong>: Enzalutamide, abiraterone (CYP17 inhibitor), PARP inhibitors (olaparib for BRCA2+), Lu-177 PSMA for mCRPC</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Regular exercise (reduces mortality 30-40%), plant-rich diet, maintain healthy weight, lycopene-rich foods (tomatoes)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Vitamin D (if deficient), selenium, zinc, omega-3 fatty acids, curcumin (anti-inflammatory, preclinical evidence)<br />
