<h1>Ocular Hypertension</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Ocular Hypertension refers to Intraocular Pressure (IOP) consistently above the normal range (usually &gt;21 mmHg) in the absence of optic nerve damage or visual field loss. It is a major risk factor for developing <strong>Glaucoma</strong>. It occurs when the eye produces too much fluid (aqueous humor) or it drains too slowly.</p>
<h2>Treatment</h2>
<p>Not all cases require treatment. The decision to treat is based on the risk of progression to glaucoma. Treatment involves lowering IOP.</p>
