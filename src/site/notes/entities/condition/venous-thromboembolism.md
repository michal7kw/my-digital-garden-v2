<h1>Venous Thromboembolism</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A condition where a blood clot forms in a vein, encompassing Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) and Pulmonary Embolism (PE).</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>DVT</strong>: Leg swelling, pain or tenderness (usually unilateral), redness, warmth, and visible distention of superficial veins</li>
<li><strong>PE</strong>: Sudden shortness of breath, sharp pleuritic chest pain, rapid heart rate, coughing up blood (hemoptysis)</li>
<li>Massive PE may present with syncope, hypotension, or cardiovascular collapse</li>
<li>Post-thrombotic syndrome: chronic leg swelling, pain, skin changes after DVT</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Anticoagulation (first-line)</strong>: Direct oral anticoagulants (rivaroxaban, apixaban) preferred; heparin bridging to warfarin as alternative; minimum 3 months for provoked VTE, indefinite for unprovoked recurrence</li>
<li><strong>Thrombolysis</strong>: Systemic tPA for massive PE with hemodynamic instability; catheter-directed thrombolysis for large DVT (iliofemoral)</li>
<li><strong>IVC filter</strong>: For patients with contraindication to anticoagulation or recurrent PE despite adequate anticoagulation</li>
<li><strong>Compression therapy</strong>: Graduated compression stockings (30-40 mmHg) for DVT -- reduces post-thrombotic syndrome risk</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Regular ambulation and calf exercises (especially during travel), adequate hydration, avoidance of prolonged immobility, weight management</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Omega-3 fatty acids (mild antithrombotic), vitamin E (platelet function), NAC, curcumin (anti-inflammatory)</li>
<li><strong>Prevention</strong>: Pharmacological thromboprophylaxis (enoxaparin) for hospitalized/surgical patients, early mobilization<br />
