<h1>Adenomatous Polyposis Coli</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Adenomatous Polyposis Coli, more commonly known as <strong>Familial Adenomatous Polyposis (FAP)</strong>, is a rare genetic condition. It is caused by a defect in the <em>APC</em> tumor suppressor gene. Without treatment (usually surgical removal of the colon), colorectal cancer develops in nearly 100% of patients, often before age 40.</p>
<h2>Pathophysiology</h2>
<p>The <em>APC</em> gene normally helps control how often a cell divides. Mutations lead to uncontrolled cell growth and polyp formation.</p>
