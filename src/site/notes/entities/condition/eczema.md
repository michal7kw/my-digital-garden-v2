<h1>Eczema</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Atopic dermatitis (eczema) is the most common chronic inflammatory skin disease, affecting up to 20% of children and 10% of adults. Driven by epidermal barrier dysfunction (often filaggrin mutations) and Th2-skewed immune dysregulation. Part of the &quot;atopic march&quot; -- eczema in infancy often precedes asthma and allergic rhinitis. Severity fluctuates with flares and remissions triggered by environmental factors, stress, and infections.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Intense pruritus (itching) that significantly impacts quality of life and sleep</li>
<li>Dry, cracked, scaly skin with erythematous patches</li>
<li>In infants: face, scalp, and extensor surfaces; in adults: flexural areas (antecubital/popliteal fossae, neck, wrists)</li>
<li>Lichenification (thickened, leathery skin) from chronic scratching</li>
<li>Secondary bacterial infection (Staphylococcus aureus colonization in 90% of patients)</li>
<li>Eczema herpeticum (HSV superinfection) -- dermatologic emergency</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Emollients (foundation of therapy)</strong>: Fragrance-free moisturizers applied liberally 2-3x daily and immediately after bathing; ceramide-containing creams help restore barrier</li>
<li><strong>Topical corticosteroids</strong>: Low-potency (hydrocortisone) for face/intertriginous areas; medium-high potency (triamcinolone, mometasone) for body -- used for flares, not maintenance</li>
<li><strong>Topical calcineurin inhibitors</strong>: Tacrolimus, pimecrolimus -- steroid-sparing for sensitive areas (face, eyelids, groin)</li>
<li><strong>PDE4 inhibitors</strong>: Crisaborole ointment -- non-steroidal topical for mild-moderate disease</li>
<li><strong>JAK inhibitors</strong>: Topical ruxolitinib; oral abrocitinib, upadacitinib for moderate-severe (rapid itch relief)</li>
<li><strong>Biologics</strong>: Dupilumab (anti-IL-4/IL-13, first-line biologic) and tralokinumab (anti-IL-13) for moderate-severe refractory disease</li>
<li><strong>Phototherapy</strong>: Narrowband UVB for widespread moderate disease</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Lukewarm baths (not hot), gentle cleansers, cotton clothing, humidity control, identify and avoid triggers (dust mites, pet dander, certain foods), stress management</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Vitamin D (immune modulation), probiotics (gut-skin axis, may reduce flares in children), omega-3 fatty acids, evening primrose oil (GLA), zinc<br />
