<h1>Kidney Stones</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Hard deposits made of minerals and salts that form inside the kidneys, often causing severe pain when passing through the urinary tract.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Severe, colicky pain in the back, side, or lower abdomen (renal colic)</li>
<li>Pain radiating to the groin or inner thigh</li>
<li>Blood in the urine (hematuria)</li>
<li>Nausea and vomiting</li>
<li>Frequent or painful urination</li>
<li>Cloudy or foul-smelling urine</li>
<li>Fever and chills (if concurrent infection — urological emergency)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Acute management</strong>: NSAIDs (ketorolac — first-line for pain), alpha-blockers (tamsulosin — medical expulsive therapy for stones &lt;10mm), IV fluids</li>
<li><strong>Interventional</strong>: Extracorporeal shock wave lithotripsy (ESWL) for stones &lt;2cm, ureteroscopy with laser lithotripsy, percutaneous nephrolithotomy (PCNL) for large staghorn calculi</li>
<li><strong>Prevention by stone type</strong>: Calcium oxalate (most common — increase fluid to &gt;2.5L/day, moderate oxalate intake, adequate dietary calcium), uric acid (alkalinize urine with potassium citrate, low-purine diet), struvite (treat underlying infection), cystine (high fluid intake, alkalinization)</li>
<li><strong>Dietary</strong>: Increase water intake to produce &gt;2.5L urine/day (single most important prevention measure), reduce sodium (&lt;2300 mg/day), limit animal protein, increase citrus fruit intake (citrate inhibits stone formation)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Magnesium (inhibits calcium oxalate crystallization), vitamin B6 (reduces oxalate production), potassium citrate (urinary alkalinization), probiotics (Oxalobacter formigenes degrades oxalate)</li>
<li><strong>Monitoring</strong>: 24-hour urine collection to guide targeted prevention</li>
</ul>
