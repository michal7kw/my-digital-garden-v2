<h1>Colorectal Neoplasms</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Colorectal cancer is the third most common cancer diagnosed in both men and women. Most cases begin as small, noncancerous (benign) clumps of cells called <strong>adenomatous polyps</strong>. Over time, some of these polyps can become colon cancers. Screening tests (colonoscopy) can find polyps so they can be removed before they turn into cancer.</p>
<h2>Types</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Adenocarcinoma:</strong> (95% of cases) Mucus-secreting cells.</li>
<li><strong>Carcinoid Tumors:</strong> Hormone-producing cells.</li>
<li><strong>Gastrointestinal Stromal Tumors (GISTs):</strong> Interstitial cells of Cajal.</li>
<li><strong>Lymphoma.</strong></li>
</ul>
