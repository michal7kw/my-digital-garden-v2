<h1>Dermatomyositis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Dermatomyositis is an idiopathic inflammatory myopathy characterized by symmetric proximal muscle weakness and pathognomonic skin findings. The hallmark cutaneous manifestations include heliotrope rash (violaceous discoloration of the eyelids), Gottron papules (erythematous papules over the knuckles), the shawl sign, V-sign, and mechanic's hands. The underlying pathology is complement-mediated microangiopathy affecting muscle capillaries.</p>
<p>The disease has a bimodal age distribution (children 5-15 and adults 40-60) with a 2:1 female predominance. In adults over 40, dermatomyositis is associated with increased malignancy risk (particularly ovarian, lung, and GI cancers), necessitating age-appropriate cancer screening. Myositis-specific antibodies (anti-Mi-2, anti-MDA5, anti-TIF1-gamma) help define clinical subtypes and guide prognosis. The anti-MDA5 subtype carries a particularly high risk of rapidly progressive interstitial lung disease.</p>
