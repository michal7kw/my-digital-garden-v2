<h1>Malnutrition and Malabsorption</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>While related, these conditions have different roots. <strong>Malnutrition</strong> refers to an imbalance of nutrients (too little or too much), while <strong>Malabsorption</strong> specifically refers to the gut's failure to absorb nutrients despite adequate intake. Together, they lead to profound systemic decline and multiple vitamin/mineral deficiencies.</p>
<h2>Common Causes of Malabsorption</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Celiac Disease:</strong> Gluten-induced damage to the small intestine.</li>
<li><strong>Pancreatic Insufficiency:</strong> Lack of digestive enzymes.</li>
<li><strong>Biliary Obstruction:</strong> Lack of bile for fat absorption.</li>
<li><strong>Short Bowel Syndrome:</strong> Surgical removal of intestine.</li>
</ul>
