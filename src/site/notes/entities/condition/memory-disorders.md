<h1>Memory Disorders</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Memory Disorders affect the brain's ability to encode, store, or retrieve information. They range from mild, temporary forgetfulness to severe, progressive dementia. Memory loss can be specific (amnesia) or general.</p>
<h2>Common Types</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dementia:</strong> An umbrella term for decline in mental ability severe enough to interfere with daily life (e.g., Alzheimer's, Vascular Dementia).</li>
<li><strong>Amnesia:</strong> Loss of memories, often due to brain injury or trauma.</li>
<li><strong>Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI):</strong> Problems with memory greater than normal for age but not severe enough to be dementia.</li>
</ul>
