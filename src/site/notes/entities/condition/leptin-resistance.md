<h1>Leptin Resistance</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Leptin resistance occurs when the hypothalamus becomes insensitive to leptin signaling despite high circulating levels. In obesity, adipocytes produce excessive leptin, but the brain fails to register the &quot;satiety&quot; signal — creating a paradox where the body has abundant energy stores but the brain perceives starvation. This drives persistent hunger, reduced energy expenditure, and resistance to weight loss. Mechanisms include impaired BBB leptin transport, SOCS3 inhibition of leptin receptor signaling, ER stress, and hypothalamic inflammation.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Constant hunger and food cravings despite adequate caloric intake</li>
<li>Difficulty losing body fat despite caloric restriction</li>
<li>Fatigue after eating, energy crashes</li>
<li>Increased abdominal fat accumulation</li>
<li>Weight rebound after dieting</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Anti-inflammatory diet</strong>: Reduce processed foods and seed oils that drive hypothalamic inflammation. Mediterranean pattern with whole foods</li>
<li><strong>Exercise</strong>: Regular aerobic and resistance training improves leptin sensitivity independent of weight loss</li>
<li><strong>Sleep optimization</strong>: Sleep deprivation increases leptin resistance; 7-9 hours restores sensitivity</li>
<li><strong>Intermittent fasting</strong>: May improve leptin signaling by reducing chronic hyperleptinaemia</li>
<li><strong>Reduce triglycerides</strong>: High triglycerides impair leptin transport across the BBB. Omega-3, berberine, exercise</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Omega-3 (reduce hypothalamic inflammation), alpha-lipoic acid (improves leptin sensitivity), berberine (AMPK activation), NAC</li>
</ul>
