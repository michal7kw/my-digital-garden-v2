<h1>Atrial Fibrillation</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A common heart rhythm disorder (arrhythmia) characterized by rapid and irregular beating of the atrial chambers of the heart.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Heart palpitations (rapid, fluttering, or pounding sensation)</li>
<li>Fatigue and exercise intolerance</li>
<li>Shortness of breath</li>
<li>Dizziness or lightheadedness</li>
<li>Chest pain or discomfort</li>
<li>Fainting (syncope) in severe cases</li>
<li>Some patients are asymptomatic (detected incidentally)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Rate control</strong>: Beta-blockers (metoprolol, atenolol), calcium channel blockers (diltiazem, verapamil), digoxin — control ventricular rate to &lt;110 bpm at rest</li>
<li><strong>Rhythm control</strong>: Antiarrhythmic drugs (flecainide, amiodarone, sotalol), cardioversion (electrical or pharmacological) — restore and maintain sinus rhythm</li>
<li><strong>Anticoagulation</strong>: DOACs (apixaban, rivarelbaan, dabigatran) or warfarin — prevent stroke based on CHA2DS2-VASc score. Stroke risk is 5x higher in AFib</li>
<li><strong>Catheter ablation</strong>: Pulmonary vein isolation — increasingly first-line for paroxysmal AFib. 70-80% success rate, superior to drugs for symptom control</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle modification</strong>: Weight loss (10% reduces AFib recurrence by 50%), alcohol reduction or abstinence, OSA treatment (strongly associated), regular moderate exercise (avoid excessive endurance exercise)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Magnesium (antiarrhythmic, 300-400 mg/day), omega-3 fatty acids (anti-inflammatory), CoQ10 (mitochondrial support for cardiomyocytes), taurine (stabilizes cardiac membrane potential)</li>
<li><strong>Risk factor management</strong>: Treat hypertension, diabetes, obesity, thyroid dysfunction, and sleep apnea</li>
</ul>
