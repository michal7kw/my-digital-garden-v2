<h1>Sleep Disorder</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Sleep disorders encompass a broad category of conditions disrupting sleep quality, quantity, or timing. Chronic sleep disruption affects every organ system — it impairs immune function within one night of restriction, dysregulates glucose metabolism within one week, and is an independent risk factor for cardiovascular disease, obesity, depression, and Alzheimer's disease. The CDC estimates 70 million Americans have a chronic sleep disorder. Insomnia (difficulty falling/staying asleep) is the most common, affecting 30% of adults.</p>
<h2>Subtypes</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Insomnia</strong> — Difficulty initiating or maintaining sleep (most common; 30% prevalence)</li>
<li><strong>Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA)</strong> — Repeated upper airway collapse during sleep; affects 15-30% of men</li>
<li><strong>Restless Leg Syndrome (RLS)</strong> — Irresistible urge to move legs, worse at rest/evening</li>
<li><strong>Narcolepsy</strong> — Excessive daytime sleepiness with or without cataplexy</li>
<li><strong>Circadian Rhythm Disorders</strong> — Delayed/advanced sleep phase, shift work disorder</li>
<li><strong>Parasomnias</strong> — Sleepwalking, sleep terrors, REM sleep behavior disorder</li>
</ul>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Excessive daytime sleepiness</li>
<li>Difficulty falling or staying asleep</li>
<li>Snoring or gasping during sleep (sleep apnea)</li>
<li>Irritability and mood disturbance</li>
<li>Impaired concentration and memory</li>
<li>Morning headaches</li>
<li>Unrefreshing sleep despite adequate duration</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia)</strong>: First-line treatment — more effective than medications long-term. Includes sleep restriction, stimulus control, cognitive restructuring</li>
<li><strong>Sleep hygiene</strong>: Consistent schedule, cool/dark room (65-68°F), morning light exposure, caffeine cutoff 10+ hours before bed, no screens 1-2 hours before bed</li>
<li><strong>Medications</strong>: Melatonin (0.5-3mg for circadian disorders), trazodone (low-dose), orexin antagonists (suvorexant), Z-drugs (short-term only)</li>
<li><strong>Sleep apnea</strong>: CPAP therapy (gold standard), mandibular advancement devices, positional therapy, weight loss</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Magnesium glycinate (400mg before bed), L-theanine (200mg), glycine (3g), melatonin (low-dose 0.5-1mg), ashwagandha</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Regular exercise (but not within 3 hours of bed), stress management, limiting alcohol</li>
</ul>
