<h1>Alcohol Use Disorder</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A chronic relapsing brain disease characterized by an impaired ability to stop or control alcohol use despite adverse social, occupational, or health consequences.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Craving, loss of control, withdrawal symptoms, tolerance</li>
<li>Liver disease, cardiomyopathy, pancreatitis, neuropathy, Wernicke-Korsakoff</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Withdrawal</strong>: Benzodiazepines (CIWA protocol monitoring)</li>
<li><strong>Pharmacotherapy</strong>: Naltrexone (craving), acamprosate (maintenance), disulfiram (aversion)</li>
<li><strong>Psychosocial</strong>: CBT, motivational interviewing, 12-step, SMART Recovery</li>
<li><strong>Nutritional repletion</strong>: Thiamine (B1 -- critical, give BEFORE glucose), folate, magnesium, zinc</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Thiamine, NAC, B-complex, magnesium, zinc, omega-3</li>
</ul>
