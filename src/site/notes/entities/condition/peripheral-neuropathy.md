<h1>Peripheral Neuropathy</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Peripheral Neuropathy indicates that the nerves carrying signals to and from the central nervous system are damaged. This can affect sensory nerves (pain/temp), motor nerves (muscle control), or autonomic nerves (organ function). It is most commonly a complication of <strong>Diabetes</strong>.</p>
<h2>Common Causes</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Diabetic Neuropathy:</strong> High blood sugar damages nerve fibers.</li>
<li><strong>Vitamin B12 Deficiency:</strong> Essential for the myelin sheath.</li>
<li><strong>Alcoholic Neuropathy:</strong> Direct toxic effect of alcohol and associated malnutrition.</li>
<li><strong>Toxic Neuropathy:</strong> Side effect of certain medications or heavy metals.</li>
</ul>
