<h1>Peripheral Neuropathy</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A condition caused by damage to the nerves outside the brain and spinal cord (peripheral nerves), often resulting in numbness, tingling, and pain.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Numbness and tingling (pins and needles), typically starting in feet/hands</li>
<li>Burning or sharp, stabbing pain</li>
<li>Extreme sensitivity to touch (allodynia)</li>
<li>Muscle weakness and atrophy</li>
<li>Lack of coordination and falls</li>
<li>Loss of reflexes</li>
<li>Autonomic symptoms (orthostatic hypotension, bladder dysfunction, gastroparesis)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Address underlying cause</strong>: Strict glycemic control in diabetic neuropathy, B12 repletion in deficiency, alcohol cessation, chemotherapy dose adjustment</li>
<li><strong>Pain management</strong>: Gabapentin or pregabalin (first-line for neuropathic pain), duloxetine (SNRI), topical capsaicin or lidocaine patches, TCAs (amitriptyline — low dose)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Alpha-lipoic acid (600 mg/day — best evidence in diabetic neuropathy, multiple RCTs), B-complex vitamins (especially B12, B6, methylfolate), acetyl-L-carnitine (2-3 g/day — supports nerve regeneration), NAC (antioxidant nerve protection)</li>
<li><strong>Physical therapy</strong>: Balance training, gait rehabilitation, TENS (transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation), strengthening exercises to compensate for weakness</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Foot care and regular inspection (prevent ulceration), appropriate footwear, fall prevention strategies, regular moderate exercise (improves nerve perfusion)</li>
<li><strong>Emerging</strong>: Scrambler therapy, photobiomodulation (red/infrared light), high-dose thiamine (benfotiamine for diabetic neuropathy)</li>
</ul>
