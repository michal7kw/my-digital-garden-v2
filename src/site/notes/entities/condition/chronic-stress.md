<h1>Chronic Stress</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Chronic stress is a prolonged state of HPA axis activation where cortisol levels remain elevated or the cortisol rhythm becomes dysregulated (flattened diurnal curve). Unlike acute stress (adaptive, time-limited), chronic stress causes allostatic overload — cumulative wear on the body that drives hippocampal atrophy, immune suppression, metabolic syndrome, cardiovascular disease, and accelerated aging. The concept of &quot;allostatic load&quot; (McEwen) captures how repeated stress cycles without adequate recovery progressively damage organ systems. Chronic stress is the #1 modifiable risk factor for most chronic diseases.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Anxiety, irritability, and emotional reactivity</li>
<li>Sleep disturbances (insomnia, early waking, unrefreshing sleep)</li>
<li>Chronic fatigue despite adequate rest</li>
<li>Muscle tension (neck, shoulders, jaw clenching)</li>
<li>Digestive problems (IBS symptoms, reduced motility)</li>
<li>Impaired concentration and memory</li>
<li>Reduced libido</li>
<li>Frequent illness (immune suppression)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>High-pressure work environment or job insecurity</li>
<li>Financial difficulties</li>
<li>Relationship conflict or social isolation</li>
<li>Caregiving burden</li>
<li>Traumatic life events (ACEs in childhood)</li>
<li>Perfectionism and type-A personality traits</li>
<li>Poor sleep hygiene</li>
<li>Excessive social media / news consumption</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Psychotherapy</strong>: CBT, ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy), EMDR for trauma</li>
<li><strong>Mindfulness-based interventions</strong>: MBSR (8-week program reduces cortisol 14-28%), meditation (10+ min daily)</li>
<li><strong>Exercise</strong>: Regular aerobic exercise normalizes HPA axis; acute exercise burns off cortisol</li>
<li><strong>Nature exposure</strong>: Forest bathing (shinrin-yoku), 20+ min in nature reduces cortisol measurably</li>
<li><strong>Social connection</strong>: Oxytocin release buffers cortisol; loneliness is as damaging as smoking</li>
<li><strong>Sleep optimization</strong>: Consistent schedule, 7-9 hours; sleep debt amplifies stress reactivity</li>
<li><strong>Breathwork</strong>: Box breathing, 4-7-8 technique, physiological sigh — direct vagal nerve activation</li>
<li><strong>Adaptogens</strong>: Ashwagandha (KSM-66, 600mg — strongest RCT evidence), Rhodiola rosea, L-theanine, phosphatidylserine</li>
<li><strong>Magnesium</strong>: Glycinate form, 400mg/day — calms HPA axis and improves sleep</li>
</ul>
