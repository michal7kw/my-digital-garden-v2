<h1>Chronic Stress</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Chronic Stress occurs when the body's natural &quot;fight or flight&quot; response is triggered repeatedly or stays on for long periods. This results in the sustained release of <strong>Cortisol</strong> and adrenaline, which can damage nearly every system in the body, particularly the cardiovascular and immune systems.</p>
<h2>Physiological Effects</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>HPA Axis Activation:</strong> Sustained cortisol production.</li>
<li><strong>Immune Suppression:</strong> Increased susceptibility to infections.</li>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular Strain:</strong> Hypertension and increased heart rate.</li>
<li><strong>Metabolic Changes:</strong> Insulin resistance and weight gain (visceral fat).</li>
</ul>
