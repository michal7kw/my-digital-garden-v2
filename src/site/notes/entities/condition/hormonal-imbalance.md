<h1>Hormonal Imbalance</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A condition where there is too much or too little of one or more hormones in the body, which can disrupt many essential processes.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Fatigue and low energy</li>
<li>Unexplained weight gain or loss</li>
<li>Mood swings, anxiety, or depression</li>
<li>Sleep disturbances</li>
<li>Hair loss or thinning</li>
<li>Irregular menstrual periods (in women)</li>
<li>Low libido</li>
<li>Skin changes (acne, dryness)</li>
<li>Temperature dysregulation</li>
<li>Cognitive difficulties (brain fog)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Identify root cause</strong>: Comprehensive hormonal panel (thyroid, cortisol, sex hormones, insulin). Address underlying conditions (thyroid disease, PCOS, adrenal dysfunction, pituitary disorders)</li>
<li><strong>Hormone replacement</strong>: Bioidentical hormone therapy when indicated (estrogen, progesterone, testosterone, thyroid hormone, DHEA) — individualized based on lab results and symptoms</li>
<li><strong>Stress management</strong>: Critical for HPA axis normalization — cortisol dysregulation is a common driver. Meditation, yoga, adequate sleep, time in nature</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Ashwagandha (adaptogen — modulates cortisol and thyroid), vitamin D (steroid hormone precursor, target &gt;40 ng/mL), zinc (testosterone and thyroid synthesis), magnesium (involved in 300+ enzymatic reactions including hormone production), inositol (insulin sensitization, ovarian function)</li>
<li><strong>Diet</strong>: Anti-inflammatory whole foods diet, adequate healthy fats (hormones are synthesized from cholesterol), cruciferous vegetables (support estrogen metabolism via DIM/I3C), fiber (promotes estrogen excretion), limit sugar and refined carbs (insulin dysregulation)</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Regular exercise (improves insulin sensitivity, cortisol regulation, and sex hormone balance), adequate sleep (7-9 hours — GH, cortisol, melatonin rhythms), reduce endocrine disruptor exposure (BPA, phthalates, parabens)</li>
</ul>
