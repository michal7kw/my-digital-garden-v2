<h1>Gallbladder Disease</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A range of conditions affecting the gallbladder, most commonly involving gallstones or inflammation (cholecystitis).</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Upper right abdominal pain (biliary colic), often after fatty meals</li>
<li>Nausea and vomiting</li>
<li>Fever and chills (if cholecystitis or cholangitis)</li>
<li>Jaundice (yellowing of skin/eyes — if bile duct obstruction)</li>
<li>Bloating, indigestion, and gas</li>
<li>Clay-colored stools (biliary obstruction)</li>
<li>Dark urine (conjugated bilirubin)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Surgical</strong>: Laparoscopic cholecystectomy — definitive treatment for symptomatic gallstones and cholecystitis. One of the most common surgeries performed</li>
<li><strong>Medical dissolution</strong>: Ursodeoxycholic acid (UDCA) — for small cholesterol stones in patients unfit for surgery. Slow (6-24 months) and high recurrence rate</li>
<li><strong>Acute management</strong>: IV antibiotics for cholecystitis, ERCP for common bile duct stones, pain management (NSAIDs preferred over opioids for biliary colic)</li>
<li><strong>Dietary</strong>: Low-fat diet (reduces biliary colic episodes), high-fiber diet (reduces gallstone formation), maintain healthy weight, avoid rapid weight loss (increases stone risk)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Vitamin C (reduces cholesterol saturation of bile — epidemiological evidence), curcumin (choleretic — stimulates bile flow), omega-3 fatty acids (improves bile composition), probiotics (gut-liver axis support)</li>
<li><strong>Prevention</strong>: Maintain healthy BMI, gradual weight loss if needed, regular physical activity, adequate fiber intake, moderate coffee consumption (associated with reduced gallstone risk)</li>
</ul>
