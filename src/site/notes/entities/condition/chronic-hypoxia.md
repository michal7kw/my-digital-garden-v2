<h1>Chronic Hypoxia</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Chronic Hypoxia differs from acute hypoxia by its gradual onset and the body's adaptive responses, such as <strong>Polycythemia</strong> (increased red blood cell production). Over time, it leads to structural changes in the pulmonary vasculature and can cause right-sided heart failure.</p>
<h2>Complications</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Pulmonary Hypertension.</strong></li>
<li><strong>Cor Pulmonale:</strong> Right heart failure.</li>
<li><strong>Secondary Polycythemia.</strong></li>
<li><strong>Cognitive Decline.</strong></li>
</ul>
