<h1>Hearing Loss</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A partial or total inability to hear sound in one or both ears, ranging from mild to profound.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Muffled speech and difficulty understanding words, especially in noisy environments</li>
<li>Frequently asking others to speak louder or more slowly</li>
<li>Tinnitus (ringing, buzzing, or hissing in the ears)</li>
<li>Need to increase volume on TV or devices</li>
<li>Social withdrawal, frustration, and cognitive fatigue from listening effort</li>
<li>In children: delayed speech/language development</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Hearing aids</strong>: Digital behind-the-ear (BTE) or in-the-ear (ITE) devices -- first-line for sensorineural hearing loss; modern aids include Bluetooth, directional microphones, and noise reduction</li>
<li><strong>Cochlear implants</strong>: For severe-to-profound bilateral sensorineural hearing loss not benefiting from hearing aids</li>
<li><strong>Medical/surgical</strong>: Tympanoplasty for perforated eardrum, stapedectomy for otosclerosis, PE tubes for chronic otitis media with effusion</li>
<li><strong>Assistive listening devices</strong>: FM systems, captioning, vibrating/visual alerts</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Noise avoidance and ear protection (limit exposure &gt; 85 dB), cardiovascular fitness (improves cochlear blood flow), manage ototoxic medications</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Magnesium (protects against noise-induced damage), NAC (antioxidant cochlear protection), alpha-lipoic acid, omega-3 fatty acids, folate (homocysteine reduction)</li>
<li><strong>Emerging</strong>: Gene therapy, hair cell regeneration research (FX-322), OTC hearing aids now available<br />
