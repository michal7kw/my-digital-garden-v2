<h1>Diabetes</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Diabetes mellitus encompasses a group of metabolic diseases characterized by chronic hyperglycemia. Type 2 diabetes (T2DM) accounts for 90-95% of cases and is characterized by progressive insulin resistance in muscle, liver, and adipose tissue, combined with relative beta cell failure. The pathophysiology involves the &quot;ominous octet&quot; (DeFronzo): muscle insulin resistance, hepatic glucose overproduction, impaired insulin secretion, increased lipolysis, incretin deficiency, increased glucagon, increased renal glucose reabsorption, and brain insulin resistance. Type 1 diabetes (T1DM) is autoimmune destruction of pancreatic beta cells causing absolute insulin deficiency. T2DM is largely preventable — the DPP trial showed 58% risk reduction with lifestyle intervention (vs. 31% with metformin).</p>
<h2>Subtypes</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Type 2 (T2DM)</strong> — Insulin resistance + relative beta cell failure; 90-95% of cases</li>
<li><strong>Type 1 (T1DM)</strong> — Autoimmune beta cell destruction; absolute insulin deficiency; 5-10%</li>
<li><strong>Gestational Diabetes (GDM)</strong> — Onset during pregnancy; increases future T2DM risk 7-fold</li>
<li><strong>LADA</strong> — Latent autoimmune diabetes of adults; slow-onset T1DM in adults</li>
<li><strong>MODY</strong> — Monogenic diabetes; single gene mutations affecting beta cell function</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Lifestyle (foundation for all)</strong>: Weight loss (5-10% body weight significantly improves insulin sensitivity), regular exercise (150 min/week — both aerobic and resistance), Mediterranean or low-glycemic diet</li>
<li><strong>First-line medication</strong>: Metformin (reduces hepatic glucose output, no weight gain, cardioprotective)</li>
<li><strong>GLP-1 receptor agonists</strong>: Semaglutide, liraglutide (weight loss + glycemic control + cardiovascular benefit)</li>
<li><strong>SGLT2 inhibitors</strong>: Empagliflozin, dapagliflozin (cardiovascular and renal protection)</li>
<li><strong>Insulin</strong>: Required for T1DM; added in advanced T2DM when oral agents insufficient</li>
<li><strong>Adjunctive supplements</strong>: Berberine (metformin-like effects), chromium, magnesium, alpha-lipoic acid</li>
</ul>
<h2>Complications</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Macrovascular</strong>: Coronary artery disease (2-4x risk), stroke, peripheral arterial disease</li>
<li><strong>Microvascular</strong>: Retinopathy (leading cause of blindness), nephropathy, neuropathy</li>
<li><strong>Other</strong>: Diabetic foot ulcers, increased infection risk, non-alcoholic fatty liver disease</li>
<li><strong>Emerging</strong>: Accelerated cognitive decline, increased Alzheimer's risk (&quot;type 3 diabetes&quot;)</li>
</ul>
