<h1>Cocaine-Related Disorders</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Cocaine-Related Disorders include acute toxicity, addiction (Use Disorder), and withdrawal syndromes associated with cocaine, a powerful central nervous system stimulant. Cocaine blocks the reuptake of <strong>dopamine</strong>, serotonin, and norepinephrine, leading to intense euphoria and increased energy, followed by severe depletion and craving.</p>
<h2>Complications</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular:</strong> Heart attack, arrhythmia, stroke, sudden cardiac death.</li>
<li><strong>Neurological:</strong> Seizures, headaches, cognitive impairment.</li>
<li><strong>Psychiatric:</strong> Anxiety, paranoia, psychosis (&quot;cocaine bugs&quot;), depression.</li>
<li><strong>Respiratory:</strong> &quot;Crack lung&quot; (if smoked), nasal septum perforation (if snorted).</li>
</ul>
