<h1>Coronary Artery Disease</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Coronary artery disease (CAD) is atherosclerotic plaque buildup in the coronary arteries that supply the myocardium. Progressive narrowing causes stable angina; plaque rupture causes acute coronary syndrome (unstable angina, NSTEMI, STEMI). The #1 killer worldwide. Atherosclerosis begins with endothelial dysfunction and oxidized LDL infiltration — it is fundamentally an inflammatory process (hs-CRP independently predicts events).</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Stable angina: Chest pressure/tightness with exertion, relieved by rest or nitroglycerin</li>
<li>ACS: Crushing chest pain, radiation to left arm/jaw, diaphoresis, dyspnea, nausea</li>
<li>May present as sudden cardiac death in 15-20% (no prior symptoms)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Lifestyle (foundation)</strong>: Mediterranean diet, regular exercise (cardiac rehab post-event), smoking cessation, weight management, stress reduction</li>
<li><strong>Medications</strong>: Statin (high-intensity — atorvastatin 40-80mg), aspirin (75-100mg), ACE inhibitor (if hypertension/diabetes/LV dysfunction), beta-blocker (post-MI), ezetimibe (if statin alone insufficient), PCSK9 inhibitor (for very high risk)</li>
<li><strong>Revascularization</strong>: PCI with drug-eluting stent (for 1-2 vessel disease), CABG (for 3-vessel or left main disease)</li>
<li><strong>Secondary prevention targets</strong>: LDL &lt;70 mg/dL (or &lt;55 for very high risk), BP &lt;130/80, HbA1c &lt;7%, non-smoking</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Omega-3 (EPA 4g/day — REDUCE-IT), CoQ10 (statin-depleted), vitamin K2 (arterial calcification), magnesium</li>
</ul>
