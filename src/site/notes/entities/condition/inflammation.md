<h1>Inflammation</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Inflammation is the immune system's response to tissue damage, infection, or toxins. Acute inflammation is protective — neutrophils and macrophages eliminate threats, and the tissue heals. But chronic low-grade inflammation (&quot;inflammaging&quot;) is now recognized as the common denominator in virtually all age-related diseases: cardiovascular disease, cancer, neurodegeneration, diabetes, autoimmune conditions, and depression. Chronic inflammation is driven by visceral adiposity (adipocytes secrete TNF-alpha, IL-6), gut dysbiosis (endotoxemia), chronic stress (cortisol resistance), poor sleep, and environmental toxins. The NF-kB pathway is the master inflammatory switch.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Acute</strong>: Redness (rubor), heat (calor), swelling (tumor), pain (dolor), loss of function</li>
<li><strong>Chronic</strong>: Often subclinical — fatigue, body aches, brain fog, weight gain, frequent infections, skin problems, digestive issues, depression/anxiety</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Biomarkers</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>hs-CRP</strong>: Best validated systemic marker (&lt;1 mg/L optimal, &gt;3 mg/L high risk)</li>
<li><strong>IL-6</strong>: Pro-inflammatory cytokine upstream of CRP production</li>
<li><strong>TNF-alpha</strong>: Master inflammatory cytokine from macrophages and adipocytes</li>
<li><strong>ESR</strong>: Non-specific but useful for monitoring inflammatory conditions</li>
<li><strong>Fibrinogen</strong>: Acute phase reactant and cardiovascular risk marker</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Anti-inflammatory diet</strong>: Mediterranean pattern, omega-3 rich foods, polyphenols, fiber. Eliminate: refined sugar, seed oils, processed foods, excess alcohol</li>
<li><strong>Exercise</strong>: 150+ min/week moderate exercise reduces CRP by 20-30%</li>
<li><strong>Stress management</strong>: Chronic stress → cortisol resistance → unchecked inflammation. Meditation, nature exposure, social connection</li>
<li><strong>Sleep optimization</strong>: Sleep deprivation increases IL-6 and CRP within 1 week</li>
<li><strong>Weight loss</strong>: Visceral fat is the #1 source of inflammatory cytokines; 5-10% weight loss significantly reduces CRP</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Curcumin (NF-kB inhibitor), omega-3 (resolvin/protectin production), quercetin, resveratrol, NAC (glutathione precursor)</li>
<li><strong>Medications</strong>: NSAIDs (short-term), low-dose aspirin (cardiovascular), colchicine (LoDoCo2 trial for cardiovascular inflammation)</li>
</ul>
