<h1>Allergy</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>An exaggerated immune response to Ordinarily harmless substances (allergens), characterized by the production of IgE antibodies and release of inflammatory mediators like histamine.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Allergic rhinitis: sneezing, nasal congestion, itchy/watery eyes</li>
<li>Allergic dermatitis: hives, eczema flares, contact dermatitis</li>
<li>Anaphylaxis: throat swelling, hypotension, dyspnea -- medical emergency</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Avoidance</strong>: Identify and avoid triggers (allergy testing: skin prick, specific IgE)</li>
<li><strong>Antihistamines</strong>: H1 blockers (cetirizine, loratadine -- non-sedating)</li>
<li><strong>Nasal corticosteroids</strong>: Fluticasone, mometasone -- first-line for allergic rhinitis</li>
<li><strong>Immunotherapy</strong>: Subcutaneous or sublingual -- 3-5 year courses, only disease-modifying treatment</li>
<li><strong>Biologics</strong>: Omalizumab (anti-IgE), dupilumab for atopic dermatitis</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Quercetin (mast cell stabilizer), vitamin C, probiotics, NAC</li>
</ul>
