<h1>Edema</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Edema is the abnormal accumulation of fluid in the interstitial tissue spaces, causing visible and palpable swelling. Governed by Starling forces: increased capillary hydrostatic pressure (heart failure, venous insufficiency), decreased plasma oncotic pressure (nephrotic syndrome, liver disease/hypoalbuminemia), increased capillary permeability (inflammation, sepsis), or impaired lymphatic drainage (lymphedema). Can be localized (unilateral -- DVT, cellulitis, lymphedema) or generalized (bilateral/anasarca -- systemic disease). Identifying the underlying cause is essential for effective treatment.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Swelling of extremities, most commonly legs and ankles (pitting edema leaves indentation with pressure)</li>
<li>Taut, shiny, or stretched-appearing skin</li>
<li>Rapid weight gain (1-2 kg/day indicates significant fluid retention)</li>
<li>Decreased flexibility and range of motion in affected joints</li>
<li>Periorbital edema (renal causes, especially nephrotic syndrome)</li>
<li>Pulmonary edema: progressive dyspnea, orthopnea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea, pink frothy sputum</li>
<li>Ascites: abdominal distension, shifting dullness, fluid wave</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Treat underlying cause (primary strategy)</strong>: Heart failure optimization (ACE inhibitors, ARBs, SGLT2 inhibitors, beta-blockers), renal disease management, liver disease treatment, albumin infusion for severe hypoalbuminemia</li>
<li><strong>Diuretics</strong>: Loop diuretics (furosemide, bumetanide) for moderate-severe edema; thiazides for mild edema; potassium-sparing (spironolactone) for cirrhotic ascites and heart failure; combination therapy for diuretic resistance</li>
<li><strong>Sodium restriction</strong>: Limit to &lt; 2g/day sodium diet for all causes of generalized edema</li>
<li><strong>Compression therapy</strong>: Graduated compression stockings (20-30 or 30-40 mmHg) for venous insufficiency and lymphedema; complete decongestive therapy (CDT) for lymphedema</li>
<li><strong>Elevation</strong>: Elevate affected limbs above heart level several times daily</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Regular exercise (calf muscle pump activation), weight management, avoid prolonged standing or sitting, adequate protein intake, limit alcohol</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Magnesium (mild diuretic effect, electrolyte balance), potassium (if depleted from diuretics), horse chestnut extract (aescin -- venous insufficiency, reduces capillary permeability), vitamin B6 (mild diuretic), dandelion leaf extract<br />
