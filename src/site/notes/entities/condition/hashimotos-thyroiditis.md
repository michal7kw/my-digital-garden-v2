<h1>Hashimotos Thyroiditis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A chronic autoimmune disorder where the immune system attacks and gradually destroys the thyroid gland, leading to hypothyroidism.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Fatigue and sluggishness</li>
<li>Unexplained weight gain</li>
<li>Increased sensitivity to cold</li>
<li>Constipation</li>
<li>Dry skin and thinning hair</li>
<li>Joint and muscle pain</li>
<li>Puffy face and periorbital edema</li>
<li>Depression and memory problems</li>
<li>Goiter (enlarged thyroid)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Thyroid hormone replacement</strong>: Levothyroxine (T4) — first-line; dose titrated to normalize TSH. Some patients benefit from combination T4/T3 therapy (liothyronine)</li>
<li><strong>Immune modulation</strong>: Selenium supplementation (200 mcg/day) reduces TPO antibodies in multiple RCTs. Vitamin D repletion to &gt;40 ng/mL supports immune regulation</li>
<li><strong>Dietary</strong>: Gluten-free diet in subset with celiac/gluten sensitivity. Anti-inflammatory diet rich in omega-3, vegetables, and fermented foods. Moderate iodine intake (excess can worsen)</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Stress management (cortisol elevation worsens thyroid conversion), adequate sleep, regular moderate exercise</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Zinc (supports T4-to-T3 conversion), omega-3 fatty acids (anti-inflammatory), inositol (supports TSH signaling), iron if deficient (required for thyroid peroxidase function)</li>
<li><strong>Monitoring</strong>: Regular TSH, Free T4, Free T3, and TPO antibody testing every 6-12 months</li>
</ul>
