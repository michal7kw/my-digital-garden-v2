<h1>Type 1 Diabetes</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Type 1 diabetes (T1DM) results from autoimmune destruction of insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreatic islets of Langerhans, mediated by autoreactive CD8+ T cells. Autoantibodies (anti-GAD, anti-IA2, anti-ZnT8, anti-insulin) can be detected years before clinical onset. Symptoms typically appear when 80-90% of beta cells are destroyed. Unlike T2DM, T1DM is not caused by lifestyle factors and cannot be prevented with diet or exercise. The landmark DCCT/EDIC trial established that intensive insulin therapy (targeting HbA1c &lt;7%) dramatically reduces microvascular complications.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Polyuria (frequent urination), polydipsia (excessive thirst), polyphagia (excessive hunger)</li>
<li>Rapid weight loss despite increased appetite</li>
<li>Fatigue, blurred vision</li>
<li><strong>DKA presentation</strong> (in 30% at diagnosis): Nausea/vomiting, abdominal pain, Kussmaul breathing, fruity breath odor, altered consciousness — medical emergency</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Family history (6% risk if sibling affected; 30-50% concordance in identical twins)</li>
<li>HLA-DR3/DR4 genotype (accounts for 50% of genetic risk)</li>
<li>Northern European descent (highest incidence: Finland, Sardinia)</li>
<li>Enteroviral infections (coxsackievirus B — molecular mimicry)</li>
<li>Vitamin D deficiency in early life (emerging evidence)</li>
<li>Early introduction of cow's milk protein (controversial)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Insulin (essential, lifelong)</strong>: Basal-bolus regimen (long-acting + mealtime rapid-acting) or insulin pump (CSII). No oral medication can replace insulin in T1DM</li>
<li><strong>Technology</strong>: Continuous glucose monitors (CGM — Dexcom, Libre), insulin pumps, hybrid closed-loop systems (&quot;artificial pancreas&quot;)</li>
<li><strong>Monitoring</strong>: HbA1c target &lt;7% (DCCT evidence); time-in-range &gt;70% (CGM metric)</li>
<li><strong>Screening</strong>: Annual screening for thyroid disease, celiac disease (autoimmune clustering)</li>
<li><strong>Immunotherapy</strong>: Teplizumab (anti-CD3 antibody — FDA-approved to delay T1DM onset in high-risk individuals by 2-3 years)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Vitamin D (immune modulation — may slow beta cell loss), omega-3, chromium (adjunctive glucose management)</li>
</ul>
