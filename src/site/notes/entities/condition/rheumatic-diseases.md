<h1>Rheumatic Diseases</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Rheumatic diseases (Rheumatism) affect the joints, tendons, ligaments, bones, and muscles. Many are <strong>autoimmune</strong> in nature, where the immune system attacks the body's own tissues. They are typically chronic and can lead to significant disability if untreated.</p>
<h2>Common Types</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Osteoarthritis:</strong> Degenerative joint disease.</li>
<li><strong>Rheumatoid Arthritis:</strong> Autoimmune joint inflammation.</li>
<li><strong>Lupus (SLE):</strong> Systemic autoimmune disease.</li>
<li><strong>Gout:</strong> Metabolic arthritis (uric acid).</li>
<li><strong>Ankylosing Spondylitis:</strong> Inflammatory spine disease.</li>
</ul>
