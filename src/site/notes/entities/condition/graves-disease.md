<h1>Graves Disease</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>An autoimmune disorder that causes overactivity of the thyroid gland (hyperthyroidism) through the production of stimulating antibodies.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Heat intolerance and excessive sweating</li>
<li>Anxiety, irritability, and emotional lability</li>
<li>Hand tremor</li>
<li>Weight loss despite increased appetite</li>
<li>Bulging eyes (Graves' ophthalmopathy) — in ~30% of patients</li>
<li>Rapid or irregular heartbeat (palpitations)</li>
<li>Goiter (diffuse thyroid enlargement)</li>
<li>Pretibial myxedema (thickened skin on shins — rare)</li>
<li>Fatigue and muscle weakness</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Antithyroid drugs</strong>: Methimazole (first-line) — block thyroid hormone synthesis. 12-18 month course with 40-50% remission rate. Monitor for agranulocytosis (rare but serious)</li>
<li><strong>Radioactive iodine (RAI)</strong>: Definitive therapy — ablates overactive thyroid tissue. Most common treatment in the US. Results in hypothyroidism in most patients</li>
<li><strong>Thyroidectomy</strong>: Near-total or total thyroidectomy for large goiters, ophthalmopathy, or failed medical therapy</li>
<li><strong>Beta-blockers</strong>: Propranolol or atenolol for symptomatic control (tachycardia, tremor, anxiety)</li>
<li><strong>Ophthalmopathy management</strong>: Selenium (200 mcg/day — reduces eye disease progression in mild cases, EUGOGO trial), lubricating eye drops, prisms, orbital decompression surgery for severe cases, teprotumumab (anti-IGF-1R — FDA-approved for thyroid eye disease)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Selenium (anti-inflammatory, thyroid protective), vitamin D (immunomodulatory), L-carnitine (reduces hyperthyroid symptoms), omega-3 fatty acids</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Smoking cessation (strongest modifiable risk factor for ophthalmopathy), stress management, protective eyewear</li>
</ul>
