<h1>Knee Osteoarthritis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The most common form of arthritis, affecting 250+ million people worldwide. Knee OA involves progressive loss of articular cartilage, subchondral bone remodeling, osteophyte formation, and synovial inflammation. No longer considered simple &quot;wear and tear&quot; — it is an active inflammatory process with metabolic, mechanical, and genetic components. Obesity is the strongest modifiable risk factor (each 5 kg weight gain increases risk 36%).</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Knee pain with activity (worse with stairs, squatting)</li>
<li>Morning stiffness (&lt;30 min, unlike RA which is &gt;1 hour)</li>
<li>Joint crepitus (grinding sensation)</li>
<li>Reduced range of motion</li>
<li>Joint swelling (effusion)</li>
<li>Bony enlargement (osteophytes)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Exercise (strongest evidence)</strong>: Quadriceps strengthening, aquatic therapy, cycling — reduces pain equivalent to NSAIDs</li>
<li><strong>Weight loss</strong>: 5-10% body weight reduces pain scores by 50%</li>
<li><strong>Physical therapy</strong>: Manual therapy, gait training, bracing/orthotics</li>
<li><strong>Medications</strong>: Topical NSAIDs (first-line), oral NSAIDs (short courses), duloxetine (chronic pain), intra-articular corticosteroids</li>
<li><strong>Regenerative</strong>: PRP (platelet-rich plasma) — moderate evidence, hyaluronic acid injections</li>
<li><strong>Surgery</strong>: Total knee replacement for severe disease refractory to conservative measures</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Collagen peptides (type II), glucosamine sulfate (1500mg), curcumin, omega-3, vitamin C</li>
</ul>
