<h1>Sepsis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Sepsis is life-threatening organ dysfunction caused by a dysregulated host immune response to infection. Defined by the Sepsis-3 criteria as a suspected infection with an acute increase of 2+ SOFA score points. Septic shock is a subset with vasopressor requirement and lactate &gt; 2 mmol/L despite adequate fluid resuscitation. Leading cause of in-hospital death, with mortality rates of 20-40% (50%+ for septic shock). The &quot;golden hour&quot; principle: each hour of delay in antibiotic administration increases mortality by 7-8%.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Fever (&gt; 38.3C) or hypothermia (&lt; 36C)</li>
<li>Tachycardia (HR &gt; 90), tachypnea (RR &gt; 20), altered mental status</li>
<li>Hypotension (SBP &lt; 90 or MAP &lt; 65) -- hallmark of septic shock</li>
<li>Warm, flushed skin (early/warm shock) progressing to cold, mottled, cyanotic extremities (late/cold shock)</li>
<li>Oliguria or anuria (acute kidney injury)</li>
<li>Petechiae or purpura (DIC, meningococcemia)</li>
<li>Multi-organ dysfunction: ARDS, hepatic dysfunction, coagulopathy, encephalopathy</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Early goal-directed therapy (Hour-1 Bundle)</strong>: Measure lactate, obtain blood cultures before antibiotics, administer broad-spectrum IV antibiotics within 1 hour, begin IV crystalloid resuscitation (30 mL/kg for hypotension or lactate &gt;= 4), start vasopressors for refractory hypotension (norepinephrine first-line)</li>
<li><strong>Source control</strong>: Drain abscesses, remove infected devices, debride necrotic tissue -- ideally within 6-12 hours</li>
<li><strong>Vasopressors</strong>: Norepinephrine (first-line), vasopressin (adjunctive), epinephrine (if additional needed); target MAP &gt;= 65 mmHg</li>
<li><strong>Corticosteroids</strong>: Low-dose hydrocortisone (200 mg/day) for refractory septic shock not responding to fluids and vasopressors</li>
<li><strong>Organ support</strong>: Mechanical ventilation for ARDS (lung-protective strategy), renal replacement therapy for AKI, blood product support for DIC</li>
<li><strong>De-escalation</strong>: Narrow antibiotic spectrum based on culture results; procalcitonin-guided duration (typically 7-10 days)</li>
<li><strong>Recovery/lifestyle</strong>: Post-sepsis rehabilitation (physical, cognitive, psychological), nutritional optimization, vaccination to prevent recurrent infections</li>
<li><strong>Supplements (adjunctive, not primary therapy)</strong>: Vitamin C (controversial -- LOVIT trial showed no benefit, but earlier trials suggested harm reduction), thiamine (cofactor for aerobic metabolism), vitamin D (immune function in deficient patients), selenium, zinc<br />
