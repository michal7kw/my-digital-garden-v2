<h1>PCOS</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is the most common endocrine disorder in women of reproductive age (6-12% prevalence). Insulin resistance is present in 70-80% of cases and drives hyperandrogenism. Rotterdam criteria require 2 of 3: oligo/anovulation, hyperandrogenism, polycystic ovaries. PCOS significantly increases risk of type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, endometrial cancer, and depression.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Irregular or absent periods, hirsutism, acne (jawline), androgenic alopecia</li>
<li>Weight gain (central), difficulty conceiving, acanthosis nigricans</li>
<li>Mood disturbances (anxiety, depression — 40% comorbidity)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Family history (70% heritability), obesity, insulin resistance, sedentary lifestyle, high-glycemic diet</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Lifestyle (first-line)</strong>: 5-10% weight loss restores ovulation. Low-glycemic/Mediterranean diet. Combined aerobic + resistance training</li>
<li><strong>Insulin sensitizers</strong>: Metformin, inositol (myo-inositol 4g + D-chiro-inositol 0.4g daily)</li>
<li><strong>Hormonal</strong>: Combined OCs (regulate cycles, reduce androgens), spironolactone (anti-androgen)</li>
<li><strong>Fertility</strong>: Letrozole (first-line ovulation induction), IVF</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Inositol, berberine, vitamin D, zinc, omega-3, NAC</li>
</ul>
