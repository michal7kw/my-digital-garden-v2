<h1>Weight Gain</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Weight gain results from chronic energy surplus, but the modern understanding goes far beyond &quot;calories in, calories out.&quot; Hormonal regulation (insulin, leptin, ghrelin, cortisol, thyroid hormones), gut microbiome composition, sleep quality, stress, medications, and genetic predisposition all modulate energy balance. Obesity (BMI &gt;30) affects 42% of US adults and is a leading driver of type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, NAFLD, sleep apnea, certain cancers, and reduced lifespan. The adipose tissue itself is an endocrine organ secreting inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-6) and hormones (leptin, adiponectin) that create systemic metabolic dysfunction.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Progressive increase in body fat (especially visceral/abdominal)</li>
<li>Increased waist circumference (&gt;40&quot; men, &gt;35&quot; women indicates metabolic risk)</li>
<li>Fatigue and reduced exercise tolerance</li>
<li>Joint pain (weight-bearing joints)</li>
<li>Shortness of breath with activity</li>
<li>Skin changes (striae, acanthosis nigricans)</li>
<li>Sleep disruption (sleep apnea)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Caloric surplus and ultra-processed food consumption</li>
<li>Sedentary lifestyle</li>
<li>Poor sleep (&lt;7 hours — increases ghrelin, decreases leptin)</li>
<li>Chronic stress (elevated cortisol promotes visceral fat deposition)</li>
<li>Genetic predisposition (FTO, MC4R variants — 40-70% heritable)</li>
<li>Medications (antipsychotics, SSRIs, corticosteroids, insulin, beta-blockers)</li>
<li>Gut dysbiosis (reduced Bacteroidetes:Firmicutes ratio)</li>
<li>Hypothyroidism</li>
<li>PCOS</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dietary</strong>: Caloric deficit of 500-750 kcal/day for 1-2 lb/week loss. Mediterranean, low-carb, and intermittent fasting all effective when sustained. Protein (1.2-1.6 g/kg) preserves muscle during weight loss</li>
<li><strong>Exercise</strong>: Combination of aerobic (150-300 min/week) + resistance training (2-3x/week). Resistance training preserves lean mass during caloric deficit</li>
<li><strong>Behavioral</strong>: CBT for binge eating, mindful eating, food journaling, stress management</li>
<li><strong>GLP-1 receptor agonists</strong>: Semaglutide (Wegovy — 15-17% body weight loss), tirzepatide (Mounjaro — 20-25% loss). Game-changing pharmacotherapy</li>
<li><strong>Bariatric surgery</strong>: For BMI &gt;40 or &gt;35 with comorbidities; 25-35% sustained weight loss</li>
<li><strong>Sleep optimization</strong>: 7-9 hours; sleep restriction increases hunger hormones</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Berberine (AMPK activation), chromium (insulin sensitivity), fiber/psyllium (satiety), green tea extract, probiotics</li>
</ul>
