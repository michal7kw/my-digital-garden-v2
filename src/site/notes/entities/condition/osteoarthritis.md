<h1>Osteoarthritis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Osteoarthritis (OA) is the most common form of arthritis, affecting over 300 million people worldwide. Once considered a simple &quot;wear and tear&quot; disease, OA is now understood as a whole-joint disease involving cartilage degradation, subchondral bone remodeling, osteophyte formation, synovial inflammation, and changes in periarticular tissues. It has inflammatory, mechanical, and metabolic components that interact in complex ways.</p>
<p>The hallmark symptom is joint pain that worsens with activity and improves with rest, distinguishing it from inflammatory arthritis where morning stiffness is prolonged. Knee, hip, and hand joints are most commonly affected. Obesity is a particularly important modifiable risk factor for knee OA, both through mechanical loading and systemic metabolic inflammation. First-line management centers on exercise, weight management, and physical therapy. Pharmacological options include topical and oral NSAIDs, intra-articular injections, and duloxetine for chronic pain. Joint replacement surgery is reserved for end-stage disease refractory to conservative management.</p>
