<h1>Parkinson's Disease</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A progressive neurodegenerative disorder characterized by motor symptoms including tremor, rigidity, and bradykinesia, caused by dopaminergic neuron loss in the substantia nigra and alpha-synuclein aggregation.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Resting tremor (often starts unilaterally in hand — &quot;pill rolling&quot;)</li>
<li>Bradykinesia (slowness of movement)</li>
<li>Rigidity (cogwheel rigidity)</li>
<li>Postural instability and gait disturbance (shuffling gait, festination)</li>
<li>Non-motor: Anosmia (loss of smell — often earliest symptom), REM sleep behavior disorder, constipation, depression, cognitive decline</li>
<li>Autonomic dysfunction (orthostatic hypotension, urinary urgency)</li>
<li>Masked facies (reduced facial expression)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dopamine replacement</strong>: Levodopa/carbidopa (Sinemet) — most effective symptomatic therapy. Dose titrated gradually. &quot;Wearing off&quot; and dyskinesias develop over years</li>
<li><strong>Dopamine agonists</strong>: Pramipexole, ropinirole, rotigotine patch — used as monotherapy early or adjunct to levodopa. Monitor for impulse control disorders</li>
<li><strong>MAO-B inhibitors</strong>: Rasagiline, selegiline, safinamide — mildly effective as monotherapy or levodopa adjunct. Possible neuroprotective properties</li>
<li><strong>COMT inhibitors</strong>: Entacapone, opicapone — extend levodopa duration, reduce &quot;off&quot; time</li>
<li><strong>Deep brain stimulation (DBS)</strong>: Subthalamic nucleus or globus pallidus — for motor fluctuations refractory to medication. Significant improvement in motor symptoms and quality of life</li>
<li><strong>Exercise</strong>: Regular vigorous exercise (cycling, boxing, dance, tai chi) — strong evidence for slowing motor decline and improving balance. 150+ min/week</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: CoQ10 (mitochondrial support), omega-3 DHA (neuroprotective), creatine (energy metabolism), NAC (glutathione precursor), curcumin (anti-inflammatory, alpha-synuclein aggregation inhibitor)</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Sleep optimization, constipation management (fiber, hydration), fall prevention, speech therapy (LSVT LOUD), occupational therapy</li>
</ul>
