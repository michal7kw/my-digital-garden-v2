<h1>Liver Disease</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A broad range of conditions that damage the liver and impair its essential functions, including detoxification, metabolism, and bile production.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes) from bilirubin accumulation</li>
<li>Abdominal pain and swelling (ascites) in advanced disease</li>
<li>Chronic fatigue and weakness</li>
<li>Dark urine, pale stools, and pruritus (itchy skin)</li>
<li>Easy bruising and bleeding (impaired clotting factor synthesis)</li>
<li>Spider angiomata, palmar erythema, and gynecomastia in cirrhosis</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cause-specific therapy</strong>: Antivirals for hepatitis B/C (tenofovir, sofosbuvir-based regimens -- 95%+ SVR for HCV), alcohol abstinence for alcoholic liver disease, weight loss for NAFLD/NASH</li>
<li><strong>Cirrhosis management</strong>: Diuretics (spironolactone + furosemide) for ascites, lactulose for hepatic encephalopathy, beta-blockers (propranolol) for portal hypertension/variceal prophylaxis</li>
<li><strong>Liver transplant</strong>: For decompensated cirrhosis (MELD score guides allocation), hepatocellular carcinoma within Milan criteria, acute liver failure</li>
<li><strong>Emerging therapies</strong>: Resmetirom (THR-beta agonist, FDA-approved for NASH with fibrosis), GLP-1 agonists showing hepatic benefit</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Alcohol elimination, Mediterranean diet, regular exercise (reduces hepatic steatosis), hepatitis A/B vaccination, avoidance of hepatotoxic drugs</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: NAC (glutathione precursor -- hepatoprotective), milk thistle/silymarin, alpha-lipoic acid, vitamin E (for NASH), glutathione, sulforaphane<br />
