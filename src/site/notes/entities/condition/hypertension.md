<h1>Hypertension</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Hypertension (high blood pressure) is the single largest contributor to global cardiovascular mortality. It is defined as systolic BP ≥130 mmHg or diastolic BP ≥80 mmHg (2017 ACC/AHA guidelines). Approximately 90-95% of cases are &quot;essential&quot; (primary) hypertension with no identifiable cause — resulting from the interaction of genetic predisposition, excess sodium intake, obesity, physical inactivity, and chronic stress. The remaining 5-10% are secondary hypertension caused by identifiable conditions (renal artery stenosis, aldosteronism, pheochromocytoma, sleep apnea). Untreated hypertension damages the endothelium, accelerates atherosclerosis, and causes end-organ damage to the heart, brain, kidneys, and eyes.</p>

<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Usually asymptomatic (&quot;silent killer&quot;) until end-organ damage occurs</li>
<li><strong>When symptomatic</strong>: Headache (especially occipital, morning), dizziness, visual changes, chest pain, dyspnea</li>
<li><strong>Hypertensive emergency</strong>: Severe headache, confusion, nausea/vomiting, seizures, chest pain</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Excess dietary sodium (&gt;2,300 mg/day)</li>
<li>Obesity (BMI &gt;30) — each 10 kg weight gain raises BP ~3 mmHg</li>
<li>Physical inactivity</li>
<li>Excess alcohol consumption (&gt;2 drinks/day)</li>
<li>Family history (30-50% heritable)</li>
<li>Age (arterial stiffness increases with age)</li>
<li>Chronic psychological stress (HPA axis activation)</li>
<li>Sleep apnea</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Lifestyle (first-line for all stages)</strong>: DASH diet, sodium restriction (&lt;1,500 mg/day), regular exercise (150 min/week), weight loss, alcohol moderation, stress management</li>
<li><strong>First-line medications</strong>: ACE inhibitors (lisinopril), ARBs (losartan), calcium channel blockers (amlodipine), thiazide diuretics (chlorthalidone)</li>
<li><strong>Target</strong>: &lt;130/80 mmHg for most adults (SPRINT trial evidence)</li>
<li><strong>Resistant hypertension</strong>: Spironolactone as fourth agent, renal denervation in severe cases</li>
</ul>
<h2>Complications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Left ventricular hypertrophy and heart failure</li>
<li>Coronary artery disease and myocardial infarction</li>
<li>Stroke (ischemic and hemorrhagic)</li>
<li>Chronic kidney disease</li>
<li>Retinopathy</li>
<li>Aortic aneurysm</li>
</ul>
