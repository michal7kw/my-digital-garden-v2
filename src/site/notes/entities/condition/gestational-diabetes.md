<h1>Gestational Diabetes</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Gestational Diabetes Mellitus (GDM) is high blood sugar (hyperglycemia) that develops during pregnancy and usually disappears after giving birth. It occurs because the placenta produces hormones that can cause <strong>insulin resistance</strong>. If the pancreas cannot make enough insulin to overcome this resistance, blood glucose levels rise.</p>
<h2>Complications</h2>
<p><strong>For the Baby:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Macrosomia (excessive birth weight).</li>
<li>Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) after birth.</li>
<li>Respiratory distress syndrome.</li>
<li>Increased risk of Type 2 Diabetes later in life.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>For the Mother:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Preeclampsia (high blood pressure).</li>
<li>C-section delivery.</li>
<li>Higher risk of developing Type 2 Diabetes post-pregnancy.</li>
</ul>
