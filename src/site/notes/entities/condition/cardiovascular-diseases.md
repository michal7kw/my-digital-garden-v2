<h1>Cardiovascular Diseases</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Cardiovascular Diseases (CVDs) are the leading cause of death globally. They encompass a wide range of conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels. <strong>Atherosclerosis</strong> (plaque buildup in arteries) is the underlying cause of many CVDs.</p>
<h2>Common Types</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Coronary Heart Disease (CHD):</strong> Disease of the blood vessels supplying the heart muscle.</li>
<li><strong>Cerebrovascular Disease:</strong> Disease of the blood vessels supplying the brain (Stroke).</li>
<li><strong>Peripheral Arterial Disease:</strong> Disease of blood vessels supplying arms and legs.</li>
<li><strong>Rheumatic Heart Disease:</strong> Damage to the heart muscle and valves from rheumatic fever.</li>
<li><strong>Congenital Heart Disease:</strong> Malformations of heart structure existing at birth.</li>
<li><strong>Deep Vein Thrombosis and Pulmonary Embolism.</strong></li>
</ul>
