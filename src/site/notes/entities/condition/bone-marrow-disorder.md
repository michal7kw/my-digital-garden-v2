<h1>Bone Marrow Disorder</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A group of disorders affecting the bone marrow's ability to produce healthy blood cells (red cells, white cells, or platelets).</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Fatigue, weakness, and pallor (anemia from reduced red blood cell production)</li>
<li>Easy bruising, petechiae, and mucosal bleeding (thrombocytopenia — low platelets)</li>
<li>Frequent or severe infections, fever, mouth ulcers (neutropenia — low white blood cells)</li>
<li>Bone pain (marrow expansion or infiltration)</li>
<li>Night sweats and unexplained weight loss (systemic symptoms)</li>
<li>Splenomegaly and hepatomegaly (extramedullary hematopoiesis)</li>
<li>Shortness of breath and exercise intolerance</li>
<li>Dizziness and tachycardia (compensatory response to anemia)</li>
<li>Lymphadenopathy (in lymphoproliferative disorders)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Supportive care</strong>: Red blood cell transfusions for symptomatic anemia (Hb &lt;7-8 g/dL), platelet transfusions for bleeding or severe thrombocytopenia (&lt;10,000/uL), growth factors (erythropoietin, G-CSF for neutropenia, thrombopoietin receptor agonists for thrombocytopenia)</li>
<li><strong>Aplastic anemia</strong>: Immunosuppressive therapy (antithymocyte globulin + cyclosporine) for non-transplant candidates, allogeneic stem cell transplant for severe disease in younger patients (&lt;40 with matched donor), eltrombopag (adjunctive)</li>
<li><strong>Myelodysplastic syndromes</strong>: Hypomethylating agents (azacitidine, decitabine), lenalidomide (for del(5q)), luspatercept (for ring sideroblasts), stem cell transplant for high-risk disease</li>
<li><strong>Neutropenia management</strong>: Infection prevention (hand hygiene, food safety, avoid crowded places), prophylactic antibiotics and antifungals for severe neutropenia (ANC &lt;500), G-CSF (filgrastim) to stimulate neutrophil production</li>
<li><strong>Thrombocytopenia management</strong>: Avoid NSAIDs and anticoagulants, TPO receptor agonists (romiplostim, eltrombopag) for chronic ITP, corticosteroids and IVIG for acute immune thrombocytopenia, splenectomy for refractory ITP</li>
<li><strong>Nutritional optimization</strong>: Iron, folate, and B12 supplementation when deficient (common contributors to cytopenias), copper assessment (copper deficiency mimics myelodysplasia), adequate protein intake for hematopoiesis</li>
<li><strong>Monitoring</strong>: CBC with differential and reticulocyte count, peripheral blood smear, bone marrow biopsy (for diagnosis and disease progression), iron studies, B12, folate, LDH, haptoglobin</li>
</ul>
