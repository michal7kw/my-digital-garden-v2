<h1>Celiac Disease</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>An autoimmune disorder in which ingestion of gluten (a protein in wheat, barley, and rye) triggers an immune response that damages the villi of the small intestine. Affects approximately 1% of the population, though many remain undiagnosed. The only validated treatment is strict lifelong gluten-free diet. Associated with increased risk of lymphoma, osteoporosis, and other autoimmune conditions if untreated.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Chronic diarrhea, steatorrhea, abdominal bloating, and cramping</li>
<li>Iron deficiency anemia resistant to oral supplementation (impaired duodenal absorption)</li>
<li>Fatigue, weight loss, and failure to thrive in children</li>
<li>Dermatitis herpetiformis (pruritic papulovesicular rash on elbows, knees, buttocks)</li>
<li>Bone loss (osteopenia/osteoporosis from calcium and vitamin D malabsorption)</li>
<li>Neurological manifestations: peripheral neuropathy, ataxia, brain fog</li>
<li>Many adults present with atypical or &quot;silent&quot; celiac disease</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Gluten-free diet (only proven treatment)</strong>: Strict lifelong avoidance of wheat, barley, rye, and cross-contaminated oats; mucosal healing typically within 6-24 months</li>
<li><strong>Nutritional repletion</strong>: Iron, folate, vitamin B12, calcium, vitamin D, and zinc supplementation to correct malabsorption deficiencies</li>
<li><strong>Bone health</strong>: DEXA scan at diagnosis; calcium (1000-1500 mg/day) + vitamin D (1000-2000 IU/day)</li>
<li><strong>Monitoring</strong>: Serial tTG-IgA antibodies to confirm dietary compliance; repeat biopsy if non-responsive</li>
<li><strong>Refractory celiac disease</strong>: Corticosteroids (budesonide), azathioprine; rule out enteropathy-associated T-cell lymphoma</li>
<li><strong>Emerging therapies</strong>: Latiglutenase (enzyme to degrade gluten), tight junction modulators (larazotide), gluten-tolerizing vaccines in clinical trials</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Dietitian counseling, celiac support groups, label reading education, dedicated kitchen preparation areas</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Probiotics (gut barrier support), L-glutamine (intestinal mucosal repair), digestive enzymes (adjunctive, not a substitute for GFD), omega-3 fatty acids<br />
