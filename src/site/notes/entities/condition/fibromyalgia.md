<h1>Fibromyalgia</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Fibromyalgia is a chronic pain syndrome characterized by widespread musculoskeletal pain, fatigue, sleep disturbance, and cognitive dysfunction. The 2016 ACR criteria require widespread pain (4 of 5 body regions) plus fatigue, unrefreshing sleep, or cognitive symptoms for 3+ months. The central mechanism is &quot;central sensitization&quot; — amplified pain processing in the spinal cord and brain where normal sensory input is perceived as painful (allodynia) or mildly painful stimuli are perceived as severe (hyperalgesia). Neuroimaging shows increased activity in pain-processing brain regions (insula, anterior cingulate cortex, somatosensory cortex) even with minimal peripheral input.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Widespread pain (all four quadrants of body + axial skeleton)</li>
<li>Fatigue (often debilitating, unrelated to exertion)</li>
<li>&quot;Fibro fog&quot; — cognitive dysfunction (memory, concentration, word-finding)</li>
<li>Unrefreshing sleep (alpha-delta sleep pattern — alpha wave intrusion into deep sleep)</li>
<li>Headaches, IBS symptoms, TMJ pain, restless legs</li>
<li>Sensitivity to temperature, noise, light, chemicals</li>
<li>Depression and anxiety (comorbid in 30-50%)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Female sex (7:1 female-to-male ratio)</li>
<li>Family history (8x increased risk in first-degree relatives)</li>
<li>Prior physical or emotional trauma</li>
<li>Other chronic pain conditions (RA, lupus, OA)</li>
<li>Childhood adversity</li>
<li>Sleep disorders</li>
<li>Psychological stress</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Exercise (strongest evidence)</strong>: Graduated aerobic exercise (walking, swimming, cycling) — Level A evidence. Start very low, increase slowly (&quot;start low, go slow&quot;). Reduces pain scores 20-30%</li>
<li><strong>Pharmacotherapy</strong>: Pregabalin (FDA-approved, blocks alpha-2-delta calcium channels), duloxetine (SNRI, FDA-approved), milnacipran (SNRI), amitriptyline (low-dose TCA, off-label but well-studied)</li>
<li><strong>CBT</strong>: Addresses catastrophizing, pacing, sleep hygiene — moderate effect sizes</li>
<li><strong>Sleep optimization</strong>: Critical — addressing alpha-delta sleep improves all other symptoms</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Magnesium (400mg — reduces pain and tenderness), CoQ10 (200-300mg — fatigue), vitamin D (if deficient), SAMe, 5-HTP</li>
<li><strong>Complementary</strong>: Tai chi, yoga, acupuncture, hydrotherapy — all show benefit in RCTs</li>
</ul>
