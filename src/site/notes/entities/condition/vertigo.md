<h1>Vertigo</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Vertigo is an illusory sensation of movement, most commonly spinning, caused by vestibular dysfunction. Peripheral causes (inner ear) account for 80% of cases and include benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV -- most common), vestibular neuritis, and Meniere's disease. Central causes (brainstem or cerebellum) include stroke, multiple sclerosis, and vestibular migraine. Key diagnostic distinction: peripheral vertigo is typically episodic with hearing changes and nystagmus that inhibits with fixation; central vertigo often has associated neurological signs.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Illusion of spinning or tilting (self or environment)</li>
<li>Nausea, vomiting, and diaphoresis during episodes</li>
<li>Nystagmus (direction and pattern help localize cause)</li>
<li>Imbalance and gait instability, increased fall risk</li>
<li>Hearing loss and tinnitus (Meniere's disease, labyrinthitis)</li>
<li>Headache and photophobia (vestibular migraine)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>BPPV</strong>: Epley maneuver (canalith repositioning) -- 80-90% success rate in single session; Semont maneuver as alternative; no medications needed</li>
<li><strong>Vestibular neuritis</strong>: Short course of vestibular suppressants (meclizine, dimenhydrinate) for acute phase only (1-3 days); corticosteroids (methylprednisolone) may improve recovery; early vestibular rehabilitation</li>
<li><strong>Meniere's disease</strong>: Low-sodium diet (&lt; 2g/day), thiazide diuretics, betahistine (widely used outside US), intratympanic dexamethasone or gentamicin for refractory cases</li>
<li><strong>Vestibular migraine</strong>: Migraine prophylaxis (propranolol, topiramate, amitriptyline), trigger avoidance, triptans for acute episodes</li>
<li><strong>Vestibular rehabilitation therapy (VRT)</strong>: Exercise-based program to promote central compensation -- gaze stabilization, habituation exercises, balance training; strongest long-term evidence</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Fall prevention measures, adequate hydration, stress management, regular sleep, limit caffeine and alcohol</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Ginkgo biloba (may improve vestibular blood flow), magnesium (migraine prevention), vitamin D (associated with BPPV recurrence when deficient), B-complex vitamins, CoQ10<br />
