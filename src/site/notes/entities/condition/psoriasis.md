<h1>Psoriasis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A chronic immune-mediated inflammatory disease causing accelerated keratinocyte proliferation (normal 28-day cycle compressed to 3-4 days). Affects 2-3% of the global population. Psoriasis is now understood as a systemic inflammatory condition — patients have increased risk of psoriatic arthritis (30%), cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, depression, and IBD. The IL-23/IL-17 axis is the central immunological driver. Skin manifestations range from mild (limited plaques) to severe (erythrodermic, generalized pustular).</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Well-demarcated erythematous plaques with silvery scales (extensor surfaces, scalp, lower back)</li>
<li>Itching (pruritus) — present in 70-90% of patients</li>
<li>Nail changes (pitting, onycholysis, oil drop sign) — in 50% of patients</li>
<li>Koebner phenomenon (new lesions at sites of skin trauma)</li>
<li>Joint pain/swelling (psoriatic arthritis — 30% of patients)</li>
<li>Significant psychological impact (depression, social withdrawal, reduced quality of life)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Topical (mild)</strong>: Corticosteroids (first-line), vitamin D analogs (calcipotriol), calcineurin inhibitors, coal tar</li>
<li><strong>Phototherapy (moderate)</strong>: Narrowband UVB (most common), PUVA</li>
<li><strong>Systemic (moderate-severe)</strong>: Methotrexate, cyclosporine, apremilast (PDE4 inhibitor)</li>
<li><strong>Biologics (severe/refractory)</strong>: IL-17 inhibitors (secukinumab, ixekizumab — 90%+ clearance), IL-23 inhibitors (guselkumab, risankizumab), TNF inhibitors (adalimumab, infliximab)</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Mediterranean diet (anti-inflammatory), weight loss (reduces severity and improves biologic response), stress management, alcohol reduction</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Omega-3 (adjunctive, reduces inflammation), vitamin D (immune modulation), curcumin, probiotics</li>
</ul>
