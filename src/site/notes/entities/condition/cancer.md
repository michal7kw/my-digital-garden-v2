<h1>Cancer</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A large group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells, which can invade nearby tissues and metastasize to distant organs.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Varies by type: unexplained weight loss, fatigue, persistent pain, lumps, skin changes, unusual bleeding</li>
<li>Many cancers asymptomatic in early stages -- screening is critical</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Surgery</strong>: Primary treatment for solid tumors</li>
<li><strong>Chemotherapy</strong>: Cytotoxic agents targeting rapidly dividing cells</li>
<li><strong>Immunotherapy</strong>: Checkpoint inhibitors (pembrolizumab), CAR-T cells -- revolutionary</li>
<li><strong>Targeted therapy</strong>: Small molecules targeting specific mutations (imatinib, trastuzumab)</li>
<li><strong>Radiation</strong>: External beam, brachytherapy for local control</li>
<li><strong>Prevention</strong>: Mediterranean diet, exercise, avoid tobacco/excess alcohol, sun protection</li>
<li><strong>Supplements (adjunctive)</strong>: Vitamin D, selenium, curcumin, green tea (EGCG), medicinal mushrooms</li>
</ul>
