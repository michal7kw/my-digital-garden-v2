<h1>NAFLD</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD) is the most common chronic liver disease worldwide, affecting an estimated 25% of the global population. It encompasses a spectrum from simple steatosis (fat accumulation &gt;5% of hepatocytes) to non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH) with inflammation and fibrosis, potentially progressing to cirrhosis and hepatocellular carcinoma. NAFLD is the hepatic manifestation of metabolic syndrome — strongly associated with insulin resistance, obesity, and type 2 diabetes. Recently renamed to MASLD (Metabolic dysfunction-Associated Steatotic Liver Disease) to reflect its metabolic basis.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Usually asymptomatic in early stages (incidental finding on imaging)</li>
<li>Fatigue and malaise (most common symptom when present)</li>
<li>Right upper quadrant discomfort</li>
<li>Hepatomegaly (enlarged liver on exam)</li>
<li>In advanced disease: jaundice, ascites, spider angiomata</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Obesity (present in 80-90% of NAFLD patients)</li>
<li>Type 2 diabetes / insulin resistance</li>
<li>Metabolic syndrome</li>
<li>Dyslipidemia (high triglycerides, low HDL)</li>
<li>PCOS</li>
<li>Hypothyroidism</li>
<li>Obstructive sleep apnea</li>
<li>Genetic variants (PNPLA3, TM6SF2)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Lifestyle (cornerstone)</strong>: Weight loss of 7-10% body weight reverses steatosis; 10%+ can resolve fibrosis. Mediterranean diet is preferred. Regular exercise (150-300 min/week) independently reduces liver fat</li>
<li><strong>No FDA-approved drugs yet</strong>: Several in Phase 3 trials (resmetirom/Rezdiffra recently approved for NASH with fibrosis)</li>
<li><strong>Diabetes medications with liver benefit</strong>: Pioglitazone (reduces NASH histology), GLP-1 agonists (semaglutide reduces liver fat 40-70%)</li>
<li><strong>Vitamin E</strong>: 800 IU/day shown to improve NASH histology in non-diabetic patients (PIVENS trial)</li>
<li><strong>Bariatric surgery</strong>: For obese NAFLD patients; resolves NASH in 85% of cases</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Berberine (reduces hepatic fat), omega-3 (reduces triglycerides), NAC (glutathione precursor for liver protection), alpha-lipoic acid, milk thistle (silymarin)</li>
</ul>
