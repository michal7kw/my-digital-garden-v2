<h1>Irritable Bowel Syndrome</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>IBS is the most common functional GI disorder, affecting 10-15% of the population globally. Rome IV criteria define it as recurrent abdominal pain at least 1 day/week in the last 3 months, associated with defecation, change in stool frequency, or change in stool form. Subtypes: IBS-D (diarrhea-predominant), IBS-C (constipation-predominant), IBS-M (mixed). The pathophysiology involves visceral hypersensitivity (increased pain signaling from gut to brain), altered gut motility, gut microbiome dysbiosis, increased intestinal permeability (&quot;leaky gut&quot;), mast cell activation, and psychological factors (gut-brain axis bidirectionality — 50-90% have comorbid anxiety/depression).</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Recurrent abdominal pain/cramping (often relieved by defecation)</li>
<li>Bloating and abdominal distension</li>
<li>Altered bowel habits (diarrhea, constipation, or alternating)</li>
<li>Mucus in stool</li>
<li>Urgency, incomplete evacuation</li>
<li>Symptoms often worsened by stress, certain foods, menstruation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Female sex (2:1 ratio)</li>
<li>Age &lt;50</li>
<li>Prior GI infection (post-infectious IBS in 10-15% after gastroenteritis)</li>
<li>Psychological stress, anxiety, depression</li>
<li>Childhood adversity</li>
<li>Antibiotic use (microbiome disruption)</li>
<li>Food intolerances (FODMAPs, gluten sensitivity)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dietary (most impactful)</strong>: Low-FODMAP diet (70% response rate — strongest evidence), elimination diet, increased soluble fiber (psyllium), adequate hydration</li>
<li><strong>Gut-directed psychotherapy</strong>: Gut-directed hypnotherapy (NNT=4), CBT for IBS (addresses catastrophizing and visceral anxiety)</li>
<li><strong>Medications</strong>: Antispasmodics (hyoscine, peppermint oil), rifaximin (for IBS-D/SIBO overlap), linaclotide (IBS-C), loperamide (IBS-D), low-dose TCAs (amitriptyline 10-25mg for pain)</li>
<li><strong>Probiotics</strong>: Multi-strain formulations (Bifidobacterium infantis 35624, VSL#3) — moderate evidence for bloating and global symptoms</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Peppermint oil (enteric-coated — NNT=3 for pain), psyllium, L-glutamine (intestinal barrier), berberine, probiotics</li>
<li><strong>Stress management</strong>: Yoga, meditation, regular exercise — all reduce IBS symptom severity</li>
</ul>
