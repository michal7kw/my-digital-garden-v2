<h1>Treatment-Resistant Depression</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Treatment-Resistant Depression (TRD) is defined as major depressive disorder that fails to achieve adequate response (typically &lt;50% reduction in depression rating scale scores) after at least two adequate antidepressant trials of different classes. It affects approximately 30% of patients with MDD, representing one of the greatest unmet needs in psychiatry.</p>
<p>TRD is associated with greater functional disability, higher healthcare utilization, increased cardiovascular risk, and significantly elevated suicide risk compared to treatment-responsive depression. The STAR*D trial demonstrated that remission rates decline sharply with successive treatment steps: 37% with first SSRI, 31% with second trial, 14% with third, and 13% with fourth.</p>

<h2>Why SSRIs Fail: Biological Mechanisms</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Pharmacogenomics</strong>: CYP2D6/CYP2C19 ultra-rapid metabolizers may never achieve therapeutic SSRI levels</li>
<li><strong>5-HTTLPR genotype</strong>: SLC6A4 S/S carriers may have reduced SSRI response</li>
<li><strong>Neuroinflammation</strong>: ~30% of depression involves elevated inflammatory markers (IL-6, CRP) — these patients respond poorly to SSRIs but may respond to anti-inflammatory augmentation</li>
<li><strong>Glutamate dysfunction</strong>: NMDA receptor hyperactivity not addressed by serotonergic drugs → ketamine targets this</li>
<li><strong>HPA axis dysregulation</strong>: Persistent cortisol elevation causes glucocorticoid receptor desensitization</li>
<li><strong>Reduced BDNF signaling</strong>: Impaired neuroplasticity limits antidepressant-induced hippocampal remodeling</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Strategies</h2>
<h3>Optimization</h3>
<ul>
<li>Ensure adequate dose and duration (≥6 weeks at maximum tolerated dose)</li>
<li>Confirm medication adherence</li>
<li>Check pharmacogenomic profile (CYP2D6, CYP2C19 testing)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Switching</h3>
<ul>
<li>Within class: Try different SSRI</li>
<li>Between classes: SSRI → SNRI (venlafaxine, duloxetine) → bupropion → TCA → MAOI</li>
</ul>
