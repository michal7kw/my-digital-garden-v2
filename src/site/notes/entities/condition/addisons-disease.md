<h1>Addisons Disease</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A rare, chronic disorder where the adrenal glands do not produce enough cortisol and often insufficient aldosterone, usually due to autoimmune damage.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Fatigue, weakness, weight loss, hypotension, hyperpigmentation</li>
<li>Salt craving, nausea; adrenal crisis: acute shock -- life-threatening</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Glucocorticoid replacement</strong>: Hydrocortisone 15-25mg/day in divided doses</li>
<li><strong>Mineralocorticoid</strong>: Fludrocortisone 0.05-0.2mg daily</li>
<li><strong>Sick day rules</strong>: Double/triple dose during illness -- prevents adrenal crisis</li>
<li><strong>Emergency kit</strong>: IM hydrocortisone injection; medical alert bracelet essential</li>
<li><strong>DHEA</strong>: 25-50mg/day in women (well-being, libido)</li>
</ul>
