<h1>Addisons Disease</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Addison's Disease (Primary Adrenal Insufficiency) occurs when the adrenal cortex is damaged. Most cases in developed nations are <strong>Autoimmune</strong>. Without treatment, it can lead to a life-threatening <strong>Adrenal Crisis</strong>, characterized by shock and organ failure.</p>
<h2>Clinical Indicators</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Hyperpigmentation:</strong> Due to excess ACTH cross-reacting with melanocyte receptors.</li>
<li><strong>Electrolyte Abnormalities:</strong> Low sodium and high potassium.</li>
</ul>
