<h1>Migraine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Migraine is not just a headache — it is a complex neurological disorder involving cortical spreading depression (a wave of neuronal depolarization across the cortex), trigeminovascular system activation, and release of CGRP (calcitonin gene-related peptide) causing neurogenic inflammation and vasodilation of meningeal vessels. The aura phase (present in ~25% of migraineurs) corresponds to the cortical spreading depression wave. Migraine is the third most prevalent disease globally, causing more disability than any other neurological condition.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Prodrome</strong> (hours-days before): Mood changes, food cravings, yawning, neck stiffness</li>
<li><strong>Aura</strong> (5-60 min, in 25%): Visual disturbances (scintillating scotoma, zigzag lines), sensory tingling, speech difficulty</li>
<li><strong>Headache</strong> (4-72 hours): Moderate-to-severe pulsating pain, usually unilateral, worsened by physical activity</li>
<li><strong>Associated</strong>: Nausea/vomiting (80%), photophobia, phonophobia, osmophobia</li>
<li><strong>Postdrome</strong>: Fatigue, cognitive difficulty, mood changes for 1-2 days</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Female sex (3:1 female-to-male ratio)</li>
<li>Family history (50-60% genetic component)</li>
<li>Hormonal fluctuations (menstrual migraine)</li>
<li>Stress, sleep disruption, weather changes</li>
<li>Certain foods (aged cheese, alcohol, MSG, nitrates)</li>
<li>Magnesium deficiency (found in 50% of migraineurs)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Acute treatment</strong>: Triptans (sumatriptan — 5-HT1B/1D agonists), NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen), gepants (ubrogepant, rimegepant — CGRP antagonists)</li>
<li><strong>Prevention (episodic)</strong>: Propranolol, topiramate, amitriptyline, valproate</li>
<li><strong>Prevention (CGRP-targeted)</strong>: Anti-CGRP monoclonal antibodies (erenumab, fremanezumab, galcanezumab) — game-changing for chronic migraine</li>
<li><strong>Nutraceutical prevention</strong>: Magnesium (400-600mg/day — Level A evidence), riboflavin/B2 (400mg/day), CoQ10 (300mg/day), feverfew</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Regular sleep schedule, stress management, regular exercise, trigger avoidance, adequate hydration</li>
<li><strong>Neuromodulation</strong>: Cefaly (supraorbital nerve stimulation), gammaCore (vagus nerve stimulation)</li>
</ul>
