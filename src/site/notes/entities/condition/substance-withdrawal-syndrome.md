<h1>Substance Withdrawal Syndrome</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Substance Withdrawal Syndrome occurs when an individual who is physically dependent on a substance (like alcohol, opioids, or benzodiazepines) stops or drastically reduces their use. The symptoms are the body's reaction to the absence of the substance it has adapted to.</p>
<h2>Common Syndromes</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Alcohol Withdrawal:</strong> Can be life-threatening (Delirium Tremens).</li>
<li><strong>Opioid Withdrawal:</strong> Extremely uncomfortable (flu-like) but rarely fatal.</li>
<li><strong>Benzodiazepine Withdrawal:</strong> High seizure risk.</li>
</ul>
