<h1>Frontotemporal Dementia</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A group of neurodegenerative disorders characterized by progressive degeneration of the frontal and temporal lobes. Causes changes in personality, behavior, and language, typically with earlier onset than Alzheimer's disease.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Behavioral variant: Personality changes, social disinhibition, apathy, loss of empathy, compulsive behaviors, dietary changes (sweet cravings)</li>
<li>Semantic variant: Progressive loss of word meaning, difficulty naming objects, impaired face/object recognition</li>
<li>Nonfluent/agrammatic variant: Effortful speech, grammatical errors, speech apraxia</li>
<li>Executive dysfunction with relatively preserved memory (unlike Alzheimer's)</li>
<li>Motor symptoms may overlap with ALS or parkinsonism (corticobasal syndrome, PSP)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>No disease-modifying therapy available</strong> -- management is symptomatic and supportive</li>
<li><strong>SSRIs</strong>: Sertraline, trazodone -- for behavioral symptoms (disinhibition, compulsive behaviors, agitation); best evidence among pharmacotherapy</li>
<li><strong>Antipsychotics</strong>: Low-dose quetiapine for severe agitation (use with extreme caution -- high sensitivity to side effects)</li>
<li><strong>Speech therapy</strong>: For progressive aphasia variants -- communication strategies, augmentative devices</li>
<li><strong>Behavioral management</strong>: Structured routines, environmental modification, caregiver education and support</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Regular physical exercise (may slow progression), social engagement, music therapy, occupational therapy</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Omega-3 fatty acids, CoQ10 (mitochondrial support), lion's mane (NGF stimulation -- preclinical), curcumin, NAC</li>
<li><strong>Clinical trials</strong>: Anti-tau therapies, progranulin-targeting agents (for GRN mutations) under active investigation<br />
