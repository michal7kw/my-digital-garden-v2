<h1>Burnout Syndrome</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>State of chronic physical and emotional exhaustion resulting from prolonged occupational or caregiving stress. Classified by WHO (ICD-11) as an occupational phenomenon characterized by emotional exhaustion, depersonalization/cynicism, and reduced personal accomplishment. Distinct from clinical depression though frequently comorbid. Associated with HPA axis dysregulation, chronic inflammation, and autonomic dysfunction.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Emotional exhaustion, depersonalization/cynicism, reduced accomplishment</li>
<li>Physical: chronic fatigue, insomnia, headaches, frequent illness</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Boundary setting</strong>: Work-life boundaries, delegation, saying no</li>
<li><strong>Recovery</strong>: Prioritize sleep, exercise, social connection, nature</li>
<li><strong>Therapy</strong>: CBT, ACT, coaching</li>
<li><strong>Mindfulness</strong>: MBSR, daily meditation</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Ashwagandha, rhodiola rosea, magnesium, B-complex</li>
</ul>
