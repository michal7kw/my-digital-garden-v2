<h1>Pancreatitis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Inflammation of the pancreas, which can be acute (sudden and severe) or chronic (long-term and progressive).</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Severe epigastric abdominal pain radiating to the back</li>
<li>Nausea and vomiting</li>
<li>Abdominal tenderness and distension</li>
<li>Fever and tachycardia</li>
<li>In chronic pancreatitis: steatorrhea (fatty stools), weight loss, diabetes mellitus (pancreatic insufficiency)</li>
<li>Jaundice (if bile duct obstruction)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Acute management</strong>: NPO (nil per os) initially, IV fluid resuscitation, pain management (opioids if needed, avoid morphine — may cause sphincter of Oddi spasm), treat underlying cause (gallstone removal via ERCP, alcohol cessation)</li>
<li><strong>Chronic management</strong>: Pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy (PERT — lipase with meals), pain management (WHO pain ladder), fat-soluble vitamin supplementation (A, D, E, K)</li>
<li><strong>Dietary</strong>: Low-fat diet (especially during recovery), small frequent meals, absolute alcohol avoidance, medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs) for fat absorption</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Omega-3 fatty acids (anti-inflammatory), NAC (antioxidant — may reduce pain in chronic pancreatitis), curcumin (anti-inflammatory, NF-kB inhibition), probiotics (gut barrier support), antioxidant combinations (selenium, vitamin C, vitamin E)</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Complete alcohol cessation (most important in alcohol-induced pancreatitis), smoking cessation (independent risk factor for progression), stress management</li>
<li><strong>Surgical</strong>: Endoscopic interventions (stenting, lithotripsy), surgical drainage or resection for refractory chronic pancreatitis</li>
</ul>
