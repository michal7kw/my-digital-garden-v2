<h1>Psychotic Disorders</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A group of serious mental illnesses that affect the mind, making it difficult to distinguish between what is real and what is not.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Positive symptoms</strong>: Hallucinations (auditory most common — hearing voices), delusions (paranoid, grandiose, referential), disorganized speech (tangentiality, derailment, word salad)</li>
<li><strong>Negative symptoms</strong>: Flat affect (diminished emotional expression), alogia (reduced speech output), avolition (lack of motivation), anhedonia (inability to experience pleasure), social withdrawal</li>
<li><strong>Cognitive symptoms</strong>: Impaired working memory, attention deficits, reduced processing speed, poor executive function</li>
<li><strong>Disorganized behavior</strong>: Catatonia, bizarre behavior, poor self-care and hygiene</li>
<li><strong>Mood symptoms</strong>: Depression (common comorbidity in schizophrenia), anxiety, irritability</li>
<li><strong>Psychomotor changes</strong>: Agitation, restlessness, or conversely psychomotor retardation</li>
<li><strong>Insight impairment</strong>: Anosognosia (unawareness of illness — present in ~50% of schizophrenia patients)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Antipsychotic medication</strong>: Second-generation (atypical) preferred — risperidone, olanzapine, quetiapine, aripiprazole, lurasidone. Clozapine reserved for treatment-resistant schizophrenia (requires blood monitoring for agranulocytosis). First-generation (typical) — haloperidol, chlorpromazine — effective but higher EPS risk</li>
<li><strong>Psychosocial interventions</strong>: Cognitive behavioral therapy for psychosis (CBTp), family psychoeducation, social skills training, supported employment (IPS model), assertive community treatment (ACT)</li>
<li><strong>Long-acting injectable antipsychotics</strong>: Paliperidone palmitate, aripiprazole lauroxil — improve adherence and reduce relapse</li>
<li><strong>Nutritional support</strong>: Omega-3 fatty acids (may help in early/prodromal psychosis), folate and B12 (elevated homocysteine common), vitamin D supplementation (deficiency prevalent), NAC (adjunctive — oxidative stress reduction)</li>
<li><strong>Metabolic monitoring</strong>: Antipsychotics cause significant metabolic side effects — regular monitoring of weight, glucose, lipids, HbA1c, blood pressure. Metformin for antipsychotic-induced weight gain</li>
<li><strong>Substance use management</strong>: Cannabis and stimulant use worsen psychosis — integrated dual-diagnosis treatment essential</li>
<li><strong>Early intervention</strong>: Duration of untreated psychosis (DUP) is the strongest modifiable predictor of outcome — early detection and treatment programs improve long-term prognosis</li>
</ul>
