<h1>Osteoporosis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A chronic condition characterized by decreased bone mass and deterioration of bone tissue, making bones fragile and more likely to fracture. Bone is a living tissue constantly remodeled by osteoblasts (builders) and osteoclasts (resorbers). Osteoporosis develops when resorption outpaces formation, most commonly after menopause (estrogen withdrawal accelerates osteoclast activity). Affects an estimated 200 million people worldwide. Often called a &quot;silent disease&quot; because bone loss is asymptomatic until a fracture occurs.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Often asymptomatic until fracture (&quot;silent disease&quot;)</li>
<li>Loss of height over time (vertebral compression fractures)</li>
<li>Stooped posture (kyphosis/dowager's hump)</li>
<li>Fragility fractures (hip, wrist, spine) from minimal trauma</li>
<li>Back pain from vertebral fractures</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Postmenopausal status (estrogen decline)</li>
<li>Age &gt;65</li>
<li>Family history of osteoporotic fractures</li>
<li>Low body weight (BMI &lt;19)</li>
<li>Prolonged corticosteroid use</li>
<li>Smoking and excessive alcohol</li>
<li>Vitamin D deficiency</li>
<li>Sedentary lifestyle</li>
<li>Calcium-poor diet</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Lifestyle foundation</strong>: Weight-bearing exercise (walking, resistance training, dancing), fall prevention, adequate calcium (1200 mg/day) and vitamin D (1000-2000 IU/day)</li>
<li><strong>First-line medications</strong>: Bisphosphonates (alendronate, risedronate, zoledronic acid) — inhibit osteoclast activity</li>
<li><strong>Anabolic agents</strong>: Teriparatide (PTH analog), romosozumab (sclerostin inhibitor) — stimulate bone formation</li>
<li><strong>Hormonal</strong>: Estrogen replacement (postmenopausal women with vasomotor symptoms), raloxifene (SERM)</li>
<li><strong>Monitoring</strong>: DEXA scan every 1-2 years; bone turnover markers (CTX, P1NP, osteocalcin)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Vitamin D + K2 (directs calcium to bone), magnesium, collagen peptides, strontium</li>
</ul>
