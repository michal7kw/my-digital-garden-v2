<h1>Scarlet Fever</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Scarlet Fever (Scarlatina) is caused by the same bacteria that cause strep throat (<em>Streptococcus pyogenes</em>). The bacteria release a toxin that results in the characteristic red rash and tongue changes. While once a serious childhood disease, it is now easily treatable with antibiotics.</p>
<h2>Complications</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Rheumatic Fever.</strong></li>
<li><strong>Post-streptococcal Glomerulonephritis</strong> (Kidney inflammation).</li>
<li><strong>Otitis Media</strong> (Ear infection).</li>
<li><strong>Peritonsillar Abscess.</strong></li>
</ul>
