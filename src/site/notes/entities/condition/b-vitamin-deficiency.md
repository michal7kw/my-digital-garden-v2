<h1>B Vitamin Deficiency</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A deficiency in one or more of the B-complex vitamins (e.g., Folate, B6, B12), which are essential for energy metabolism, red blood cell production, and neurological function.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Fatigue and weakness</li>
<li>Numbness or tingling in hands/feet (peripheral neuropathy — especially B12, B6)</li>
<li>Sore, red tongue (glossitis) and mouth ulcers</li>
<li>Memory problems, confusion, and cognitive decline</li>
<li>Pale skin (megaloblastic anemia)</li>
<li>Shortness of breath</li>
<li>Depression and mood disturbances</li>
<li>Angular cheilitis (cracked corners of mouth)</li>
<li>Dermatitis (B2, B3, B6 deficiency)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>B12 repletion</strong>: Intramuscular B12 injections (cyanocobalamin or hydroxocobalamin 1000 mcg) for severe deficiency or malabsorption, followed by oral/sublingual methylcobalamin (1000-5000 mcg/day) for maintenance. Sublingual may bypass GI absorption issues</li>
<li><strong>Folate supplementation</strong>: Methylfolate (active form — especially important for MTHFR variants), 400-1000 mcg/day. Always check and correct B12 before high-dose folate (can mask B12 deficiency neuropathy)</li>
<li><strong>B-complex supplement</strong>: Covers all B vitamins — useful for general deficiency. Look for active forms (methylcobalamin, methylfolate, pyridoxal-5-phosphate, riboflavin-5-phosphate)</li>
<li><strong>Dietary sources</strong>: Organ meats (liver — richest B12 source), eggs, dairy, leafy greens (folate), legumes, whole grains, nutritional yeast (fortified), fish and poultry</li>
<li><strong>Address underlying causes</strong>: Review medications (metformin depletes B12, PPIs reduce absorption, oral contraceptives deplete B6/folate), treat malabsorption (celiac, Crohn's, atrophic gastritis, pernicious anemia), reduce alcohol (depletes B1, B6, folate)</li>
<li><strong>Monitoring</strong>: Serum B12, methylmalonic acid (MMA — elevated in functional B12 deficiency even when serum B12 is normal), homocysteine (elevated in B12 and folate deficiency), RBC folate, CBC with indices (MCV elevation)</li>
</ul>
