<h1>Gouty Arthritis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Gouty Arthritis (Gout) is a metabolic disorder where high levels of <strong>uric acid</strong> (hyperuricemia) lead to the formation of needle-like urate crystals in and around joints. These crystals trigger an intense inflammatory response. The classic presentation is &quot;podagra&quot;—acute inflammation of the base of the big toe—but it can affect ankles, knees, elbows, wrists, and fingers.</p>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<p>Uric acid is a breakdown product of <strong>purines</strong>. In gout, either the body produces too much uric acid or the kidneys excrete too little. When concentrations exceed the saturation point, crystallization occurs.</p>
