<h1>Oxidative Stress</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Oxidative stress is an imbalance between the production of reactive oxygen species (ROS) and the biological system's ability to detoxify or repair the resulting damage. It is implicated in many pathological conditions including neurodegeneration, cardiovascular diseases, and metabolic disorders.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Chronic fatigue and low energy</li>
<li>Accelerated aging (skin damage, wrinkles)</li>
<li>Muscle and joint pain</li>
<li>Brain fog and memory issues</li>
<li>Frequent infections (immune suppression)</li>
<li>Poor wound healing</li>
<li>Headaches</li>
<li>Noise sensitivity</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Antioxidant supplementation</strong>: NAC (600-1800 mg/day — glutathione precursor), vitamin C (500-2000 mg/day), vitamin E (mixed tocopherols), alpha-lipoic acid (300-600 mg/day — both water and fat soluble)</li>
<li><strong>Nrf2 pathway activation</strong>: Sulforaphane (broccoli sprout extract), resveratrol, curcumin — upregulate endogenous antioxidant enzymes (SOD, catalase, glutathione peroxidase)</li>
<li><strong>Mitochondrial support</strong>: CoQ10 (100-300 mg/day), PQQ, NMN/NR (NAD+ precursors)</li>
<li><strong>Diet</strong>: High intake of colorful fruits and vegetables (polyphenols, carotenoids), green tea, dark chocolate, berries. Mediterranean diet pattern. Avoid excessive processed foods, refined oils, alcohol</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Regular moderate exercise (upregulates endogenous antioxidants — avoid overtraining which increases ROS), adequate sleep, stress management, cold exposure (hormetic stressor)</li>
<li><strong>Environmental</strong>: Minimize toxin exposure (air pollution, heavy metals, pesticides, cigarette smoke), use water filtration, avoid excessive UV exposure</li>
</ul>
