<h1>Chronic Pain</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Chronic pain is pain that persists or recurs for more than 3 months, now classified by the ICD-11 as a disease in its own right. Affects approximately 20% of adults globally. The pathophysiology involves central sensitization (amplified pain signaling in the CNS), neuroinflammation, and neuroplastic changes in pain-processing brain regions. Categorized as chronic primary pain (fibromyalgia, chronic widespread pain) or chronic secondary pain (musculoskeletal, neuropathic, visceral, post-surgical, cancer-related). Biopsychosocial model is essential for effective management.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Persistent or recurring pain beyond expected healing time (&gt; 3 months)</li>
<li>Allodynia (pain from normally non-painful stimuli) and hyperalgesia (amplified pain response)</li>
<li>Chronic fatigue and unrefreshing sleep</li>
<li>Depression, anxiety, and emotional distress (comorbid in 30-50%)</li>
<li>Reduced physical function, deconditioning, and disability</li>
<li>Cognitive dysfunction (&quot;brain fog&quot; -- impaired attention, memory, executive function)</li>
<li>Social withdrawal and reduced quality of life</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Multimodal approach (gold standard)</strong>: Combining physical, psychological, and pharmacological therapies -- superior to any single modality</li>
<li><strong>Physical therapy</strong>: Graded exercise therapy (strongest evidence), aquatic therapy, manual therapy, TENS for localized pain</li>
<li><strong>Psychological</strong>: CBT for chronic pain (Level A evidence), acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT), mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR), pain neuroscience education</li>
<li><strong>Non-opioid pharmacotherapy</strong>: Duloxetine/venlafaxine (SNRIs -- neuropathic and musculoskeletal), pregabalin/gabapentin (neuropathic), low-dose amitriptyline (sleep and pain), NSAIDs (short-term, with GI protection)</li>
<li><strong>Interventional</strong>: Nerve blocks, epidural injections, radiofrequency ablation, spinal cord stimulation for refractory neuropathic pain</li>
<li><strong>Opioids</strong>: Last resort, lowest effective dose, short duration with clear functional goals; high risk of dependence and hyperalgesia</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Regular aerobic exercise (releases endogenous opioids), anti-inflammatory diet, sleep optimization, stress reduction, social connection</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Magnesium (NMDA receptor modulation), omega-3 fatty acids (anti-inflammatory), curcumin, palmitoylethanolamide (PEA -- endocannabinoid modulator), vitamin D (if deficient), SAMe<br />
