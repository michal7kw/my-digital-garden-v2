<h1>Insomnia</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Insomnia is the most prevalent sleep disorder — 30% of adults report intermittent insomnia symptoms, and 10% meet criteria for chronic insomnia disorder (symptoms 3+ nights/week for 3+ months). It is classified as difficulty falling asleep (sleep-onset insomnia), difficulty staying asleep (sleep-maintenance insomnia), or early morning awakening. Chronic insomnia is not simply &quot;can't sleep&quot; — it involves hyperarousal of the HPA axis and sympathetic nervous system that persists even during the day. This hyperarousal model (Spielman's 3P model: predisposing, precipitating, perpetuating factors) explains why insomnia often persists long after the original trigger resolves.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Difficulty falling asleep (&gt;30 min sleep onset latency)</li>
<li>Frequent nighttime awakenings with difficulty returning to sleep</li>
<li>Early morning awakening (2+ hours before desired wake time)</li>
<li>Non-restorative sleep despite adequate duration</li>
<li>Daytime fatigue, irritability, difficulty concentrating</li>
<li>Anxiety about sleep itself (conditioned arousal)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Risk Factors</h2>
<ul>
<li>Female sex (1.4x risk)</li>
<li>Age &gt;60</li>
<li>Comorbid anxiety or depression (bidirectional relationship)</li>
<li>Chronic pain</li>
<li>Shift work or irregular schedules</li>
<li>Excessive caffeine, alcohol, or screen use</li>
<li>Hyperarousal personality traits (rumination, perfectionism)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>CBT-I (first-line, most effective)</strong>: Sleep restriction, stimulus control, cognitive restructuring, relaxation training. 70-80% response rate; effects persist long after treatment ends</li>
<li><strong>Sleep hygiene</strong>: Consistent schedule, dark/cool room, morning light, caffeine cutoff, no screens before bed</li>
<li><strong>Medications (second-line)</strong>: Melatonin (0.5-3mg for circadian component), trazodone (low-dose), orexin antagonists (suvorexant, lemborexant), Z-drugs (zolpidem — short-term only, dependence risk)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Magnesium glycinate (400mg), L-theanine (200mg), glycine (3g), ashwagandha, valerian root</li>
<li><strong>Avoid</strong>: Benzodiazepines for chronic insomnia (tolerance, dependence, cognitive impairment); diphenhydramine/Benadryl (anticholinergic burden)</li>
</ul>
