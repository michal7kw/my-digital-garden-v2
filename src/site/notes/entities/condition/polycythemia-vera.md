<h1>Polycythemia Vera</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A slow-growing blood cancer in which the bone marrow makes too many red blood cells, causing the blood to thicken and increasing the risk of clots.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Itchy skin (aquagenic pruritus), especially after warm baths or showers</li>
<li>Headaches, dizziness, and lightheadedness</li>
<li>Fatigue and weakness despite elevated red cell mass</li>
<li>Blurred vision or visual disturbances</li>
<li>Feeling of fullness or early satiety (splenomegaly)</li>
<li>Erythromelalgia (burning pain and redness in hands/feet)</li>
<li>Night sweats and unexplained weight loss</li>
<li>Facial plethora (ruddy complexion)</li>
<li>Tinnitus and numbness/tingling in extremities</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Phlebotomy</strong>: First-line treatment — regular blood removal (typically 500 mL) to maintain hematocrit below 45%. Reduces thrombotic risk by lowering blood viscosity</li>
<li><strong>Low-dose aspirin</strong>: 81-100 mg/day for all patients without contraindications — reduces thrombotic events and microvascular symptoms (erythromelalgia, headache)</li>
<li><strong>Cytoreductive therapy</strong>: Hydroxyurea (first-line) for high-risk patients (age &gt;60 or prior thrombosis). Pegylated interferon-alpha for younger patients or those intolerant to hydroxyurea. Ruxolitinib (JAK2 inhibitor) for hydroxyurea-resistant/intolerant disease</li>
<li><strong>Risk factor management</strong>: Aggressive cardiovascular risk reduction (hypertension, diabetes, dyslipidemia), smoking cessation, weight management</li>
<li><strong>Monitoring</strong>: CBC with differential, hematocrit (target &lt;45%), iron studies (phlebotomy-induced deficiency), uric acid, LDH, spleen size assessment, JAK2 V617F allele burden</li>
<li><strong>Symptom management</strong>: Antihistamines or SSRIs for pruritus, allopurinol for hyperuricemia, adequate hydration</li>
</ul>
