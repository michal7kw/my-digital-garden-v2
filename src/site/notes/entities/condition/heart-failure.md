<h1>Heart Failure</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Heart failure (HF) is a clinical syndrome where the heart cannot pump sufficient blood to meet metabolic demands. Two main types: HFrEF (reduced ejection fraction &lt;40%, systolic failure) and HFpEF (preserved EF ≥50%, diastolic failure). Affects 6.2 million US adults. Leading cause of hospitalization in patients &gt;65. Five-year mortality remains ~50% despite advances. Key biomarker: BNP/NT-proBNP reflects ventricular wall stress.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Dyspnea (on exertion, then at rest), orthopnea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea</li>
<li>Peripheral edema (ankles, legs), weight gain from fluid retention</li>
<li>Fatigue, exercise intolerance</li>
<li>Jugular venous distension, hepatomegaly</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Guideline-directed medical therapy (GDMT)</strong>: ACE inhibitor/ARB/ARNI (sacubitril-valsartan), beta-blocker (carvedilol, metoprolol succinate, bisoprolol), mineralocorticoid antagonist (spironolactone), SGLT2 inhibitor (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin — benefit in HFrEF AND HFpEF)</li>
<li><strong>&quot;Fantastic Four&quot;</strong>: ARNI + beta-blocker + MRA + SGLT2i — all four classes reduce mortality</li>
<li><strong>Diuretics</strong>: Loop diuretics (furosemide) for volume management (symptom relief, not mortality benefit)</li>
<li><strong>Device therapy</strong>: ICD (sudden death prevention if EF ≤35%), CRT (biventricular pacing if wide QRS)</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Sodium restriction (&lt;2g/day), fluid restriction (1.5-2L/day if severe), cardiac rehabilitation, daily weight monitoring</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: CoQ10 (Q-SYMBIO trial — 43% reduction in cardiovascular death), omega-3, magnesium, thiamine (depleted by diuretics)</li>
</ul>
