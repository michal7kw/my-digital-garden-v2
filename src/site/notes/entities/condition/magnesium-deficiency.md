<h1>Magnesium Deficiency</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A condition where the body does not have enough magnesium, an essential mineral involved in over 300 biochemical reactions.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Muscle cramps, spasms, and twitching</li>
<li>Fatigue and weakness</li>
<li>Heart palpitations and arrhythmias</li>
<li>Anxiety, irritability, and nervousness</li>
<li>Numbness and tingling</li>
<li>Insomnia and poor sleep quality</li>
<li>Headaches and migraines</li>
<li>Constipation</li>
<li>Hypertension</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Oral magnesium supplementation</strong>: Magnesium glycinate (best tolerated, good for anxiety/sleep), magnesium citrate (good bioavailability, mild laxative effect), magnesium threonate (crosses blood-brain barrier — cognitive benefits), magnesium taurate (cardiovascular support). Dose: 200-400 mg elemental magnesium/day</li>
<li><strong>IV magnesium</strong>: For severe deficiency, eclampsia, or acute cardiac arrhythmias. Magnesium sulfate IV — monitor reflexes and respiratory rate</li>
<li><strong>Dietary sources</strong>: Dark leafy greens (spinach, Swiss chard), nuts and seeds (pumpkin seeds, almonds), dark chocolate, avocado, whole grains, legumes</li>
<li><strong>Cofactor optimization</strong>: Vitamin D (magnesium is required for vitamin D activation, and D helps magnesium absorption), vitamin B6 (enhances intracellular magnesium retention), taurine (synergistic for cardiovascular protection)</li>
<li><strong>Address underlying causes</strong>: Reduce alcohol intake, review medications (PPIs and diuretics deplete magnesium), treat GI conditions affecting absorption (celiac, IBD), manage diabetes (renal magnesium wasting)</li>
<li><strong>Monitoring</strong>: Serum magnesium (insensitive — only 1% of body magnesium is extracellular), RBC magnesium (better indicator of intracellular stores), 24-hour urine magnesium</li>
</ul>
