<h1>Leaky Gut</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Increased intestinal permeability (&quot;leaky gut&quot;) allows bacterial endotoxins (LPS), undigested food particles, and antigens to cross the gut barrier into systemic circulation, triggering immune activation and chronic low-grade inflammation. Formally termed &quot;increased intestinal permeability&quot; — recognized as a contributing factor in autoimmune diseases, metabolic syndrome, and neuroinflammation. The intestinal barrier is a single-cell-thick epithelium held together by tight junction proteins (zonulin, occludin, claudins). Disruption is caused by dysbiosis, NSAIDs, alcohol, gluten (in sensitive individuals), stress, and ultra-processed foods.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Chronic bloating, gas, abdominal discomfort</li>
<li>Food sensitivities (especially to gluten, dairy, histamine-rich foods)</li>
<li>Fatigue, brain fog</li>
<li>Skin issues (eczema, acne, rashes)</li>
<li>Joint pain</li>
<li>Autoimmune symptom flares</li>
<li>Mood disturbances (gut-brain axis)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Remove triggers</strong>: Eliminate processed foods, alcohol, NSAIDs (where possible), gluten (if sensitive)</li>
<li><strong>Gut barrier repair</strong>: L-glutamine (5-10g/day — primary fuel for enterocytes), zinc carnosine (75mg 2x/day — mucosal healing), collagen peptides</li>
<li><strong>Restore microbiome</strong>: Diverse fiber intake, fermented foods, multi-strain probiotics, prebiotic fiber (FOS, GOS)</li>
<li><strong>Anti-inflammatory support</strong>: Omega-3, curcumin, quercetin, NAC</li>
<li><strong>Stress management</strong>: Chronic stress increases intestinal permeability via cortisol and mast cell activation</li>
<li><strong>Testing</strong>: Zonulin (serum), LPS antibodies, lactulose-mannitol test (research use), calprotectin (fecal — rules out IBD)</li>
</ul>
