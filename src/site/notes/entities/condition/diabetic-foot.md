<h1>Diabetic Foot</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Diabetic Foot Syndrome is a major complication of diabetes mellitus. It stems from the combination of <strong>peripheral neuropathy</strong> (loss of sensation) and <strong>peripheral artery disease</strong> (poor circulation). The loss of protective sensation means minor injuries go unnoticed, while poor blood flow prevents healing, leading to chronic ulcers and potentially amputation.</p>
<h2>Pathogenesis</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Neuropathy:</strong> Sensory (loss of pain), Motor (muscle atrophy/deformity), Autonomic (dry skin).</li>
<li><strong>Ischemia:</strong> Reduced blood flow.</li>
<li><strong>Infection:</strong> Bacteria enter open wounds.</li>
</ol>
