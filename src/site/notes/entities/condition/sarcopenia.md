<h1>Sarcopenia</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A progressive and generalized skeletal muscle disorder characterized by the age-related loss of muscle mass, strength, and function.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Progressive loss of muscle mass</li>
<li>Decreased grip strength</li>
<li>Reduced walking speed and balance</li>
<li>Fatigue and low endurance</li>
<li>Difficulty rising from a chair</li>
<li>Increased fall risk</li>
<li>Unintentional weight loss (lean mass)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Resistance exercise</strong>: Progressive resistance training 2-3x/week is the most effective intervention — targets both muscle hypertrophy and neuromuscular function. Combined with balance and functional training</li>
<li><strong>Protein intake</strong>: 1.2-1.6 g/kg/day protein (higher than general population recommendation). Leucine-rich sources (whey, eggs, meat) preferentially stimulate mTOR/muscle protein synthesis. Distribute protein across 3-4 meals</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Creatine monohydrate (3-5 g/day — strong evidence for strength gains in elderly), vitamin D (target &gt;40 ng/mL — directly affects muscle fiber function), HMB (3 g/day — reduces muscle breakdown), omega-3 fatty acids (anti-inflammatory, may enhance anabolic signaling)</li>
<li><strong>Pharmacological</strong>: Testosterone replacement (in hypogonadal males), emerging therapies (myostatin inhibitors, SARMs — investigational)</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Adequate sleep (growth hormone is secreted during deep sleep), stress management (cortisol is catabolic), minimize prolonged sedentary periods</li>
<li><strong>Nutritional</strong>: Ensure adequate caloric intake (caloric deficit accelerates muscle loss), vitamin K2, magnesium, zinc</li>
</ul>
