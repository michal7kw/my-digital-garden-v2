<h1>Peptic Ulcer</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A sore that develops on the lining of the esophagus, stomach, or small intestine.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Epigastric burning pain (gastric: worse with eating; duodenal: relieved by eating)</li>
<li>Nausea, bloating; complications: GI bleeding, perforation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>H. pylori eradication</strong>: Triple therapy (PPI + clarithromycin + amoxicillin) x 14 days</li>
<li><strong>PPI therapy</strong>: Omeprazole 4-8 weeks for healing</li>
<li><strong>NSAID ulcers</strong>: Discontinue NSAID; PPI for healing</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Zinc carnosine (75mg 2x/day), DGL licorice, L-glutamine, probiotics</li>
</ul>
