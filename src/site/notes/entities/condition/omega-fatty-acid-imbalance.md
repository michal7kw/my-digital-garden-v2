<h1>Omega Fatty Acid Imbalance</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Omega-3 and Omega-6 are essential polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFAs). While Omega-6 is pro-inflammatory (necessary for acute immune response), Omega-3 is anti-inflammatory. A high Omega-6 to Omega-3 ratio (common in modern diets, often &gt;15:1) is linked to chronic low-grade inflammation and increased risk of chronic diseases.</p>
<h2>Clinical Markers</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Omega-3 Index:</strong> Measurement of EPA and DHA in red blood cell membranes.</li>
<li><strong>Omega-6/Omega-3 Ratio:</strong> Ideal ratio is considered to be between 1:1 and 4:1.</li>
</ul>
