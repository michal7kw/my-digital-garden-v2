<h1>Epilepsy</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A chronic neurological disorder characterized by recurrent, unprovoked seizures due to abnormal electrical activity in the brain. Affects approximately 1% of the population worldwide. Seizures are classified as focal (arising from one brain region) or generalized (involving both hemispheres). Diagnosis requires at least two unprovoked seizures or one seizure with high recurrence risk.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Focal seizures: Altered awareness, automatisms (lip smacking, hand movements), sensory auras (visual, olfactory), deja vu</li>
<li>Generalized tonic-clonic: Loss of consciousness, muscle rigidity (tonic phase), rhythmic jerking (clonic phase), postictal confusion</li>
<li>Absence seizures: Brief staring episodes (5-30 seconds), eyelid fluttering, common in children</li>
<li>Myoclonic seizures: Brief involuntary muscle jerks</li>
<li>Status epilepticus: Prolonged seizure (&gt; 5 min) -- medical emergency</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>First-line AEDs</strong>: Levetiracetam (broad-spectrum, favorable side-effect profile), lamotrigine (focal and generalized), valproate (broad-spectrum but teratogenic -- avoid in women of childbearing age), carbamazepine/oxcarbazepine (focal seizures)</li>
<li><strong>Adjunctive AEDs</strong>: Clobazam, brivaracetam, lacosamide, perampanel for refractory epilepsy</li>
<li><strong>Surgery</strong>: Temporal lobectomy for drug-resistant temporal lobe epilepsy (60-70% seizure freedom); laser interstitial thermal therapy (LITT) for focal lesions</li>
<li><strong>Neuromodulation</strong>: Vagus nerve stimulation (VNS), responsive neurostimulation (RNS), deep brain stimulation (DBS) of anterior thalamus</li>
<li><strong>Ketogenic diet</strong>: High-fat, low-carbohydrate diet -- effective in pediatric drug-resistant epilepsy (50% reduction in seizures in 50% of patients)</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Sleep hygiene (sleep deprivation is a major trigger), stress management, alcohol avoidance, seizure diary tracking</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Magnesium (neuronal excitability modulation), vitamin B6 (cofactor in GABA synthesis), omega-3 fatty acids, vitamin D (often depleted by AEDs), taurine<br />
