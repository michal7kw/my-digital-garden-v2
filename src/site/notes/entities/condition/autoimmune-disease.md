<h1>Autoimmune Disease</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A condition in which the immune system mistakenly attacks and damages the body's own healthy cells, tissues, and organs.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Fatigue and malaise</li>
<li>Joint pain and swelling</li>
<li>Intermittent fever</li>
<li>Skin rashes and inflammation</li>
<li>Digestive issues (bloating, diarrhea)</li>
<li>Muscle aches and weakness</li>
<li>Recurrent infections or slow healing</li>
<li>Brain fog and cognitive difficulty</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Immunosuppressive therapy</strong>: DMARDs (methotrexate, hydroxychloroquine), biologics (anti-TNF, anti-IL-6), corticosteroids for acute flares (minimize long-term use)</li>
<li><strong>Anti-inflammatory diet</strong>: Mediterranean or autoimmune protocol (AIP) — eliminate processed foods, added sugar, seed oils. Emphasize omega-3 rich fish, colorful vegetables, bone broth</li>
<li><strong>Gut health</strong>: Probiotics, L-glutamine for intestinal barrier repair, address dysbiosis and intestinal permeability (common driver of autoimmunity)</li>
<li><strong>Lifestyle</strong>: Stress reduction (HPA axis dysregulation is a major trigger), adequate sleep (7-9 hours), regular moderate exercise (avoid overtraining during flares)</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Vitamin D (target &gt;40 ng/mL — immunomodulatory), omega-3 fatty acids (2-4 g/day), curcumin (NF-kB inhibition), glutathione/NAC (antioxidant support), low-dose naltrexone (emerging evidence for immune regulation)</li>
<li><strong>Environmental</strong>: Reduce toxin exposure (BPA, heavy metals, pesticides), manage infections (EBV reactivation is a common trigger)</li>
</ul>
