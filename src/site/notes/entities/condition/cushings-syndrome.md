<h1>Cushings Syndrome</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A hormonal disorder caused by prolonged exposure to high levels of cortisol, resulting in a variety of physical and metabolic changes.</p>
<h2>Symptoms</h2>
<ul>
<li>Central obesity (moon face, buffalo hump), thin extremities</li>
<li>Purple striae, easy bruising, hypertension, hyperglycemia</li>
<li>Proximal muscle weakness, depression, osteoporosis</li>
</ul>
<h2>Treatment Approaches</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Surgery</strong>: Transsphenoidal surgery for pituitary adenoma (first-line)</li>
<li><strong>Medical</strong>: Ketoconazole, metyrapone, osilodrostat, pasireotide, mifepristone</li>
<li><strong>Exogenous</strong>: Taper corticosteroids to lowest effective dose</li>
<li><strong>Supplements</strong>: Vitamin D + calcium (bone protection), potassium, magnesium</li>
</ul>
