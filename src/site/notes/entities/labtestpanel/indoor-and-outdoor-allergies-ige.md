<h1>indoor_and_outdoor_allergies_ige</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Indoor and Outdoor Allergies IgE Panel is a comprehensive 64-allergen test that identifies IgE-mediated sensitivities to environmental allergens across four major categories: molds, weeds, trees, and grasses, plus indoor allergens such as dust mites, cockroach, and mouse proteins. Total serum IgE is also measured to assess overall atopic burden.</p>
<p>Identifying specific environmental allergen triggers is critical for effective allergy management, guiding allergen avoidance strategies, immunotherapy (allergy shot) planning, and targeted pharmacotherapy. The panel covers region-specific allergens and is relevant for patients with allergic rhinitis, allergic asthma, allergic conjunctivitis, atopic dermatitis, and chronic urticaria. Allergens tested may vary by geographic region.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>Mold Panel (8 species) -- Aspergillus, Cladosporium, Penicillium, Alternaria, Mucor, Aureobasidium</li>
<li>Weed Panel (13 species) -- Ragweed, Mugwort, Plantain, Lamb’s Quarters, Russian Thistle, and others</li>
<li>Tree Panel (13 species) -- Maple, Cedar, Oak, Olive, Eucalyptus, Pine, Cottonwood, and others</li>
<li>Grass Panel (12 species) -- Timothy, Bermuda, Rye, Kentucky Blue, Bahia, and others</li>
<li>Dust Mites (D. pteronyssinus, D. farinae, Blomia tropicalis) -- Indoor perennial allergens</li>
<li>Cockroach (I6) IgE -- Common indoor allergen</li>
<li>Mouse Urine Proteins (E72) IgE -- Indoor allergen in urban settings</li>
<li>IgE, Total, Serum -- Overall atopic burden assessment</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Chronic nasal congestion, sneezing, or rhinorrhea (allergic rhinitis)</li>
<li>Seasonal or perennial asthma exacerbation evaluation</li>
<li>Pre-immunotherapy allergen identification</li>
<li>Chronic or recurrent conjunctivitis</li>
<li>Atopic dermatitis trigger identification</li>
</ul>
