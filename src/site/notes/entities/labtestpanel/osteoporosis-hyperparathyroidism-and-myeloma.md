<h1>osteoporosis_hyperparathyroidism_and_myeloma</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Osteoporosis, Hyperparathyroidism, and Myeloma Panel is a specialized 13-biomarker panel that evaluates three interconnected conditions affecting bone health: osteoporosis (bone density loss), hyperparathyroidism (excessive parathyroid hormone causing calcium imbalance), and multiple myeloma (a plasma cell cancer that destroys bone tissue). These conditions can present with similar symptoms -- bone pain, fractures, fatigue -- making a comprehensive panel essential for differential diagnosis.</p>
<p>Ionized calcium and intact parathyroid hormone (PTH) assess calcium-PTH axis dysregulation. Serum protein electrophoresis (SPEP) with globulin fractions and abnormal protein bands screens for monoclonal gammopathies, including multiple myeloma and its precursor conditions (MGUS). This panel goes beyond the basic bone health panel by addressing both metabolic and malignant causes of bone disease.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>Ionized Calcium -- Biologically active calcium fraction</li>
<li>Parathyroid Hormone, Intact (PTH) -- Parathyroid function and calcium regulation</li>
<li>Alpha-1 Globulin -- Serum protein fraction (acute phase)</li>
<li>Alpha-2 Globulin -- Serum protein fraction</li>
<li>Beta-1 Globulin -- Serum protein fraction (includes transferrin)</li>
<li>Beta-2 Globulin -- Serum protein fraction</li>
<li>Abnormal Protein Bands (1, 2, 3) -- Monoclonal protein detection for myeloma screening</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Unexplained bone pain or pathologic fractures</li>
<li>Hypercalcemia workup (elevated calcium requiring further investigation)</li>
<li>Suspected primary hyperparathyroidism</li>
<li>Screening for multiple myeloma or monoclonal gammopathy (MGUS)</li>
<li>Advanced osteoporosis evaluation beyond standard bone health panel</li>
</ul>
