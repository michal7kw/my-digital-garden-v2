<h1>food_allergies_ige</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Food Allergies IgE Panel measures immunoglobulin E (IgE) antibodies specific to 20 common food allergens. IgE-mediated food allergies represent true allergic reactions -- immediate hypersensitivity responses that can range from mild hives and oral tingling to severe anaphylaxis. This distinguishes them from IgG-mediated food sensitivities, which cause delayed, less acute responses.</p>
<p>The panel covers the most prevalent food allergens including tree nuts (macadamia, brazil, hazelnut, cashew), shellfish (scallop, lobster), fish (codfish), legumes (soybean, peanut), seeds (sesame), dairy (cow's milk), and eggs. Positive results indicate sensitization and, when correlated with clinical history, guide allergen avoidance strategies and emergency preparedness planning including epinephrine auto-injector prescriptions.</p>

<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>History of immediate allergic reactions after eating specific foods</li>
<li>Suspected food allergy requiring confirmation before elimination diet</li>
<li>Anaphylaxis workup to identify the causative allergen</li>
<li>Pediatric food allergy evaluation</li>
<li>Pre-food challenge testing for reintroduction protocols</li>
</ul>
