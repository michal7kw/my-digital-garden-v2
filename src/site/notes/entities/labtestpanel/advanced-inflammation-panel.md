<h1>advanced_inflammation_panel</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Advanced Inflammation Panel is a multi-marker blood panel that evaluates systemic inflammation through complementary biochemical pathways. Unlike a single CRP test, this panel captures the full spectrum of inflammatory activity -- from acute-phase proteins to cytokine-level signaling -- enabling clinicians and individuals to distinguish between vascular, chronic, and acute inflammatory processes.</p>
<p>By combining markers such as hs-CRP (vascular inflammation), ESR (chronic inflammation), fibrinogen (acute phase and clotting risk), ferritin (iron stores and acute-phase reactant), TNF-alpha, and IL-6 (pro-inflammatory cytokines), the panel provides a comprehensive inflammatory burden assessment that guides both diagnosis and intervention strategies.</p>

<ul>
<li><a href="/entities/labtest/high-sensitivity-c-reactive-protein-hs-crp/" class="internal-link">high sensitivity c reactive protein hs crp</a> -- Vascular and systemic inflammation marker</li>
<li><a href="/entities/labtest/esr/" class="internal-link">esr</a> -- Erythrocyte sedimentation rate for chronic inflammation</li>
<li><a href="/entities/labtest/fibrinogen/" class="internal-link">fibrinogen</a> -- Acute-phase protein and clotting risk factor</li>
<li><a href="/entities/labtest/ferritin/" class="internal-link">ferritin</a> -- Iron storage marker and acute-phase reactant</li>
<li><a href="/entities/labtest/tnf-alpha/" class="internal-link">TNF Alpha</a> -- Pro-inflammatory cytokine</li>
<li><a href="/entities/labtest/interleukin-6/" class="internal-link">Interleukin 6</a> -- Key mediator of acute-phase response</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Chronic inflammatory conditions (autoimmune diseases, metabolic syndrome)</li>
<li>Cardiovascular risk stratification</li>
<li>Monitoring response to anti-inflammatory therapies</li>
<li>Evaluation of persistent fatigue, joint pain, or unexplained symptoms</li>
<li>Differentiating between acute and chronic inflammatory processes</li>
</ul>
