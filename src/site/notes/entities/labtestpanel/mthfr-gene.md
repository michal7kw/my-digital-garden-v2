<h1>mthfr_gene</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The MTHFR Gene Test identifies mutations in the methylenetetrahydrofolate reductase gene, which encodes a key enzyme in the folate/methylation cycle. The two most clinically significant variants are C677T and A1298C. The C677T variant (especially homozygous TT) reduces enzyme activity by up to 70%, leading to elevated homocysteine levels and impaired methylation capacity.</p>
<p>Impaired MTHFR function affects folate metabolism, homocysteine clearance, DNA synthesis and repair, neurotransmitter production, and detoxification pathways. Elevated homocysteine is an independent risk factor for cardiovascular disease, stroke, and venous thromboembolism. MTHFR variants also influence the response to certain medications (methotrexate, 5-fluorouracil) and supplementation strategies -- individuals with MTHFR mutations benefit from methylated forms of folate (5-MTHF) rather than synthetic folic acid.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>MTHFR Genotype (C677T and A1298C) -- Identifies enzyme activity-reducing mutations</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Elevated homocysteine levels unresponsive to standard B vitamin supplementation</li>
<li>Recurrent pregnancy loss or neural tube defect history</li>
<li>Family history of premature cardiovascular disease or stroke</li>
<li>Personalized supplementation planning (methylfolate vs folic acid)</li>
<li>Pharmacogenomic guidance for methotrexate or anti-folate medications</li>
</ul>
