<h1>forever_chemicals_pfas_exposure</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The PFAS Exposure Panel quantifies blood levels of 10 per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS), commonly known as “forever chemicals” due to their extreme environmental persistence. PFAS are synthetic compounds found in nonstick cookware, waterproof clothing, food packaging, firefighting foam (AFFF), and contaminated drinking water. Detectable levels are present in approximately 97% of the U.S. population.</p>
<p>PFAS accumulate in the body with half-lives of 2-8 years and have been linked to thyroid dysfunction, liver damage, immune suppression, reproductive harm, increased cholesterol, and several cancers (kidney, testicular). The panel measures both linear and branched isomers of PFOA and PFOS (the most studied PFAS compounds) plus longer-chain variants, with a NASEM summation score providing an overall exposure assessment referenced against National Academies guidelines.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>PFAS - Linear Perfluorooctanoic Acid (PFOA) Isomers -- Most studied PFAS compound</li>
<li>PFAS - Branched Perfluorooctanoic Acid Isomers -- Alternative PFOA metabolites</li>
<li>PFAS - Linear Perfluorooctane Sulfonic Acid (PFOS) Isomers -- Persistent environmental contaminant</li>
<li>PFAS - Branched Perfluorooctane Sulfonic Acid Isomers -- Alternative PFOS metabolites</li>
<li>PFAS - Perfluorohexane Sulfonic Acid (PFHxS) -- Short-chain PFAS variant</li>
<li>PFAS - Perfluorononanoic Acid (PFNA) -- Long-chain PFAS</li>
<li>PFAS - Perfluorodecanoic Acid (PFDA) -- Long-chain PFAS</li>
<li>PFAS - Perfluoroundecanoic Acid (PFUnDA) -- Long-chain PFAS</li>
<li>PFAS - NASEM Summation -- Aggregate exposure score per National Academies guidelines</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Suspected PFAS contamination from drinking water or occupational exposure</li>
<li>Thyroid dysfunction of unclear etiology</li>
<li>Unexplained elevated cholesterol unresponsive to standard interventions</li>
<li>Immune suppression or vaccine non-response evaluation</li>
<li>Residents of communities near PFAS-contaminated sites or military bases</li>
</ul>
