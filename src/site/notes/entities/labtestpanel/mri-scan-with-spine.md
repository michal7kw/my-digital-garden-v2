<h1>mri_scan_with_spine</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The MRI Scan with Spine combines the full-body MRI assessment with dedicated spinal imaging covering the cervical, thoracic, and lumbar regions. This addition is critical because spinal pathology is extremely common (affecting up to 80% of adults at some point) and often underlies chronic pain, radiculopathy, and functional limitations that may not be apparent on standard body MRI sequences.</p>
<p>The spine-specific imaging detects herniated and bulging discs, spinal stenosis (central and foraminal), spondylosis (degenerative disc disease), compression fractures, scoliosis, spinal cord tumors, syrinx, and inflammatory conditions. This panel is particularly valuable for individuals with back or neck pain, radiculopathy symptoms, or those at risk for osteoporotic vertebral fractures.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>Full-Body MRI -- All organs from standard MRI Scan</li>
<li>Cervical Spine MRI -- Neck vertebrae, discs, spinal cord, and nerve roots</li>
<li>Thoracic Spine MRI -- Mid-back vertebrae and spinal cord</li>
<li>Lumbar Spine MRI -- Lower back vertebrae, discs, cauda equina, and nerve roots</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Chronic back or neck pain requiring imaging evaluation</li>
<li>Radiculopathy symptoms (arm or leg pain, numbness, weakness)</li>
<li>Suspected spinal stenosis or herniated disc</li>
<li>History of spinal trauma or osteoporotic fracture risk</li>
<li>Combined whole-body and spinal screening for comprehensive assessment</li>
</ul>
