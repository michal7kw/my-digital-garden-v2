<h1>bisphenol_a_bpa_exposure</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The BPA Exposure Panel measures urinary levels of Bisphenol A, one of the most prevalent endocrine-disrupting chemicals in modern environments. BPA is found in polycarbonate plastics, epoxy can linings, thermal receipt paper, and personal care products. It mimics estrogen and can interfere with hormonal signaling at very low concentrations.</p>
<p>Chronic BPA exposure has been associated with reproductive dysfunction, insulin resistance, obesity, thyroid disruption, and increased cardiovascular risk. Because BPA has a relatively short half-life (approximately 6 hours), urinary levels reflect recent exposure patterns. Serial testing can help evaluate the effectiveness of exposure reduction strategies such as switching to BPA-free products, avoiding canned foods, and supporting hepatic detoxification pathways.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>Bisphenol A (BPA), Urine -- Quantifies recent BPA exposure via urine sample</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Suspected endocrine disruption from environmental chemical exposure</li>
<li>Unexplained hormonal imbalances or reproductive issues</li>
<li>Metabolic syndrome or insulin resistance of unclear etiology</li>
<li>Monitoring effectiveness of BPA avoidance strategies</li>
<li>Occupational exposure assessment (plastics manufacturing, retail workers)</li>
</ul>
