<h1>heart_lungs_ct_scan</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Heart and Lungs CT Scan is a low-dose chest computed tomography (CT) that simultaneously evaluates both pulmonary and cardiac structures. The scan detects lung abnormalities including early-stage lung cancer, pulmonary nodules, emphysema, and interstitial lung disease. AI-enhanced post-processing of the same images generates a coronary artery calcium (CAC) score, a powerful predictor of future cardiovascular events.</p>
<p>The CAC score quantifies calcified plaque in the coronary arteries on a scale from 0 (no detectable plaque) to over 400 (extensive calcification). A CAC score of 0 in low-to-intermediate risk individuals provides strong reassurance, while elevated scores prompt aggressive risk factor modification. This dual-purpose imaging approach maximizes diagnostic yield from a single low-radiation-dose scan, making it efficient for comprehensive cardiopulmonary screening.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>Low-Dose Chest CT -- Lung parenchyma and mediastinal evaluation</li>
<li>Coronary Artery Calcium (CAC) Score -- AI-derived cardiac plaque quantification</li>
<li>Pulmonary Nodule Assessment -- Detection and characterization of lung nodules</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Lung cancer screening in current or former heavy smokers (age 50-80)</li>
<li>Cardiovascular risk stratification via coronary calcium scoring</li>
<li>Chronic cough or respiratory symptoms requiring imaging workup</li>
<li>Family history of lung cancer or premature coronary artery disease</li>
<li>Proactive health screening for individuals at intermediate cardiovascular risk</li>
</ul>
