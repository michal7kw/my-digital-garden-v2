<h1>genetic_heart_risks</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Genetic Heart Risks Panel identifies specific single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) associated with increased risk for cardiovascular events. The 9p21 locus (rs1333049, rs10757278) is one of the most validated genetic risk factors for coronary artery disease and myocardial infarction, independent of traditional risk factors like cholesterol and blood pressure. The 4q25 variants (rs2200733, rs10033464) are associated with atrial fibrillation risk.</p>
<p>The LPA Aspirin Genotype assesses whether an individual is likely to benefit from aspirin therapy for cardiovascular prevention based on their lipoprotein(a) genetic profile. These genetic markers provide lifelong risk information from a single test, enabling early and aggressive preventive strategies in genetically high-risk individuals. Results should be interpreted alongside traditional cardiovascular risk factors and lipid profiles.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>9p21 Genotype (rs1333049) -- Coronary artery disease and MI risk variant</li>
<li>9p21 Genotype (rs10757278) -- Additional CAD risk locus</li>
<li>4q25-Atrial Fibrillation Risk (rs2200733) -- AF susceptibility variant</li>
<li>4q25-Atrial Fibrillation Risk (rs10033464) -- Additional AF risk locus</li>
<li>LPA Aspirin Genotype -- Aspirin response prediction based on Lp(a) genetics</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Family history of premature heart attack or sudden cardiac death</li>
<li>Personal or family history of atrial fibrillation</li>
<li>Cardiovascular risk stratification beyond traditional risk factors</li>
<li>Guiding aspirin therapy decisions for primary prevention</li>
<li>Young individuals with strong family history of coronary artery disease</li>
</ul>
