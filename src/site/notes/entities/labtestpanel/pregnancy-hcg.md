<h1>pregnancy_hcg</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Pregnancy HCG Panel measures serum levels of human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG), the hormone produced by the placenta after embryo implantation. Quantitative serum hCG testing is more sensitive and precise than urine-based home pregnancy tests, capable of detecting pregnancy as early as 6-8 days after ovulation. The test provides a numerical hCG value rather than a simple positive/negative result.</p>
<p>Serial hCG measurements are clinically valuable beyond pregnancy confirmation: normally doubling hCG levels every 48-72 hours in early pregnancy indicate a healthy progressing pregnancy, while abnormal doubling times may suggest ectopic pregnancy, miscarriage, or molar pregnancy. Extremely elevated hCG levels can indicate multiple gestation (twins or more) or gestational trophoblastic disease. Declining hCG levels after treatment are monitored in certain cancers (testicular, ovarian).</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>

</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Pregnancy confirmation (earlier and more sensitive than urine tests)</li>
<li>Early pregnancy viability assessment via serial hCG monitoring</li>
<li>Ectopic pregnancy evaluation when hCG doubling time is abnormal</li>
<li>Post-miscarriage hCG clearance monitoring</li>
<li>Tumor marker for gestational trophoblastic disease and certain germ cell tumors</li>
</ul>
