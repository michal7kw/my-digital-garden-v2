<h1>coagulation_panel</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Coagulation Panel provides a comprehensive evaluation of the blood clotting system, covering both the intrinsic (contact activation) and extrinsic (tissue factor) coagulation pathways, as well as fibrinolysis and platelet function. PT/INR assesses the extrinsic pathway and is the standard for monitoring warfarin therapy, while PTT evaluates the intrinsic pathway and is used for heparin monitoring.</p>
<p>D-dimer measures fibrin degradation products, making it a sensitive marker for thrombotic events such as deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism (PE). Fibrinogen serves as both a clotting factor (the substrate for fibrin formation) and an acute-phase reactant. Platelet count completes the assessment by evaluating the cellular component of hemostasis.</p>

<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Pre-surgical bleeding risk assessment</li>
<li>Evaluation of unexplained bleeding or bruising</li>
<li>Suspected deep vein thrombosis or pulmonary embolism</li>
<li>Anticoagulant therapy monitoring (warfarin, heparin)</li>
<li>Disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC) workup</li>
</ul>
