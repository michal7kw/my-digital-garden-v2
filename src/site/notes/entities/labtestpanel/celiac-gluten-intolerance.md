<h1>celiac_gluten_intolerance</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Celiac and Gluten Intolerance Panel detects antibodies associated with celiac disease, an autoimmune condition where gluten ingestion triggers immune-mediated damage to the small intestinal lining. Celiac disease affects approximately 1 in 100 people worldwide, though the majority remain undiagnosed due to variable and often subtle symptom presentation.</p>
<p>The panel measures both IgA and IgG antibodies to tissue transglutaminase (tTG) and deamidated gliadin peptides (DGP), providing redundancy in case of IgA deficiency (present in 2-3% of celiac patients). Total IgA is included to identify IgA-deficient individuals for whom IgA-based tests would yield false negatives. A positive result warrants confirmation with intestinal biopsy and clinical correlation.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>Tissue Transglutaminase (IgA) -- Primary screening antibody for celiac disease</li>
<li>Tissue Transglutaminase (IgG) -- Alternative marker when IgA is deficient</li>
<li>Deamidated Gliadin (IgA) -- Supplementary marker for gluten immune reactivity</li>
<li>Deamidated Gliadin (IgG) -- IgG-based gluten reactivity marker</li>
<li>Immunoglobulin A (IgA) -- Total IgA to detect IgA deficiency affecting test validity</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Chronic digestive symptoms (bloating, diarrhea, abdominal pain)</li>
<li>Unexplained iron-deficiency anemia or nutrient malabsorption</li>
<li>Family history of celiac disease or autoimmune conditions</li>
<li>Dermatitis herpetiformis (skin manifestation of celiac disease)</li>
<li>Monitoring dietary compliance in diagnosed celiac patients</li>
</ul>
