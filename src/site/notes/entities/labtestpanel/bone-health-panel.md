<h1>bone_health_panel</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Bone Health Panel evaluates both bone formation and bone resorption alongside the calcium-vitamin D metabolic axis. This combination of markers provides a dynamic picture of skeletal health beyond what a static DEXA scan reveals, allowing clinicians to detect accelerated bone turnover before significant bone density loss occurs.</p>
<p>Osteocalcin reflects osteoblast activity (bone building), while alkaline phosphatase indicates overall bone metabolic activity. Calcium, phosphorus, and vitamin D 25-OH complete the picture by assessing the mineral and hormonal substrates required for healthy bone remodeling. This panel is essential for osteoporosis risk assessment, fracture risk evaluation, and monitoring the efficacy of treatments such as bisphosphonates or vitamin D supplementation.</p>

<ul>
<li><a href="/entities/labtest/osteocalcin/" class="internal-link">osteocalcin</a> -- Bone formation marker produced by osteoblasts</li>
<li><a class="internal-link is-unresolved" href="/404">phosphorus</a> -- Essential mineral for bone mineralization</li>
<li><a href="/entities/labtest/vitamin-d-25oh/" class="internal-link">vitamin d 25oh</a> -- Key regulator of calcium absorption and bone metabolism</li>
<li><a href="/entities/labtest/calcium/" class="internal-link">calcium</a> -- Primary mineral component of bone matrix</li>
<li><a href="/entities/labtest/alkaline-phosphatase-alp/" class="internal-link">alkaline phosphatase alp</a> -- Enzyme indicating bone and liver metabolic activity</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Osteoporosis risk assessment, especially in postmenopausal women</li>
<li>Monitoring bisphosphonate or denosumab therapy effectiveness</li>
<li>Evaluation of metabolic bone diseases (Paget's, osteomalacia)</li>
<li>Vitamin D deficiency screening</li>
<li>Fracture risk assessment in elderly or immobilized patients</li>
</ul>
