<h1>brain_injury_degeneration_screening</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Brain Injury and Degeneration Screening Panel measures neurofilament light chain (NfL), a structural protein released into the bloodstream when neurons are damaged or destroyed. NfL is one of the most sensitive and versatile blood-based biomarkers for active neuronal injury, applicable across a wide range of neurological conditions.</p>
<p>Elevated NfL levels indicate ongoing axonal damage regardless of the underlying cause, making this test valuable for evaluating traumatic brain injuries (TBIs), neurodegenerative diseases (ALS, Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, multiple sclerosis), and treatment response monitoring. NfL levels correlate with disease severity and progression rate, providing objective data to complement clinical assessments and neuroimaging.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>Neurofilament Light Chain (NfL) -- Blood-based marker of active neuronal and axonal damage</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Suspected traumatic brain injury from sports, accidents, or falls</li>
<li>Early detection of neurodegenerative diseases (ALS, Alzheimer's, Parkinson's)</li>
<li>Monitoring disease activity and progression in multiple sclerosis</li>
<li>Evaluating treatment response in neurological conditions</li>
<li>Baseline assessment for individuals in high-impact sports or occupations</li>
</ul>
