<h1>extended_heavy_metals</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Extended Heavy Metals Panel measures blood or urine levels of additional toxic metals beyond the standard lead and mercury tests. This panel focuses on arsenic and aluminum, two widely encountered heavy metals that accumulate in tissues over time and can cause chronic health effects even at low-level exposure.</p>
<p>Arsenic exposure occurs through contaminated drinking water, rice, seafood, and agricultural products. Chronic arsenic exposure is associated with increased cancer risk, peripheral neuropathy, and cardiovascular disease. Aluminum exposure comes from cookware, antacids, food additives, and antiperspirants, with potential links to neurotoxicity and bone disease. Testing guides targeted detoxification strategies, dietary modifications, and environmental exposure reduction.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>Arsenic -- Toxic metalloid from water and food contamination</li>
<li>Aluminum -- Neurotoxic metal from cookware and consumer products</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Suspected environmental heavy metal exposure</li>
<li>Occupational exposure (mining, smelting, electronics manufacturing)</li>
<li>Chronic neurological symptoms of unclear etiology</li>
<li>Kidney dysfunction potentially related to metal toxicity</li>
<li>Monitoring chelation or detoxification therapy effectiveness</li>
</ul>
