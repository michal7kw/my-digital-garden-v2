<h1>food_sensitivities_igg</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Food Sensitivities IgG Panel measures immunoglobulin G (IgG) antibodies to 11 common foods, identifying delayed-type food sensitivities that differ from immediate IgE-mediated allergies. IgG-mediated reactions typically manifest hours to days after consumption, making them difficult to identify through dietary observation alone.</p>
<p>Elevated IgG responses may indicate increased intestinal permeability (leaky gut), chronic low-grade inflammation, or immune reactivity to frequently consumed foods. Common symptoms associated with food sensitivities include bloating, brain fog, headaches, joint pain, skin issues, and fatigue. While the clinical significance of food-specific IgG testing is debated, it can guide elimination diets and help identify problematic foods for individuals with chronic inflammatory or digestive complaints.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>Wheat Sensitivity (IgG) -- Gluten-containing grain reactivity</li>
<li>Casein Sensitivity (IgG) -- Dairy protein reactivity</li>
<li>Egg White Sensitivity (IgG) -- Egg protein reactivity</li>
<li>Soybean Sensitivity (IgG) -- Legume reactivity</li>
<li>Peanut Sensitivity (IgG) -- Legume/nut reactivity</li>
<li>Codfish Sensitivity (IgG) -- Fish protein reactivity</li>
<li>Yeast Sensitivity (IgG) -- Candida-related reactivity</li>
<li>Maize/Corn Sensitivity (IgG) -- Grain reactivity</li>
<li>Coffee Sensitivity (IgG) -- Caffeine-related reactivity</li>
<li>Tomato Sensitivity (IgG) -- Nightshade reactivity</li>
<li>Cacao Sensitivity (IgG) -- Chocolate-related reactivity</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Chronic digestive complaints (IBS, bloating, irregular bowel habits)</li>
<li>Unexplained migraines or chronic headaches</li>
<li>Chronic fatigue with suspected dietary triggers</li>
<li>Skin conditions (eczema, acne) potentially related to diet</li>
<li>Guiding elimination diet protocols</li>
</ul>
