<h1>mri_scan</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Full-Body MRI Scan provides a comprehensive, radiation-free imaging assessment of multiple organ systems using magnetic resonance technology. This scan covers the brain, head and neck, chest, abdomen, and pelvis, screening for a wide spectrum of conditions including solid organ cancers, vascular abnormalities, and structural pathology.</p>
<p>The MRI scan can detect cancers of the brain, thyroid, liver, gallbladder, pancreas, spleen, kidneys, adrenal glands, bladder, ovaries, uterus, and prostate. It also identifies non-malignant conditions such as stroke evidence, sinus inflammation, fatty liver disease (NAFLD/NASH), gallstones, uterine fibroids, kidney stones, and abdominal aortic aneurysm. As a non-ionizing imaging modality, MRI is safe for serial screening without cumulative radiation exposure.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>Brain MRI -- Structural brain assessment, stroke detection, tumor screening</li>
<li>Abdominal MRI -- Liver, pancreas, spleen, kidneys, adrenal glands</li>
<li>Pelvic MRI -- Bladder, reproductive organs (ovaries/uterus or prostate)</li>
<li>Head and Neck MRI -- Thyroid, sinuses, cervical structures</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Proactive whole-body cancer screening</li>
<li>Unexplained symptoms requiring multi-organ evaluation</li>
<li>Family history of cancers affecting multiple organ systems</li>
<li>Baseline imaging for longitudinal health monitoring</li>
<li>Follow-up for previously detected incidental findings</li>
</ul>
