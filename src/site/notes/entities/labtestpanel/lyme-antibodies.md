<h1>lyme_antibodies</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Lyme and Tick-Borne Infections Panel is a comprehensive 20-biomarker antibody panel that screens for the five major tick-borne pathogens: Borrelia burgdorferi (Lyme disease), Babesia (babesiosis), Ehrlichia (ehrlichiosis), Anaplasma (anaplasmosis), and Bartonella. Both IgM (recent/active infection) and IgG (past exposure/chronic infection) antibodies are measured for each pathogen, with Western blot confirmation for Lyme-specific antibodies.</p>
<p>Tick-borne diseases are frequently misdiagnosed because their symptoms -- fatigue, joint pain, neurological complaints, cognitive dysfunction -- overlap with autoimmune diseases, fibromyalgia, and chronic fatigue syndrome. Co-infections (carrying multiple tick-borne pathogens simultaneously) are common and complicate both diagnosis and treatment. Early detection enables timely antibiotic therapy and helps prevent progression to chronic Lyme disease or persistent neurological damage.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>Lyme Antibody Screen -- Initial Borrelia burgdorferi screening</li>
<li>Lyme Disease AB (IgM and IgG), Western Blot -- Confirmatory Lyme testing</li>
<li>Borrelia Miyamotoi Antibodies (IgM, IgG) -- Relapsing fever Borrelia</li>
<li>Babesia Microti Antibodies (IgM, IgG) -- Common babesiosis species</li>
<li>Babesia Duncani (WA1) Antibody (IgG) -- West Coast babesiosis species</li>
<li>Anaplasma Phagocytophilum Antibodies (IgM, IgG) -- Anaplasmosis</li>
<li>Ehrlichia Chaffeensis Antibodies (IgM, IgG) -- Ehrlichiosis</li>
<li>Bartonella Henselae Antibodies (IgM, IgG) -- Cat scratch disease pathogen</li>
<li>Bartonella Quintana Antibodies (IgM, IgG) -- Trench fever pathogen</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Known tick bite with subsequent symptoms (erythema migrans rash, fever, fatigue)</li>
<li>Chronic multi-system symptoms mimicking autoimmune disease</li>
<li>Unexplained neurological symptoms, Bell's palsy, or cardiac conduction abnormalities</li>
<li>Residents of or travelers to endemic tick-borne disease regions</li>
<li>Evaluation for tick-borne co-infections in known Lyme patients</li>
</ul>
