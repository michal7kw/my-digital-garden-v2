<h1>chronic_inflammatory_response_mold_response</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The CIRS/Mold Response Panel evaluates biomarkers associated with Chronic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (CIRS), a multi-system illness triggered by biotoxin exposure -- most commonly from mold in water-damaged buildings. This panel does not directly detect mold exposure but instead measures the inflammatory cascade and immune dysregulation that characterize CIRS.</p>
<p>The panel includes MMP-9 (tissue remodeling and inflammation), C4a complement (innate immune activation), PAI-1 (coagulation and fibrinolysis dysregulation), and serum osmolality (antidiuretic hormone pathway disruption). These markers reflect the Shoemaker CIRS protocol and can help identify patients whose symptoms -- fatigue, cognitive impairment, respiratory issues, and chronic pain -- stem from biotoxin-driven inflammation rather than primary autoimmune or infectious causes.</p>
<h2>Included Tests</h2>
<ul>
<li>Matrix Metalloproteinase-9 (MMP-9) -- Tissue remodeling enzyme elevated in CIRS</li>
<li>Complement Component C4a -- Innate immune activation marker</li>
<li>Plasminogen Activator Inhibitor-1 Ag (PAI-1) -- Coagulation pathway dysregulation</li>
<li>Osmolality, Serum -- Reflects ADH pathway disruption common in CIRS</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Indications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Suspected mold illness from water-damaged building exposure</li>
<li>Chronic multi-system symptoms unresponsive to standard treatment</li>
<li>Persistent fatigue, cognitive dysfunction, and respiratory symptoms</li>
<li>Evaluation following the Shoemaker CIRS diagnostic protocol</li>
<li>Differentiating CIRS from fibromyalgia, chronic fatigue syndrome, or autoimmune disease</li>
</ul>
