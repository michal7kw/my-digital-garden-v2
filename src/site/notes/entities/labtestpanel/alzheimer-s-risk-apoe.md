<h1>alzheimer_s_risk_apoe</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The APOE Genotyping Panel identifies genetic variations in the apolipoprotein E (ApoE) gene, which plays a central role in cholesterol transport and lipid metabolism. The three common APOE alleles (e2, e3, e4) confer different risk profiles: the e4 allele is the strongest known genetic risk factor for late-onset Alzheimer’s disease, while also increasing cardiovascular disease risk. The e2 allele is associated with lower Alzheimer’s risk but may increase triglyceride levels.</p>
<p>Understanding your APOE genotype provides actionable information for personalized prevention strategies. APOE4 carriers benefit from aggressive cardiovascular risk management, anti-inflammatory dietary patterns (such as Mediterranean diet), regular aerobic exercise, and enhanced cognitive monitoring. This single-biomarker genetic test offers lifelong risk stratification from a one-time blood draw.</p>
