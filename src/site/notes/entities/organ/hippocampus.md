<h1>Hippocampus</h1>
<h2>Overview (Consumer-Friendly)</h2>
<p>The hippocampus is a seahorse-shaped structure deep in your brain that acts as your memory center. Think of it as your brain's &quot;save button&quot; - it helps you form new memories and recall past experiences. It's also crucial for finding your way around and learning new skills.</p>
<h3>Key Functions</h3>
<ul>
<li>Creating new memories from experiences</li>
<li>Remembering facts and events (like where you parked your car)</li>
<li>Navigating spaces and finding your way</li>
<li>Learning new information and skills</li>
<li>Emotional memory formation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Lifestyle Tips for Hippocampal Health</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Exercise regularly</strong> - Aerobic exercise promotes new neuron growth in the hippocampus</li>
<li><strong>Get quality sleep</strong> - Memory consolidation happens during sleep</li>
<li><strong>Learn new things</strong> - Challenge your brain with novel experiences</li>
<li><strong>Manage stress</strong> - Chronic stress can shrink the hippocampus</li>
<li><strong>Stay socially active</strong> - Social engagement supports brain health</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Information (Medical Professional)</h2>
<h3>Neuroanatomy</h3>
<p>The hippocampus is a paired structure located in the medial temporal lobe, consisting of the cornu ammonis (CA) fields CA1-CA4, dentate gyrus, and subiculum. It receives major inputs from the entorhinal cortex via the perforant pathway and projects to various cortical and subcortical regions via the fornix.</p>
<h3>Cytoarchitecture</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>CA1</strong>: Large pyramidal neurons, vulnerable to ischemia (Sommer's sector)</li>
<li><strong>CA2</strong>: Resistant to excitotoxicity, important for social memory</li>
<li><strong>CA3</strong>: Dense recurrent collaterals, pattern completion</li>
<li><strong>CA4</strong>: Part of dentate gyrus, neurogenesis zone</li>
<li><strong>Dentate Gyrus</strong>: Granule cells, one of few sites of adult neurogenesis</li>
</ul>
<h3>Common Pathologies</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Alzheimer's Disease</strong>: Early and severe atrophy, neurofibrillary tangles</li>
<li><strong>Temporal Lobe Epilepsy</strong>: Hippocampal sclerosis, mesial temporal sclerosis</li>
<li><strong>Depression</strong>: Reduced volume, impaired neurogenesis</li>
<li><strong>PTSD</strong>: Structural and functional abnormalities</li>
<li><strong>Hypoxic-ischemic injury</strong>: CA1 particularly vulnerable</li>
</ul>
<h3>Clinical Assessment</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Structural MRI</strong>: Volumetric analysis, asymmetry assessment</li>
<li><strong>Functional MRI</strong>: Activation during memory encoding/retrieval</li>
<li><strong>Neuropsychological Testing</strong>: Rey Auditory Verbal Learning Test, Logical Memory</li>
<li><strong>PET Imaging</strong>: Glucose metabolism (FDG-PET), amyloid imaging</li>
<li><strong>Diffusion Tensor Imaging</strong>: White matter tract integrity</li>
</ul>
<h3>Differential Diagnosis</h3>
<ul>
<li>Memory impairment: Alzheimer's vs. vascular dementia vs. depression vs. normal aging</li>
<li>Seizures: Temporal lobe epilepsy vs. frontal lobe epilepsy vs. psychogenic non-epileptic seizures</li>
</ul>

<h3>Structural Connectivity</h3>
<p><strong>Afferent Connections:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Entorhinal cortex (perforant pathway) - 95% strength</li>
<li>Medial septum (cholinergic, GABAergic) - 70% strength</li>
<li>Raphe nuclei (serotonergic) - 50% strength</li>
<li>Locus coeruleus (noradrenergic) - 40% strength</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Efferent Connections:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Subiculum → Entorhinal cortex - 90% strength</li>
<li>CA1 → Subiculum - 85% strength</li>
<li>CA3 → CA1 (Schaffer collaterals) - 95% strength</li>
<li>Fornix → Mammillary bodies - 80% strength</li>
</ul>
<h3>Gene Expression Profile</h3>
<p><strong>Top Expressed Genes</strong> (Allen Brain Atlas):</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>BDNF</strong>: Brain-derived neurotrophic factor - neuroplasticity, survival</li>
<li><strong>CREB1</strong>: cAMP response element-binding protein - memory consolidation</li>
<li><strong>ARC</strong>: Activity-regulated cytoskeleton-associated protein - synaptic plasticity</li>
<li><strong>CAMK2A</strong>: Calcium/calmodulin-dependent protein kinase II alpha - LTP induction</li>
<li><strong>NR2B (GRIN2B)</strong>: NMDA receptor subunit - synaptic plasticity</li>
</ul>
<h2>Supplements That Support Hippocampal Function</h2>
<h3>High Evidence (Level 4-5)</h3>
<h4>Omega-3 Fatty Acids (DHA/EPA)</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Evidence Level</strong>: 5/5</li>
<li><strong>Mechanism</strong>: Structural component of neuronal membranes, anti-inflammatory, enhances BDNF</li>
<li><strong>Molecular Targets</strong>: Membrane phospholipids, PPARγ, BDNF signaling</li>
<li><strong>Effect Type</strong>: Neuroprotective, enhances neurogenesis</li>
<li><strong>Clinical Trials</strong>:
<ul>
<li>NCT01953705 - Omega-3 for memory in older adults</li>
<li>NCT02941172 - DHA and cognitive aging</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Meta-Analyses</strong>:
<ul>
<li>PMID:32483598 - Beneficial effects on hippocampal volume</li>
<li>PMID:29331906 - Improved memory performance</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Evidence Quality</strong>: HIGH (12 RCTs, 3 meta-analyses, n&gt;5000)</li>
<li><strong>Consumer Note</strong>: Take with food for best absorption, 1000-2000mg combined DHA/EPA daily</li>
<li><strong>Dosing</strong>: 1000-2000mg DHA+EPA daily</li>
<li><strong>Safety</strong>: Very safe, may interact with blood thinners</li>
<li><strong>Contraindications</strong>: Bleeding disorders, upcoming surgery</li>
<li><strong>Drug Interactions</strong>: Warfarin, aspirin (monitor bleeding)</li>
</ul>
<h4>Bacopa Monnieri</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Evidence Level</strong>: 4/5</li>
<li><strong>Mechanism</strong>: Enhances dendritic arborization, modulates cholinergic transmission, antioxidant</li>
<li><strong>Molecular Targets</strong>: Acetylcholinesterase inhibition, CREB phosphorylation, dendritic growth</li>
<li><strong>Effect Type</strong>: Enhances memory consolidation and retrieval</li>
<li><strong>Clinical Trials</strong>:
<ul>
<li>PMID:23772955 - Memory enhancement in healthy adults</li>
<li>PMID:24252493 - Cognitive benefits in elderly</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Evidence Quality</strong>: MODERATE (6 RCTs, 2 systematic reviews, n&gt;800)</li>
<li><strong>Consumer Note</strong>: Takes 8-12 weeks for full effects, best with food</li>
<li><strong>Dosing</strong>: 300mg standardized extract (50% bacosides) daily</li>
<li><strong>Safety</strong>: Generally well-tolerated, mild GI upset possible</li>
<li><strong>Contraindications</strong>: Pregnancy, thyroid disorders (may affect thyroid hormones)</li>
<li><strong>Drug Interactions</strong>: Anticholinergic medications, sedatives</li>
</ul>
<h4>Lion's Mane Mushroom (Hericium erinaceus)</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Evidence Level</strong>: 3/5</li>
<li><strong>Mechanism</strong>: Stimulates NGF and BDNF synthesis, promotes neurogenesis</li>
<li><strong>Molecular Targets</strong>: NGF/BDNF pathways, ERK1/2 signaling</li>
<li><strong>Effect Type</strong>: Neuroprotective, enhances neurogenesis</li>
<li><strong>Clinical Trials</strong>:
<ul>
<li>PMID:30513922 - Cognitive improvement in mild cognitive impairment</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Evidence Quality</strong>: LOW-MODERATE (2 RCTs, preclinical data strong, n&lt;100)</li>
<li><strong>Consumer Note</strong>: Supports nerve growth and regeneration</li>
<li><strong>Dosing</strong>: 500-1000mg daily</li>
<li><strong>Safety</strong>: Very safe, rare allergic reactions</li>
<li><strong>Contraindications</strong>: Mushroom allergies</li>
<li><strong>Drug Interactions</strong>: None known</li>
</ul>
<h4>Phosphatidylserine</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Evidence Level</strong>: 4/5</li>
<li><strong>Mechanism</strong>: Membrane phospholipid, supports neurotransmitter release, enhances glucose metabolism</li>
<li><strong>Molecular Targets</strong>: Cell membranes, acetylcholine release, cortisol regulation</li>
<li><strong>Effect Type</strong>: Enhances memory, neuroprotective</li>
<li><strong>Clinical Trials</strong>:
<ul>
<li>PMID:25933483 - Memory improvement in elderly</li>
<li>PMID:28471731 - Cognitive benefits in age-related decline</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><strong>Evidence Quality</strong>: MODERATE-HIGH (8 RCTs, 1 meta-analysis, n&gt;600)</li>
<li><strong>Consumer Note</strong>: Particularly effective for age-related memory decline</li>
<li><strong>Dosing</strong>: 100-300mg daily</li>
<li><strong>Safety</strong>: Well-tolerated</li>
<li><strong>Contraindications</strong>: Blood thinners (may potentiate effects)</li>
<li><strong>Drug Interactions</strong>: Huperzine A (synergistic cholinergic effects)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Moderate Evidence (Level 3)</h3>
<h4>Curcumin</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Evidence Level</strong>: 3/5</li>
<li><strong>Mechanism</strong>: Anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, enhances BDNF, promotes neurogenesis</li>
<li><strong>Molecular Targets</strong>: NF-κB inhibition, BDNF upregulation, amyloid-β reduction</li>
<li><strong>Effect Type</strong>: Neuroprotective, anti-inflammatory</li>
<li><strong>Studies</strong>: PMID:28595948, PMID:29065496</li>
<li><strong>Consumer Note</strong>: Poor bioavailability; use with piperine or liposomal formulations</li>
<li><strong>Dosing</strong>: 500-1000mg with piperine (BioPerine) daily</li>
<li><strong>Safety</strong>: Generally safe, high doses may cause GI upset</li>
<li><strong>Contraindications</strong>: Gallbladder disease, bile duct obstruction</li>
<li><strong>Drug Interactions</strong>: Blood thinners, diabetes medications</li>
</ul>
<h4>Ginkgo Biloba</h4>
<ul>
<li><strong>Evidence Level</strong>: 3/5</li>
<li><strong>Mechanism</strong>: Improves cerebral blood flow, antioxidant, PAF antagonism</li>
<li><strong>Molecular Targets</strong>: Cerebrovascular function, free radical scavenging</li>
<li><strong>Effect Type</strong>: Enhances perfusion, neuroprotective</li>
<li><strong>Studies</strong>: PMID:25896806, PMID:20590480</li>
<li><strong>Consumer Note</strong>: May improve memory in age-related decline</li>
<li><strong>Dosing</strong>: 120-240mg standardized extract daily</li>
<li><strong>Safety</strong>: Generally safe</li>
<li><strong>Contraindications</strong>: Bleeding disorders, upcoming surgery</li>
<li><strong>Drug Interactions</strong>: Anticoagulants, antiplatelets</li>
</ul>
<h2>Recent Research Highlights</h2>
<h3>Memory Consolidation Mechanisms</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Replay during sleep</strong>: Sharp-wave ripples in CA1 during slow-wave sleep consolidate memories (PMID:31253972)</li>
<li><strong>Pattern separation</strong>: Dentate gyrus critical for distinguishing similar memories (PMID:30996115)</li>
<li><strong>Adult neurogenesis</strong>: Ongoing debate about extent in adult human hippocampus (PMID:30104579)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Therapeutic Targets</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Exercise-induced BDNF</strong>: Mediates neurogenesis and cognitive benefits (PMID:32165585)</li>
<li><strong>Inflammation</strong>: Microglial activation in aging and neurodegeneration (PMID:31806905)</li>
<li><strong>Epigenetic regulation</strong>: Histone modifications in memory formation (PMID:29973725)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Summary</h2>
<p>The hippocampus is critical for episodic memory, spatial navigation, and new learning. Multiple supplements show evidence for supporting hippocampal function, with omega-3 fatty acids, bacopa, and phosphatidylserine having the strongest research support. Lifestyle interventions including exercise, sleep, and stress management are foundational for hippocampal health.</p>
