# Prefrontal Cortex

## Overview (Consumer-Friendly)

Your prefrontal cortex is your brain's "CEO" - it's responsible for planning, decision-making, and controlling your impulses. Located at the very front of your brain (behind your forehead), it helps you focus on tasks, make good decisions, and achieve your goals.

### Key Functions
- Planning and organizing complex tasks
- Making decisions and solving problems
- Controlling impulses and delaying gratification
- Maintaining focus and working memory
- Regulating emotions and social behavior

### Lifestyle Tips for PFC Health
- **Get enough sleep** - PFC is especially vulnerable to sleep deprivation
- **Practice mindfulness** - Meditation strengthens PFC function
- **Challenge yourself** - Novel and complex tasks build PFC capacity
- **Limit multitasking** - Focused attention strengthens PFC
- **Manage stress** - Chronic stress impairs PFC function

## Clinical Information (Medical Professional)

### Neuroanatomy
The prefrontal cortex comprises the anterior third of the frontal lobe, divided into:
- **Dorsolateral PFC (DLPFC)**: Working memory, cognitive flexibility, planning
- **Ventromedial PFC (VMPFC)**: Emotion regulation, decision-making, social cognition
- **Orbitofrontal cortex (OFC)**: Reward processing, impulse control
- **Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)**: Conflict monitoring, error detection

Brodmann areas: 9, 10, 11, 12, 46, 47 primarily

### Cytoarchitecture
- **Layer III**: Pyramidal neurons with extensive dendritic arbors
- **Layer V**: Large pyramidal neurons projecting to subcortical structures
- **Inverted U-shaped dopamine modulation**: Optimal PFC function requires balanced DA signaling

### Common Pathologies
- **ADHD**: Reduced volume, hypometabolism, dopaminergic dysfunction
- **Schizophrenia**: Hypofrontality, working memory deficits
- **Depression**: Reduced activity, rumination, negative bias
- **Frontotemporal Dementia**: Progressive atrophy, behavior and personality changes
- **TBI**: Executive dysfunction, personality changes

### Clinical Assessment
- **Neuropsychological Testing**:
  - Wisconsin Card Sorting Test (WCST) - cognitive flexibility
  - Stroop Color-Word Test - inhibitory control
  - Trail Making Test Part B - set-shifting
  - N-back task - working memory
- **Functional MRI**: Activation during executive tasks
- **PET/SPECT**: Dopamine receptor imaging, glucose metabolism

### Differential Diagnosis
- ADHD vs. anxiety disorder vs. depression
- Frontotemporal dementia vs. Alzheimer's disease
- Traumatic brain injury vs. psychiatric disorder

## Supplements That Support PFC Function

### High Evidence (Level 4-5)

#### L-Tyrosine
- **Evidence Level**: 4/5
- **Mechanism**: Precursor to dopamine and norepinephrine, increases catecholamine synthesis under stress
- **Molecular Targets**: Tyrosine hydroxylase, catecholamine synthesis pathway
- **Effect Type**: Enhances working memory and executive function during stress/fatigue
- **Clinical Trials**:
  - PMID:25797188 - Cognitive flexibility under stress
  - PMID:10956379 - Working memory in demanding conditions
- **Evidence Quality**: MODERATE-HIGH (8 RCTs, consistent effects under stress, n>400)
- **Consumer Note**: Most effective during cognitively demanding or stressful tasks
- **Dosing**: 500-2000mg 30-60min before demanding tasks
- **Safety**: Generally safe
- **Contraindications**: MAOIs, hyperthyroidism, phenylketonuria
- **Drug Interactions**: Levodopa, thyroid hormones

#### Citicoline (CDP-Choline)
- **Evidence Level**: 4/5
- **Mechanism**: Acetylcholine precursor, enhances dopamine receptor function, neuroprotective
- **Molecular Targets**: Cholinergic transmission, phospholipid synthesis, DA receptor density
- **Effect Type**: Enhances attention, working memory, executive function
- **Clinical Trials**:
  - PMID:26179181 - Cognitive enhancement in healthy adults
  - PMID:25933483 - Executive function improvement
- **Evidence Quality**: MODERATE-HIGH (12 RCTs, 2 meta-analyses, n>800)
- **Consumer Note**: Supports mental energy and cognitive performance
- **Dosing**: 250-500mg daily
- **Safety**: Very safe, well-tolerated
- **Contraindications**: None significant
- **Drug Interactions**: Minimal, may enhance cholinergic drugs

#### Rhodiola Rosea
- **Evidence Level**: 4/5
- **Mechanism**: Adaptogen, modulates stress response, enhances catecholamine function
- **Molecular Targets**: HPA axis modulation, monoamine oxidase inhibition, cortisol regulation
- **Effect Type**: Reduces mental fatigue, enhances focus under stress
- **Clinical Trials**:
  - PMID:26502953 - Anti-fatigue effects
  - PMID:19016404 - Cognitive performance under stress
- **Evidence Quality**: MODERATE (11 RCTs, variable quality, n>500)
- **Consumer Note**: Reduces mental fatigue and improves focus during demanding periods
- **Dosing**: 200-600mg standardized extract daily
- **Safety**: Generally safe
- **Contraindications**: Bipolar disorder (may trigger mania)
- **Drug Interactions**: MAOIs, stimulants (additive effects)

#### Caffeine + L-Theanine
- **Evidence Level**: 5/5
- **Mechanism**: Caffeine blocks adenosine (arousal), theanine increases GABA/dopamine (calm focus)
- **Molecular Targets**: Adenosine receptors, GABA, alpha brain waves
- **Effect Type**: Enhanced attention, reduced jitteriness, smooth focus
- **Clinical Trials**:
  - PMID:18681988 - Improved attention and task switching
  - PMID:21040626 - Synergistic cognitive effects
- **Evidence Quality**: HIGH (15+ RCTs, consistent effects, n>1000)
- **Consumer Note**: Synergistic combination for smooth, focused energy without jitters
- **Dosing**: 100mg caffeine + 200mg L-theanine (2:1 theanine:caffeine ratio)
- **Safety**: Very safe for most people
- **Contraindications**: Anxiety disorders (caffeine), pregnancy (high doses)
- **Drug Interactions**: Stimulants, blood thinners (theanine may potentiate)

### Moderate Evidence (Level 3)

#### Panax Ginseng
- **Evidence Level**: 4/5
- **Mechanism**: Modulates neurotransmitters, neuroprotective, anti-inflammatory
- **Molecular Targets**: Multiple pathways including GABA, dopamine, serotonin
- **Effect Type**: Reduces mental fatigue, enhances working memory
- **Studies**: PMID:29363121, PMID:30036016
- **Consumer Note**: Improves mental performance and reduces fatigue
- **Dosing**: 200-400mg standardized extract daily
- **Safety**: Generally safe
- **Contraindications**: Diabetes (may lower blood sugar), bleeding disorders
- **Drug Interactions**: Warfarin, insulin, MAOIs

#### Alpha-GPC
- **Evidence Level**: 3/5
- **Mechanism**: Highly bioavailable choline source for acetylcholine synthesis
- **Molecular Targets**: Cholinergic transmission, growth hormone release
- **Effect Type**: Rapid cognitive enhancement, working memory support
- **Studies**: PMID:12637119, PMID:21156029
- **Consumer Note**: Fast-acting cognitive boost, especially for working memory tasks
- **Dosing**: 300-600mg daily
- **Safety**: Very safe
- **Contraindications**: None significant
- **Drug Interactions**: Anticholinergic drugs (antagonistic)

## Recent Research Highlights

### Working Memory Mechanisms
- **Persistent activity**: Sustained firing of PFC neurons during delay periods maintains information (PMID:30068546)
- **Dopamine inverted-U**: Optimal D1 receptor stimulation critical for working memory (PMID:29503436)
- **Theta-gamma coupling**: Cross-frequency coupling supports working memory maintenance (PMID:30089978)

### Therapeutic Targets
- **D1 agonists**: Potential for ADHD and cognitive enhancement (PMID:31806541)
- **α2A agonists**: Guanfacine for ADHD, executive dysfunction (PMID:28912095)
- **Transcranial magnetic stimulation**: DLPFC stimulation for depression (PMID:31003908)

## Summary

The prefrontal cortex is the brain's executive control center, critical for planning, decision-making, working memory, and impulse control. Supplements with strong evidence include L-tyrosine (especially under stress), citicoline, and the caffeine-theanine combination. Lifestyle factors including adequate sleep, stress management, and cognitive challenge are foundational for optimal PFC function.
