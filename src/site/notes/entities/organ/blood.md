<h1>Blood</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Blood is a specialized fluid connective tissue that circulates through the cardiovascular system, serving as the body's primary transport medium. The average adult contains approximately 5 liters of blood, comprising roughly 7-8% of total body weight. Blood consists of a liquid matrix (plasma, ~55% of volume) and formed elements (red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets, ~45% of volume), with the ratio of packed cells to total volume expressed as the hematocrit.</p>
<p>Plasma is a complex aqueous solution containing over 700 proteins, including albumin (osmotic pressure regulation, transport), immunoglobulins (immune defense), clotting factors (hemostasis), and lipoproteins (lipid transport). Red blood cells (erythrocytes) are the most abundant cells in the body, numbering approximately 25 trillion, each packed with 270 million hemoglobin molecules that collectively transport 1 billion oxygen molecules per cell. Erythrocytes are uniquely anucleate and biconcave, maximizing surface area for gas exchange and deformability for passage through capillaries as narrow as 3 micrometers.</p>
<p>Blood maintains homeostasis through multiple mechanisms: acid-base buffering (bicarbonate, phosphate, protein buffer systems maintaining pH 7.35-7.45), thermoregulation (distributing heat from core to periphery), immune surveillance (circulating leukocytes and antibodies), and hemostasis (platelet adhesion, coagulation cascade, fibrinolysis). Blood cells are produced in the bone marrow through hematopoiesis, with red blood cells having a lifespan of approximately 120 days before being removed by the spleen and liver.</p>
<h2>Key Functions</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Oxygen transport</strong>: Hemoglobin in erythrocytes binds oxygen in the lungs and releases it to tissues based on partial pressure, pH, temperature, and 2,3-DPG levels</li>
<li><strong>Carbon dioxide removal</strong>: Carries CO2 as bicarbonate (70%), carbaminohemoglobin (23%), and dissolved gas (7%) to the lungs for exhalation</li>
<li><strong>Nutrient delivery</strong>: Transports absorbed glucose, amino acids, lipids, vitamins, and minerals from the gut and liver to all tissues</li>
<li><strong>Hormone transport</strong>: Carries endocrine signals (insulin, thyroid hormones, cortisol, sex hormones) to target tissues</li>
<li><strong>Immune defense</strong>: White blood cells (neutrophils, lymphocytes, monocytes, eosinophils, basophils) patrol for pathogens; antibodies and complement circulate in plasma</li>
<li><strong>Hemostasis</strong>: Platelets and clotting factors seal vascular injuries through primary hemostasis (platelet plug) and secondary hemostasis (fibrin mesh)</li>
<li><strong>Waste removal</strong>: Transports urea, creatinine, bilirubin, and other metabolic wastes to kidneys and liver for excretion</li>
<li><strong>pH buffering</strong>: Bicarbonate-carbonic acid system maintains arterial pH between 7.35-7.45</li>
</ul>
<h2>Clinical Significance</h2>
<p>Anemia affects approximately 1.8 billion people globally, with iron deficiency anemia being the most common nutritional deficiency worldwide. Vitamin B12 and folate deficiencies cause megaloblastic anemia with abnormally large, dysfunctional erythrocytes. Sickle cell disease results from a single hemoglobin mutation causing red blood cell rigidity and vascular occlusion. Leukemia involves malignant proliferation of white blood cell precursors. Thrombocytopenia (low platelets) increases bleeding risk, while thrombocytosis increases clotting risk. Hemophilia involves inherited deficiencies in specific clotting factors. Complete blood count (CBC) is the most commonly ordered laboratory test, providing critical diagnostic information about hematologic, infectious, and metabolic conditions. Key nutrients for blood health include iron (hemoglobin synthesis), vitamin B12 and folate (erythrocyte DNA synthesis and maturation), vitamin B6 (heme synthesis), vitamin K (clotting factor activation), and erythropoietin (kidney-derived hormone stimulating red blood cell production).</p>
