<h2>Abstract</h2>
<p>This comprehensive mechanistic review elucidates how resveratrol activates SIRT1 through allosteric modulation, triggering downstream effects on PGC-1alpha, mitochondrial biogenesis, and metabolic homeostasis. The paper provides critical evidence for resveratrol's role in mimicking caloric restriction benefits through the SIRT1/PGC-1alpha axis.</p>

<p>resveratrol, SIRT1, PGC-1alpha, mitochondrial biogenesis, caloric restriction, NAD+, sirtuins, aging</p>

<h2>Results</h2>
<h3>Key Findings</h3>
<pre><code class="language-yaml">primary:
  - name: SIRT1 activation mechanism
    result: Resveratrol binds SIRT1 N-terminus, requires specific substrate sequence
    finding: Activation is substrate-specific, not universal
  - name: PGC-1alpha deacetylation
    result: Resveratrol increases PGC-1alpha deacetylation via SIRT1
    p_value: &lt;0.001
secondary:
  - name: Mitochondrial biogenesis markers
    result: Increased expression of NRF1, TFAM, cytochrome c
  - name: Oxygen consumption
    result: Enhanced mitochondrial respiration
  - name: Metabolic improvements
    result: Improved glucose tolerance in obese mice
</code></pre>
<h3>Statistical Analysis</h3>

<h2>Limitations</h2>
<ul>
<li>Mouse-to-human translation uncertain</li>
<li>Bioavailability issues with oral resveratrol</li>
<li>Substrate-specific activation complicates interpretation</li>
<li>Some findings not replicated in human trials</li>
<li>Dose-response relationships differ between species</li>
