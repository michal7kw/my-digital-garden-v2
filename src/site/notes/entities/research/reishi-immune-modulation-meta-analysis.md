<h1>Reishi Immune Modulation Meta-Analysis</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Systematic review and meta-analysis examining the immunomodulatory effects of Ganoderma lucidum (Reishi) supplementation across multiple randomized controlled trials. The analysis provides Level 1a evidence for immune-enhancing effects.</p>
<h2>Key Findings</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>NK Cell Activity</strong>: Significant increase (p&lt;0.01) in NK cell cytotoxicity across 12 RCTs</li>
<li><strong>Cytokine Production</strong>: Enhanced IL-2, IL-6, and TNF-alpha production from immune cells</li>
<li><strong>Macrophage Function</strong>: Increased phagocytic activity by 30-50% in supplemented groups</li>
<li><strong>T-cell Proliferation</strong>: Enhanced CD4+ and CD8+ T-cell proliferation in response to antigens</li>
<li><strong>Safety</strong>: No serious adverse events; mild GI upset in &lt;5% of participants</li>
</ol>
<h2>Clinical Implications</h2>
<ul>
<li>Reishi is a broad-spectrum immunomodulator with Level 1a evidence</li>
<li>May be beneficial as adjunct therapy in immunocompromised states</li>
<li>Sleep-promoting and stress-reducing effects may synergize with immune benefits</li>
<li>Dual extraction (water + alcohol) needed to capture both beta-glucans and triterpenes</li>
</ul>
<h2>Limitations</h2>
<ul>
<li>Heterogeneity in extract preparation across studies</li>
<li>Most studies conducted in Asian populations</li>
<li>Limited long-term safety data beyond 24 weeks</li>
</ul>
