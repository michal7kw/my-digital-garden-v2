<h1>Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin (MCH)</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>meen kor-pus-kyoo-lur hee-muh-gloh-bin (m-c-h) Tested twice a year Mean corpuscular hemoglobin (MCH) measures the average amount of hemoglobin, the oxygen-carrying protein, in each red blood cell. Low MCH often points to iron deficiency anemia, while high MCH can signal a vitamin B12 or folate deficiency.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5)</p>
