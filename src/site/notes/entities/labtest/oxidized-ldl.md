<h1>Oxidized LDL (ox-LDL)</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The oxidized LDL (ox-LDL) test measures LDL cholesterol particles that have undergone oxidative modification. While native LDL is taken up by cells through regulated LDL receptors, oxidized LDL is recognized by scavenger receptors on macrophages in an unregulated manner, leading to cholesterol accumulation and foam cell formation - the hallmark of early atherosclerosis.</p>
<p>Ox-LDL measurement provides a more specific assessment of atherogenic risk compared to standard LDL cholesterol. Two individuals with identical LDL levels may have very different cardiovascular risk profiles depending on the proportion of their LDL that is oxidized. Ox-LDL also directly promotes endothelial dysfunction, platelet activation, and smooth muscle cell proliferation in vessel walls.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> ⭐⭐⭐ (3/5)</p>
