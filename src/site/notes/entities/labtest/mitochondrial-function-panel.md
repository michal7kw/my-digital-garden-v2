<h1>Mitochondrial Function Panel</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Mitochondrial Function Panel is a specialty laboratory assessment that evaluates multiple biomarkers of mitochondrial energy production. By measuring intermediates and cofactors across the glycolysis, TCA cycle, beta-oxidation, and electron transport chain pathways, this panel can identify specific bottlenecks or dysfunction patterns in cellular energy metabolism.</p>
<p>This panel is particularly useful for evaluating patients with unexplained fatigue, exercise intolerance, muscle weakness, and suspected mitochondrial disorders. It goes beyond standard metabolic panels by specifically targeting mitochondrial function markers.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 4/5</p>
<h2>Typical Components</h2>
<h3>Electron Transport Chain Markers</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>CoQ10 (Serum)</strong>: Electron carrier between Complex I/II and Complex III</li>
<li><strong>Lactate/Pyruvate ratio</strong>: Reflects cytoplasmic NAD+/NADH ratio (elevated = ETC dysfunction)</li>
<li><strong>Lactate</strong>: Elevated when aerobic metabolism is impaired</li>
</ul>
<h3>TCA Cycle Intermediates (via Organic Acids)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Citrate</strong>: First intermediate of TCA cycle</li>
<li><strong>Alpha-ketoglutarate</strong>: TCA cycle intermediate (B1, B2 dependent)</li>
<li><strong>Succinate</strong>: Complex II substrate</li>
<li><strong>Fumarate, Malate</strong>: Downstream TCA intermediates</li>
</ul>
<h3>Fatty Acid Oxidation</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Carnitine (free and total)</strong>: Required for fatty acid transport into mitochondria</li>
<li><strong>Acylcarnitine profile</strong>: Reflects beta-oxidation intermediate accumulation</li>
</ul>
<h3>Antioxidant Defense</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Glutathione (reduced)</strong>: Mitochondrial antioxidant status</li>
<li><strong>8-OHdG</strong>: Oxidative DNA damage marker</li>
</ul>
