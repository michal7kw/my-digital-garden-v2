# Squamous Epithelial Cells

## Overview
Tested twice a year Squamous epithelial cells in urine usually come from the lower urinary tract or genital area and are often a sign of sample contamination rather than disease. However, when present in higher numbers alongside other abnormalities, they can point to chronic inflammation, infection, or other conditions of the urinary tract.
