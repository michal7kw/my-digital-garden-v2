<h1>Serotonin Reuptake</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Serotonin reuptake is the primary mechanism for terminating serotonergic signaling at CNS synapses. The serotonin transporter (SERT), encoded by the SLC6A4 gene, actively transports serotonin (5-HT) from the synaptic cleft back into the presynaptic neuron using the electrochemical gradient of sodium and chloride ions.</p>
<p><strong>Transport stoichiometry</strong>: 1 serotonin + 1 Na+ + 1 Cl- (co-transported inward) : 1 K+ (counter-transported outward)</p>
<p>Once inside the presynaptic terminal, reuptaken serotonin is either:</p>
<ol>
<li>Repackaged into synaptic vesicles by VMAT2 (vesicular monoamine transporter 2) for re-release</li>
<li>Degraded by MAO-A (monoamine oxidase A) to 5-HIAA (5-hydroxyindoleacetic acid)</li>
</ol>
<h2>Process Steps</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Serotonin release</strong>: Action potential → vesicle fusion → serotonin released into synaptic cleft</li>
<li><strong>Postsynaptic signaling</strong>: Serotonin binds 5-HT receptors (5-HT1A, 5-HT2A, 5-HT2C, 5-HT3, etc.)</li>
<li><strong>Reuptake</strong>: SERT on presynaptic membrane binds serotonin + Na+/Cl- → conformational change → serotonin transported intracellularly</li>
<li><strong>Vesicular repackaging or degradation</strong>: VMAT2 or MAO-A</li>
<li><strong>Signal termination</strong>: Reduced synaptic serotonin → receptor deactivation</li>
</ol>
<h2>SSRI Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>All SSRIs work by competitively binding to SERT and blocking step 3:</p>
<ul>
<li>Serotonin remains in the synaptic cleft longer</li>
<li>Extended postsynaptic receptor activation</li>
<li>Gradual downstream adaptations (autoreceptor desensitization, BDNF induction, neurogenesis)</li>
<li>Therapeutic onset: 2-4 weeks (except PMDD: 1-2 days via different mechanism)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Regulation</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>SLC6A4 expression</strong>: 5-HTTLPR polymorphism affects SERT density and reuptake efficiency</li>
<li><strong>Protein kinase C (PKC)</strong>: Phosphorylates SERT → reduces surface expression → decreased reuptake</li>
<li><strong>p38 MAPK</strong>: Enhances SERT activity</li>
<li><strong>Interleukin-1β</strong>: Cytokine that increases SERT activity → may explain depression-inflammation link</li>
</ul>
