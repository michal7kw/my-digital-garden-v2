<h1>Breathwork</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Deliberate control of breathing patterns to influence autonomic nervous system function, stress response, and cognitive state. Encompasses slow breathing (4-6 breaths/min for parasympathetic activation), cyclic hyperventilation (Wim Hof, Tummo), box breathing, and physiological sighs. Evidence strongest for stress reduction and autonomic regulation.</p>
<p><strong>Type</strong>: mind_body</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Slow breathing activates vagal tone and parasympathetic nervous system via baroreceptor stimulation. Cyclic hyperventilation (Wim Hof) induces transient respiratory alkalosis, catecholamine release, and temporary immune suppression (reduced inflammatory cytokines). Physiological sighs re-inflate alveoli and rapidly reduce autonomic arousal.</p>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<h3>Dosing Information</h3>
<p>5-20 minutes daily. Slow breathing (4-6 breaths/min) for calm. Cyclic hyperventilation (30 breaths + hold) for arousal. Physiological sigh (double inhale + long exhale) for acute stress.</p>
<h2>Safety Information</h2>
<h3>Contraindications</h3>
<ul>
<li>Severe cardiovascular disease</li>
<li>Uncontrolled hypertension</li>
<li>History of seizures (hyperventilation techniques)</li>
<li>Pregnancy (vigorous techniques)</li>
<li>Severe anxiety/panic disorder (aggressive breathing may trigger episodes)</li>
<li>Pneumothorax history</li>
</ul>
