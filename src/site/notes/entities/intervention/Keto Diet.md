# Keto Diet

## Overview
The ketogenic diet is a popular eating pattern known for its potential to promote weight loss, improve blood sugar control, and enhance cognitive function. By significantly restricting carbohydrates, the body enters a state of ketosis, where it burns fat for fuel, producing ketones.

## Properties
```yaml
intervention_type: Dietary Intervention
mechanism_of_action: Induces ketosis, shifting the body's primary fuel source from carbohydrates to fats.
dosing_protocol: Typically involves consuming <50g net carbs per day, moderate protein, and high fat.
contraindications:
  - Type 1 diabetes
  - Pancreatic disease
  - Liver conditions
  - Fat-malabsorption disorders
side_effects:
  - Keto flu (fatigue, headache, nausea)
  - Constipation
  - Nutrient deficiencies
interactions: May interact with medications for diabetes or blood pressure.
duration: Can be short-term or long-term, depending on health goals and individual tolerance.
frequency: Daily adherence to dietary macronutrient ratios.
timing: Consistent adherence throughout the day.
```
