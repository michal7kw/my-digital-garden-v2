<h1>Meditation</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Meditation encompasses a family of contemplative practices that train attention, awareness, and emotional regulation. Mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR), the most extensively studied form, has demonstrated significant effects on stress biomarkers, immune function, and brain structure in rigorous clinical trials.</p>
<p>From a bioenergetics perspective, meditation's primary relevance is in the Stress Resilience domain — chronic psychological stress elevates cortisol, activates the HPA axis, increases allostatic load, and promotes mitochondrial dysfunction through oxidative stress and inflammation. Regular meditation practice counteracts these effects by reducing cortisol, lowering inflammatory markers (hs-CRP, IL-6), and improving autonomic nervous system balance.</p>
<p><strong>Evidence Level:</strong> 4/5</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Neuroendocrine Effects</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reduces cortisol secretion by 15-25% in chronic practitioners</li>
<li>Modulates HPA axis reactivity to acute stressors</li>
<li>Increases DHEA-S (protective counter-regulatory hormone)</li>
<li>Improves cortisol:DHEA-S ratio</li>
</ul>
<h3>Anti-Inflammatory Effects</h3>
<ul>
<li>Reduces hs-CRP, IL-6, and TNF-alpha</li>
<li>Modulates NF-kB gene expression in immune cells</li>
<li>Effects comparable to moderate exercise in some studies</li>
</ul>
<h3>Autonomic Balance</h3>
<ul>
<li>Increases parasympathetic (vagal) tone</li>
<li>Improves heart rate variability (HRV)</li>
<li>Reduces sympathetic nervous system activation</li>
</ul>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<h3>Standard Practice</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Dose</strong>: 20 minutes/day minimum; 30-45 min optimal</li>
<li><strong>Form</strong>: Mindfulness meditation (MBSR), loving-kindness, body scan</li>
<li><strong>Duration</strong>: Benefits measurable after 8 weeks of daily practice</li>
<li><strong>Evidence-based programs</strong>: MBSR (8-week structured program)</li>
</ul>
