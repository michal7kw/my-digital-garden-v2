<h1>Caloric Restriction</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>A dietary intervention involving sustained reduction of caloric intake (typically 20-40% below ad libitum) without malnutrition. The most robust and reproducible intervention for extending lifespan and healthspan across multiple species, from yeast to primates.</p>
<p><strong>Type</strong>: dietary_pattern</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Caloric restriction activates multiple longevity pathways including AMPK activation, sirtuin upregulation, mTOR inhibition, enhanced autophagy, improved insulin sensitivity, reduced oxidative stress, and metabolic reprogramming toward efficient energy utilization.</p>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<h3>Administration</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Continuous daily caloric deficit</li>
<li><strong>Frequency</strong>: Daily</li>
<li><strong>Duration</strong>: Long-term (months to years for maximum benefit)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Safety Information</h2>
<h3>Contraindications</h3>
<ul>
<li>Pregnancy and breastfeeding</li>
<li>Children and adolescents</li>
<li>Underweight individuals (BMI &lt;18.5)</li>
<li>History of eating disorders</li>
<li>Active cancer treatment (unless supervised)</li>
<li>Severe chronic illness</li>
<li>Elderly frail individuals</li>
</ul>
<h3>Side Effects</h3>
<ul>
<li>Initial hunger and food preoccupation</li>
<li>Reduced body temperature</li>
<li>Decreased libido</li>
<li>Menstrual irregularities</li>
<li>Mood changes</li>
<li>Reduced physical performance initially</li>
<li>Potential bone density loss</li>
</ul>
