<h1>Caloric Restriction</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Caloric Restriction (CR) is the reduction of caloric intake below ad libitum (free-feeding) levels while maintaining adequate nutrition. It is the most well-studied and consistently effective intervention for extending lifespan across species, from yeast and worms to rodents and primates.</p>
<p>Typical CR protocols involve reducing caloric intake by 20-40% compared to normal consumption while ensuring adequate intake of protein, vitamins, minerals, and essential fatty acids. The key is restriction of total energy, not specific macronutrients.</p>
<p>The landmark CALERIE trial (Comprehensive Assessment of Long-term Effects of Reducing Intake of Energy) demonstrated that even modest CR (11.9% actual reduction) in healthy non-obese humans produced metabolic benefits including reduced markers of cardiovascular disease risk, improved insulin sensitivity, and decreased oxidative stress.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>CR triggers a coordinated metabolic response that shifts the body from growth/reproduction to maintenance/repair:</p>
<h3>Nutrient Sensing Pathways</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>AMPK Activation</strong>: Low energy status activates AMP-activated protein kinase</li>
<li><strong>Sirtuin Upregulation</strong>: Increased NAD+/NADH ratio activates SIRT1, SIRT3</li>
<li><strong>mTOR Inhibition</strong>: Reduced amino acids and growth factors suppress mTOR</li>
<li><strong>Insulin/IGF-1 Reduction</strong>: Lower circulating insulin and IGF-1 levels</li>
</ol>
<h3>Cellular Effects</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Enhanced Autophagy</strong>: Increased cellular cleanup and recycling</li>
<li><strong>Improved Mitochondrial Function</strong>: Better oxidative capacity, reduced ROS</li>
<li><strong>Reduced Inflammation</strong>: Decreased NF-kB activity and inflammatory cytokines</li>
<li><strong>DNA Repair Enhancement</strong>: Increased repair enzyme activity</li>
<li><strong>Stem Cell Maintenance</strong>: Preserved regenerative capacity</li>
</ul>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<h3>Standard CR Protocol</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Reduction Level</strong>: 20-30% below calculated energy needs</li>
<li><strong>Nutrient Adequacy</strong>: Meet all micronutrient RDAs</li>
<li><strong>Protein Intake</strong>: Maintain at 0.8-1.0 g/kg body weight</li>
<li><strong>Implementation</strong>: Gradual reduction over weeks to months</li>
</ul>
<h3>Modified Approaches</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Every Other Day Fasting</strong>: Alternate between CR and normal eating</li>
<li><strong>5:2 Diet</strong>: 5 normal days, 2 days of severe restriction (~500 kcal)</li>
<li><strong>Time-Restricted Eating</strong>: Limit eating window while naturally reducing intake</li>
</ul>

<h3>Human Studies</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>CALERIE Trial</strong> (PMID: 26278052): 2 years of CR improved cardiometabolic markers</li>
<li><strong>Biosphere 2</strong> (PMID: 12079842): Unintentional CR showed metabolic improvements</li>
<li><strong>Okinawan Centenarians</strong>: Historically practiced moderate lifelong CR</li>
</ul>
<h2>Safety Information</h2>
<h3>Monitoring Requirements</h3>
<ul>
<li>Regular body composition assessment</li>
<li>Bone density monitoring (DEXA)</li>
<li>Complete metabolic panel</li>
<li>Hormone levels (thyroid, sex hormones)</li>
<li>Nutritional status assessment</li>
</ul>
<h3>Risk Mitigation</h3>
<ul>
<li>Ensure adequate protein intake</li>
<li>Supplement micronutrients as needed</li>
<li>Maintain resistance exercise</li>
<li>Monitor psychological wellbeing</li>
</ul>
