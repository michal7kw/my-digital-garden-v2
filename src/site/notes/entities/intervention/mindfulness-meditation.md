<h1>Mindfulness Meditation</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Mindfulness meditation is a secular attention-training practice with roots in the Buddhist Vipassana tradition, popularized in Western medicine through Jon Kabat-Zinn's Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) program. The core instruction is deceptively simple: pay attention to present-moment experience (breath, body sensations, thoughts) with an attitude of non-judgmental acceptance. Neuroimaging research has revealed that regular practice produces measurable structural and functional brain changes, including increased cortical thickness in the prefrontal cortex and insula, reduced amygdala reactivity, and enhanced connectivity between attention networks.</p>
<p><strong>Type</strong>: lifestyle</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Strengthens prefrontal cortical control over amygdala reactivity, enhances interoceptive awareness via insula activation, and increases default mode network regulation (reduced mind-wandering). Epigenetic effects include reduced NF-κB expression and preserved telomere length.</p>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<h3>Evidence-Based Programs</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>MBSR</strong> (Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction) — 8 weeks, 2.5 hr/week + home practice</li>
<li><strong>MBCT</strong> (Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy) — 8 weeks, prevents depressive relapse</li>
<li><strong>Minimum effective dose</strong> — 10-15 min/day shows measurable benefit in 8 weeks</li>
</ul>
<h3>Administration</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Frequency</strong>: Daily</li>
<li><strong>Duration</strong>: 10-45 minutes per session</li>
</ul>
<h2>Documented Benefits</h2>
<ul>
<li>Reduced cortisol and inflammatory markers (IL-6, CRP, NF-κB)</li>
<li>Preserved telomere length (Elizabeth Blackburn's research)</li>
<li>Reduced anxiety and depression symptoms (moderate effect sizes)</li>
<li>Improved attention and working memory</li>
<li>Enhanced emotional regulation</li>
<li>Reduced chronic pain perception</li>
</ul>
