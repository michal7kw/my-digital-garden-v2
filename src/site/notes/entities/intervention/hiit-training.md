<h1>HIIT Training</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>High-Intensity Interval Training - exercise protocol alternating short bursts of intense anaerobic activity with recovery periods. Highly time-efficient method for improving cardiovascular fitness, insulin sensitivity, mitochondrial function, and metabolic health in as little as 10-20 minutes.</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Maximizes cardiovascular and metabolic adaptations through repeated high-intensity efforts. Depletes glycogen rapidly, increases GLUT4 translocation for enhanced glucose uptake, stimulates mitochondrial biogenesis via PGC-1α, elevates EPOC (excess post-exercise oxygen consumption) for prolonged calorie burn, and activates AMPK pathway similar to fasting.</p>

<h2>Safety Information</h2>
<h3>Contraindications</h3>
<ul>
<li>Uncontrolled hypertension (&gt;180/110 mmHg)</li>
<li>Recent cardiac event (&lt;6 months)</li>
<li>Severe aortic stenosis</li>
<li>Uncontrolled arrhythmias</li>
<li>Acute illness or infection</li>
<li>Decompensated heart failure</li>
<li>Severe COPD exacerbation</li>
<li>Orthopedic limitations preventing high-intensity movement</li>
</ul>
<h3>Side Effects</h3>
<ul>
<li>Extreme fatigue during/after session (expected)</li>
<li>Nausea if intensity too high or inadequate warm-up</li>
<li>Muscle soreness (DOMS) 24-48 hours post-workout</li>
<li>Increased injury risk without proper form</li>
<li>Overtraining if inadequate recovery</li>
<li>Potential for rhabdomyolysis (rare, extreme overexertion)</li>
</ul>
