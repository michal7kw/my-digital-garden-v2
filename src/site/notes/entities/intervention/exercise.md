<h1>Exercise</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Exercise is structured physical activity performed with the goal of improving or maintaining health and fitness. It is one of the most powerful and well-documented interventions for preventing disease, improving quality of life, and extending healthspan.</p>
<p>The benefits of regular exercise span virtually every organ system: cardiovascular improvements, metabolic optimization, musculoskeletal strengthening, cognitive enhancement, and psychological wellbeing. Exercise is often called &quot;the best medicine&quot; due to its broad therapeutic effects.</p>
<p>Current guidelines recommend at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous activity per week, combined with muscle-strengthening activities at least 2 days per week.</p>
<h2>Types of Exercise</h2>
<h3>Aerobic (Cardiovascular)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Zone 2 Training</strong>: Low-intensity, sustained (60-70% max HR) - optimal for fat oxidation</li>
<li><strong>HIIT</strong>: High-intensity interval training - time-efficient, metabolic benefits</li>
<li><strong>Running, Cycling, Swimming</strong>: Traditional cardio activities</li>
</ul>
<h3>Resistance (Strength)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Weight Training</strong>: Progressive overload for muscle hypertrophy</li>
<li><strong>Bodyweight Exercises</strong>: Accessible strength training</li>
<li><strong>Resistance Bands</strong>: Portable, joint-friendly options</li>
</ul>
<h3>Flexibility &amp; Mobility</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Stretching</strong>: Static and dynamic flexibility work</li>
<li><strong>Yoga</strong>: Combined flexibility, strength, and mindfulness</li>
<li><strong>Mobility Drills</strong>: Joint range of motion maintenance</li>
</ul>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<h3>Metabolic Effects</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>AMPK Activation</strong>: Energy depletion activates cellular energy sensor</li>
<li><strong>PGC-1α Upregulation</strong>: Master regulator of mitochondrial biogenesis</li>
<li><strong>Improved Insulin Sensitivity</strong>: Enhanced glucose uptake in muscle</li>
<li><strong>NAD+ Elevation</strong>: Exercise increases NAD+/NADH ratio</li>
</ol>
<h3>Systemic Effects</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular</strong>: Improved cardiac output, reduced resting heart rate, better endothelial function</li>
<li><strong>Musculoskeletal</strong>: Increased muscle mass, bone density, tendon strength</li>
<li><strong>Neurological</strong>: BDNF release, neurogenesis, improved cognition</li>
<li><strong>Immune</strong>: Moderate exercise enhances immune function</li>
<li><strong>Endocrine</strong>: Improved hormone regulation, reduced chronic inflammation</li>
</ul>

<h3>Disease Prevention</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular Disease</strong>: 35% risk reduction</li>
<li><strong>Type 2 Diabetes</strong>: 40-60% risk reduction</li>
<li><strong>Depression</strong>: Comparable to antidepressants</li>
<li><strong>Dementia</strong>: 30% risk reduction</li>
<li><strong>Cancer (certain types)</strong>: 10-30% risk reduction</li>
</ul>
<h2>Protocol Recommendations</h2>
<h3>Minimum Effective Dose</h3>
<ul>
<li>150 min/week moderate aerobic OR 75 min/week vigorous</li>
<li>2+ days/week resistance training</li>
<li>Break up prolonged sitting</li>
</ul>
<h3>Optimal Health Protocol</h3>
<ul>
<li>Zone 2 cardio: 180-210 min/week (3-4 sessions)</li>
<li>HIIT/VO2max work: 1 session/week</li>
<li>Resistance training: 2-3 sessions/week</li>
<li>Daily mobility/stretching</li>
</ul>
<h2>Safety Information</h2>
<h3>Pre-Exercise Screening</h3>
<ul>
<li>PAR-Q (Physical Activity Readiness Questionnaire)</li>
<li>Medical clearance for high-risk individuals</li>
<li>Gradual progression for sedentary individuals</li>
</ul>
<h3>Injury Prevention</h3>
<ul>
<li>Proper warm-up and cool-down</li>
<li>Progressive overload principle</li>
<li>Adequate recovery between sessions</li>
<li>Attention to proper form</li>
</ul>
