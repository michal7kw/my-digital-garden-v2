<h1>Exercise</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Physical activity performed to improve or maintain physical fitness, health, and overall wellness. Encompasses aerobic (cardio), anaerobic (resistance), flexibility, and balance training. One of the most effective interventions for preventing chronic disease and extending healthspan.</p>
<p><strong>Type</strong>: physical_activity</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Exercise induces acute metabolic stress that triggers adaptive responses including mitochondrial biogenesis, improved insulin sensitivity, enhanced cardiovascular function, increased BDNF for neuroprotection, activation of AMPK and PGC-1α, and promotion of autophagy and cellular quality control.</p>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<h3>Administration</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Timing</strong>: Varies by exercise type (morning, afternoon, or evening)</li>
<li><strong>Frequency</strong>: 150+ minutes moderate or 75+ minutes vigorous per week</li>
<li><strong>Duration</strong>: Ongoing, lifelong practice recommended</li>
</ul>
<h2>Safety Information</h2>
<h3>Contraindications</h3>
<ul>
<li>Unstable angina</li>
<li>Uncontrolled hypertension (&gt;180/110)</li>
<li>Acute myocardial infarction (recent)</li>
<li>Severe aortic stenosis</li>
<li>Uncontrolled arrhythmias</li>
<li>Acute infection or illness</li>
<li>Recent surgery (depends on type)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Side Effects</h3>
<ul>
<li>Muscle soreness (DOMS)</li>
<li>Injury risk if improper form</li>
<li>Fatigue during adaptation</li>
<li>Overtraining syndrome (if excessive)</li>
<li>Joint stress with high-impact activities</li>
</ul>
