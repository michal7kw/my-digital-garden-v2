<h1>Mediterranean Diet</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>The Mediterranean diet is a dietary pattern modeled on the traditional eating habits of countries bordering the Mediterranean Sea (Greece, southern Italy, Spain). It emphasizes high consumption of extra-virgin olive oil (primary fat source), vegetables, fruits, legumes, nuts, whole grains, and fish; moderate consumption of poultry, eggs, and dairy (primarily yogurt and cheese); limited red meat; and optional moderate red wine with meals. It is the most extensively studied dietary pattern in nutrition science, with the landmark PREDIMED trial (7,447 participants) establishing its efficacy.</p>
<p>The Mediterranean diet has the strongest evidence base of any dietary pattern for cardiovascular disease prevention (30% relative risk reduction for major cardiovascular events), with robust data also supporting benefits for type 2 diabetes prevention, cognitive decline, depression, and all-cause mortality. The MIND diet (Mediterranean-DASH Intervention for Neurodegenerative Delay) is a hybrid specifically designed for brain health. Key bioactive components include polyphenols (olive oil, wine), omega-3 fatty acids (fish), fiber (legumes, whole grains), and antioxidant vitamins. The diet is sustainable long-term with high adherence rates compared to more restrictive diets.</p>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<p>The Mediterranean diet exerts pleiotropic effects through multiple bioactive compounds. Extra-virgin olive oil polyphenols (oleocanthal, hydroxytyrosol) inhibit COX-2 and reduce NF-kB-mediated inflammation. Omega-3 fatty acids from fish (EPA, DHA) compete with arachidonic acid, reducing pro-inflammatory eicosanoid production and enhancing specialized pro-resolving mediators (resolvins, protectins). High fiber from legumes and whole grains improves gut microbiome diversity and short-chain fatty acid production (butyrate), strengthening gut barrier function. Polyphenols activate Nrf2 antioxidant pathways and inhibit amyloid-beta aggregation. The overall dietary pattern reduces oxidative stress, systemic inflammation, insulin resistance, and endothelial dysfunction -- the shared pathophysiology underlying cardiometabolic and neurodegenerative disease.</p>
<h2>Evidence Summary</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular disease</strong>: PREDIMED trial showed 30% relative risk reduction for major CV events (Estruch et al., 2018, NEJM, Evidence Level 1b)</li>
<li><strong>All-cause mortality</strong>: 20-25% reduction in prospective cohort studies (meta-analysis, Evidence Level 1a)</li>
<li><strong>Type 2 diabetes</strong>: 23% reduced incidence in PREDIMED; improved glycemic control (Evidence Level 1b)</li>
<li><strong>Cognitive decline</strong>: MIND diet associated with 53% reduced Alzheimer's risk in observational studies; PREDIMED-Plus showed improved cognitive scores (Evidence Level 2a)</li>
<li><strong>Depression</strong>: Mediterranean diet reduces depression risk by 33% (meta-analysis of 41 studies, Evidence Level 1a)</li>
<li><strong>Cancer</strong>: 10% reduced overall cancer risk; strongest for colorectal and breast cancer (Evidence Level 2a)</li>
<li><strong>Gut microbiome</strong>: Increases Bacteroidetes:Firmicutes ratio and SCFA production (Evidence Level 2b)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<h3>Dosing Information</h3>
<p>Daily: 3-4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, 5+ servings vegetables, 2-3 servings fruit, whole grains, legumes 3+/week. Fish 2-3x/week, nuts daily (30g). Limit red meat to &lt;2x/week. Optional: 1 glass red wine with meals.</p>
<h2>Safety Information</h2>
<h3>Contraindications</h3>
<ul>
<li>Nut or fish allergies (substitute alternative protein sources)</li>
<li>Alcohol use disorder (omit wine component)</li>
<li>Histamine intolerance (limit aged cheeses, wine, fermented foods)</li>
</ul>
