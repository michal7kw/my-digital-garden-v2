<h1>Sauna</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Heat therapy using dry heat (Finnish sauna, 80-100C) or infrared radiation (infrared sauna, 40-60C). Regular use associated with reduced cardiovascular mortality, improved endothelial function, and enhanced heat shock protein expression. Triggers hormetic stress response with adaptations similar to moderate exercise.</p>
<p><strong>Type</strong>: heat_therapy</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Induces heat shock proteins (HSP70, HSP90) via hormetic thermal stress. Increases heart rate to 100-150 bpm (mimicking moderate exercise). Enhances nitric oxide production and endothelial function. Activates sympathetic nervous system acutely. Promotes sweating and toxin elimination. Triggers growth hormone release.</p>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<h3>Dosing Information</h3>
<p>15-20 minutes per session, 2-7 times per week at 80-100C (Finnish) or 40-60C (infrared)</p>
<h2>Safety Information</h2>
<h3>Contraindications</h3>
<ul>
<li>Unstable angina or recent myocardial infarction</li>
<li>Severe aortic stenosis</li>
<li>Acute illness with fever</li>
<li>Pregnancy (especially first trimester)</li>
<li>Uncontrolled hypertension</li>
<li>Alcohol intoxication</li>
</ul>
