<h1>Resistance Band Training</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Resistance band training uses elastic bands or tubes to provide external resistance for strength exercises. Unlike gravity-dependent free weights, bands provide variable resistance that increases as the band stretches, closely matching the human strength curve for many movements. Meta-analyses demonstrate that resistance band training produces comparable muscle strength and hypertrophy gains to free weight training when matched for effort level. The low cost, portability, joint-friendliness, and scalability make it an ideal modality for home training, rehabilitation, and populations who cannot access or tolerate traditional weight training.</p>
<p><strong>Type</strong>: exercise</p>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<h3>Administration</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Frequency</strong>: 2-4 times per week (minimum 48h between same muscle groups)</li>
<li><strong>Duration</strong>: 30-45 minutes per session</li>
<li><strong>Progression</strong>: Move to heavier bands or increase reps/sets as strength improves</li>
</ul>
<h2>Key Benefits</h2>
<ul>
<li>Comparable strength gains to free weights (meta-analysis evidence)</li>
<li>Lower joint stress (no heavy axial loading)</li>
<li>Accommodating resistance (harder at end range where muscles are strongest)</li>
<li>Constant tension (no momentum cheating)</li>
<li>Portable and inexpensive</li>
<li>Can replicate nearly all free weight exercises</li>
</ul>
