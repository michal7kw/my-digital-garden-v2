<h1>Strength Training</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Strength training (resistance training) uses progressive overload with external resistance (weights, bands, bodyweight) to increase muscular strength, hypertrophy, endurance, and power. It is foundational for metabolic health, bone density, functional independence, and longevity. WHO and ACSM recommend 2+ sessions per week targeting all major muscle groups for all adults. Strength training is the primary intervention for sarcopenia prevention -- the age-related loss of muscle mass (3-8% per decade after age 30) that accelerates frailty and mortality.</p>
<p>Meta-analyses demonstrate that regular strength training reduces all-cause mortality by 15%, cardiovascular mortality by 17%, and cancer mortality by 14% (independent of aerobic exercise). It is the single most effective intervention for improving insulin sensitivity via increased skeletal muscle glucose disposal, with effects comparable to metformin. Strength training also provides robust neuroprotective benefits: resistance exercise increases BDNF, IGF-1, and irisin, reduces neuroinflammation, and improves executive function. The combination of strength training with Zone 2 aerobic training forms the foundation of Dr. Peter Attia's longevity exercise framework.</p>
<p><strong>Type</strong>: exercise</p>
<h2>Mechanism</h2>
<p>Mechanical tension and metabolic stress on muscle fibers activate the mTOR (mechanistic target of rapamycin) signaling pathway, the master regulator of muscle protein synthesis. Mechanotransduction through titin and integrin receptors triggers satellite cell activation for muscle fiber repair and hypertrophy. Hormonal responses include acute growth hormone release (300-500% increase), testosterone elevation, and IGF-1 secretion. Mechanical loading on bone (Wolff's law) stimulates osteoblast activity, increasing bone mineral density by 1-3% annually. Increased skeletal muscle mass enhances basal metabolic rate (each kg of muscle burns ~13 kcal/day at rest) and serves as a glucose sink: GLUT4 translocation during and after resistance exercise dramatically improves glucose uptake. Myokine release (IL-6, irisin, BDNF) mediates cross-organ communication with brain, liver, and adipose tissue.</p>
<h2>Evidence Summary</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>All-cause mortality</strong>: 15% reduction with regular resistance training (meta-analysis, Evidence Level 1a)</li>
<li><strong>Cardiovascular mortality</strong>: 17% reduction independent of aerobic exercise (Evidence Level 1a)</li>
<li><strong>Cancer mortality</strong>: 14% reduction (meta-analysis of prospective cohorts, Evidence Level 2a)</li>
<li><strong>Insulin sensitivity</strong>: Improvement comparable to metformin; enhanced glucose disposal via GLUT4 (Evidence Level 1a)</li>
<li><strong>Bone density</strong>: 1-3% annual BMD increase; primary treatment for osteoporosis prevention (Evidence Level 1a)</li>
<li><strong>Sarcopenia</strong>: Only intervention with Level 1a evidence for preventing/reversing age-related muscle loss (Evidence Level 1a)</li>
<li><strong>Cognitive function</strong>: Improved executive function and reduced dementia risk with regular resistance training (Evidence Level 2a)</li>
<li><strong>Mental health</strong>: Significant antidepressant effect (SMD=-0.66) comparable to SSRIs in meta-analyses (Evidence Level 1a)</li>
<li><strong>Dose-response</strong>: 2-4 sessions/week optimal; diminishing returns above 4; 1 session/week maintains gains (Evidence Level 1b)</li>
</ul>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<h3>Dosing Information</h3>
<p>2-4 sessions per week, 8-12 reps per set for hypertrophy, 3-6 reps for strength, 2-4 sets per exercise. Progressive overload (increase load 2-5% when reps are achieved). Compound movements prioritized (squat, deadlift, bench press, row, overhead press). 48h recovery between same muscle groups.</p>
<h2>Safety Information</h2>
<h3>Contraindications</h3>
<ul>
<li>Acute injury to target muscles/joints</li>
<li>Uncontrolled hypertension (Valsalva risk with heavy loads)</li>
<li>Severe osteoporosis (fracture risk with heavy loads; moderate loads beneficial)</li>
<li>Post-surgical recovery (site-specific, physician clearance)</li>
<li>Unstable cardiovascular disease</li>
</ul>
