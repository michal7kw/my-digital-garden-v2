<h1>Sleep Hygiene</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Sleep hygiene refers to the set of behavioral and environmental practices that promote consistent, high-quality sleep. It is the first-line treatment for insomnia (CBT-I protocols build on it) and the foundation for all health optimization — sleep affects every biological system. Matthew Walker's research popularized the understanding that even modest sleep restriction (6 hours vs. 8 hours) measurably impairs immune function, glucose metabolism, hormonal balance, emotional regulation, and cognitive performance. Sleep hygiene is not about any single trick but the cumulative effect of consistent practices.</p>
<p><strong>Type</strong>: lifestyle</p>
<h2>Core Practices</h2>
<h3>Light Management (Most Important)</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Morning</strong>: 10+ min bright outdoor light within 30 min of waking (resets circadian clock)</li>
<li><strong>Evening</strong>: Dim lights 2-3 hours before bed (supports melatonin rise)</li>
<li><strong>Night</strong>: Complete darkness for sleep (blackout curtains, no LEDs)</li>
<li><strong>Blue light</strong>: Filter screens after sunset (Night Shift, f.lux, blue-blocking glasses)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Timing</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Consistent schedule</strong>: Same bed/wake time ±30 min (including weekends)</li>
<li><strong>Sleep opportunity</strong>: 7.5-9 hours in bed (allows for sleep latency)</li>
<li><strong>Avoid late naps</strong>: No naps after 3 PM (preserves sleep pressure)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Environment</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Temperature</strong>: 65-68°F / 18-20°C (core body cooling required for sleep onset)</li>
<li><strong>Darkness</strong>: Complete (or eye mask)</li>
<li><strong>Quiet</strong>: Earplugs or white noise if needed</li>
<li><strong>Bed = sleep</strong>: No screens, work, or worry in bed (stimulus control)</li>
</ul>
<h3>Substances</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Caffeine cutoff</strong>: 10+ hours before bed (half-life 5-7 hours)</li>
<li><strong>Alcohol</strong>: Avoid within 3 hours of bed (fragments sleep architecture)</li>
<li><strong>Heavy meals</strong>: Avoid within 2-3 hours of bed</li>
</ul>
