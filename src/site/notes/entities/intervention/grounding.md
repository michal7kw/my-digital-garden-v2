<h1>Grounding</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Practice of direct physical contact with the Earth's surface (barefoot walking, lying on ground) or using conductive systems connected to ground. Proposed mechanism involves transfer of free electrons from Earth's surface, reducing inflammation and normalizing cortisol rhythm. Evidence is preliminary but growing, with small studies showing improved sleep, pain, and inflammatory markers.</p>
<p><strong>Type</strong>: environmental</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>Proposed electron transfer from Earth's surface provides antioxidant effects by neutralizing reactive oxygen species. May influence cortisol circadian rhythm, reduce blood viscosity (zeta potential changes), and modulate autonomic nervous system. Mechanism still under investigation.</p>
<h2>Protocol</h2>
<h3>Dosing Information</h3>
<p>30-60 minutes barefoot contact with earth daily. Indoor grounding mats/sheets for overnight use. Conductive surfaces include grass, soil, sand, concrete (not asphalt or wood).</p>
<h2>Safety Information</h2>
<h3>Contraindications</h3>
<ul>
<li>Parasitic infections risk in tropical areas (barefoot outdoors)</li>
<li>Open wounds on feet</li>
<li>Electrical grounding pads may interfere with certain medical devices</li>
</ul>
