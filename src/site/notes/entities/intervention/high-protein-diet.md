<h1>High-Protein Diet</h1>
<h2>Overview</h2>
<p>Diet providing 25-35% of calories from protein (1.6-2.4 g/kg/day), emphasizing lean meats, fish, eggs, dairy, and legumes. Supports muscle protein synthesis, satiety, and body composition improvement.<br />
<strong>Type</strong>: diet</p>
<h2>Mechanism of Action</h2>
<p>High protein increases diet-induced thermogenesis (20-30% of protein calories). Leucine-rich meals stimulate mTOR pathway for muscle protein synthesis. Protein promotes satiety via GLP-1 and PYY release. Preserves lean mass during caloric deficit.</p>

<h2>Safety Information</h2>
<h3>Contraindications</h3>
<ul>
<li>advanced chronic kidney disease (GFR &lt;30)</li>
<li>hepatic encephalopathy</li>
<li>gout (if purine-rich sources dominate)</li>
<li>uric acid kidney stones</li>
</ul>
